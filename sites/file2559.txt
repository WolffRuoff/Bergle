	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p><a href="https://www.democracynow.org/about"><img class="size-medium alignleft" src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/01/Democracy_Now%21_logo.svg/1200px-Democracy_Now%21_logo.svg.png" width="1200" height="840" />Democracy Now!</a> produces a daily, global, independent news hour hosted by award-winning journalists Amy Goodman and Juan González. Democracy Now! Internships are paid, 20 hour per week, temporary work placements to help students, people early in their careers and career changers to gain entry-level skills and experience in the fields of outreach, social media, education, fundraising, translation and archiving in a non-profit setting. All internships take place at our Manhattan studio where we produce our daily TV and radio news hour.</p>
<h3>Eligibility Requirements</h3>
<p>Applicants must have completed a high school diploma, <span class="caps">GED</span> or equivalent and must have a valid US work authorization.</p>
<p>Democracy Now! is an equal opportunity employer. People of color, people with disabilities, women and <span class="caps">LGBTQ</span>-identified people are encouraged to apply.</p>
<h3>To Apply</h3>
<p>Review the Internship Descriptions below to choose which department you’d like to work with. Candidates may only apply for one internship per session. <a href="https://www.democracynow.org/about/jobs/internships">Click here for more information</a>.</p>
<p>Interns may work in one of five departments:</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jomr/">Archives Intern</a>.</strong> Archives interns are responsible for cataloging videos, turning paper and tape records into digital media and processing all incoming news feeds. The individuals must have experience working with Mac OS X and Office.</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jtom/"><em><u>Democracy Now!</u></em> en Español</a>.</strong> This internship involves translating English content to Spanish, updating social media accounts and assisting with efforts to grow a Spanish-speaking audience. Individuals must be bilingual and have basic journalism skills.</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jomi">Development &amp; Outreach</a>.</strong> Interns in this area are responsible for maintaining the donor database and processing contributions. Familiarity with Microsoft Word and Excel is preferred.</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jomg/">Education Intern</a>.</strong> Interns in this area invite educators and their classes to visit the set and use the broadcast as a part of their curricula. Previous experience with teaching is beneficial for the role.</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jo6k/">Digital Intern</a>.</strong> Digital interns produce content for <em><u>Democracy Now!</u></em> social media platforms and are responsible for updating social media accounts. Knowledge of social media and basic journalism skills are required.</p>
<p>To apply, send a one page resume, a cover letter, and two work or academic references to the email address specified in the appropriate internship description. In your cover letter, please address these questions:</p>
<ol>
<li>How did you discover Democracy Now!?</li>
<li>What are your goals in seeking out this internship?</li>
<li>What makes you a good candidate for this internship?</li>
</ol>
<p>For summer internship applications, the <span style="color: #ff0000"><strong>deadline is May 15, 2019</strong></span>. Due to the volume of applications received, you will only be contacted if you are selected for an interview. No mail submissions or phone calls please.</p>

		<p><a href="http://wdiy.org/">WDIY Lehigh Valley Public Radio</a> is seeking interns to work in their Bethlehem office this summer. Interns will assist with promotion and marketing including social media updates, recording, editing and voice work. This internship provides experience in production and communications, and could be a great opportunity for anyone interested in moving into radio or sound producing or PR / marketing in the future!</p>
<p>Internships are unpaid but you can register your internship for academic credit.</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re interested, please share your resume and a paragraph expressing your interest with Professor Aggie Ebrahimi Bazaz who can review and share your materials with the WDIY team. Prof. Bazaz can be reached via email: aggiebazaz@muhlenberg.edu</p>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p><strong><span id="JobFullTime">Rodale Publishing: Runner&#8217;s World Digital Editorial Intern</span></strong></p>
<p><span id="JobFullTime">Part-Time<br />
</span><br />
<br />Emmaus, PA, United States</p>
<div class="row">
<div class="col-md-24">
<div>
<div class="row">
<div class="col-md-18">
<div class="row">
<div class="col-md-24">
<p>Rodale is seeking a motivated student to work as a Digital Editorial intern for Runner&#8217;s World Online.  The position will be located in our Emmaus, PA headquarters from June-August 2017 and be part time (30 hours per week.)</p>
<p>This position will be focused on editorial and production-related duties involving the Runner’s World web site and other digital properties. Below are the anticipated responsibilities. Others may be added, depending on business needs and experience or skills of the intern.</p>
<p>
<strong>Responsibilities: </strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Report and write news articles on a regular basis. This will include finding and pitching stories, as well as completing assigned stories.</li>
<li>Help conceive and produce slideshows.</li>
<li>Help with research for online articles, such as contacting sources, conducting internet searches, etc.</li>
<li>Refresh, revise and repackage older content for presentation on the site and in social media.</li>
<li>PhotoShop design work for various projects on the website.</li>
<li>Reviewing legacy articles for SEO strengths and weaknesses, editing as necessary.</li>
</ul>
<p>
<strong>The Ideal Candidate will possess:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Strong awareness of how people use the Web to find information.</li>
<li> Accuracy and attention to detail are mandatory.</li>
<li>Interest in fitness content, and running content in particular.</li>
<li>Ability to handle multiple tasks at once and complete assignments on schedule.</li>
<li>Coursework in journalism or related field.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Objectives:</strong></p>
<p>The intern will gain experience in the daily and long-term production of a magazine web site.</p>
<p><strong>This will include experience in:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Tailoring content for a specific audience</li>
<li>Researching, gathering and confirming information</li>
<li>Reporting and writing effectively for a web site</li>
<li>Understanding search engine optimization</li>
<li>Packaging and delivering content in various digital formats</li>
<li>Understanding what content resonates with readers and tailoring content to meet that appetite</li>
</ul>
<p>Note: This position is <strong><em>not</em> </strong>focused on magazine writing or production.</p>
<p><strong><em>About Rodale Inc.</em></strong> Rodale Inc. is a global health and wellness content company based in Emmaus, Pennsylvania and New York, New York. Known for launching the organic movement in the U.S. in 1942, the company operates today with a mission to “inspire health, healing, happiness and love in the world. Starting with you.” Home to a broad portfolio of best-in-class brands, Rodale engages more than 100 million people globally through an array of products and services that include magazines, books, online, mobile, e-commerce, direct-to-consumer, social, video and events. With 100 editions in 67 countries, Rodale publishes the largest, most established health and wellness lifestyle brands, including <em>Men’s Health</em>, <em>Prevention</em>, <em>Women’s Health</em>, <em>Runner’s World</em>, <em>Bicycling</em>, and <em>Rodale’s Organic Life</em>. </p>
<p>Rodale Books is the premier destination for wellness content with a purpose, publishing conversation-changing titles that include Jessica Alba’s <em>The Honest Life</em>, Alicia Silverstone’s <em>The Kind Diet</em>, former Vice President Al Gore’s <em>An Inconvenient Truth</em>, Starbucks CEO Howard Schultz’s <em>Onward</em>, Maria Rodale’s <em>Organic Manifesto</em> and <em>Scratch</em>, and Dave Asprey’s <em>The Bulletproof Diet</em>.  The Rodale Books website, <a href="http://www.rodalewellness.com/" target="_blank">RodaleWellness.com</a>, is a content and community hub where visitors can find inspiration and information from the world’s top wellness experts. </p>
<p>Rodale also produces numerous branded experiences that draw more than 100,000 participants annually. Rodale is a certified member of The Women’s Business Enterprise National Council (WBENC) and has received numerous industry accolades, including spots on Folio’s Top Places to Work in Media and Greatist’s Healthiest Companies in America lists, and has also been recognized as one of the Top 100 Best Adoption Friendly Workplaces.</p>

<p>Sports Information is a dynamic field that has led to fulfilling careers both in the profession and in related areas such as public relations, marketing, journalism, athletic administration and others. The intern will be introduced to and involved in all aspects of Sports Information at Muhlenberg College, aspects which may include gameday coverage, production of publications, website maintenance, statistics, shooting and editing of photos and videos, social media strategy, live streaming and writing of releases and feature stories. Applicants must have good communication skills and knowledge of sports and should not be an athlete in their main season of competition (i.e., fall athletes can intern in the spring; winter and spring athletes in the fall).</p>
<p>Contact Mike Falk, Sports Information Director, at 484-664-3232 or falk@muhlenberg.edu for more information or to apply.</p>
