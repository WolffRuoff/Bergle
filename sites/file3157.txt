	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=401" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-07-22T15:00:24-04:00">July 22, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-07-22T15:29:55-04:00">July 22, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=401" rel="bookmark">Data on Printing and the Print Quota</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172331" rel="category">Printing and WEPA</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=20867" rel="category">Projects</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=printing" rel="tag">printing</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=wepa" rel="tag">WEPA</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=401#comments">1 Comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>I&#8217;ve compiled the data that we used for establishing the print quota. Please see below for figures, a graphic, and some interpretation.</p>
<p>First, the overall usage chart. The graphic charts dollars <em>loaded</em> to the &#8220;virtual dollar&#8221; accounts, not actual dollars spent. However, since these funds were loaded in $25 increments (which is relatively small) it&#8217;s a reasonable approximation of actual usage.</p>
<p>The horizontal axis is simply a count of students. So it&#8217;s the 2071st student in the data set at the right side. Those numbers are <em>not</em> BergIDs or anything representing a specific student. The vertical axis is dollars spent. The data is obviously sorted from lowest to highest amount loaded.</p>
<p><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Untitled.png" rel="attachment wp-att-402"><img class="wp-image-402 size-full" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Untitled.png" alt="Untitled" width="1440" height="810" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Untitled.png 1440w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Untitled-300x169.png 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Untitled-768x432.png 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Untitled-1024x576.png 1024w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Untitled-1200x675.png 1200w" sizes="(max-width: 1440px) 100vw, 1440px" /></a></p>
<p>There is some &#8220;fuzziness&#8221; to the data on the left. Because all users had at least $25 loaded, even a student that didn&#8217;t print a single page shows up with some amount provided. However, it is still obviously that usage is fairly consistent for most students. What we see is a sudden and extreme spike on the right side.<strong><em> A small number of users represented the majority of printing</em></strong>.</p>
<p>We have adjusted the quota slightly to $75 per student, per semester. This is slightly higher than the 75th percentile. It is also about $10 more than the average amount used for all students. The funds do not carry over from term to term.</p>
<p>Next are some specific numbers of sheets of paper used (this ignores double vs single-sided).</p>
<ul>
<li>September 2015 &#8211; 117,000*</li>
<li>October 2015 &#8211; 101,000*</li>
<li>February 2016 &#8211; 121,088</li>
<li>March 2016 &#8211; 128,622</li>
<li>April 2016 &#8211; 171,923</li>
</ul>
<p>*September and October data is pre-WEPA. This is how many sheets we placed into labs. It is not an exact representation of how many sheets were actually used for printing, as we don&#8217;t know if printers sat empty and unusable for periods of time.</p>
<p>January and May are left out because school was not in session for the entirety of those months.</p>
<p>The pattern is obvious &#8211; usage went from &#8220;very high&#8221; to &#8220;extremely high.&#8221; Even with a release-based system, through which students must purposely select &#8220;print this document that I know I want right now&#8221; as compared to just hitting &#8220;print,&#8221; usage went up significantly. Perhaps this is because of having more stations around campus. Perhaps it&#8217;s because we had a monitoring system that told us when paper was low. <strong><em>Regardless, usage was very high</em></strong>.</p>
<p>With this data, it is clear to us that we cannot continue to offer 100% free printing. We were already concerned about sustainability with our existing usage. The increase raises this concern even further.</p>
<p>Please note that we now see this primarily as a paper consumption and sustainability issue. Yes, OIT has an ancillary desire to control operational and repair costs. But as we examined the problem as a group, we realized that this is about sustainability and paper consumption, not about how much it costs to maintain a printer. The college has a commitment to reducing paper usage, among a number of other initiatives under consideration by the President&#8217;s Committee on Sustainability.</p>
<p>We also recognize that paper usage is a multi-faceted issue. For instance, we will hold workshops on options for interacting with digital documents without printing &#8211; annotating PDFs directly, for instance, through optimized note-taking applications. And of course conversations with faculty will take place as well. Simply ending free printing is not a solution to high paper consumption unto itself.</p>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->

<article id="post-370" class="post post-370 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-printing-and-wepa">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=370" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-05-18T09:34:37-04:00">May 18, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-05-18T09:46:12-04:00">May 18, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=370" rel="bookmark">More on the New Print Quota</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172331" rel="category">Printing and WEPA</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=370#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>We just <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/05/18/new-print-quota/">announced the implementation of a new print quota</a> system starting this summer.</p>
<p>Let&#8217;s face it &#8211; no one likes a print quota. We in OIT would love to keep printing free, and of course no one wants to pay per print. However, after a semester of gathering data put together with the realities of maintaining our own printer fleet, we need to shift how we manage this service on campus.</p>
<p>When we rolled out the <a href="http://www.wepanow.com">WEPA print kiosks</a> in January, we had three goals. The first was to get out of the high-touch printer maintenance business. Our printers ranged from old to very old and required a lot of work to keep going. The second was to gather data. We knew how much paper and toner we were supplying to labs, but we didn&#8217;t know how much print volume was happening overall, per student, high to low, duplex to simplex, etc. Note that we usually found out about printers being out of paper from custodial staff &#8211; we almost never got reports from students. This raised serious concerns about whether we had empty printers sitting out there for extended periods of time. Finally, we feel that supporting sustainability is a key aspiration for every department, and we hoped that the new system would help. It is release-based, meaning one has to purposefully select a job before printing (rather than hitting print and perhaps walking away), there is an emphasis and benefit to double-sided and B&amp;W printing, which is lower impact, and the printers themselves are newer, which mean they use up less energy.</p>
<p>We absolutely did not have a goal to institute a print quota. It was on the table, but it was not a goal, per se.</p>
<p>One thing we were very cognizant of is that there was a &#8220;cost&#8221; to printing far beyond how many dollars we spent on paper, toner, and repairs. For every dollar we spend on supplies and repairs, it&#8217;s one less dollar we can spend on other projects such as WiFi or improvements to our labs or classrooms. We have to find a way to balance all of these priorities, and one area we had to look at was our free printing policy.</p>
<p>We find ourselves staring at data that says a print quota is our best option. Overall usage of the printers is extremely high. That&#8217;s good in the sense that we have adoption of the new kiosks, but it&#8217;s bad in the sense that we aren&#8217;t seeing the usage and supplies savings we had hoped for. We were still sapping dollars from projects such as WiFi. However, after a semester&#8217;s worth of usage data, we find that dollars spent are relatively flat until the 70th percentile, when the amount begins to spike. This means that it is possible to provide a dollar amount that the vast majority of students will find sufficient. We will still be able to provide a high quality experience.</p>
<p>We are still working on the exact dollar amount that meets the right percentage of students.</p>
<p>I realize this does by definition leave some users outside of the quota, and that those students will have to pay for usage. We are trying to find ways to address this, but so far we haven&#8217;t found many easy wins. For instance, there is some correlation between high usage and enrollment in a science CUE course, but not enough to say definitively that we could provide $X to those students and meet that need consistently. We&#8217;ll keep digging through the data.</p>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->

</article><!-- #post-## -->

	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=375" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-05-18T09:15:49-04:00">May 18, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-05-18T09:45:15-04:00">May 18, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=375" rel="bookmark">New Print Quota</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=22152" rel="category">Announcements</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172331" rel="category">Printing and WEPA</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=375#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Starting with Summer 2016, we will be instituting a print quota. Each student will receive $50, at no direct cost. The Office of IT (OIT) will bear this initial cost. However, unlike during spring, we will not be reloading funds after you hit a certain minimum threshold. After the $50 is expended, printing will be at cost (see below for costs per page).</p>
<p>We intend to integrate the <a href="http://www.wepanow.com">WEPA</a> kiosks (<a href="https://www.wepanow.com/maps">campus locations</a>) with your Berg ID card by fall. For now, though, you will need add additional funds either through the WEPA site (<a href="https://www.wepanow.com/login">login using your Muhlenberg email address</a>) or at a kiosk.</p>
<p>I have <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/05/18/printing-muhlenberg-the-print-quota/">another post discussing the reasoning behind this quota</a>. But please know that we did not plan on a quota when we deployed the kiosks. We wanted data to understand printing around campus, and whether it was a sustainable practice and service.</p>
<p>Also, consider that the &#8220;cost&#8221; of printing goes far beyond paper, toner, and printer repairs. Printing has never truly been free to students &#8211; we simply were not explicitly charging for them. For every dollar we spend on toner we spend less on classroom equipment, computer refreshes, and wifi upgrades. Overtime, keeping up with printing for free has become hard to maintain. We are now trying to strike a balance between funding a sufficient quota for your use and funding these other projects.</p>
<div>Per-Page printing costs:</div>
<div>$.09 for single-sided B&amp;W</div>
<div>$.17 for double-sided B&amp;W</div>
<div></div>
<div>$.35 for single-sided Color</div>
<div>$.68 for double Color</div>

<article id="post-354" class="post post-354 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-announcements category-communication-collaboration category-projects">

	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=354" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-05-11T14:19:03-04:00">May 11, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-05-12T10:29:45-04:00">May 12, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=354" rel="bookmark">Google Apps for Education &#8211; Our Next Collaboration Platform</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=22152" rel="category">Announcements</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=170064" rel="category">Communication &amp; Collaboration</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=20867" rel="category">Projects</a></li>
									</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>It is with great pleasure that I announce that we&#8217;ll be moving to Google Apps for Education in the 16-17 Academic Year. This system will not only replace Groupwise for e-mail and calendaring, but also provide powerful collaboration tools such as cloud storage, easy creation and sharing of files, instant messaging, and collaborative online sites.</p>
<p>The Communication &amp; Collaboration committee worked diligently over the past several months to arrive at this recommendation. The committee was chaired by  Tina Hertel, Director of Trexler Library. The Office of IT (OIT) has vetted the technical implementation challenges, and agree that we can move forward with this solution. The exact schedule of the migration will be announced at a later date, in a separate post.</p>
<p>I personally extend my deepest gratitude to Tina and the entire committee (named below) for their work and and dedication. The committee examined all the pros and cons of Google Apps for Education and Microsoft Office365 (the two dominant options in higher education).  It was not an easy decision, and the process was not taken lightly. Their entire process is detailed on their <a href="http://ccec.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/">blog</a>.</p>
<p>Shifting to a new platform that includes enhanced collaboration tools is inline with the current needs of students, faculty and staff and also opens many new doors. We&#8217;ll see impact with group projects in classes, collaborative develop of new policies, project management and a number of new efficiencies. This change will not happen overnight, but I know that Muhlenberg College will see tremendous benefits.</p>
<p>Note that e-mail addresses will <em>not</em> change. The underlying system that drives e-mail will, but addresses will stay the same.</p>
