<p>Valentine’s Day, also known as Saint Valentine’s Day, is observed on February 14th.  Saint Valentine is the patron saint of love, young people, and happy marriages. The tradition of giving a Valentine’s day card comes from Saint Valentine giving the blind daughter of his jailer a note signed “From, your Valentine”.</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Love is in the air…but STI’s don’t have to be!</strong></span></p>
<p>Did you know that February 14th is also National Condom Day? So when you return from your dinner date, make sure you have protection!</p>
<ul>
<li>The condom is one of the most accessible and inexpensive forms of birth control available. In fact, they are free in the Health Center! Stop by!</li>
<li>When it comes to HIV, using a condom makes sex 10,000 times safer than not using a condom.</li>
<li>You should use a condom every time you have sex, even if you are utilizing another method of birth control.</li>
<li>There is no medical reason why someone can’t use a condom. Even an allergy!</li>
</ul>
<p>In case your evening does not go as planned, Plan B is available in the Health Center!</p>
<p><a href="http://muhlenbergpham.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/condoms.jpg"><img src="http://muhlenbergpham.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/condoms.jpg" alt="condoms" width="153" height="153" /></a></p>
<p><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Don’t let your erection get you an infection! (Fun Facts)</span></strong></p>
<ul>
<li>87 condoms are used each second in the U.S. on Valentine’s Day</li>
<li>The earliest evidence of condom use were in cave drawings in 10,000 B.C.</li>
<li>A regular condom can hold 4 qts of liquid</li>
<li>Condoms have been used in various industries for holding corrosive fuel and materials</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="http://muhlenbergpham.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/1354592234_cupid1.jpg"><img src="http://muhlenbergpham.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/1354592234_cupid1-258x300.jpg" alt="1354592234_cupid1" width="137" height="160" /></a></p>
<p><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Benefits of Sex</span></strong></p>
<p>WebMD lists some health benefits of sex &#8211; but be sure to use protection!</p>
<ul>
<li>Helps keep your immune system strong, as long as your partner doesn’t have the flu!</li>
<li>Lowers blood pressure</li>
<li>Helps reduce the risk of heart attack</li>
<li>Eases stress</li>
<li>Reduces pain and helps relieve menstrual cramps</li>
<li>Improves sleep</li>
<li>And always remember to Get tested! &#8211; It’s important to talk to your partner about their sexual history to understand if either of you should be tested for an STI.</li>
</ul>
<p><span style="color: #ff00ff;">Have fun and be safe this Valentine’s Day!</span></p>
<p><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Heart Healthy Month</span></strong></p>
<p>Heart Disease is the leading cause of death in the United States, but don’t worry! There are many ways to reduce your risk for heart disease! These include</p>
<ul>
<li>Maintaining a healthy diet</li>
<li>Exercising daily</li>
<li>Refraining from tobacco use and excessive alcohol consumption</li>
<li>Maintaining a healthy weight</li>
<li>Monitoring your blood pressure and cholesterol</li>
</ul>

<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Look what just Flu in!  (How to Avoid the Flu)</strong></span></p>
<ul>
<li>Number one way to avoid getting the flu is to get the flu vaccine!</li>
<li>Wash your hands frequently with warm water and soap</li>
<li>If washing your hands is not an option, keep alcohol-based hand sanitizer available</li>
<li>Cover your nose and mouth with a tissue when you sneeze or cough; if a tissue is not handy, use your elbow</li>
<li>Avoid close contact with sick people</li>
<li>Stay home and seek medical attention if you’re not feeling well</li>
<li>Clean and disinfect potentially contaminated surfaces</li>
<li>Maintain a healthy lifestyle that includes getting enough rest and exercise</li>
</ul>
