			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/procrastination/" rel="bookmark">Procrastination</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/procrastination/" title="1:06 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 2, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Being a student comes with many challenges. There are essays¬†to write, books to read for classes, speeches to attend, making time for friends, not going crazy and one of the daily struggles every student has probably faced in his/her college career¬†is procrastinating.</p>
<p>You know&#8230; deciding to watch another episode of Gilmore Girls when you know you have to start working on a essay, or deciding to FaceTime your best friend who&#8217;s abroad in Italy when you could be reviewing notes for your Arabic class.</p>
<p>Last night, I could not do what I needed to get done. During the day, I do what I need to do, before I need to do it, but when I get to my room after 8pm, Felisa + homework = bad combination. I would rather do my laundry than focus on my work. I would rather go binge-watch Netflix episodes before sitting down and reviewing my notes from Ancient Philosophy. I work so hard during the day that by the time I get to my room all off that energy goes towards spontaneous activities.</p>
<p>This morning I woke up a little bit earlier to finish my paper and the lovely Essay gods must have heard my prayers last night, because my professor emailed me letting me know that the paper deadline would be extended and that class for the day was¬†canceled. I know I won&#8217;t get lucky like this all the time, but I&#8217;m just going to take in a few seconds to bask in this moment of good fortune, because there&#8217;s a high chance it&#8217;ll never happen again.</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/halloween/" rel="bookmark">Halloween</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/halloween/" title="1:05 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 2, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Well, it&#8217;s that time of the year again. Halloween is right around the corner, and it is time for people to break out their costumes and props. This got me thinking, and I realized that Halloween just doesn&#8217;t come close to having the impact that it used to have. For most kids, this is the most anticipated day and night of the year.</p>
<p>A few weeks before Halloween, my Mom would take my brother and me to the store to pick out that year&#8217;s costume. With so many options, there was always one thing that would stand out and I knew I just had to have. After trying on my costume, we would buy it, and the struggle of waiting for Halloween would only grow that much more.</p>
<p>Eventually, the day would come and everyone would get into their costume before heading to school. After arriving at school, everyone would look at each other&#8217;s costumes and decide which they liked, didn&#8217;t like, or wish they had chosen for themselves. No one could pay attention during class (seems obvious because we were all dressed up in costumes).</p>
<p>After a very entertaining day of school, it was time for the night of all nights. Trick or Treating is¬†something that every kid loves. Excitement would always fill us as we would go from door to door without any idea of what we would be getting at each house. Everyone would race to see who could get the most and best candy. After a night of running, the day would finally come to an end. We would all see what we received and how much we collected. At that point, the count down until next Halloween would begin.</p>
<p>I hope you enjoyed this trip down memory lane; I would imagine that this is something that most people can¬†relate to.</p>

			<div id="post-3393" class="post-3393 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/family-weekend/" rel="bookmark">Family Weekend</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/family-weekend/" title="11:28 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 28, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I know you all have been waiting patiently by your computers for me to write another post, probably refreshing the tab every hour, searching for my name to appear, slightly devastated¬†each time when you realized that a new post was no where to be found.</p>
<p>But IM BACCCKKKK&#8230;</p>
<p>Then again, I didn&#8217;t even go anywhere. I just didn&#8217;t write any posts.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m not one to make excuses, but there was a serious lack of motivation, lack of inspiration, lack of imagination&#8230; and a whole bunch of other lacking going on this past week.</p>
<p>But I&#8217;m back, and better ¬†than ever!</p>
<p>Renewed, refreshed, and slightly recycled. I don&#8217;t know where I&#8217;m going with this, but please attempt to follow my stream of consciousness. My mind&#8217;s all over the place.</p>
<p>This past weekend was Family Weekend, awwhhhh yea. One of my favorite weekends of the year (absolutely not, my family never comes). Seeger&#8217;s Union is jam-packed with families maneuvering about, and there&#8217;s little old me swerving and slipping and sliding in between trying to reach the dining commons. It usually takes me only 30 seconds to get from the entrance of Seeger&#8217;s Union to the entrance to the Dining Commons and¬†let me tell you, it took me about 6 minutes this weekend! The thing is, I knew to stay out of Seeger&#8217;s Union. I&#8217;ve known since freshman year to stay out of Seeger&#8217;s Union during Family Weekend, but by the time 1pm rolled around, my stomach growled, my mood grumped and before I knew it, my feet were¬†heading in the direction of sweet smelling bacon and endless cups of coffee.</p>
<p>I survived, obviously. The coffee helped. I swear I&#8217;m becoming just like my mother. Soon if I don&#8217;t have 3 cups in the morning people will know not to talk to me. (joking, hi mom&#8230; I know you&#8217;re probably the only person who refreshed the tab looking for a new post by me&#8230; miss you).</p>
<p>Some thing I find hysterical about Family Weekend is that most people get more excited about the dogs coming to visit than the actual parents. Why don&#8217;t we just have a Pet Weekend? Imagine the puppies and dogs all over campus&#8230; That would actually be super relaxing and way more fun. That&#8217;s all that college kids ever complain about anyways. Its ridiculous the amount of times I hear my friends and random strangers walking down Academic Row sigh miserably and say, &#8220;I wish my dog was here&#8230;&#8221; or¬†&#8220;Why can&#8217;t we have pets in our dorms again?&#8221; or¬†&#8220;I just want to cuddle with my puppy&#8230;&#8221;.</p>
<p>But it&#8217;s actually a really good question.</p>
<p>The Felisa W Academy of Academic Research has done studies that prove that the companionship¬†between a person and their dog is seven times greater than between that¬†person and another person. Dogs also help lower blood pressure, stress, and are useful for those who have anger issues.</p>
<p>With that being said, I, with the power invested in me by Student Government, propose an amendment to the Academic calendar¬†to¬†set aside a weekend, once a month, and dedicate that weekend to the pets of Muhlenberg students.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<div id="post-3337" class="post-3337 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/greece-the-most-magical-place-on-earth-including-disney/" rel="bookmark">Greece, the Most Magical Place on Earth (including Disney)</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/greece-the-most-magical-place-on-earth-including-disney/" title="11:23 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 28, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/zoebriggs/" title="View all posts by Zo√´ Briggs">Zo√´ Briggs</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<div id="attachment_3385" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/IslandSantorini.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3385" class="size-medium wp-image-3385" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/IslandSantorini-300x168.jpg" alt="Santorini from the ship" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/IslandSantorini-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/IslandSantorini-1024x576.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3385" class="wp-caption-text">Santorini from the ship</p></div>
<p>So our last two port stops while on the cruise were Santorini and Katakolon. Now, back in January, when I was at &#8216;Berg and thinking/planning out my fall break and where I wanted to go, what I wanted to do, etc., I kept coming back to Santorini. I have always wanted to go to Greece and in my mind, that means Santorini, probably because I was obsessed with The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants for way too long when I was younger, but also because everybody who comes abroad and goes to Santorini says, GO TO SANTORINI!! So obviously, when planning my fall break I made sure that I would get to go to Santorini.</p>
<div id="attachment_3387" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/MoreSantorini.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3387" class="size-medium wp-image-3387" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/MoreSantorini-300x168.jpg" alt="Santorini" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/MoreSantorini-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/MoreSantorini-1024x576.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3387" class="wp-caption-text">Santorini</p></div>
<p>The night before we docked in Santorini, I made sure that Danielle and Jakeim (my two friends I went on the cruise with) knew what the game plan was. I wanted everybody up and ready to get off the ship by 8:30am, and I was not messing around. Danielle was all for it; Jakeim was a little more difficult, mostly because he loves sleep so much, but after a whole lot of coaxing and just a tad bit of begging, we all agreed to get off the boat as early as possible. ¬†We also decided to spend the day with Danielle&#8217;s parents who were on the cruise with us too! ¬†I barely slept the night before we docked, because I was so excited.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/MeandGreece.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-3386 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/MeandGreece-168x300.jpg" alt="MeandGreece" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/MeandGreece-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/MeandGreece-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a></p>
<p>The morning as we were approaching the island, I stood on the top deck of the ship and just gawked at the sight before me. ¬†Santorini is incredible. ¬†My pictures could never do it justice. ¬†The island is huge, much larger than I was expecting, and from the water I could see all of the different towns on the different sides of the island, and of course the infamous white houses built into the island itself. ¬†I kept having to pinch myself to make sure I wasn&#8217;t dreaming.</p>
<div id="attachment_3388" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Puppy.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3388" class="size-medium wp-image-3388" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Puppy-300x168.jpg" alt="A little puppy in Santorini" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Puppy-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Puppy-1024x576.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3388" class="wp-caption-text">A little puppy in Santorini</p></div>
<p>There are many towns that make up Santorini, but the one that you have to go to if you ever travel there is Oia. ¬†This is the town that all of the Google images are when you search Santorini. ¬†To get to Oia, we had to take a cable car up from the port and then a mini bus over from Fira. ¬†In lieu of the cable car, you could¬†ride a donkey up the hill, but it was a little too steep and a little too high for my taste (don&#8217;t worry I got to see the donkeys later). We all clambered into a mini bus after our cable car ride (which was also very steep and high, but at least I was in an enclosed space) and headed over to Oia to explore the sights.</p>
<p>(Side note, our driver was crazy awesome and sang a couple of songs with us)</p>
<p align="center"><iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/746P-ntrNRs?rel=0" width="560" height="315" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen"></iframe></p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Skyline.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-3389 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Skyline-300x168.jpg" alt="Skyline" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Skyline-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Skyline-1024x576.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>As soon as we got out of the bus, I was surrounded by beautiful white buildings, marble streets, and sights that stretched out over the entire Mediterranean Sea. ¬†Everywhere you looked, you could see the sea. ¬†We didn&#8217;t really do much in Santorini except take in the sights and walk around, but it was perfect. ¬†Before we got back on the bus to go back to the port, we all had a gyro, of course, and I even bought some Greek cookies (which were so good!)</p>
<p>When we got¬†back to Fira, the city where the port was, we walked past a little shop that had tons of fish tanks in it. ¬†I was confused at first but then realized that it was one of those places where you can get a pedicure by having all these little fishies eat the dead skin off your feet.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Fishfeet.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-3383 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Fishfeet-168x300.jpg" alt="Fishfeet" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Fishfeet-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Fishfeet-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a></p>
<p>It was so gross but also so cool, so we of course had to try it! ¬†After a whole lot of squealing and picture to prove that I did it, we headed back to the cable car to go back to the ship.</p>
<p>Here is a lovely video: enjoy the terror, excitement, and disbelief.</p>
<p align="center"><iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/MrELaIf-kyA?rel=0" width="560" height="315" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen"></iframe></p>
<p>However, once we were there, we realized that the line for the cable car was incredibly long &#8212; like three-hours wait long. ¬†So we all decided to walk down the hill, you know, the way the donkeys go, the really high up and steep pathway that we had successfully avoided when traveling up the mountain. ¬†Well now I found myself walking down, squeezing through donkeys, and trying to not slip on the stone steps. ¬†It was a little terrifying but we made it! ¬†And I got to see a whole lot of donkeys, which kind of look like Mules, which is the mascot of &#8216;Berg, so naturally I was super excited; even though I know that donkeys are not mules, I promise, I still got excited.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Donkey.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-3382 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Donkey-300x168.jpg" alt="Donkey" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Donkey-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/Donkey-1024x576.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>Overall my fall break was incredible! ¬†I got to travel all around Europe, live on a boat, and spend time with really amazing people. ¬†Being abroad is hard sometimes &#8212; really hard sometimes &#8212; but so unbelievably worth it. ¬†I would not trade this experience for the world and am dreading the day I have to leave this wonderful place. ¬†But for now, I get to come home each day to the villa, to these incredible experiences, and to these amazing people.</p>
<p>Until next time, Ciao!</p>
<div id="attachment_3384" style="width: 178px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/HeadstandOlympia.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3384" class="size-medium wp-image-3384" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/HeadstandOlympia-168x300.jpg" alt="One last headstand in Greece" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/HeadstandOlympia-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/HeadstandOlympia-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3384" class="wp-caption-text">One last headstand in Greece</p></div>
