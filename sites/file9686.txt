				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>The last full weekend of the fall season has two teams competing for Centennial Conference championships and another trying to earn a playoff spot.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-46" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>Men’s Soccer vs. Ursinus (Saturday, 11:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules (9-5-1, 5-3) are in a three-way tie for third place in the CC with Franklin &amp; Marshall and Swarthmore. They need a win or a tie to clinch a spot in the CC playoffs for the 11th straight year.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg is undefeated (5-0-1) at home this season and undefeated (25-0-2) against Ursinus since 1985. The Bears (3-11-3, 2-5-1) have already played spoiler once this season, defeating then-No. 9 Swarthmore two weeks ago.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/fhlive/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/msoclive/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><strong>Cross Country at Centennial Conference Championships (Saturday, 11:00)</strong></p>
<p>Dickinson is the host for the first CC championship event of the 2011-12 school year. The Mule women are ranked fifth in the region and will look to improve on their sixth-place finish of a year ago. The men also were sixth in 2010.</p>
<p><strong>Football vs. Dickinson (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>It may be Family Weekend at Muhlenberg, but it will feel like Homecoming for the football team, which has not played at home in four weeks. Turnovers could play a role in the outcome, as the Mules (4-3, 4-2) lead the CC with 22 takeaways and the Red Devils (3-4, 3-3) have a league-high 22 giveaways.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg leads the all-time series 39-25-2. In the last two games at Scotty Wood Stadium, a team came back from at least 16 points down to win.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/livestats/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/livestats/xlive.htm</a></p>

<p>the game will also be televised live on Service Electric TV2</p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey vs. Ursinus (Friday, 7:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules wrap up their season on Senior Night against the 11th-ranked team in Division III.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/fhlive/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/fhlive/xlive.htm</a></p>

						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/18/going-dancing/" rel="bookmark">Going Dancing</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/18/going-dancing/" title="5:11 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-10-18T17:11:11-04:00">October 18, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>In the college sports vernacular, the &#8220;Big Dance&#8221; has come to mean the NCAA Tournament. And while the Muhlenberg women&#8217;s cross country team has as good a chance as it ever has to earn an NCAA bid &#8211; the Mules are ranked fifth in the Mideast Region &#8211; one of the team members has another &#8220;Big Dance&#8221; on her mind.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/greenberghs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-334" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/greenberghs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Senior Ashley Greenberg is one of the co-chairs of Dance for a Cure, a charity event scheduled for Friday, November 11, from 10 p.m. to midnight in the Seegers Union Event Space.</p>
<p>This is the third straight year Greenberg has been one of the committee chairs, and she is hoping to make her last year the best. This year the committee partnered with Colleges Against Cancer, an on-campus organization that helps run Relay for Life, to assist with promoting the event.</p>
<p>&#8220;We&#8217;re trying to make it a bigger deal,&#8221; said Greenberg, who estimates that the event raised a few thousand dollars the last two years. &#8220;In the past it was mostly a fraternity-sorority thing. This year we want to get more athletes and teams involved. The softball team has been helping with the planning.&#8221;</p>
<p>Money will be raised through a raffle (the big prize is a Kindle), admission tickets and donations. The proceeds will go to the American Cancer Society.</p>
<p>&#8220;I haven&#8217;t been directly affected by cancer, but some of my friends have so I&#8217;ve seen the <a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/GREENBERG.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-335" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/GREENBERG.jpg" alt="" width="309" height="325" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/GREENBERG.jpg 309w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/GREENBERG-285x300.jpg 285w" sizes="(max-width: 309px) 100vw, 309px" /></a>impact on others,&#8221; said Greenberg. &#8220;I just really like to help people. It&#8217;s fun to see people show up for a good cause.&#8221;</p>
<p>To do so with a dance is a natural for Greenberg, a dance minor who has been dancing she was in second grade. She went to a performing arts middle school, where dance was a part of her day, and danced competitively in the Maccabi Games for two summers.</p>
<p>Dancing has taken its toll on Greenberg and made her unable to run for most of this season. But she still has enjoyed the incredible success of the Mules, who finished 25th at the regional race just two years ago.</p>
<p>&#8220;I&#8217;ve really been proud of our team and my teammates,&#8221; she said. &#8220;I can&#8217;t believe how driven each teammate has been, and how much hard work and effort have gone into the team.&#8221;</p>
<p>Just like the effort she is putting into Dance for a Cure. &#8220;We want to make an impact on Muhlenberg&#8217;s campus with this event,&#8221; she said. &#8220;We really want to make people want to do something good for others.&#8221;</p>

			<p>Is it October already? Indeed it is, and the Mules teams begin the second half of their 2011 seasons on a weekend filled with home events.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-104" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>Volleyball vs. Dickinson (Saturday, 11:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules dig playing at home. In their last four matches at Memorial Hall, they defeated Albright and McDaniel and were very competitive with strong Moravian and Haverford teams. Now they’ll try to even their Centennial Conference record against 2-10 Dickinson.</p>
<p>The Mules also dig pediatric cancer research. Muhlenberg volleyball and the Centennial Conference are working together this year to raise money for Tanner&#8217;s Touch, a non-profit organization out of Westminster, Md., that raises awareness and funds to support pediatric cancer research. The Mules (who also play Mount St. Mary at 3:00) will collect pledges and donations for every dig they record in the match against Dickinson.</p>

<p>Muhlenberg will try to make it two wins in a row and capture its first Centennial Conference win of the season. Dickinson is also 0-3 in the CC.</p>

	<article id="post-219" class="post-219 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-cross-country category-softball category-track-field category-volleyball">
		<header class="entry-header">
						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/29/poster-children-for-true-student-athletes/" rel="bookmark">Poster Children for True Student-Athletes</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/29/poster-children-for-true-student-athletes/" title="2:27 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-29T14:27:17-04:00">September 29, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>Last Friday, 45 Muhlenberg College students presented at the Science Summer Research Poster Session, a culmination of their summer work. Each on-campus summer science research student was awarded an 8-10 week stipend supported by either internal College grants, some external funding or generous alumni.</em></p>
<p><em>Among those presenting were five athletes.  We asked them about their research projects and life as a student-athlete.</em></p>
<p><strong>Michael Baer, Track &amp; Field</strong> (A Possible Role for nhr-239 in Sensory Response)</p>
<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/baer.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-220" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/baer.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="287" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/baer.jpg 500w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/baer-300x172.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a>Describe your research project.</em> I study feeding behavior in the nematode C. elegans. Specifically, I am working on uncovering the function of a poorly studied gene, nhr-239, because when we delete this gene the nematodes show a different feeding pattern. Right now we think that this change has to do with oxygen sensation.</p>
<p><em>Why did you choose this topic? </em>I originally joined Dr. Wightman&#8217;s lab because genetics is a field of study that I find very interesting and may continue to study in the future. I enjoy my topic because of all the unknowns present. Our lab is the first to work on the function of nhr-239.</p>
<p><em>What did it mean to you to be named an Academic All-American last year? </em>Being named an Academic All-American last year was a great honor, especially since athletics and academics are the largest parts of my life at Muhlenberg. I&#8217;ve always viewed myself as a student-athlete, which made this award such an honor for me.</p>
<p><strong>Nicki Cronin and Nate Crossette, Cross Country/Track &amp; Field</strong> (Calibrations for High Energy Physics)</p>
<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/crossette.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-221" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/crossette.jpg" alt="" width="490" height="310" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/crossette.jpg 490w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/crossette-300x189.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/crossette-474x300.jpg 474w" sizes="(max-width: 490px) 100vw, 490px" /></a>Describe your research project.</em> (NIcki) We were studying the properties of the quark gluon plasma, which was created from the collisions of gold nuclei. This interests us because it recreates the conditions microseconds after the big bang and we are interested studying these properties of the early universe. Specifically, we worked with a detector called the Muon Piston Calorimeter and calibrating its response to the photons it detected from the pi zero decays.</p>
<p>(Nate) The Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), a particle collider in Long Island, is powerful enough to create a quark gluon plasma, the hottest known matter in the universe, when it smashes gold nuclei together at great enough speeds. Detectors surrounding the collision make measurements by detecting particles that were created when the plasma cools and expands. My contribution to the research collaboration at RHIC was to help calibrate the Muon Piston Calorimeter, a subsystem of a detector, using computer simulations and collision data.</p>
<p><em>Why did you choose this topic?</em> (Nate) I chose this research because when asked what I want to do with physics, I don’t have an answer. I want to find an answer to that question. My favorite part of this experience was learning how to write computer code that models physical phenomena.</p>
<p><em>What did you enjoy most about it? </em>(Nicki) This was an area of physics that I had never explored before and I truly learned a lot in the process about nuclear and particle physics as well as computer programming. It was an amazing and unique experience to not only be able to work with other students from Muhlenberg, but with physicists from around the world who have been doing research like this for years.</p>
<p><em>Do you take more pride in your athletic achievements or your academic achievements?</em> (Nicki) Personally, I take more pride in my academic achievements. Athletics is a good addition in my life, I love running with my team and it helps keep me balanced, but ultimately, it is my academics that is going to take me where I want to be in life. For me, academics is the number one priority.</p>
<p><em>Is it more challenging to understand physics or run an 8K in the mud?</em> (Nate) Understanding physics and running an 8k through the mud are both fun, yet challenging in opposite ways. Running is simple, so it’s very easy to overcomplicate. During a race, the challenge is to focus on telling your body to relax and run faster, even when you are very tired. Physics is really complicated, so the challenge is thinking of scenarios that demonstrate physics principles in a way that makes it easy to understand while not oversimplifying. I don’t think I could pick which one is harder. I enjoy both.</p>
<p><strong>Melissa Ugelow, Cross Country/Track &amp; Field</strong> (Tailoring Porous Silicon for Analyte Response)</p>
<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/ugelow.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-222" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/ugelow.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="321" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/ugelow.jpg 500w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/ugelow-300x192.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/ugelow-467x300.jpg 467w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a>Describe your research project.</em> My research this summer focused on assessing and characterizing porous silicon and its sensing properties. The ultimate goal of the project is to be able to tailor the surface of porous silicon for analyte response.  </p>
<p><em>What did you enjoy most about it?</em> What I enjoyed the most about this topic was that it was so new to me.  I was able to learn so many new analytical techniques and use new instruments.  </p>
<div><em>How do you balance being a year-round athlete with being a chemistry major?</em> Sometimes it is hard to balance being a chemistry major with sports all year round but I have been very lucky that all of my professors have been very understanding and helpful. All the chemistry professors leave their office doors open and want you to ask them questions if you need help. Sometimes it is difficult to balance the workload with the practice and meet schedule, but I think that if I did not run, I would not be as focused and motivated as I am now.</div>
<div> </div>
<div><strong>Erica Wenzel, Volleyball/Softball</strong> (POGIL Patterns: Indentifying &amp; Documenting Effective Elements &amp; Practices)</div>
<div> </div>
<div><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/wenzel.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-223" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/wenzel.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="330" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/wenzel.jpg 500w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/wenzel-300x198.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/wenzel-454x300.jpg 454w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a>Describe your research project. </em>Over the summer, I worked with Dr. Kussmaul on researching patterns in POGIL (process oriented guided inquiry learning) activities. I analyzed the questions of the activities to determine what types of questions followed each other and then created another set of patterns, which would be used in future research.</div>
<div> </div>
<div><em>What did you enjoy most about it?</em> I am extremely grateful to get this experience. The part I liked the most was learning about POGIL and how to implement it in the classroom.</div>
<div> </div>
<div><em>What are some of the qualities you learn as an athlete that help you as a student? </em>Two important qualities I learned through athletics are time management and responsibility. As an athlete, you need to manage homework, studying, practices and games. It&#8217;s not always easy, but its important to stay on top of school as well as sports. Being on a team is a big responsibility. You have to look out for your teammates, make sure everything is ready for practice/games, and you need to take responsibility for your schoolwork if you are missing class due to a game as well.  Both of these qualities will be extremely helpful after college.</div>
