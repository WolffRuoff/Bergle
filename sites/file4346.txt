			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/08/summer-sun-into-fall-fun/" title="9:59 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">August 21, 2012</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/danielle/" title="View all posts by Danielle">Danielle</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hi everyone! I am Danielle and I am a junior at Muhlenberg. I am an American Studies major and I am on the education track! I’ve always known I wanted to be a teacher, and I am excited to continue studying education this fall! Welcome to my first blog post! While I love meeting new people, I anxiously anticipate meeting the new freshman class at Muhlenberg. In fact, I am an orientation leader, so I will be one of the first to meet the new “Muhlenbergers.” Orientation is a whirlwind of introductions. I expect the new students are probably a bit more anxious than I am, but I hope they are just as excited! I can’t wait to share my Muhlenberg experience with them!</p>
<p>I spent this summer living at Muhlenberg taking classes and working in Admissions. Muhlenberg is much more active during the school year, but it has been a great experience—I even learned how to cook (kind of), since I was on my own for dinner.  In those times where my cooking was inedible, microwavable meals never served to fail me. This summer, my friends and I who lived at Muhlenberg, spent our free time barbecuing, hanging out, watching the Olympics, and OFTEN going out for frozen yogurt. Two new frozen yogurt places opened up by Muhlenberg, YoFresh and Menchies—it’s debatable which one is better! I say both are worth the trip!</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Student Workers from Admissions:</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/08/summer1.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1103" title="summer" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/08/summer1.jpg" alt="" width="159" height="212" /></a><span style="text-align: center;"> </span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Frozen Yogurt Outing:</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/08/yofresh2.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1105" title="yofresh" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/08/yofresh2.jpg" alt="" width="221" height="220" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/08/yofresh2.jpg 221w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/08/yofresh2-150x150.jpg 150w" sizes="(max-width: 221px) 100vw, 221px" /></a></p>
<p>While it was a change living away from my home in Connecticut this summer, it was nice to live on campus! I am from a small town on the shoreline called Madison, CT. The beach is a few miles from my house, which made it a little bit more difficult to be away from home this summer. I am so spoiled to be close to a beach in CT, but Muhlenberg is also home to me, so I felt right in place living here in Allentown! After spending two years at Muhlenberg it is truly a second home to me. I sometimes refer to it as home in front of my mother, which I am not sure she appreciates, haha. I am so excited for everyone to be back on campus! Muhlenberg is  so vibrant when it is full of students. This year I am living in my sorority house with a close friend, and I can’t wait to move in. 12 days and counting until the first official day of school! Next time you here from me, I’ll be all moved in, and will have experienced my first day of freshman orientation as an orientation leader:D</p>

			<div id="post-1114" class="post-1114 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/08/senior-year-already/" rel="bookmark">Senior Year Already?</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/08/senior-year-already/" title="9:19 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">August 21, 2012</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>As the shopping list for my room at college lengthens, I am getting really excited for the year to come. I&#8217;ve had a great summer. I was lucky enough to get an internship at a theatre company this summer in Maryland. I worked in the education department doing administrative work for the summer programs. It was a great learning experience and seeing the children having such a great time because of the work I was doing was rewarding. People say that internships are important and I wholeheartedly agree because I was able to gain real work experience in a field I am interested in this summer.<br />
Also, I was able to go up to Muhlenberg twice over the summer to see the amazing shows that the Summer Music Theatre at Muhlenberg put on. The productions are always such high quality so I love making the trip up to see them.<br />
But now the summer is drawing to a close, which means the start of senior year. I cannot believe three years of college have already passed by. That being said, I am very excited for all that is in store for me during my senior year. So far, I was elected to be President for the Muhlenberg Theatre Association which is a huge responsibility but i am exited for it. I will be directing a Mainstage production in the Spring semester. Along with that i will be a Resident Liason for ORS.<br />
With all of these things going on during my senior year, there is also that idea that this is the time to be searching for that next step whether that be a job, grad school or something else. There are a lot of resources at Berg to help me figure out what my next step will be. Great professors and wonderful advisors are behind me and they are helping me to figure it out.So I will be sure to keep you all posted as I navigate my senior year whatever choice I decide to make.  I know it is going to be a busy year but I honestly cannot wait. I am ready to find out what my next step will be but first I have senior year. I am ready for the year to come and I am excited for what is in store.</p>

			<div id="post-1058" class="post-1058 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/05/this-isnt-goodbye-its-just-see-you-later/" rel="bookmark">This Isn’t Goodbye, It’s Just See You Later</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/05/this-isnt-goodbye-its-just-see-you-later/" title="12:39 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">May 23, 2012</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Throughout my time at Muhlenberg, I have gotten to know members of the class of 2012 incredibly well. They have been people that I have looked up to and have become some of my closest friends. I got to go back up to campus this week for Senior Ball. It is a sort of last dance with all the seniors, and I was lucky enough to go.</p>
<div id="attachment_1059" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/P1010810.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1059" class="size-medium wp-image-1059" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/P1010810-300x201.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="201" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/P1010810-300x201.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/P1010810-1024x686.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1059" class="wp-caption-text">My friends at Senior Ball. Seniors Ari and Amanda and my friend Molly.</p></div>
<p>It was wonderful to see all the seniors one last time before they graduate. The seniors that I know are confident, loud, thoughtful, caring, crazy, exciting and so many other things, and that is what I am going to miss the most about them. They have accomplished so much over their four years at Muhlenberg, and I am so lucky to call them my friends.</p>
<div id="attachment_1060" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/P1010821.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1060" class="size-medium wp-image-1060" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/P1010821-300x203.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="203" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/P1010821-300x203.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/P1010821-1024x694.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1060" class="wp-caption-text">My roommates all together at last! Senior Dan, my friend James, and seniors Kelly and Ari (from left to right)</p></div>
<p>Now, if I can talk to the seniors for a second, I just want to say a little something. You have gone on a four-year journey, and by the time you  read this, you will have graduated. This isn’t the end though. It is just the closing of one chapter of your life that was filled with many ups, and some downs as well, throughout your time at college. All of these moments have prepared you for whatever is to come next in your lives. Based on the class of 2012 that I do know, you are all ready to take on what comes next. Go for it. Congratulations!</p>
<p>And for me? Well, I have the summer just preparing myself for what will come in the final chapter of my Muhlenberg experience. I was elected to be the President of the Muhlenberg Theatre Association, and I am getting the opportunity to direct my own show in the spring. While it is a lot to take on, I am just so appreciative of getting these opportunities that I am ready for whatever comes my way. It is going to be a challenging but more than that its going to be an exciting senior year. I can’t wait.</p>

			<div id="post-1042" class="post-1042 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/05/bye-bye-sophomore-year/" rel="bookmark">Bye Bye, Sophomore Year!!!</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/05/bye-bye-sophomore-year/" title="11:21 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">May 14, 2012</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/eliana/" title="View all posts by eliana">eliana</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Right now I am sitting at gate B10 at the Philadelphia International airport, anxiously awaiting the plane that will take me back to Chicago for the first since the beginning of January. Finals are over. And I am officially a Junior in college. I can&#8217;t believe it.</p>
<p>The last couple weeks of school are always an interesting mix of work, stress, fun, friends and good-byes. Although good-byes are always hard, I got to say farewell to the school with style! I went to the Muhlenberg Theatre Association formal at Brewworks the Friday before finals. It was a fabulous way to blow off steam before we all had to hit the books for finals.</p>
<div class="mceTemp" style="text-align: center;">
<dl>
<dt><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/581623_10150772636041314_505001313_9905425_1261709788_n4.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-1047 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/581623_10150772636041314_505001313_9905425_1261709788_n4-225x300.jpg" alt="" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/581623_10150772636041314_505001313_9905425_1261709788_n4-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/581623_10150772636041314_505001313_9905425_1261709788_n4.jpg 720w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></dt>
<dd class="wp-caption-dd">Me with Lana, and seniors Angela, and Laura. I&#8217;m gonna miss these girls!!!</dd>
</dl>
</div>
<p>MTA formal was a blast (albeit a little sweaty), and when I returned to campus I got to celebrate one of my favorite events on campus&#8230;.Midnight Breakfast!</p>
<p>From 11-2am, the Friday before finals in the Spring semester, the administrative staff prepare and serve breakfast to hundreds of hungry students. Pancakes have never tasted so good.</p>
<p>When I then woke up on Saturday, I hit the books and started working on several term papers and began studying for exams and preparing for scene presentations&#8230;.Phew.</p>
<p>On Sunday I had the opportunity to spend some quality time with the Muhlenberg Tour Guide community. The exec board planned and organized a tie-dye and ice cream get together. I was very adamant about the tie-dyeing part&#8230;who doesn&#8217;t like tie-dyeing? No one. That&#8217;s who.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/1-13-e1336834110791.jpeg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-1051 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/1-13-e1336834110791-225x300.jpg" alt="The product of my efforts. I am pleased!" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/1-13-e1336834110791-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/1-13-e1336834110791.jpeg 240w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>Since I am going abroad next Fall, I will be relieved of my tour guide/tour guide exec  duties. We had a lot of good times this semester, and I will miss it a lot!</p>
<div id="attachment_1052" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/1-2.jpeg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1052" class="size-medium wp-image-1052" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/1-2-300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/1-2-300x225.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/1-2.jpeg 320w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1052" class="wp-caption-text">Me and the rest of tour guide exec board (Minus Avi and Josh)</p></div>
<p>After that, I was done with study breaks for about five days. I spent what felt like hundreds of hours in Seegers Union, and drank what felt like gallons of coffee, but despite the work and the stress, I got the opportunity to spend some quality time with my friends.</p>
<p>The papers were written. The scene presentations were performed. And the exams were completed. And now I am here. With about ten minutes before I board the plane &#8211; the only thing between me and home.</p>
<p>I am going to miss the Seniors terribly. These were the people that I looked up to the minute I stepped on this campus, and now they are moving on to the real world. I know we will keep in touch. Among the hardest people to say good bye to was Ari, my MTA big:</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/576456_10151658977765511_731420510_23590447_1345095361_n.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-1053 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/576456_10151658977765511_731420510_23590447_1345095361_n-229x300.jpg" alt="Me and my Big! I'm gonna miss this guy! " width="229" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/576456_10151658977765511_731420510_23590447_1345095361_n-229x300.jpg 229w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/576456_10151658977765511_731420510_23590447_1345095361_n.jpg 735w" sizes="(max-width: 229px) 100vw, 229px" /></a>&#8212;-&gt;Note the sad look on my face.</p>
<p>It was also hard saying good bye to some of my friends that I knew I wouldn&#8217;t see for the next eight months as I journey to London for the Fall. It is sad, but I cannot wait to begin this exciting chapter of my life.</p>
<p>This is my last blog for the year. It has been a pleasure writing for it. I hope everyone has a fantastic Summer!</p>
<p>Peace out!</p>
