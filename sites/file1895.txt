	    <div class="post-meta">
		
			<span class="post-date"><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=1531" title="Communication Internship (Allentown School District Foundation)&#8211;paid!">April 27, 2016</a></span>
			
			<span class="date-sep"> / </span>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p><strong>Foundation Information:</strong></p>
<p>The Allentown School District Foundation is the non-profit arm of the Allentown School District. The mission is to encourage, promote and support innovative educational experiences with enriched programs for K-12 students. We also offer every year Teacher Innovation Grants and “Scholarships to Seniors” through the ASDF endowment.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Communications Internship:</strong></p>
<p>For the foundation to succeed, it must be able to communicate effectively the programs and services it offers to support its mission. This internship, communications, requires a strong writing background —to work on news releases, update brochures, initiate direct mail, compile an annual report, a digital newsletter and announcements. Also building and managing the email list to carry out distribution is a priority. The intern candidate must be working toward a communication degree. In addition, familiarity with such programs as MS-Excel, MS-Word, MS-PowerPoint, Constant Contact, Mail Chimp, GoFundMe, Survey Monkey and social media programs (Facebook, LinkedIn, Pinterest, etc.) is important. WordPress, video and camera skills are a plus. The ability to independently research and correspond using telephone, Internet, libraries, ASD information support is key to successfully meet foundation objectives in this area. You will be responsible to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Research a subject quickly yet thoroughly.</li>
<li>Compile and format to support the ASD Foundation image.</li>
<li>Obtain approvals, as needed</li>
<li>Provide creative slogans and taglines, concept outlines for presentations, etc.</li>
<li>Write quickly and accurately with the appropriate length, tone and style; be a good speller, fact checker and proofreader.</li>
<li>Quickly gather the foundation communication objective of each assignment and help build the case for our work.</li>
<li>Assist in both project organization and deadlines, and creative flair as needed.</li>
<li>Develop direct mail e-lists by category.</li>
<li>Develop the e-systems we need to manage reservations, using such tools as EventBrite, and direct mail, such as Mail Chimp, for announcements of all kinds.</li>
<li>Be a team player – friendly, enthusiastic, confidential are all necessary traits.</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Stipends:</strong></p>
<p>A stipend is available for three months of work at $1,800.00, payable as the project progresses every month or at the end of the internship. This translates to 15 hours (flexible) per week at $10.00 per hour for 12 weeks, which can be modified to match an intern’s time availability. You will be working at the Allentown School District Administration Center for the foundation. Parking costs, if any, will be paid by the foundation.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>To apply:</strong></p>
<p>Please email your resume to Susan L. Williams, Executive Director, Allentown School District Foundation at williamssu@allentownsd.org. Please include your technology skills, any digital design abilities and communication strengths.<br />
Or, you can also mail your resume to: Allentown School District Foundation, ASD Administration Center, PO Box 328, 31 South Penn Street, Allentown, PA 18105. Thank you.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>For more information:</strong></p>
<p>Call 484-765-4093 or 610-216-3334 for more information.</p>

		<p>The Swain School is a private school in Allentown serving preschool through 8th grades. The primary responsibility of the intern is to become acquainted with the following roles, and to implement these activities as appropriate and under the supervision of the Director of Communications:</p>
<ol>
<li>Media relations (identifying appropriate news items, writing releases and sending them to appropriate media)</li>
</ol>
<ol>
<li>Web manager (updating the site with appropriate information)</li>
<li>Publications (writing, designing and proofing newsletters and brochures, both print and electronic)</li>
<li>Internal communications (creating and producing informational flyers, both print and electronic; creating electronic communications)</li>
<li>Social media (posting appropriate news and photos)</li>
</ol>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Specifically, the intern will have responsibility for these projects:</p>
<ol>
<li>Create a marketing plan for two school events, utilizing resources such as internal newsletters and flyers, news releases, advertisements, website information, social media, and online calendars</li>
<li>Develop news releases and photo features about specific school events</li>
<li>Contribute articles to at least four school e-newsletters</li>
</ol>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The intern will:</p>
<ul>
<li>Report directly to the Director of Communications and work with other staff as appropriate.</li>
<li>Successfully secure clearance from “Request for Criminal Record Check”, “Pennsylvania Child Abuse History Clearance”, and the FBI (the school will assume this cost).</li>
<li>Submit two letters of character recommendation.</li>
<li>Work a minimum of ten hours per week.</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The intern should have these qualifications:</p>
<ul>
<li>Excellent verbal and written communication skills</li>
<li>Proficiency in Microsoft Word; familiarity with Google documents is a plus</li>
<li>Digital photography and photo editing skills are a plus</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This is an unpaid internship and may be taken for credit or not. Available both during the school year and summers.</p>

Carol Burns; Director of Communications; 610-433-4542, ext. 150; cburns@swain.org</p>

		<p align="left"><strong>Gallery at the Jewish Community Center</strong></p>
<p align="left"><strong>Internship Opportunity: Community Outreach Coordinator</strong></p>
<p align="left">
<p align="left">REPORTS TO: Assistant Executive Director</p>
<p align="left">
EDUCATIONAL REQUIREMENTS: Enrolled as student working towards Bachelor’s Degree in Marketing, Communications, Art History, Commercial Design or a related discipline.</p>
<p align="left">PROFESSIONALREQUIREMENTS:Minimum of two years educational credits toward degree.</p>
<p align="left">REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES: Computer skills including MS Office, CS Applications, Photoshop, InDesign, Illustrator, and related graphics programs. Working knowledge of social media operations, including Facebook and Twitter.  Ability to write press releases and web content management.</p>

<p align="left">Attention to detail and accuracy and capable of working independently.</p>
<p align="left">POSITION SUMMARY: Promote the program outreach goals of the JCC.  Utilize social media and existing media resources to promote the programs to the community. Develop databases and target potential opportunities to grow community participation in JCC programs. Coordinate with management and volunteer committees for events including the JCC Art Gallery, the JCC Craft Show and other projects as outlined by the Assistant Executive Director. Meet weekly with volunteer mentors to coordinate opportunities for Gallery artists to interface with JCC members of all ages.</p>
<p align="left">JCC COMMITMENT: The successful candidate will participate in an innovative environment and learn to understand non-profit operations. The JCC management will provide oversight and learning opportunities for the student interested in augmenting their academic studies with hands on experience. Hours are flexible and designed to integrate into the classroom schedule.  The JCC will coordinate with the academic advisors to insure the internship meets academic requirements as determined by the educational institution.  This is an unpaid internship. The JCC will provide reports and assessments as required by the academic institution in order to be eligible for internship accreditation.</p>
