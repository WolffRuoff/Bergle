<h3>Update on campus phishing simulations</h3>
<p><!-- /wp:heading --></p>
<p><!-- wp:paragraph --></p>
<p>Our cybersecurity awareness and training continues with monthly phishing simulations.</p>
<p><!-- /wp:paragraph --></p>
<p><!-- wp:paragraph --></p>
<p>Thank you to those who reported a phish through Google and/or to the Support Desk. Due to your increased awareness and vigilance, our phish-prone percentage has decreased from 8.9% to 5.9% over the past two months!</p>
<p><!-- /wp:paragraph --></p>
<p><!-- wp:paragraph --></p>
<p>Note: The follow-up cybersecurity training module will be updated with new content on Friday, January 10. If you were auto enrolled in this training as a result of clicking, forwarding or opening an attachment from a phishing simulation email and have not yet completed the training, you will be enrolled in the new training module.</p>

<p><strong>What to do if you receive a suspicious email</strong></p>
<p><!-- /wp:paragraph --></p>
<p><!-- wp:list --></p>
<ul>
<li>Report Phish in Gmail
<ul>
<li>Open the message you&#8217;d like to report.</li>
<li>At the top-right corner of the message, click the down arrow next to the &#8220;Reply&#8221; button</li>
<li>Select Report Phishing</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>If you receive something suspicious and wish to report it to OIT, call the Support Desk at (#3566) or take a screenshot and send it to <a href="mailto:supportdesk@muhlenberg.edu" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">supportdesk@muhlenberg.edu</a> (<strong>DO NOT forward suspicious emails</strong>).</li>
</ul>
