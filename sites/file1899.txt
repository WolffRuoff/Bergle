<p>The spring semester has ended and summer session has begun. Many changes have occurred over the last few months. The one constant is the support and collaborative efforts throughout the Muhlenberg College community, whether on or off campus. </p>



<p>While we are teaching, learning and working remotely, we want to make sure we stay connected and you stay informed. </p>



<p> IT Connects! blog posts will be published monthly keeping you up to date on the latest OIT news, important notices, maintenance schedules, training and professional development opportunities and more. </p>



<p><strong>Events</strong></p>



<ul><li>Weekly LIVE Google Meet on Wednesdays from 3pm-4pm<br>Start May 27. An open forum for faculty, staff and students to ask their technology questions.  <a href="https://calendar.google.com/event?action=TEMPLATE&amp;tmeid=NzZqODdlZGRwZWMyZDJqbWc0b243NzcyYWJfMjAyMDA1MjdUMTkwMDAwWiBtdWhsZW5iZXJnLmVkdV9ubjgzODRpbDYwc2gyOG1ndXRjMDhodHNmb0Bn&amp;tmsrc=muhlenberg.edu_nn8384il60sh28mgutc08htsfo%40group.calendar.google.com&amp;scp=ALL" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">The Meet link is available in the Google Calendar event</a>. You must be logged into your Muhlenberg account to access it.  </li><li>Access the OIT calendar for training opportunities, scheduled maintenance and other OIT events (Note:  You must be logged into your Muhlenberg account to access it). <a href="https://calendar.google.com/calendar/embed?src=muhlenberg.edu_nn8384il60sh28mgutc08htsfo%40group.calendar.google.com&amp;ctz=America%2FNew_York">Google Calendar link</a></li></ul>



<p><strong>Updates</strong></p>



<ul><li><strong>Extension of&nbsp;Adobe&nbsp;Creative&nbsp;Cloud&nbsp;At-Home Access</strong><br>Adobe provided temporary at-home access to Creative Cloud for impacted students due to campus closures, so that they may continue their work remotely.    Adobe has recently announced that temporary at-home access of Creative Cloud has been extended through July 6th, 2020.  </li><li><strong>G Suite</strong><ul><li><strong>Google Meet &#8211; video meetings made easy </strong><br>Advanced features available campuswide through September 30, 2020. Features include: video conference features, such as larger meetings (up to 250 participants) and recording.</li><li><strong>Google Drive shortcuts</strong><br>Create pointers to any file or folder in Drive.  Shortcuts make it easier for you or your team to find and organize files and folders in multiple Google Drives. Learn more at  <a href="https://support.google.com/drive/answer/9700156">https://support.google.com/drive/answer/9700156</a> </li></ul></li><li><strong>Workday Student</strong><br>The College is currently in the 12th month of a 32-month Workday Student Implementation project. Workday Student will provide Muhlenberg staff, faculty, and students many benefits, including a reduction in some current paper processes, quicker access to more data, and automated workflows. <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/initiatives/oit/workday/">Visit muhlenberg.edu/workday to learn more</a>.&nbsp; </li></ul>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Earlier this month, we launched Philo Internet Television (IPTV) on campus. At its functional root, this service lets students, faculty and staff access our current television programming via mobile devices (tablets, phones) on computers, and via the Roku family of streaming devices. It also offers a 20 hours Digital Video Recorder (DVR) so that users can save their favorite programming.</p>
<p>The more fundamental benefits are plentiful. First, all of the video is stored locally on campus. It&#8217;s encoded at the Service Electric headquarters then pushed to us via a dedicated, extremely high speed connection. This means that it doesn&#8217;t use our campus bandwidth either getting her, or when streaming. It all happens on campus, which is also a very fast connection. This compares to Netflix or Hulu, both of which stream across the open internet. Second, the DVR means that faculty can queue up video footage easily for class use. Finally, this is a great example of how we&#8217;re not just about infrastructure. The WiFi network is great, but this is an example of the cool things we can do with it.</p>
<p>Virtually all channels available previously across the traditional coaxial cable connection in each residence is on Philo, in high definition where possible. The service is available to residential students and all faculty and staff. It works only on campus.</p>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/q_logo_banner.png"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-462" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/q_logo_banner-300x135.png" alt="q_logo_banner" width="300" height="135" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/q_logo_banner-300x135.png 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/q_logo_banner-768x346.png 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/q_logo_banner.png 800w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p><strong>General Announcement</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg College announces the availability of the Qualtrics Insight Platform.</p>
<p><strong>What is Qualtrics?</strong></p>
<p>Qualtrics is a collection of powerful online survey tools.  Qualtrics is available now for Administrative Staff and Faculty use supporting Muhlenberg-related projects.  The Qualtrics Insight Platform allows researchers to create online accounts, build and administer sophisticated surveys, and analyze survey responses.  Additionally, Qualtrics permits incorporation of College-approved branding through the use of pre-built survey templates.</p>
<p><strong>Can Qualtrics replace other online survey software?</strong></p>
<p>If your department already utilizes an online survey tool, Qualtrics may permit the cancellation of subscriptions to SurveyGizmo or SurveyMonkey, for instance.  In many cases, surveys and responses may be exported from tools currently in use, and imported into Qualtrics.  If you have already obtained a personal Qualtrics account for use in your research or classroom, you may now take advantage of a campus-wide license.</p>
<p><strong>Training and Support</strong></p>
<p>In addition to providing access to Qualtrics software, please know resources are available to help ensure your research endeavor is a successful one.  As before, staff from the Office of Institutional Effectiveness &amp; Planning are available to collaborate on survey design and other elements that may help obtain successful research outcomes.  Contact Kathy Harring at <a href="mailto:harring@muhlenberg.edu">harring@muhlenberg.edu</a> for information.</p>
<p>Qualtrics offers award-winning support through the Qualtrics Support Portal (<a href="http://www.qualtrics.com/support/">http://www.qualtrics.com/support/</a>).  There you may find numerous instructional videos, guided webinars, and FAQ documents.  Technical assistance is available directly from Qualtrics staff via email or telephone.</p>
<p>To request a Qualtrics account, or for local assistance, please contact the Faculty/Staff Support Desk (x3566).</p>
<p>Also, watch for hands-on training opportunities as they are announced during the Fall 2016 and Spring 2017 semesters.</p>
<p><strong>Before Publishing a Survey, Please Consider the Following:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>At the present time, Qualtrics is available to faculty and administrative staff.  Student uses of Qualtrics require a faculty research collaborator.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="2">
<li>Before utilizing Qualtrics, please note that in many instances, administrating institution-wide surveys shall be closely coordinated with the Office of Institutional Effectiveness &amp; Planning. Please refer to the Guidelines for Administrating a Survey Instrument here:    <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/ir/administratingsurvey">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/ir/administratingsurvey</a></li>
</ol>
<ol start="3">
<li>Before you administer any survey, please consult with Human Research Coordinator in your department or the chair of the IRB to determine if your research needs IRB review.  A list of Departmental Coordinators is available here: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/provost/committees/institutionalreviewboard/departmentalreview/">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/provost/committees/institutionalreviewboard/departmentalreview/</a></li>
</ol>
<ol start="4">
<li><strong>Research in which human beings participate as subjects conducted under the auspices of Muhlenberg College, by its faculty, students, and staff, is subject to review by the College’s Institutional Review Board (IRB)</strong>.  The IRB is the body  charged with reviewing, prior to its commencement, all research and experimental activities in which human beings participate as subjects.  Information about IRB policies is available here:  <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/provost/committees/institutionalreviewboard/policies/">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/provost/committees/institutionalreviewboard/policies/</a></li>
</ol>

		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=448" rel="bookmark">Office 2016 Deployment</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=22152" rel="category">Announcements</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172342" rel="category">Upgrades and Updates</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=office" rel="tag">office</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=software" rel="tag">software</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=upgrades" rel="tag">upgrades</a></li>

</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<div></div>
<div>We will be migrating to Office 2016 during Fall term. This is an important step for us as the new version brings not only feature upgrades and parity on both Windows and Macintosh platforms, but also addresses a number of important security requirements. This long-overdue upgrade has already been completed in labs and classrooms for the start of the year.</div>
<div></div>
<div>We have been testing 2016 in OIT for some time now, especially in terms of compatibility with previous versions. We have not found any conflicts or issues moving between Office versions. The interface is slightly different, but overall we feel the transition from one to the other is relatively smooth.</div>
<div></div>
<div>Please note that there are a number of short training materials online at Lynda.com (accessible through our <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">Application Portal</a>).</div>
<div></div>
<div>We will be deploying 2016 to individual machines slowly throughout the term. We are still testing the most effective ways of doing this that minimize disruption and maximizes reliability with some degree of automation. However, rest assured that we will be distributing 2016 to individual computers at some point during Fall term.</div>

<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=22152" rel="category">Announcements</a></li>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Muhlenberg has signed a contract with <a href="http://www.lynda.com">lynda.com</a>, the leading online training site, to provide educational materials of all kinds to faculty, staff and students. Lynda has thousands of courses, ranging from 10 minute introductions to 20 part in-depth series on a huge variety of topics. Whether it&#8217;s learning how to do formulas in Excel to the concepts of project management to doing editing in Photoshop, it&#8217;s on Lynda.</p>
<p>We bring this to the community through the combined efforts of the library, the Digital Learning Team and OIT. We see the incredible value it presents for professional development for all members of the community, for classes, work, and scholarship.</p>
<p>The Digital Learning Team will offer Tech Tuesday opportunities for faculty to explore the pedagogical uses of integrating instructional video from Lynda.com into courses, particularly in courses where students may benefit from deeper familiarity with technical skills that cannot fully be introduced during class time.</p>
<p>Lynda.com can be accessed via <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">our new application portal</a>.</p>
