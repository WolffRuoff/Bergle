The film, based on Ayn Rand&#8217;s long controversial 1957 novel of the same name, opened on April 15. This is probably news to many of this blog&#8217;s readers, and it&#8217;s news to us as well. In writing the novel &#8220;Atlas Shrugged,&#8221; Rand incorporated her philosophical viewpoints into a highly debated novel that has been drawing negative criticism but a devoted fanbase for decades. Redbox taking on netflix with new digital streaming movie service. <em>The Hollywood Reporter</em>.   Retrieved from <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/redbox-taking-netflix-new-digital-100543" target="_blank">http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/redbox-taking-netflix-new-digital-100543</a>.<a href="http://mediaindustries11.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/02/gamefly-redbox.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-843" src="http://mediaindustries11.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/02/gamefly-redbox-150x150.jpg" alt="" width="150" height="150" /></a></p>
<p>In an era when media industries must constantly adapt to developing technologies and consumer desires, DVD rental company Redbox will soon be making technology-based improvements in an effort to compete with its rival Netflix. Redbox, known for its compact, bright red kiosks stationed by grocery store and gas station exits, works by vending DVDs to customers at a rate of $1 per day via credit card. According to Redbox president Mitch Lowe, the company will begin offering a subscription-based streaming Internet service by the end of this year. For a monthly fee, Redbox will soon offer on-demand movie access online in addition to continuing their kiosk services. Netflix, widely perceived as Redbox&#8217;s main competitor, currently draws in 20 million subscribers per year with its convenient online and mail delivery services. As media companies are often devising strategies to remain financially successful and keep up with their competitors, Redbox is hoping to expand its consumer base and cater to individuals who want an expedient service with the least amount of human communication possible.<a class="more-link button" href="http://mediaindustries11.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/02/17/839/">Read More</a></p>

This year&#8217;s nominees are Jon Harris for &#8220;127 Hours,&#8221; Andrew Weisblum for &#8220;Black Swan,&#8221; Pamela Martin for &#8220;The Fighter,&#8221; Tariq Anwar for &#8220;The King&#8217;s Speech&#8221; and Angus Wall &amp; Kurt Baxter for &#8220;The Social Network.&#8221; What sets these nominees apart from past years is the complete reality of the nominated films that allows for audiences to become &#8220;totally immersed.&#8221; As more films are produced every year, an editor&#8217;s task becomes increasingly difficult as they must take innovative approaches to presenting films with familiar plot lines.</p>
