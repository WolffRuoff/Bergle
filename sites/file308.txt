<p>The Trexler Library Scholar Award for Information Literacy recognizes students who have demonstrated growth in and increased understanding of information literacy through independent study. Students were nominated by faculty, and the winners were then chosen through an application process.</p>



<p>In their applications, students reflected on how they came to understand and<br>utilize one or more of the following major information literacy concepts:</p>



<div data-carousel-extra='{"blog_id":1,"permalink":"http:\/\/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu\/trexler_blog\/news\/2020-trexler-library-scholar-award-winners\/"}' class="wp-block-group"><div data-carousel-extra='{"blog_id":1,"permalink":"http:\/\/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu\/trexler_blog\/news\/2020-trexler-library-scholar-award-winners\/"}' class="wp-block-group__inner-container">
<p><strong>-Authority is constructed and contextual &nbsp;<br>-Research as inquiry<br>-Information creation as a process &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;<br>-Scholarship as conversation<br>-Information has value &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;<br>-Searching as strategic exploration</strong></p>
</div></div>



<p>Trexler Library supports the development of information literate students who can discover, organize, analyze, create, and share information in order to achieve their academic goals and to enhance their independent, lifelong learning habits. Information literate students will thereby develop an intellectual framework for critical analysis and reflection of diverse information resources.</p>



<p><strong>Congratulations to all of the nominated students!</strong></p>



<p><em>A special thank you to our judges:<br>Kelly Cannon, Scholarly Communication and Digital Learning Librarian<br>Susan Falciani Maldonado, Special Collections and Archives Librarian</em></p>

<p>It seems so simple to download a PDF and send it off to everyone or put it in Canvas for sharing. You might not realize that using a link has other advantages. The library uses reports showing the number of times library resources are used to inform decisions about new purchases and subscription renewals. If you download a PDF and share it, we will only see one use for that document. In addition, sometimes the terms of use in library licenses require links to be used rather than a PDF when sharing articles with a class.&nbsp; If you are a student, you might also need a link to add to an assignment. Whether you are faculty, student, or staff, knowing more about linking will save time and reduce frustration.&nbsp;<br></p>



<p>Jess Denke, social sciences librarian, has created a short video to walk you through the steps for identifying and choosing a link through Encompass Search. Jess shares search techniques for finding your item, gives you tips for picking from among the links available, and more.&nbsp;<br></p>



<p>To view the video, click <a href="https://cdnapisec.kaltura.com/p/1778671/sp/177867100/embedIframeJs/uiconf_id/25230661/partner_id/1778671?iframeembed=true&amp;playerId=kaltura_player&amp;entry_id=1_fx9g0y27&amp;flashvars[streamerType]=auto&amp;amp;flashvars[localizationCode]=en&amp;amp;flashvars[Kaltura.addCrossoriginToIframe]=true&amp;amp;&amp;wid=1_qsk7a3qw">here</a>. You may pick up more information than you expected!&nbsp;&nbsp;<br></p>



<p>When Amanda Riegel, who handles library reserves viewed the video, she immediately saw how useful it would be.</p>



<p>“This is a great video! I think it will be very helpful in not only showing faculty how to locate material, but also what our best practices are and rationale for them. In regards to the course reserves process, once material is located, faculty can use the Encompass details to fill out the <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/library/lendingservices/coursereservesform/">course reserves form</a> if they don&#8217;t want to place the material into Canvas themselves. I try to adhere to best practices whenever possible, so I will create pages within the Canvas class with links to the material.”<br></p>

<p style="background-color:#b8f0b5;font-size:22px" class="has-background has-text-align-center"><em>Opening up the data archive means the datasets can be shared and reused to investigate new research questions or to re-examine and attempt to validate existing research.</em></p>
</div>



<div class="wp-block-column is-vertically-aligned-center">
<p><a href="http://www.icpsr.umich.edu/index.html">SR (Inter-University Consortium for Political and Social Research)</a> provides an archive of datasets from over 13,000 research studies in the social sciences with new datasets added weekly. The archive is managed at the University of Michigan and supported by about 780 member institutions, including Muhlenberg College. <em>Who provides the datasets?</em> Anyone can submit data for deposit. ICPSR obtains data from private institutions, research agencies, and government entities and then<a href="https://www.icpsr.umich.edu/icpsrweb/content/datamanagement/index.html"> curates and stores the datasets</a> to ensure data quality and preservation. <em>Why archive data that has already been used?</em>  Datasets are a vital part of the evidence that supported the research study results reported to the public.&nbsp; In the ICPSR archive, most datasets are available for public use. Because Muhlenberg College is a member, all data is available to Muhlenberg faculty, students, and staff who create an account using their Muhlenberg email address.  </p>
</div>
</div>



<p>ICPSR also tracks what research has been done using a dataset from the archive. To find out, search the<a href="https://www.icpsr.umich.edu/icpsrweb/ICPSR/citations/"> ICPSR Bibliography of Data-related Literature</a> which includes citations to both published and unpublished analytical works using an ICPSR dataset and going back to 1962. Beyond providing the data itself, ICPSR supports learning how to conduct research with data. Teaching and learning resources are available through the website.</p>



<ul><li><strong>For students</strong><ul><li>Online resources for students are available<a href="https://www.icpsr.umich.edu/icpsrweb/instructors/support/students"> here</a>.</li><li>The annual summer<a href="https://www.icpsr.umich.edu/icpsrweb/sumprog/"> learning institute</a> held at the University of Michigan provides a deep dive into quantitative analysis and social research.</li></ul></li><li>For teachers<ul><li><a href="http://www.icpsr.umich.edu/icpsrweb/instructors/index.jsp#welcomeemail">Using Data in the Classroom</a> – Learning guides and exercise modules for faculty teaching undergraduates.</li><li>&nbsp;Videos about teaching with data are available<a href="https://www.icpsr.umich.edu/icpsrweb/content/instructors/teacher-resources.html"> here</a>.</li></ul></li></ul>



<p>ICPSR works to develop more robust data sources. They have partnered with federal statistical agencies to create topical collections and provide tools for easier data discovery and use. You can access data instruments, dictionaries, and data from<a href="https://census.icpsr.umich.edu/census/"> U.S. Census Data</a> through a specially built interface on ICPSR. For more information about all the collections available look<a href="https://www.icpsr.umich.edu/icpsrweb/content/about/thematic-collections.html"> here</a>.</p>
