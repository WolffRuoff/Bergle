                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-15574933" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-33973955" class="  clearfix">
				<p><span>Academic writing is filled with rules. &nbsp;Knowing when and how to cite your sources shows that you can take part in academic conversation and avoid any charge of&nbsp;</span>plagiarism<span>.</span></p>
<p><span><span>No professor expects you to present your own ideas only; be clear about where your ideas, phrases, and special words come from. &nbsp;</span>Any time you refer to another person's work--whether you quote directly from another's work, refer to another's work in your own words, or base your ideas on another's work--you must cite the sources you used. &nbsp;Citing your sources serves a number of very important purposes. &nbsp;For example, citing properly:</span></p>
<ul>
<li>Gives authors due credit for their work and ideas.</li>
<li>Provides evidence of the depth and rigor of your research.&nbsp;</li>
<li>Gives others a way to also find the valuable information you have used.</li>
<li>Helps you to avoid plagiarism.</li>
</ul>

				<p>Plagiarism is the act of using another person's words or ideas without giving him/her proper credit.&nbsp; It is a serious academic offense.&nbsp; </p>
<p>The <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/dean-academic/integrity/">Academic Integrity</a> page defines plagiarism in more detail and provides information about Muhlenberg's Academic Integrity Code.&nbsp; Check out <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/dean-academic/integrity/plagiarism_defined.html">Defining Plagiarism: A Guide for Students</a> with helpful frequently asked questions.&nbsp; </p>
