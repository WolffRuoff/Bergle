<div class="entry-meta">

					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172338" rel="category">Google Apps</a></li>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>The new interface for Gmail is now available as an option for all Muhlenberg G Suite users. Simply select the &#8220;gear&#8221; icon in the upper right of the screen and select the new interface. You can use the same method to switch back, for now, if you aren&#8217;t a big fan. See <a href="https://www.computerworld.com/article/3269415/email/25-tips-for-getting-the-most-out-of-the-new-gmail-features.html">this ComputerWorld article</a> for a fairly comprehensive review of new features. In particular, we highlight:</p>
<ul>
<li>The ability to use Calendar, Keep and Tasks from within the Gmail app</li>
<li>Organizing emails from your inbox</li>
<li>&#8220;Snoozing&#8221; of emails to be <em>de facto</em> reminders to act on them, rather than cluttering your inbox (Conversation view must be ON)</li>
</ul>
<p>Google will make this the only interface option in October. We suggest you give the interface a try sooner rather than later.</p>
<p>Want to learn more? Read <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/gsuite/Enabling-New-Muhlenberg-College-Mail.pdf">Enabling New Muhlenberg College Mail</a> on the OIT website.</p>

<div class="entry-meta">

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p><span style="font-weight: 400">You’ve got GBs upon GBs of files stored and shared in My Drive. So what’s next?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Are you looking for a way to share files with a group without taking on ownership in YOUR Drive? </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Have you ever wished you could view and organize your files in your computer’s file system? </span></p>
<p><b>New Google Drive features are coming to the rescue! Release date &#8211; February 27.</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">OIT and a group of early adopters have been testing Team Drive and Drive File Stream in preparation for release to the campus. </span></p>
<p><b>What is Team Drive? </b><span style="font-weight: 400">Google Team Drives are shared spaces where teams can easily store, search, and access their files anywhere, from any device.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Unlike files in My Drive, files in Team Drive belong to the team instead of an individual. Even if members leave, the files stay exactly where they are so your team can continue to share information and get work done.</span></p>
<p><b>What is Drive File Stream?</b><b><br />
</b><span style="font-weight: 400">If you want to work on files from your desktop, install Drive File Stream. All your Drive files appear and can be streamed on demand, so they don’t take up all your storage space on your computer.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Want to learn more about <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/gsuite/coreapps/teamdrive/">Team Drive</a> and <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/gsuite/coreapps/drivefilestream/">Drive File Stream</a>? Visit <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/gsuite/">muhlenberg.edu/gsuite</a> for more details.  </span></p>

<h3>G Suite: Frequently Asked Questions</h3>
<div class="content-add05">
<p dir="ltr">This list is derived from engagement during training, as well as, support inquiries. We will be adding to this resource, as well as, the <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/gsuite/documentation/">G Suite documentation library</a> and <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/gsuite/videos">video library</a>.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Why am I still receiving emails in GroupWise?</strong><br />
Email will continue to flow into GroupWise. Gmail is the only email from which you should send mail. Emails sent to any muhlenberg.edu address within GroupWise will NOT flow to Muhlenberg Gmail accounts. We are asking people to use GroupWise as a cross-reference only, reporting any anomalies. If you wish to forward any emails from GroupWise (or grouped as attachments), you must forward to your Google Test Domain Address which is username@muhlenberg.edu.test-google-a.com. For example janesmith@<a href="http://muhlenberg.edu.test-google-a.com/">muhlenberg.edu.test-google-a.com</a> Use it for no other purpose except forwarding your mail to Google.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Is there a restriction on the number of email addresses I send to in a single email? Does it matter if they are internal or external email addresses?<br />
</strong>G Suite limits the amount of mail one user can send. Limits restrict the number of messages sent per day and the number of recipients per message.</p>
<p dir="ltr">If users exceed a G Suite email sending limit, they’ll see an error message, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>You have reached a limit for sending email.</li>
<li>You reached a Gmail sending limit.</li>
<li>You exceeded the maximum number of recipients.</li>
</ul>
<p>After reaching one of these limits, a user can&#8217;t send new messages for up to 24 hours. However, they can still access their G Suite account and receive incoming email. The user can also access their other Google services. After this suspension period, sending limits are automatically reset and the user can resume sending mail.</p>
<p dir="ltr">To learn more about these restrictions read “<a href="https://support.google.com/a/answer/166852?hl=en">Gmail sending limits in G Suite</a>”</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>How do I access email Groups (formerly distributed lists in GroupWise)?</strong> <br class="kix-line-break" />Distributed email lists are now referred to as &#8220;Groups&#8221;. The ability to add Groups to our muhlenberg.edu directory is not an available feature &#8211; they will not auto-populate as you type if it is the first time you are sending to that Group. They are, however, available in the Groups apps, currently visible based on membership.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Note: Once you email your Group, they will be added to your Contacts and will auto-populate.</p>
<p dir="ltr">We are currently working on a new group management model. Departments &amp; offices will be contacted regarding the management of their group&#8217;s membership.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Supporting documentation: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/gsuite/MuhlenbergGroups.pdf">Contacting Muhlenberg Groups (formerly distribution lists)</a>‌</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Do I need to logout of G Suite apps?</strong> <br class="kix-line-break" />Yes. There is no imposed timeout on G Suite apps. While we have a 15 minute timeout on the Application Portal, you are still logged into G Suite. It is important that you logout of G Suite (and other apps) accessed via the Application Portal if you wish to end the session. This is critically important when accessing your account on a public computer.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Supporting documentation: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/gsuite/LoggingoutofGSuiteapps.pdf">Logging out of GSuite apps</a></p>
<p><strong>Can I send an email from an alias?<br />
</strong>If you want to always send from your other address, you&#8217;ll need to change both your default &#8220;From&#8221; and &#8220;reply-to&#8221; address. If you only change the &#8220;From&#8221; address, replies will go to your original Gmail address by default.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Supporting documentation: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/gsuite/Sendingemailfromanotheraddressoralias.pdf">Sending email from another address or alias</a>‌</p>
<p><strong>I sent an email to myself and did not receive. Why?</strong><br />
By default, Google does not send an email sent to an alias to your inbox &#8211; Google presumes you do not want the clutter of such emails. If you sent a message to yourself using an alias and NOT your official muhlenberg email address (<a href="mailto:firstnamelastname@muhlenberg.edu">firstnamelastname@muhlenberg.edu</a>), it will not be delivered to your inbox. For example, johnsmith will work but jsmith will not. However, this email will appear in your Sent mail and All Mail. If you wish to send emails to yourself and see them in your inbox, please use your official email address, not an alias.</p>
<p><strong>When I click on an email address in a browser window or MS Office application, I am kicked to GroupWise. How do I get this work in Gmail?<br />
</strong>You need to set Gmail as your default email client. To do this you will need to install Chrome and walk through a series of steps, after which, Gmail will be the default.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Supporting documentation: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/gsuite/Gmailasyourdefaultemailclient.pdf">How to set Gmail as your default email client</a>‌</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Is there an official logo I can use when setting my signature in Gmail?</strong><br />
Yes! Please use the following web URL for the official Muhlenberg “M”: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/images/muhl-email.png">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/images/muhl-email.png</a>.</p>

		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=538" rel="bookmark">Getting to Google in 20  Million Short Steps</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=170064" rel="category">Communication &amp; Collaboration</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172338" rel="category">Google Apps</a></li>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Getting the campus to Google &#8211; which involves moving nearly 20 million emails &#8211; is no small task. We thought we&#8217;d shed some light on the actual process.</p>
<p><strong>Final cut-over will be on December 27, 28, or 29</strong>. This is a few days before &#8220;G-Day&#8221; on January 2 when we&#8217;ll have workshops, training, and a number of other support options. But moving ahead a few days early is necessary &#8211; we need to make sure everything moves over correctly and time to make any needed course corrections.</p>
<p>First, a few reminders:</p>
<ul>
<li>data/old emails will not be migrated for student accounts</li>
<li>all faculty/staff old emails <em>not in an archive</em> will be migrated</li>
<li>GroupWise will keep working after December 29, but only as a back-up (see below)</li>
<li>The <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">Application Porta</a>l will your access path to Google Suite (Muhlenberg Network Username for login, usually firstnamelastname for faculty &amp; staff or ab123456 for students)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>GroupWise Archives</strong></p>
<p>GroupWise offers an &#8220;archive&#8221; feature that allows one to move emails out of one&#8217;s &#8220;live&#8221; mailbox into an offline space. This saves on quota, among other benefits. We have discouraged using this feature in preference to simply increasing quota, so most likely you aren&#8217;t using it. In fact, if you don&#8217;t distinctly remember configuring and turning on your archive, you almost certainly aren&#8217;t using it. Technically, however, if you have emails in your archive, they will <em>not</em> migrate over. We can only migrate emails in your live mailbox. If you have questions, contact the Support Desk.</p>
<p><strong>GroupWise Moving Forward</strong></p>
<p>As mentioned, GroupWise should only be used to cross-reference your GMail account during the first few days following the switch-up. Emails sent within the system &#8211; from one campus user to another &#8211; stay entirely internal. They never get off campus, and therefore never reach Google. This means that your Google inbox will not receive any email you send from GroupWise to another member of the college community. So it&#8217;s still there, it still works, but please don&#8217;t use it unless you have to.</p>
<p><strong>Migration</strong></p>
<p>The actual migration process has taken place in iterative steps. We did a first, large migration of all emails in the entire system up to those dated August 31, 2016 almost 2 months ago. For some users this took close to a month to complete. We then ran multiple successive migrations covering emails from shorter and shorter periods of time, slowly catching up to the latest emails. We&#8217;ve already run 2 migrations to get to 10/31. In a couple of weeks we&#8217;ll get to 11/30. We&#8217;ll probably run 3 to 4 more migrations in smaller and smaller chunks of time until we run just 1 day&#8217;s worth on 12/28 and then flip the switch.</p>
<p>All told, we&#8217;ll have spent 3 months on migrating email alone.</p>
<p><strong>What you can do to get ready for G Suite</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/11/23/gsuite-info-sessions-announced/">Attend a G Suite Information Session</a></li>
<li>Visit our Application portal at <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">login.muhlenberg.edu</a> to ensure you are able to login successfully.</li>
<li>Learn about G Suite on lynda.com (available via the application portal) and by visiting the <a href="https://gsuite.google.com/learning-center/" target="_blank">G Suite Learning Center</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Actual Cut-Over</strong></p>
<p>The switch itself is nearly instantaneous after the last migration. By the end of the day of 12/28, you will see G Suite among the options in the Application Portal. Simply click here and you&#8217;ll be routed to G Suite. It&#8217;s single sign-on so no need to log in again.</p>
<p>You&#8217;ll see a welcome screen the first time, but after that you can go straight into G Suite.</p>

<article id="post-441" class="post post-441 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-google-apps">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=441" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-08-18T10:49:17-04:00">August 18, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=441" rel="bookmark">Google Apps Update</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172338" rel="category">Google Apps</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=441#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>OIT has been working hard on our Google Apps deployment ever since our <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/05/11/google-apps-for-education-our-next-collaboration-platform/">original announcement</a>. We have made great progress on many fronts, from establishing the instance itself to beginning to create accounts automatically from our directory</p>
<p>The original plan was to deploy for students by Orientation, then faculty and staff during winter break. We will unfortunately <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/16/google-apps-for-students-update-delayed-until-winter-break/">miss the mark for students</a>. The primary challenge we face is getting GroupWise and Google to co-exist. With a phased approach, we have to make sure all the email goes to the right places. We have to make sure that email going to a Google account doesn&#8217;t get stuck in GroupWise, for instance.</p>
<p>We are close to solving this, but unfortunately cannot accomplish our goal by Orientation. Therefore we are delaying the launch for students. In order to minimize disruption, we have decided against a go-live during the term.</p>
<p>Consequently, we will be migrating the entire campus during Winter break. On January 1 we should be on Google for all users.</p>
<p>Note that email addresses will not change, and that all data for faculty and staff will migrate. You will have all of your mail and calendar info in the Google eco-system when we go live. At this time we are not certain we will be able to do the same for students, but we are working hard to explore options (migrating that much data is a non-trivial task, largely due to restrictions on Google&#8217;s side).</p>
<p>We still plan on having training during the term. These will focus on the new collaboration tools that Google provides. How to share documents in Google Drive, concurrently edit files, and use sharing as part of project management are all examples of trainings we will have.</p>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->

<article id="post-434" class="post post-434 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-google-apps tag-email tag-google tag-google-apps">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=434" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-08-16T10:00:35-04:00">August 16, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=434" rel="bookmark">Google Apps For Students Update &#8211; Delayed Until Winter Break</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172338" rel="category">Google Apps</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=email" rel="tag">email</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=google" rel="tag">google</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=google-apps" rel="tag">Google Apps</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=434#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Our original goal was to launch Google Apps for students by the start of Orientation. However, due to a number of challenges this has been delayed. In order to avoid any disruption during the term, we will migrate the entire campus &#8211; faculty, staff and students &#8211; at the same time, during Winter Break.</p>
<p>We made excellent progress throughout the summer, creating accounts, connecting to our new Application Portal, and configuring the overall Google environment. However, we unfortunately have not determined a satisfactory solution for GroupWise and Google to co-exist. The mail flow was not working correctly, and we are not able to remedy the problems by our deadline.</p>
<p>We apologize for this delay, and for the confusion it causes. We are extremely optimistic about the new timeline, and hope to make for a better product with a smooth transition.</p>
<p>We will still be holding training workshops during the term to highlight all the new features the Google Apps solution provides.</p>
