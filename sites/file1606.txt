<article id="post-739" class="post post-739 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-security tag-cyber-security tag-phishing">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=739" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2018-04-18T11:44:37-04:00">April 18, 2018</time><time class="updated" datetime="2018-05-17T14:33:24-04:00">May 17, 2018</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=739" rel="bookmark">IT Protects! &#8211; Campus Phishing Simulation</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=8070" rel="category">Security</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=cyber-security" rel="tag">cyber security</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=phishing" rel="tag">phishing</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=739#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p dir="ltr">Each year, we run a number of drills for awareness and training &#8211; fire drills, lockdown drills, etc. In the same vein, we sometimes need to run cyber security simulations to prepare for those kinds of incidents. This is simply best practice for a community such as ours. Sometime between April-July 2018, we will be running a phishing simulation, impacting all faculty and staff.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Why are we doing this?</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">More than 90% of all attacks on systems around the world start off with phishing schemes. 43% of successful breaches included such tactics, and 28% of phishing breaches were targeted and specific. This all comes from the <a href="http://www.verizonenterprise.com/verizon-insights-lab/dbir/2017/">Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report</a> (DBIR), an authoritative source on the state of cyber security.</p>
<p dir="ltr">In a phishing scheme, a bad actor poses to be a credible person in an attempt to steal your login credentials. Once your login credentials are obtained, that person can access various systems, including the Application Portal, which is a one-stop shop with access to Workday, G Suite/email, and Canvas. Phishing is also an entry point for malware, including viruses and ransomware.</p>
<p dir="ltr">For some training on phishing, check out the KnowBe4 ModStore videos, available via <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">the Application Portal</a>.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Remember the <a href="https://security.berkeley.edu/faq/phishing/what-can-i-do-avoid-phishing-attacks" target="_blank" rel="noopener">tell-tale signs of a phishing email</a>:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>
<p dir="ltr">Does the email contain a vague salutation, spelling or grammatical errors, an urgent request, and/or an offer that seems impossibly good? <b><a href="https://support.google.com/mail/answer/8253?hl=en">Report the phish via GMail&#8217;s built-in feature, </a>then click the delete button.</b></p>
</li>
<li>
<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://www.online-tech-tips.com/computer-tips/worry-verification-emails-google/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Check the sender’s e-mail address</a> to make sure it’s legitimate. Does it appears that the Support Desk is asking you to click on a link but it comes from “support@yahoo.com?” What if it’s “support@muhlenberg.edu” but it says it’s “via” another address? If it lists anything anywhere that says something other than “muhlenberg.edu<a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/?" target="_blank" rel="noopener">?</a>” <b>Report it, then delete it</b>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p dir="ltr">OIT will never ask you for your password through email. Any request for one should be treated as suspicious and reported as phishing. Your password is the key to your identity, your data, and your classmates’ and colleagues’ data. Read <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/Requiringauserpassword.pdf">Password Requirements for OIT Support</a> to learn more.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p dir="ltr">Avoid opening links and attachments from unknown senders. Get into the habit of typing known URLs into your browser. Don’t open attachments unless you’re expecting a file from someone. Give them a call if you’re suspicious.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p dir="ltr">Take extra care with mobile devices, as some of these signs are not as easily seen</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What happens if I click on the link?</strong></p>
<p>First, no one that clicks on the links will be publicly identified. The purpose of this campaign is educational. Information about individual responses will not be shared with supervisors, Human Resources, or the community. The goal of this simulation is to help you learn how to spot a phish! To that end, we will assign a basic, 25 minute training to anyone that clicks on the link. This training will provide some fundamentals of cyber security and what to look for in a phishing attack.</p>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->

<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=8070" rel="category">Security</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=cybersecurity" rel="tag">cybersecurity</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=meltdown" rel="tag">meltdown</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=os-patching" rel="tag">os patching</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=security" rel="tag">Security</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=spectre" rel="tag">spectre</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=720#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Information and cyber security is one of the highest priority items for OIT. As a combination of existing plans and the <a href="http://money.cnn.com/2018/01/03/technology/computer-chip-flaw-security/index.html">l</a><a href="http://money.cnn.com/2018/01/03/technology/computer-chip-flaw-security/index.html">atest news on the Spectre and Meltdown computer vulnerabilities</a>, OIT is making some changes in how it handles security patches to software. As per the linked article, these new exploits affect essentially all processors in all computers and devices, with very few exceptions. This ranges from desktops and laptops to smartphones, and from Windows to Macintosh to Linux. This affects both applications such as Flash and Adobe Reader as well as the core operating system.</p>
<p>On the Windows platform, we have been for several months planning on changing our patching policy. Through an upgrade to the already-installed Novell/MicroFocus Zenworks utility, we can do automated patch management. This means we can proactively push software updates for both the operating system and third-party applications to desktops and laptops. More importantly, we can check for compliance and identify trouble-spots that need to be addressed. This is compared to our method today, which relies on Microsoft&#8217;s automated system, which cannot be monitored in our environment. Note that we already roll out Windows Updates as Microsoft releases them. This is merely creating a pro-active configuration with compliance-checking.</p>
<p>This change in process was already scheduled for Winter Break, and some of you have received notice that we&#8217;ll be updating the Zenworks utility on your computer in the coming days and weeks. In the wake of Meltdown in particular, we&#8217;ll be accelerating the pace of this deployment to the rest of campus.</p>
<p>On the Apple Macintosh side, we have traditionally not done regular updates for a variety of reasons. Because both Meltdown and Spectre affect <em>all</em> computers, regardless of platform, it is critical that we make a change in this policy. Utilizing our existing management software, we will be turning on critical system updates to run <em>as soon as they are deployed by Apple</em>. In the past, our policy has been on between Friday 8PM to Saturday 5AM, but many machines are turned off by this time and do not receive the updates.</p>
<p>In addition, at a later date we will be addressing the need for major OS X updates on the Macintosh platform. Today we have computers with 4 different versions of the operating system in place. It is critical that we upgrade all machines to the latest version (currently 10.13, High Sierra) but this will be a much larger undertaking. Our goal for now is to get all machines at least up to date for the installed OS version.</p>
<p>This is unlikely to be the end of the Spectre and Meltdown story. Please stay tuned for more information. We will be updating this post and posting new material as it becomes available.</p>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>We are adding <a href="http://www.zoom.us">Zoom</a> video conferencing to the <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">Application Portal</a>, effective August 21. Zoom is a great web-based collaboration tool that offers a user-friendly audio and video conferencing solution for all members of the Muhlenberg community. You can hold full video conference sessions, or just use the audio part for a phone conference.</p>
<div>
<p>With Zoom each user is issued a dedicated virtual meeting room that can be used to conduct meetings, hold classes, and <span id="m_-3559586123182918824gmail-:15g.2" class="m_-3559586123182918824J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">advising </span>sessions with real-time audio/video and screen sharing for up to 100 participants.</p>
<div>Having Zoom on the Application Portal will make it easier to access current accounts and create new accounts.</div>
</div>
<div>
<div></div>
<div><b>If you currently have a Zoom account, </b>it will continue to work unless you were contacted directly about updating your email address.</div>
<div></div>
<div><b>New to Zoom and want an account?</b> Just click on the Zoom icon in the application portal and it will automatically create a basic account for you. Basic accounts support up to 100 participants for up to a 40 minute meeting.</div>
