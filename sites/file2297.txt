		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=477" rel="bookmark">Instructional Technology and Digital Learning Team Updates&#8230;.</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=22152" rel="category">Announcements</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=projects" rel="category">Projects</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172342" rel="category">Upgrades and Updates</a></li>

		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The Instructional Technology and Digital Learning Team has been off to a busy start this fall. Here’s a quick look at some of the things that we’ve been working on. In this post we’ll take a look at The Tech Tuesday Program, a Canvas update, and the new LC Commons.</span></p>
<ol>
<li><b>Tech Tuesdays</b><span style="font-weight: 400">&#8211; The Tech Tuesday Program began in the Fall of 2015 with a few events and has grown in size and will offer nine events for the Fall 2016 semester. These sessions provide an excellent opportunity for members of the College community to explore new technologies, techniques, and digital learning tools through brief presentations, peer collaboration, and informal discussion. Events are held every other week at 8:30 AM and usually last about an hour. Topics for the Fall range form the Canvas Gradebook to 3D modeling. For a full list of events and additional resources checkout the Tech Tuesday blog @ </span><a href="http://diglearn.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/events/techtuesday/"><span style="font-weight: 400">http://diglearn.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/events/techtuesday/</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">. Make sure you check out the Diglearn blog and come back often as new content is added frequently,. It’s a great resource for all things digital learning at Muhlenberg. For those of you who have been joining us at the Tuesday sessions, thank you… and to those who haven’t come out yet..we’d love to see you. If there are topics that you would like us to cover or if you’d like to present please let us know. sciarrino@muhlenberg.edu</span></li>
<li><b>Canvas-<span style="font-weight: 400"> We are now well into the Fall semester and our campus wide deployment of Canvas has been going well. Members of the Instructional Technology and Digital Learning Team spent the summer of ‘16 working with faculty members in a variety of venues developing, training, and troubleshooting all things Canvas. Now that the semester has started the journey isn’t over. We are still here to help you with Canvas. As the semester progresses and you are looking to get more out of Canvas, please let us know. We will continue to eat and sleep all things Canvas. That’s not to say we only care about Canvas because that’s not true at all. It’s just one of the tools we “geek” out over. If you have questions about Canvas or any other EdTech tools, we would love to hear from you. Please contact either Jordan Noyes(</span><a href="mailto:jnoyes@muhlenberg.edu"><span style="font-weight: 400">jnoyes@muhlenberg.edu</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">), Tim Clarke(</span><a href="mailto:tclarke@muhlenberg.edu"><span style="font-weight: 400">tclarke@muhlenberg.edu</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">), or myself (</span><a href="mailto:sciarrin@muhlenberg.edu"><span style="font-weight: 400">sciarrin@muhlenberg.edu</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">). </span><span style="font-weight: 400">  </span></b></li>
<li><b>The New LC Commons. An exciting new space</b>Times they are a changin’ and so are the space requirements of many programs on  campus. The Language Learning Center was no exception to this change. For some time now languages faculty were asking for an environment that was flexible in setup and more conducive to student collaboration. A space that would work well in a wide variety of configurations that would accommodate both full class instruction and small group collaboration. The evolution of this space didn’t happen overnight. It began back in the fall of 2015 when members of OIT and the Foreign Languages Department started looking at the existing space and dreaming up ways to meet departmental requirements. We wanted to design a space that was easy to use, flexible, and visually pleasing. All while aligning with our current campus technology standards. This wasn’t always the easiest task. Through this process we were able to evaluate faculty needs and pair them with some new and exciting digital tools. Here’s a look at the new space in use.</li>
</ol>

<article id="post-412" class="post post-412 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-printing-and-wepa">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=412" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-08-16T09:58:12-04:00">August 16, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-09-01T12:27:06-04:00">September 1, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=412" rel="bookmark">Print Quota &#8211; Frequently Asked Questions</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172331" rel="category">Printing and WEPA</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=412#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>This is a &#8220;living&#8221; document that we&#8217;ve worked up to help answer questions about the new print quota. As I receive new inquiries, I&#8217;ll add to this as appropriate. Eventually we will migrate this to a new, permanent web page along with general policies and guidelines.</p>
<p><strong>What is a quota, and what does it mean to me?</strong></p>
<p>At the beginning of each term, you will receive a specific amount in your WEPA account. Right now that is $75 per term (see below for more details), or 833 pages of single-sided B&amp;W pages. <strong>This amount is provided for “free” and at no expense to you</strong>. After this amount is exhausted, you will be responsible for printing costs on a per-page or sheet basis (depending on whether you print single or double-sided).</p>
<p><strong>How much free printing do we receive?</strong></p>
<p>$75 per semester for full-time students. This does not roll over from term to term. This is the equivalent of 833 pages of single-sided black and white pages.</p>
<p>Wescoe part-time students receive $17.50 per course taken.</p>
<p><strong>Do I pay for the $75 per semester?</strong></p>
<p>No – you receive $75 or 833 pages of printing at no cost to you. This is provided by the college. It is only after this allocation is exhausted that prints will cost you money.</p>
<p><strong>How did you come to this allotment?</strong></p>
<p>An <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/07/22/data-on-printing-and-the-print-quota/">analysis of data gathered during spring</a> led to this dollar amount. We looked at how much was loaded onto student accounts and aimed for the 75th percentile. $75 is actually slightly higher than that. It is also more than $10 than the average all usage across the entire student population.</p>
<p>We will be reviewing data from term to term to adjust this amount.</p>
<p><strong>Why are we instituting a print quota?</strong></p>
<p>It&#8217;s all about sustainability. The college has a specific mission to address sustainability concerns in many aspects, from electricity to water to recycling. We are addressing this in many ways, largely through the efforts of the President’s Committee on Sustainability. One of these areas includes printing, or more specifically paper and toner waste. This isn&#8217;t about preventing students from printing. It&#8217;s about minimizing papers left in printers, accidental striking of the &#8220;print&#8221; button and other reasons for abandoned print jobs.</p>
<p>Of course, we also want to promote awareness of when one chooses to print &#8211; perhaps some documents can be best accessed electronically, rather than in print. If a quota helps keep that in mind, then we&#8217;ll have met our sustainability concerns, too.</p>
<p>The data collected from Spring 2016 indicates that a small minority of users accounted for the majority of printing. The top 15% of all users accounted for 40% of all funds spent.</p>
<p><strong>Why can&#8217;t we keep printing free? Why now?</strong></p>
<p>We have seen increasing usage of printing term over term for the last 2 years. We averaged roughly 100,000-110,000 sheets of paper per month prior to Spring 2016. We saw a jump to over 120,000 sheets after the WEPA roll-out. April 2016 spiked at 170,000. Between paper, toner (which is also a sustainability concern as it must be recycled through dedicated centers) and repairs, this is proving untenable and it&#8217;s raised concerns about our ability to control paper waste and usage. Sometime in the very near future we would have had to make a shift towards a print quota. We simply could not keep up at our current pace.</p>
<p>Other schools that have instituted a quota have seen a decrease as a result of increased self-awareness of when and what to print.</p>
<p><strong>Do other schools have a print quota?</strong></p>
<p>According to a 2014 Campus Computing Survey, 81% of respondents charge for printing one way or another. 61% charge for every print – no quota is provided.</p>
<p>A sampling of schools that have offered quotas similar to ours include Lehigh, Oberlin, Dickinson, Ithaca and Bowdoin.</p>
<p><strong>When does the print quota start?</strong></p>
<p>August 15 for Fall 2016. The start date for Spring 2017 has not yet been set.</p>
<p><strong>Can I print in color?</strong></p>
<p>We offer both high-capacity, high-speed black and white-only printers on campus as well as full color units (21 in total).</p>
<p><strong>Where are printers located?</strong></p>
<p>21 printers are along Academic Row (except the Haas College Center), in Seegers, Trexler Library and all residences. Several of the residences are high-capacity black and white units &#8211; they do not print in color, but they don&#8217;t require refills as often, either.</p>
<p>The WEPA website as a <a href="https://www.wepanow.com/maps">kiosk locator</a> that shows where the units are on campus, and what type they are.</p>
<p><strong>How much are prints?</strong></p>
<p><em>Costs per page/sheet are below. These apply whether when using your quota or when paying separately.</em></p>
<ul>
<li>B&amp;W single-sided &#8211; $.09</li>
<li>B&amp;W double-sided &#8211; $.17</li>
<li>Color single-sided &#8211; $.35</li>
<li>Color double-sided &#8211; $.68</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>How can I reload my funds?</strong></p>
<p>Funds can be reloaded at <a href="http://www.wepanow.com">http://www.wepanow.com</a> (login using your muhlenberg.edu credentials) or at a kiosk. A credit or debit card can be used.</p>
<p><strong>This quota presents a disproportionate financial burden. What are my options?</strong></p>
<p>Students that are Pell Grant eligible receive a higher quota at the start of the term. This will be automatically loaded before the start of the term. The August 15 amount does not reflect these additional funds.</p>
<p><strong>I take a course or courses that require a great deal of printing. What can I do?</strong></p>
<p>Students may request a one-time quota increase. Students must provide a list of courses for which they are currently registered and indicate which course(s) are printing-intensive.</p>
<p>These requests will be considered and, if approved, funds added AFTER the add/drop date has passed.</p>
<p>Students must request a specific number of additional pages, up to 550 (roughly $50 in additional quota).</p>
<p>Requests must be made in person at the Student Help Desk in Ettinger Basement, room 001.</p>
<p><strong>Why did we switch to WEPA? What was wrong with the old printers?</strong></p>
<p>The old printers were aging poorly. Most were more than 5 years old (with some as old as 10 years). From a logistical standpoint, this made them hard to maintain. From a sustainability perspective, they did not accept paper with more than 10% recycled materials. Insufficient budgeting eliminated the option of a large scale replacement of units or the development of a new print management solution that could support needed features such as support for mobile devices.</p>
<p>The WEPA solution provided new printers, mobile support, color capabilities, and higher capacity and performance. They also offer a single “cloud” queue, which allows students to hit print in one place, but pick up the job at any of the 21 units around campus.</p>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Muhlenberg has signed a contract with <a href="http://www.lynda.com">lynda.com</a>, the leading online training site, to provide educational materials of all kinds to faculty, staff and students. Lynda has thousands of courses, ranging from 10 minute introductions to 20 part in-depth series on a huge variety of topics. Whether it&#8217;s learning how to do formulas in Excel to the concepts of project management to doing editing in Photoshop, it&#8217;s on Lynda.</p>
<p>We bring this to the community through the combined efforts of the library, the Digital Learning Team and OIT. We see the incredible value it presents for professional development for all members of the community, for classes, work, and scholarship.</p>
<p>The Digital Learning Team will offer Tech Tuesday opportunities for faculty to explore the pedagogical uses of integrating instructional video from Lynda.com into courses, particularly in courses where students may benefit from deeper familiarity with technical skills that cannot fully be introduced during class time.</p>
<p>Lynda.com can be accessed via <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">our new application portal</a>.</p>

	<header class="entry-header">

			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=398" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-08-10T15:50:21-04:00">August 10, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=398" rel="bookmark">A Single Application Portal for Muhlenberg Web Services</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=22152" rel="category">Announcements</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=398#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Starting this fall, we will be transitioning to <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">a single application portal</a> through which the community can access many resources. It&#8217;s already live and you can access our new training resources at <a href="http://www.lynda.com">Lynda.com</a> as well as <a href="http://www.qualtrics.com">Qualtrics</a> (the latter only if you&#8217;re faculty or staff). We&#8217;ve just added Canvas, as well. Eventually Google will go through here.</p>
<p>The application portal provides &#8220;single sign-on&#8221; functionality to our core services. After you login at that page, you won&#8217;t have to login again for the services within the portal. Eventually, we&#8217;ll be adding a password manager as well. For those wanting top-end security, the system supports multi-factor authentication, too, using either text messaging or an app on Android or iOS. It&#8217;s really a feature-rich solution that meets a lot of needs.</p>
<p>Our goal is to roll out services that are explicitly compatible with this system, so that we continue to keep this our one-stop-shop. Services that will go through here moving forward will include Google Apps, Zoom video conferencing, Canvas, and our Samanage Work Order system.</p>
<p>Note that the application portal is powered by OneLogin, a leading &#8220;ID-as-a-Service&#8221; and Single Sign-On provider. We&#8217;re sending you to <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">login.muhlenberg.edu</a>, but it redirects to OneLogin.</p>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->

<article id="post-419" class="post post-419 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-announcements category-phone-system tag-mitel tag-phone tag-projects">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=419" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-08-09T08:41:09-04:00">August 9, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-08-09T09:16:33-04:00">August 9, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=419" rel="bookmark">New Phone System</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=22152" rel="category">Announcements</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172334" rel="category">Phone System</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=mitel" rel="tag">mitel</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=phone" rel="tag">phone</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=projects" rel="tag">Projects</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=419#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>For the past year, OIT has been working on a new phone system to replace our aging legacy architecture. Our current system is incredibly robust and reliable, but it&#8217;s past its maintenance life and we&#8217;re essentially relying on spare parts as our contingency plan for dramatic failures. It&#8217;s been time for us to move forward for a while now.</p>
<p>After an exhaustive (and exhausting) selection process, we chose <a href="http://www.mitel.com">Mitel </a>as our new phone solution in early spring. Their Voice over IP (VoIP) phone system offers a powerful, flexible, and expandable communication platform that will open up some interesting doors in the years to come. It&#8217;s much more than merely getting us to current &#8211; it&#8217;s getting us ready for the future, too.</p>
<p>Some of the higher end features will be quite compelling as we adopt them. For instance, there is a desktop app from which one can look up anyone in the phone book and, with a single click, launch a call on the phone. Not a computer call, but a phone call. There is a mobile app that does the same thing. Imagine you&#8217;re driving and need to pull over to call someone, but don&#8217;t have his or her number. Just look it up in the mobile app and fire up the call. We don&#8217;t see these features being in use on day one, but we&#8217;re hopeful that the community will adopt them over time.</p>
<p>Even the phones themselves are upgrades &#8211; they sport multiple &#8220;pages&#8221; of programmable keys, providing up to 36 speed dials/extensions if one wishes. There is visual voicemail &#8211; no need to dial in, just access your messages right there on the phone &#8211; and, finally, full two-way speakerphone capabilities. We are also deploying proper conference phones into as many dedicated meeting spaces as we can.</p>
<p>However, this isn&#8217;t <em>just</em> a phone system upgrade. New networking equipment is needed in literally all of our buildings, and new wiring in merely almost all of our buildings. The good news is that this is a valuable chance for us to make large, critically-important upgrades to our core infrastructure. The bad news is that this all takes a lot of time.</p>
<p>Right now, we&#8217;re looking at the bulk of the installation later in the fall term. Since phone deployment takes merely minutes, we likely won&#8217;t aim for a particular break, but it will be all done in a single day. We are doing a soft-launch currently in Moyer and Ettinger halls, however, to see what questions arise. The system itself is completely stable and tested &#8211; it&#8217;s a matter of what concerns arise, what user questions come up that we might not have anticipated, and how we can be most successful during the full launch.</p>
<p>The phones will operate exactly as they do now &#8211; dial &#8220;8&#8221; for an outside line, for instance. Voicemail is working. The only thing that won&#8217;t work for now is the ability to forward voicemails from one system to the other.</p>
