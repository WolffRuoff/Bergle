				<p><strong><span style="font-size:14px;">Don&#39;t forget --</span></strong></p>

<p><em><strong>Encompass Search</strong></em>&nbsp;uses <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">KEYWORD</span></strong> searching, so your search will locate records with <u>all of your search terms anywhere in the record</u>, and not necessarily in the order you want.</p>

<ul>
	<li>To search for a <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">KNOWN TITLE, enter your search in quotes</span></strong>.&nbsp; This will force Encompass Search to locate the terms together in the order you specify.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>You can <span style="font-size:14px;"><strong>combine terms</strong></span> using boolean operators like <strong>AND, OR, and NOT</strong>&nbsp;(be sure to capitalize them!)</li>
	<li>Once you have run your basic search, use the <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">FILTERS</span></strong> on the left-hand side of the results page to narrow your results by <strong>FORMAT, AUTHOR, LANGUAGE, or TOPIC</strong>.</li>
	<li>Use the <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">SUBJECTS</span></strong> hot links found within the item record under the &quot;View Description&quot; drop down. These are <strong>Library of Congress SUBJECT headings</strong>, and can be used&nbsp;to find additional resources on the same or similar topics, or of a similar <strong>GENRE</strong>.</li>
	<li>Use <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">TRUNCATION</span></strong> (i.e., shortening a word to its root or base form using a designated symbol) for words that might have more than one significant form, OR for terms that might have alternative spellings.​​</li>
</ul>

<p>For more information on Truncation or other Advanced Searching Techniques in Encompass Search see our &quot;<a href="https://libraryguides.muhlenberg.edu/usingencompass">Guide to Encompass Search</a>&quot;</p>

			<div id="s-lg-content-58717606" class="  clearfix">
				<p><strong>Performing editions&nbsp;</strong><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family: &quot;Helvetica Neue&quot;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;">are scores that have been edited by publishers and teachers / performers to include fingerings, phrasing, and sometimes tempo indications. Performing editions range in quality, but you&#39;ll find that publishers such as <em>Henle, Wiener Urtext, Breitkopf and Hartel </em>(among others) tend to put out better editions.</span></span></p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px;">Some words associated with performing editions include:<strong> </strong></p>

<ul>
	<li style="margin-left: 40px;"><strong>&#39;Scores and parts&#39; </strong>for instrumental ensembles&nbsp;and&nbsp;</li>
	<li style="margin-left: 40px;"><strong>Vocal score (or Piano-vocal score)</strong>&nbsp;for singers and choral groups.</li>
</ul>

<p style="margin-left: 40px;"><span style="color: rgb(128, 0, 128);"></span><span style="font-weight: 700;"><em>Urtext</em></span>&nbsp;is a&nbsp;German term meaning&nbsp;first/prime/original (text); these editions have very little editorial intervention with the intention that in this way they&nbsp;represent composer&rsquo;s original intentions.</p>
		   </div>
								</div>
								
							</div>
						</div>
					</div>
							</div>
							<div id="s-lg-box-wrapper-29933135" class="s-lg-box-wrapper-29933135">
					<div id="s-lg-box-25479625-container" class="s-lib-box-container">
						<div id="s-lg-box-25479625" class="s-lib-box s-lib-box-std">
							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Study (Historical) Editions
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-25479625" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-58717642" class="  clearfix">
				<p>Critical or Scholarly Editions&nbsp;&ndash; Are based on scholarly evaluation and comparison of sources, taking into account variant readings.&nbsp;&nbsp;Editors&nbsp;examine all&nbsp;available editions of a given work, including sketches, manuscripts, publisher&rsquo;s proof copies, early printed editions, etc. to create the most scholarly edition they can.&nbsp;&nbsp;Critical commentary is included that discusses any alterations in accidentals, notes, ornaments, and so forth. References are included to any manuscript sources available and also to research on whether the composer made changes to the original (such as notes or cuts, etc.). These editions are also sometimes called &lsquo;complete editions&rsquo; or&nbsp;<strong>&lsquo;Gesamtausgabe&rsquo;.</strong></p>
		   </div>
								</div>
								
							</div>
						</div>
					</div>
							</div>
							<div id="s-lg-box-wrapper-29933176" class="s-lg-box-wrapper-29933176">
					<div id="s-lg-box-25479660-container" class="s-lib-box-container">
						<div id="s-lg-box-25479660" class="s-lib-box s-lib-box-std">
							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Complete works
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-25479660" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-58717663" class="  clearfix">
				<p>Collected Works or Complete Works&nbsp;&ndash; are multi-volume sets representing a composer&rsquo;s complete compositional output; usually edited by musical scholars or even other composers.&nbsp; These are generally given the Dewey number&nbsp;<u><strong>SCO&nbsp;780.81</strong></u>&nbsp;in the A level stacks.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>Note:</p>

<ul>
	<li>A given composer (e.g., J.S. Bach, Beethoven, Handel, etc.) can have more than one published set of complete works.&nbsp; New editions are sometimes published as new scholarship becomes available.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Complete works are generally internally organized by genre-- e.g. all Orchestral works, chamber works, vocal works together.</li>
	<li>Many include accompanying volumes of critical commentary (or&nbsp;<em>Kritische Berichte</em>).&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Major publishers of Collected works include:&nbsp; B&auml;renreiter, Breitkopf &amp; H&auml;rtel, Editions Durand, G. Henle Verlag, Scott, Stainer &amp; Bell, etc.</li>
	<li>Publication is often spread over many years, and sometimes publishers change.</li>
	<li><u>Language terms</u>&nbsp;other than English associated with collected works include:&nbsp;&nbsp;
	<ul>
		<li><em>German</em>: Werke,&nbsp;S&auml;mtliche Werke,&nbsp;Gesamtausgabe;&nbsp;</li>
		<li><em>Italian</em>:&nbsp;Opere;&nbsp;</li>
		<li><em>French:</em>&nbsp;Oeuvres.</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
</ul>

					<div id="s-lg-box-25479674-container" class="s-lib-box-container">
						<div id="s-lg-box-25479674" class="s-lib-box s-lib-box-std">
							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Facsimile editions
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-25479674" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-58717713" class="  clearfix">
				<p><strong>Facsimile Editions</strong>&nbsp;- &nbsp;<em>Grove Music Online</em>&nbsp;defines &#39;facsimile&#39; as the &ldquo;<em>Name given to a genre of book publishing based on photo-mechanical printing techniques that attempts to recreate the appearance of an original handwritten manuscript or printed edition &hellip; It is important to note that facsimile editions are not fakes or forgeries. They are produced, conceived and used as tools for study or investigation by scholars, researchers, teachers and others who might not have access to the original material</em>&nbsp;&hellip;&rdquo; Facsimile editions are generally not intended for use in performance, although they can be helpful in determining past performance practices.</p>
		   </div>
								</div>
								
							</div>
						</div>
					</div>
							</div>
							<div id="s-lg-box-wrapper-29933207" class="s-lg-box-wrapper-29933207">
					<div id="s-lg-box-25479689-container" class="s-lib-box-container">
						<div id="s-lg-box-25479689" class="s-lib-box s-lib-box-std">
							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Anthologies, 'Monuments' or Denkmäler
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-25479689" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-58717736" class="  clearfix">
				<p><strong>&#39;Monuments&#39;&nbsp;or Denkm&auml;ler</strong>&ndash; Are collected anthologies that focus on a particular country, region, time period, or type of music rather than a single composer.&nbsp;It is not uncommon for individual volumes to have their own specific editors with a general editor or editorial committee overseeing the creation of the series.&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Some important monument series titles include:&nbsp;&nbsp;<em>Musica Britannica,</em>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<em>Recent Researches in Music</em>.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Significant publishers include: A-R Editions, B&auml;renreiter, and &Eacute;ditions de l&rsquo;Oiseau-Lyre among others.</li>
</ul>
