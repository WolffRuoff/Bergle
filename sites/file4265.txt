			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/senior-year-kickoff/" title="4:43 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 24, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Last Wednesday, the Career Center had a Senior Year Kickoff for Juniors because apparently we have 400 days until graduation. <em>Can you believe it? The current seniors haven&#8217;t even graduated yet and they&#8217;re trying to get rid of us to? (kidding). </em>At this kickoff, the Career Center counselors described upcoming events and programs that should on our radar like the Muhlenberg Shadow Program, Reality MC, the numerous workshops that will be offered, and Business Career Day amongst others.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s so surreal to think that I&#8217;m going to be a senior next year. I&#8217;m pretty sure I just got to Muhlenberg last week and not almost 3 years ago. Where did the time go? And how can I make it stop? Muhlenberg has spoiled me rotten and I&#8217;m not quite sure I&#8217;m ready for the <i>real world</i> yet. I&#8217;m so used to waking up 10 minutes before class and making it on time. I&#8217;m so used to the crepes in the Life Sports Center, having Wok-a-Bilities for lunch and dinner on Fridays and knowing where to find my friends at any moment of any day.</p>
<p>In the <em>real </em><i>world,</i> I have to wake up hours before going to work because I will most likely have to commute. In the <em>real world,</em> I may not see my best friends every day, or even every week. In the <em>real world,</em> I have to pay BILLS. (enter dramatic sigh here). Just thinking about the <em>real </em><i>world</i> is giving me a headache&#8230;</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/sga-lviac-social/" rel="bookmark">SGA LVIAC Social</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/sga-lviac-social/" title="3:46 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 23, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>As chairperson of Operations Committee for Student Government Association, I am in charge of planning our very first Lehigh Valley Student Government Social. We&#8217;ve invited the Presidents, and 4 representatives (of their choice) from other universities within the Lehigh Valley to a dinner at Muhlenberg to discuss what each organization considers &#8216;their best practice&#8217;. Operations Committee has been planning this event for the past month, and I cannot believe it will be taking place next Friday. One college already declined since that night is their inauguration, and 1 college has RSVP&#8217;d, but I still have not heard back from about 6 other institutions, which is making me very nervous! I&#8217;ll send a follow-up email later this week, but it would make me feel much better knowing exactly who is coming, and if they are coming.</p>
<p>Since this is the first LVAIC SGA Social, I do not want it to be a failure. Can&#8217;t wait to tell you how it goes!</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/internship/" title="9:36 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 23, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>For my internship I have to create a promotional video for the Career Center that will hopefully be shown during Orientation Weekend for the incoming freshman class. However, I had no idea how to make a video with only texts. I asked around, learned about a professor and his talents, and sent him an email. He responded immediately, I described what I was hoping to make, and sent him a link to a video that inspired me. After he had an idea of what I was attempting to do, we set up a time to meet and during out meeting he gave me a short demo on how to use Adobe Premier to create my future work of art. It was so nice of him to take some time out of his fay to help me, even though I&#8217;m not even one of his students. I can&#8217;t wait to upload my final project!</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<div id="post-4259" class="post-4259 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/nyc/" rel="bookmark">NYC</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/nyc/" title="1:24 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 21, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>One of the best things about &#8216;Berg is being able to get away from &#8216;Berg for a while. Everyone needs a break from the hustle and bustle of college lifestyle, and last weekend I took mine. I researched the bus schedule, packed my weekend backpack, requested an Uber, made it to the bud stop with minutes to spare, and in 2 hours and 30 minutes I was meeting up with my friend in the NYC. If I had my own car, I would&#8217;ve been there much quicker, but hey, the bus wasn&#8217;t so bad!</p>
<p>I&#8217;m so grateful that Uber is now in the Lehigh Valley. It makes getting around so much easier. Having a car would be beneficial of course, but now I don&#8217;t have to bother my friends for ride, and I can get around on my time. I&#8217;m also incredibly grateful that NYC is fairly close and easy to get to. I think I might take more weekend mini vacations to the city, as long as my friend is willing to have me over :).</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>
