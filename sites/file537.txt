										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>DACA (Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals), a policy put in place under Barack Obama has protected an estimated 800,000 undocumented youth from deportation since 2012. Recently President Trump rescinded the policy and asked Congress to establish laws to regulate handling of undocumented youth.  This September, to voice solidarity with these youth, two directors, New Day filmmakers Brenda Avila-Hanna and Corey Ohama, are offering free streams of two of their short films about undocumented youth. Each film shares personal perspectives and reveals the tense reality of living under the radar while trying to succeed and grow as a person.<br />
For more information about the directors and a short Q&amp;A <strong><a href="http://blog.newday.com/?p=1223&amp;utm_source=DACA%2C+September+2017&amp;utm_campaign=September+2017+%28DACA%29&amp;utm_medium=email">click here</a></strong></p>
