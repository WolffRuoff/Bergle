			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/02/a-new-hope/" rel="bookmark">A New Hope</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/02/a-new-hope/" title="1:40 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 21, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>There&#8217;s a little over a week left in my winter break and must say that I actually felt refreshed by this vacation. Unlike my winter breaks from high school which allowed me a day or two at most to get into the christmas spirit and then another day or two to break my new years resolution, this was actually a break. Whereas winter break from high school was more like a short breath of fresh air, my month long break from college has allowed me to recharge, reorganize and feel ready to enter the second semester with confidence. I am really looking forward to my classes this semester (no math whooo!)</p>
<p>I&#8217;ll be taking the next levels of both bio and chem of course (&lt;&#8211;pun entirely intended). It&#8217;s all good though, because I love those two subjects. My other two classes will go to fulfill General Academic Requirements (We call them GARs around here). They both sound interesting and I really am quite interested in all kinds of disciplines so I&#8217;ll get to explore some philosophy and history/politics next semester. This first semester of college was a little heavy on the hard sciences, so I&#8217;m happy to be more well rounded second semester. Unfortunately I couldn&#8217;t fit a theater department class into my schedule, but I&#8217;ve pretty much given up my pipe dream of a theater major. It simply isn&#8217;t compatible with Biochem, although my friend Kat is still holding onto both. I look forward to delving into the neuroscience half of my studies too, although the psych and neuro courses will have to wait for next year.</p>
<p>I had a lot of fun this break and caught up with many old friends. However I will not let this break go out without one last hurrah. If you readers have been keeping up with my blog then you know that I&#8217;ve been a part of the show choir here at Muhlenberg, and that I&#8217;ve been the soloist for &#8220;American Idiot&#8221; the video of which is posted a few posts up from this one here. Well luckily, I&#8217;ve snagged tickets for a showing of &#8220;American Idiot&#8221; on broadway. I&#8217;m really excited to go see it. This will probably be my last or one of my last posts from break so I&#8217;ll see you all when the semester starts!</p>

					<div class="entry-content">
				<div>
<p>It’s hard for me to believe that finals are just around  the corner. I just had my last Bio lecture of the semester, wednesday  will be an exam, and then next week I’ll have my final. This semester  went curiously fast. It’s taught me how much every individual grade and  assignment actually matters. The semester is so short, and I don’t have  that much time to make a good showing. I’ll admit that I’m a little  intimidated with studying for these finals. There’s so much material to  cover and it’s all so in depth.</p>
<p>Recently, I’ve been having a lot of good, healthy conversation. A  lot of my friends seem to be in a philosophical mood lately. I’m glad  that there are people willing to tackle the big questions. Sometimes I  get tired of having a concrete and totally correct answer in all of my  classes.</p>
<p>Something else I want to comment on is a bit of a delicate subject.  Recently, the Muhlenberg community experienced a very sad happening. I  don’t know if I can go into more detail, but I don’t want to anyway. I  would only ask that you keep the Muhlenberg community in your thoughts  and prayers as we approach the holidays. Be grateful for what you have.</p>
<p>In news less ambiguous and much less melancholy, I was informed a  week ago that I was accepted as a Muhlenberg Tour Guide, so if any of  you perspectives come to visit, you may have me as your guide. If you  do, make sure you tell me you have seen my blog. It will make me love  you. Do people read this part of the site? I don’t really know. However,  as you can see I’m talking to myself and that tells me that it’s time  for me to stop rambling and bring this post to a close already.</p>
</div>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/11/marathon-theater/" rel="bookmark">Marathon Theater</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/11/marathon-theater/" title="9:02 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 18, 2010</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Over the past weekend, Muhlenberg’s Marathon Theater club hosted a  weekend event of nonstop theater. When I say nonstop, I mean it. From  noon on saturday until noon on sunday, skits, musical groups, improv  games, monologues, random dancing…?, and general performing artsy  goodness graced the Parent’s plaza. When the chill was too deep for even  the most hardcore of thespians, they stepped out of the darkness in the  night and continued the whole event in the Seeger’s union lobby  straight through the night. I was chatting with one of the admissions  officers here at Muhlenberg and she alerted me to the fact that there  were a few videos on Youtube from the event. Hopefully she’ll pass that  tip on to my fellow bloggers because I’ve chosen to share with you the  best performance of the entire event as filmed on my handy-dandy Flip  camcorder. This is Live in Color, Muhlenberg’s show choir performing  “American Idiot” from Green Day’s hit rock opera of the same name. (I’m  only telling you it’s the best because I’m in it)</p>
<p>I should warn that the video includes some obscene lyrics within the context of the song.</p>
