			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/fall-break-3/" rel="bookmark">Fall Break</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/fall-break-3/" title="3:37 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 6, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>As long as the last couple of weeks have been with tests, papers, and projects, it is finally fall break. Even though there is still some work that needs to be done, it is nice to have a couple of days to just relax. I&#8217;d have to believe that everyone has a certain couch or bed that they do all of their relaxing on. Well, I have my couch, and I haven&#8217;t done much moving from it.</p>
<p>On Sunday, the plan was to enjoy a nice day of football. During the early games, I was able to do just that. The games were exciting, close, and down to the wire. My dad and I had a great time relaxing and spending some quality time together.</p>
<p>Then, the Jets took the field to play a 4:25 game against the San Diego Chargers. To put it nicely, the team didn&#8217;t even show up. It was unfortunate that we didn&#8217;t have much to watch at all. Unless the team turns things around soon, this could be the making of a really long year.</p>
<p>Aside from that blemish, the break was really nice. Monday was a good chance to run out and get some things that I needed for school. I also got out and played a round of golf. I don&#8217;t get the chance to play often, so I like to take advantage of any opportunity I get. Tuesday was spent getting work done for the next couple of days. All in all it this was a nice chance to relax and get some work done at home.</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/what-does-a-california-kid-do-during-fall-break/" title="5:05 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 5, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Fall break has arrived, and while the short hiatus is highly necessary for my psyche, it doesn&#8217;t make sense to go home. Although it stings a bit to see all my friends return to their beds, I figured&#8230; Why not make an adventure of the few days off?</p>
<p>Four buddies and I arranged to drive to New York for the night to see a concert, then to Boston for three days (where a friend lives).</p>
<p>Jamming out to good music for long car rides is also a memory in itself. Not to mention, the dense northeast trees from Connecticut to Massachusetts are leaving me mesmerized. They just seem so endless, and to my foreign west coast eyes, the immense greenery is a rare site.</p>
<p>So to shortly answer the question posed in this blog&#8217;s title, &#8220;what does a California kid do during fall break?&#8221; &#8212; lots of cool things. There are many miles ahead until we get to Red Sox Country, so until next time, I&#8217;m out!</p>

			<div id="post-3226" class="post-3226 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/what-a-week/" rel="bookmark">What a Week!</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/what-a-week/" title="2:49 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 5, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/swedberg/" title="View all posts by Drew">Drew</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I finally found my computer! Honestly the move into October has been so hectic that it has taken me until now to reflect on all of it! Crazy! Anyway, the week started out with my first fieldwork assignment at Jefferson Elementary School! It was my second day there as I had volunteered the Thursday before and met the incredible first grade class. I am still learning names but they are helping me a lot. This Thursday I was caught off guard when the first question I was asked when I walked in was by Sebastian, asking me what his name was. It took me a few seconds, but I got it on my first guess! Progress! I spend my Mondays there helping them with math, playing outside at recess, and eating lunch with my seventeen first graders. I don&#8217;t know how many college kids can say they get to still enjoy recess so I am happy! Thursdays we work on sight words, projects, and read, so I have the full elementary school experience this year!</p>
<p>On Wednesday I was in my lab for Video Production, where we were took through an entire day on set with a thrown together script and some student-accessible film gear. Granted, we finished in under three hours, but it was still an incredibly informative experience as we look to begin shooting our films this upcoming weekend when everyone comes back from Fall Break!<a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/2014-10-01-09.15.23.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-large wp-image-3227" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/2014-10-01-09.15.23-1024x768.jpg" alt="Students of Video Production working the various roles in a film set while the lights and cameras are on an actor." width="640" height="480" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/2014-10-01-09.15.23-1024x768.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/10/2014-10-01-09.15.23-300x225.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a></p>
<p>On Thursday my two friends and I hit the highway to Philly to see a concert at a small venue in the city. About 200 people came out to see the talented and inspirational young artist, Jon Bellion, who was performing his first show in Philadelphia. After the show we met him (I got my wallet signed) and he thanked us for all the support while we exchanged stories! Pretty cool guy!</p>
<p>On Saturday my teammate and I worked the women&#8217;s field hockey and soccer game and then hopped over to Chipotle and went to see David Fincher&#8217;s &#8216;Gone Girl&#8217; (which after a second viewing could be competing for a top spot on my list of favorite films so go see it!). You could say we are living it up here alone over the break! We&#8217;re going to get pancakes at Applebee&#8217;s tomorrow to support our assistant athletic director&#8217;s son at Central Catholic and I am having dinner with my first grade teacher&#8217;s family later in the day!</p>
<p>Break is enjoyable but I am ready to get the second half of the semester kicked off!</p>
<p>Happy birthday of course!</p>
<p>Drew</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/why-do-i-study-art-basically-questioning-my-entire-existence-in-one-philosophy-class/" rel="bookmark">Why do I study art? (basically questioning my entire existence in one philosophy class)</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/why-do-i-study-art-basically-questioning-my-entire-existence-in-one-philosophy-class/" title="2:48 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 5, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/zoebriggs/" title="View all posts by Zoë Briggs">Zoë Briggs</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>So usually philosophy class involves us coming in having read an article.  We discuss the reading, talk about any questions we may have, and if we agree or disagree.  It&#8217;s really interesting and I enjoy the class, but today we did things a little differently.  Instead of us having a reading, or our teacher leading a discussion, today we got to have an open forum prompted by the question &#8220;Why do you study art?&#8221;</p>
<p>Now, being an artist I have gotten this question a lot.  Whether it was a school guidance counselor, a distant relative who doesn&#8217;t really know me, or a college application, someone always wants to know why I want to study and enter a field that is stereotypically not very stable, both emotionally and finically, and a field that can be deemed &#8220;easy&#8221; when it comes to work load or amount of intelligence needed.</p>
<p>After our professor posed the question to us, we proceeded to go around the room and answer the question one at a time.  At first I was a little intimidated by the idea of sharing  my somewhat intimate and not at all eloquent thoughts about why I do what I do, but as we started to go around the room it became clear that this was not a time for us to prove to each other who had the better reason to be studying art, but it was a time for us all to really reflect and talk about why we all have this common interest and what makes it so special.</p>
<p>What ended up happening was this incredible class where all of us could just speak about art openly and tell everyone why we love doing it.  I have never been more inspired or felt more at home then I did with this community of artists.  Some of the quotes that I wrote down I will take with me forever.  Whenever I am having a bad day, or I am questioning everything in my life (which is completely acceptable as a twenty year old) I will be able to look in my journal and see that there are 15 other people who believe in me and what I do, and that is awesome.</p>
<p>I know this probably wasn&#8217;t the coolest post I have written, and I don&#8217;t have a silly video or beautiful pictures to attach, but I wanted to blog about this class because it is something I will take with me long after Arezzo.  This is a class that has changed who I am and how I view the world, and when I really think about it, thats what I came abroad to do.</p>
<p>&#8220;Art justifies the human being.&#8221;</p>
<p>&#8220;Theatre is not selfish, it is a connection.&#8221;</p>
<p>&#8220;Art is healing.&#8221;</p>
<p>Until next time, Ciao!</p>
