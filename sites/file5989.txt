						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/04/12/division-iii-week-student-athlete-perspective/" rel="bookmark">Division III Week: Student-Athlete Perspective</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/04/12/division-iii-week-student-athlete-perspective/" title="12:44 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2012-04-12T12:44:25-04:00">April 12, 2012</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>In celebration of the NCAA&#8217;s inaugural Division III week, we&#8217;ve had perspectives on the unique Division III experience from a <a title="Division III Week: Faculty Perspective" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/04/10/division-iii-week-faculty-perspective/">faculty member </a>and a <a title="Division III Week: Coach Perspective" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/04/11/division-iii-week-coach-perspective/">coach</a> in The MuhlBox this week.</em></p>
<p><em>But it wouldn&#8217;t be Division III without the student-athletes. So we asked a few Muhlenberg athletes what Division III meant to them.</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/beckhs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1059" title="Brian Beck" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/beckhs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Brian Beck, Baseball</strong></p>
<p>What playing in Division III means to me is the ability for experiences and opportunities off the field. Although throughout the year, dedication and focus for the upcoming season is key in my mind, Division III allows me the opportunity for other focuses as well. Whether it is from an engaging academic environment or the ability to pursue things such as internships during the year, Division III allows for opportunities both on and off the field, which is what you want as a student athlete.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/meolahs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1060" title="Alyssa Meola" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/meolahs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Alyssa Meola, Women&#8217;s Soccer</strong></p>
<p>Being a Division III athlete means being able to excel in academics and athletics. The great thing about playing at Muhlenberg is that I can be fully invested in soccer, as well as academics, clubs and other organizations on campus. Besides being able to participate in a variety of different activities, I love that as a Division III athlete, I&#8217;m playing with other athletes who still play because they love the game. None of us have athletic scholarships, and most of us will never get drafted to play professionally. But, we continue to play for our love of the game, which I find to be humbling. It&#8217;s exciting to look around the field, and see that you&#8217;re playing a game with all your best friends, who are working hard for each other.</p>
<p>We play Division III because we have flexibility to pursue other activities, and because we love the game, and need it in our lives because it is such a strong part of who we are, and most of us could not live without it.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/wayhs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1061" title="Danny Way" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/wayhs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Danny Way, Men&#8217;s Soccer</strong></p>
<p>I chose to play DIII after seeing what my sister went through playing at the DI level. I wanted to go to a small school that had good education, but was also very competitive with athletics. 				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/mulebig_MB0316.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-956" title="mulebig_MB0316" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/mulebig_MB0316-300x170.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="170" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/mulebig_MB0316-300x170.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/mulebig_MB0316-500x284.jpg 500w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/mulebig_MB0316.jpg 780w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>St. Patrick&#8217;s Day weekend gives us great weather, three conference openers and one home opener. Enjoy!</p>
<p><strong>Men&#8217;s Lacrosse vs. Franklin &amp; Marshall (Saturday, 7:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg will be collecting food items and donations for the Second Harvest Food Bank at this game. Most food shelters receive plenty of donations around the holiday season. It is right around spring time when their supplies run out, so this is a very important time to give.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-104" title="Mule Marquee Matchup" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1-300x203.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="203" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1-300x203.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg 350w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>Do not expect the spirit of giving to carry over to the lacrosse field. Centennial Conference games are usually closely contested, and these two teams have taken that to an extreme, going to double overtime last year and triple overtime in 2008 and 2010. Those three games were all in Lancaster; the last meeting in Allentown was a relative blowout, with Muhlenberg winning 10-8.</p>
<p>It could be another close one. Although the Mules come in 2-4 and the Diplomats 5-1 and receiving votes in the national poll, both teams played at Susquehanna with similar results: Muhlenberg won 9-7, and F&amp;M won 9-8 in overtime.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/mlaxlive/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/mlaxlive/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><strong>Women&#8217;s Lacrosse vs. Franklin &amp; Marshall (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>As close as the men&#8217;s series with F&amp;M has been, the women&#8217;s has gone the other way. The Mules are winless in 28 all-time games against the Diplomats, but they will try to change that after getting off to a 3-0 start.</p>
<p>F&amp;M (3-1), which won national championships in 2007 and 2009, is tied for eighth in the Division III poll.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/wlaxlive/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/wlaxlive/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><strong>Baseball at Susquehanna (Friday, 3:30); vs. Penn State Brandywine (Saturday, 12:00 doubleheader)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules (10-3) can surpass their win total of 11 from all of last season by winning two of these three games. Saturday&#8217;s games are the first at home for Muhlenberg this season.</p>
<p>Live stats for Susquehanna game: <a href="http://www.susqu.edu/athletics/livestats/xlive.htm">http://www.susqu.edu/athletics/livestats/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live stats for Penn State Brandywine doubleheader:</p>
<p>Game 1: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/baselive/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/baselive/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Game 2: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/baselive2/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/baselive2/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p><strong>Women&#8217;s Tennis at Bryn Mawr (Friday, 3:30)</strong></p>
<p>The regionally ranked Mules open Centennial Conference play on the road. Last year, Muhlenberg went 7-3 in the CC to qualify for the playoffs.</p>
<p><strong>Men&#8217;s Tennis at New Jersey (Sunday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>It&#8217;s the first Division III match of the season for Muhlenberg. <a title="men's tennis preview" href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/athletics/spring12/0316page2.html">Click here </a>for a season preview.</p>

	<article id="post-945" class="post-945 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-baseball">

						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/03/13/spring-break-report-baseball/" rel="bookmark">Spring Break Report &#8211; Baseball</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/03/13/spring-break-report-baseball/" title="11:05 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2012-03-13T11:05:34-04:00">March 13, 2012</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>The last of our three Spring Break reports is by Nick Gazzillo, a sophomore outfielder on the Muhlenberg baseball team.</em></p>
<p>“My worst fear every year is that someone will get to the gate and say, ‘Coach, I don’t have my ID.’ Don’t let that happen.” This was Coach Goff’s first message to the baseball team during the meeting prior to our trip to Fort Myers, Florida.</p>
<div id="attachment_950" style="width: 330px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/flablog1.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-950" class="size-full wp-image-950" title="baseball in Ft. Myers" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/flablog1.jpg" alt="" width="320" height="239" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/flablog1.jpg 320w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/flablog1-300x224.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 320px) 100vw, 320px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-950" class="wp-caption-text">ID safely in tow, Usewick and Nick Gies get some sleep on the bus en route to the airport</p></div>
<p>Well, apparently freshman infielder Danny Usewick wasn’t paying attention during that part of the meeting. If a 5 a.m. bus ride to the Philadelphia airport wasn’t enjoyable enough for the team, watching Danny scramble through the bus, locker room and his dorm room was surely the thing to get the trip off on the right foot. He eventually found his ID and the Mules arrived in Florida ready for nine days of baseball.</p>
<p>The mission was clear. We were down there to play baseball, collectively get better as a team, learn to “hold the rope” for one another and win some games. The always classy senior Nick “Goose” Busillo kept these goals clear, stating: “We gotta play &#8217;em one day at a time. I&#8217;m just happy to be here and hope I can help the ballclub. I just wanna give it my best shot and, Good Lord willing, things&#8217;ll work out.&#8221;</p>
<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/33photo8.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-949" title="baseball in Ft. Myers" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/33photo8-300x201.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="201" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/33photo8-300x201.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/33photo8-445x300.jpg 445w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/33photo8.jpg 465w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>Bull Durham </em>quotes aside, the trip revolved around a very busy baseball schedule, one that included 6:30 a.m. bus rides to the field and late returns to the hotel. Through it all, however, the team found plenty of time to relax and get their minds off baseball for a while. Movies, video games and team dinners gave us a chance to unwind and rest.</p>
<p>The trip saw the continuation of two traditions of the Muhlenberg baseball program. Outrageous haircuts and facial hair kept the superstitious happy and the rest of the team embarrassed to be seen with them. Games of “mental warfare” kept the freshmen confused and frustrated and the upperclassmen laughing, especially junior pitcher Rob Shannon, also known as “the ghost” due to both the fact that he was never seen in his hotel room and his very pale skin.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/baseteam12_fla.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-946" title="baseball team photo" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/baseteam12_fla.jpg" alt="" width="600" height="166" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/baseteam12_fla.jpg 600w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/baseteam12_fla-300x83.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/baseteam12_fla-500x138.jpg 500w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p>A large outdoor mall located across the street from the hotel provided another getaway for many of the players, although crossing the six-lane highway proved to be a challenge for many, especially when the sprinklers decided to go off on the median.</p>
<p>Players also found time to hang out at the hotel&#8217;s pool. While it was relaxing for most, junior outfielder John Muha and freshman infielder Mike Giordano found out the hard way not to stand near the edge of the pool when sophomore infielder John Scuderi is around. On the bright side, both hits and subsequent splashes were captured on camera and can currently be seen on Facebook.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/33photo7.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-948" title="baseball in Ft. Myers" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/33photo7-175x300.jpg" alt="" width="175" height="300" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/33photo7-175x300.jpg 175w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/33photo7.jpg 314w" sizes="(max-width: 175px) 100vw, 175px" /></a>The pool wasn’t the only place that saw hostility. From the time the bus arrived at the hotel every night, it was a race to get to the only two washing machines in the hotel. Many players found their uniforms to have a more pungent scent than others, and looking at our record, that may have given us an edge over the other teams.</p>
<p>We split our first two games on the first day and won two more games over the following two days before a well-deserved day off. Players were able to spend time with families that had made the trip down, while others spent the day at the beach or by the pool.</p>
<p>The Mules went on to win three of the remaining four games, finishing with a 6-2 record for the trip and a 9-2 record overall. As senior Brian Beck put it, “It was a good, successful trip. We played well as a team and picked each other when we needed to. We were focused on winning baseball games and having fun.”</p>
<div id="attachment_947" style="width: 234px" class="wp-caption alignright"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/flablog2.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-947" class="size-medium wp-image-947" title="baseball in Ft. Myers" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/flablog2-224x300.jpg" alt="" width="224" height="300" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/flablog2-224x300.jpg 224w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/flablog2-764x1024.jpg 764w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/flablog2.jpg 1936w" sizes="(max-width: 224px) 100vw, 224px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-947" class="wp-caption-text">Sophomore Tyler Latrenta gets some studying in at the airport</p></div>
<p>Maybe even more important than the results, the team walked away from the trip closer than beforehand. Senior Mike Speroni gave his view, saying, “The chemistry we have on our team is outstanding. It was enjoyable to get to know the true personalities of everyone, especially the freshmen. I’m really excited about the next few weeks and holding on to the rope.”</p>
<p>The team is off to one of its best starts in history and looks forward to continuing the winning trend back in Allentown. No matter how the season ends up, the Fort Myers trip is sure to be one of the highlights of the year.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/03/12/athletes-of-the-week-19/" title="3:52 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2012-03-12T15:52:53-04:00">March 12, 2012</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>for contests between March 5 and March 11</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/klinehs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-938" title="Kelly Kline" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/klinehs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Female Athlete of the Week &#8211; Kelly Kline, Softball</strong></p>
<div>Kline hit .733 with a 1.600 slugging percentage in five games for the Mules. She had four doubles and three home runs with six runs scored and six RBI. In a 5-4 loss to Misericordia, she tied school records for extra-base hits, home runs and total bases in a game. Kline, also named <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/athletics/spring12/0312.html">Centennial Conference player of the week</a>, had hits in seven straight at-bats in one stretch and had at least one extra-base hit in four consecutive games.</div>
<div> </div>
<div><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/vilahs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-913" title="Joseph Vila" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/vilahs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Male Athlete of the Week &#8211; Joseph Vila, Baseball</strong></div>
<div><strong></strong> </div>
<div>Vila wins the award for the second week in a row. He hit .474 with 10 runs scored, six RBI, an .895 slugging average and a .583 on-base percentage in a 4-1 week for the Mules. He had at least one extra-base hit in four of the five games and also stole two bases. Vila scored the go-ahead run in an 11-5 win against Merchant Marine and drove in the go-ahead run in a 3-1 win against Wilmington.</div>

				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/03/05/athletes-of-the-week-18/" title="3:29 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2012-03-05T15:29:24-04:00">March 5, 2012</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>for contests between February 27 and March 4</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/chilihs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-530" title="Alexandra Chili" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/chilihs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Female Athlete of the Week &#8211; Alexandra Chili, Basketball</strong></p>
<p>Following the Mules&#8217; NCAA Tournament second-round loss to Mount Union, Purple Raider football coach Larry Kehres came to Muhlenberg coach Ron Rohn and said, &#8220;I&#8217;d take your number 12 on my football team any day!&#8221; The endorsement of one of the most successful coaches in college football history helps Chili earn athlete of the week. So do averaging 18.0 points, 10.5 rebounds and 3.0 assists in two NCAA Tournament games and becoming the first Centennial Conference basketball player to score 2,000 points.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/vilahs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-913" title="Joseph Vila" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/vilahs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Male Athlete of the Week &#8211; Joseph Vila, Baseball</strong></p>
<p>Vila hit .500 with four RBI as Muhlenberg won two of its first three games in Florida. He had an .833 slugging average and a .600 on-base percentage. Vila hit a game-tying three-run homer in the Mules&#8217; 10-9 win against Wheaton (Ill.) and singled in the middle of a two-run eighth-inning rally that led to a 4-3 defeat of Ohio Northern.</p>
