								<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>A new fall workshop series invites faculty, students and staff to come learn how to take advantage of some of the software featured in the Walson Hall digital cultures environment.  The hands-on tutorials, led by Tony Dalton, guide participants of all levels of familiarity through the steps of creating digital media tools and objects, including podcasts, screencasts, and video effects.  No prior experience is necessary.  All classes meet in Walson 115.  The schedule is as follows:</p>
<p><strong>October 7, 2009 &#8211; 5:00 p.m. &#8212; Podcasting with Garageband</strong></p>
<div id="attachment_117" style="width: 89px" class="wp-caption alignright"><strong><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-117" class="size-full wp-image-117 " src="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2009/09/ishot-1.jpg" alt="podcast" width="79" height="76" /></strong><p id="caption-attachment-117" class="wp-caption-text">podcast</p></div>
<p>Podcasting has never been easier!  Come and learn the basics of recording, editing and adding flair to your very own podcasts.  Then learn how you can get your voice heard on the Internet in a variety of different ways.</p>
<p><strong>November 4, 2009 &#8211; 5:00 p.m. &#8212; Screenflow</strong></p>
<p>With this newly added tool, you can capture the contents of your entire desktop at the same time as your video camera, microphone, and computer&#8217;s audio.  Create stunning software or system workflow videos for your own tutorials.  Capture live video chats for archival or production purposes. <img class="alignright size-full wp-image-118" src="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2009/09/ishot-3.jpg" alt="ishot-3" width="75" height="77" /></p>
<p><strong>December 2, 2009 &#8211; 4:00 p.m. &#8212; Composite Video Effects Primer</strong></p>
<p>Learn the basics of creating and layering effects using intermediate Photoshop, Motion, and Final Cut Pro skills.  Basic Final Cut Pro experience preferred but not required.  <img class="alignright size-full wp-image-119" src="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2009/09/ishot-4.jpg" alt="ishot-4" width="78" height="81" /></p>
<p>All workshops are open to the campus community.  For more information contact adalton@muhlenberg.edu, or ext. 3871.</p>

								<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-93" src="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2009/09/IMG_0495-300x205.jpg" alt="IMG_0495" width="216" height="148" srcset="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2009/09/IMG_0495-300x205.jpg 300w, http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2009/09/IMG_0495.JPG 468w" sizes="(max-width: 216px) 100vw, 216px" />HYPE&#8211;the Healthy Youth Peer Education program&#8211;will receive this year&#8217;s Allentown Human Relations Commission Award at an awards banquet on October 20.  For the last three summers, Walson Hall has been home to the HYPE summer and afterschool program, where local teenagers work together with students and faculty from the department of media and communication using digital media technologies to tell their stories of community life.  This year, twelve teens researched and produced a 20-minute documentary, &#8220;Roots of Change,&#8221; advocating for social change in Allentown.  An advance screening of their film played to a packed crowd at the Allentown Brew Works in late July.  Exploring media as tools for social change, the teens worked with Eirinn Disbrow (&#8217;10) and Tony Dalton to learn the ins and outs of FinalCut Pro.  Dr. Taub-Pervizpour, Professor Mark McKenna (theatre) and Dr. Chris Borick (political science) are part of the HYPE instructional team.   HYPE is a collaboration between the Lehigh Valley Health Network and Muhlenberg College.  For more information about HYPE visit <a href="http://www.hypeprogram.org">http://www.hypeprogram.org</a> or watch a short promo clip by Tony Dalton <a href="http://europa.muhlenberg.edu/play.php?vid=346">here</a>.</p>
