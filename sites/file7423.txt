					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>March Madness is here! Procrastination month is upon us (for those who diligently watch and keep their fingers crossed)</p>
<p>I&#8217;m not saying that I&#8217;ll be watching games in class, because that would be wrong&#8230; and harmful to my education&#8230; but the games are on during the day&#8230; and games are on during the day&#8230;</p>
<p>PRIORITIES!</p>
<p>No, I&#8217;m only joking people, there is always highlights, but if you see me with my head down walking on Academic Row&#8230;</p>
<p>WATCH OUT</p>
<p>Cause chances are I&#8217;m watching a pretty good game on my Wifi-streamed-phone, and I will not be keeping my head up&#8230; but likely apologizing to you so don&#8217;t worry!</p>
<p>With that being said I just filled out my bracket and for those of you who care&#8230; keep reading cause I&#8217;ll break it down for you.</p>
<p>First round game to watch: VCU vs. Ohio State. VCU is notorious for their success in the tournament but Ohio State behind the play of soon-to-be NBA player D&#8217;Angelo Russell could lead to an unusually early out for the Rams. I take Ohio State.</p>
<p>Biggest Upset in first round: Ok&#8230; I want to say Villanova loses, but the numbers don&#8217;t lie&#8230; its never happened&#8230; there is no chance that a one seed loses now, or in the coming years. Sorry Lafayette. 14 Albany takes down 3 Oklahoma. Albany is this years team of destiny, and if you saw how they won their conference championship you&#8217;d believe me. They don&#8217;t go any further however, since they play in arguably the weakest conference in the country.. but don&#8217;t be surprised if they pull out a second win, especially if Dayton upsets Providence as well.</p>
<p>Final Four: Kentucky, Wisconsin, Virginia, Iowa State. Kentucky is good. Wisconsin will end that with their experience. Virginia&#8217;s defense will overwhelm the East. Iowa State is the scariest team in the division, since no lead is too big for them to overcome, and that is SO important in this tournament.</p>
<p>Champion: Wisconsin (over Virginia). Great game. Experience wins.</p>
<p>Where everything goes wrong in the bracket world: Notre Dame beats Kentucky&#8230; millions of brackets catch flames and disintegrate. You heard it here first.</p>
<p>Happy birthday and good luck with your brackets!</p>
<p>Drew</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/03/two-more-months/" rel="bookmark">Two More Months</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/03/two-more-months/" title="11:26 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">March 15, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>It is hard for me to believe that I am closing in on the last two months of my college career. I doing everything I can to enjoy every second and not rush through anything this semester, but it seems like the time is moving so fast. Every time I look up another week has past and the feeling of graduation closing in feels all to real.</p>

It is hard to imagine all of the people I am friendly with now that I won&#8217;t see on a daily basis anymore. Thinking back I can remember first coming here as a freshman and I have loved each year here. There is a lot of great memories that I will remember the rest of my life.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/03/fun-in-jerusalem/" rel="bookmark">Fun in Jerusalem</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/03/fun-in-jerusalem/" title="11:25 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">March 15, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/as246905/" title="View all posts by Ariel">Ariel</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Wow, I feel like it’s been too long since my last blog! Sometimes when you’re abroad you can get so caught up from one adventure to the next, it’s hard to keep up. But have no fear, I am back and ready to update you all on my adventures!</p>
<p>Last week was a very important holiday for the Jews, Purim (a Jewish festival that commemorates the saving of the Jewish people in the book of Esther)! Similar to Halloween, it’s customary to dress up in all kinds of crazy costumes. There were parties everywhere in Jerusalem all Thursday night and street fairs during the day on Friday. There were people singing and dancing in the street wherever you went; I promise I am not making this up! It was an absolutely incredible experience.</p>
<p>Right after the Purim festivities, my friends and I went to a music festival in the Old City, called “Sounds of the Old City”. Basically, there was a designated path that looped through all four quarters of the Old City, where various street preformers of all different genres were scattered along the path. During one of the performances, my friends and I joined an amazing dancing circle with a bunch of Israelis. That was definitely by far my favorite part of the evening.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image.jpeg"><img class="  wp-image-4032 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-300x225.jpeg" alt="image" width="340" height="255" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-300x225.jpeg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-1024x768.jpeg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 340px) 100vw, 340px" /></a></p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy.jpeg"><img class="alignnone  wp-image-4033" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-169x300.jpeg" alt="image copy" width="173" height="307" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-169x300.jpeg 169w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-577x1024.jpeg 577w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy.jpeg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 173px) 100vw, 173px" /></a> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-2.jpeg"><img class="alignnone  wp-image-4034" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-2-169x300.jpeg" alt="image copy 2" width="174" height="308" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-2-169x300.jpeg 169w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-2-577x1024.jpeg 577w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-2.jpeg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 174px) 100vw, 174px" /></a> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-3-e1426431058364.jpeg"><img class="alignnone  wp-image-4035" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-3-e1426431058364-225x300.jpeg" alt="image copy 3" width="231" height="308" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-3-e1426431058364-225x300.jpeg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-3-e1426431058364-768x1024.jpeg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 231px) 100vw, 231px" /></a></p>
<p>After all of the Purim and music festival excitement, I concluded my week by running a 10k in the Jerusalem Marathon! I didn’t even run with music because everywhere you looked, there were street performers, people cheering you on, various organizations chanting things in Hebrew that I didn’t understand, and just overall beautiful scenery.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-4.jpeg"><img class="  wp-image-4036 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-4-300x225.jpeg" alt="image copy 4" width="291" height="218" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-4-300x225.jpeg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-4-1024x768.jpeg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 291px) 100vw, 291px" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-5-e1426431211775.jpeg"><img class=" size-medium wp-image-4037 aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-5-e1426431211775-225x300.jpeg" alt="image copy 5" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-5-e1426431211775-225x300.jpeg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/image-copy-5-e1426431211775-768x1024.jpeg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Now, as much as I would love to continue to share every detail of my abroad experience (and yes, there’s always more to share), I have to go study for my Hebrew quiz!</p>
<p>‘Till next time,</p>
<p>Ariel</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/03/spring-break-5/" rel="bookmark">Spring break!!</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/03/spring-break-5/" title="12:48 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">March 13, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>When I left Allentown, the weather sitting in the 25 degrees range, was not necessarily conducive of spring. However, I wasn&#8217;t about to complain about the eventful spring break that I had ahead of me.</p>
<p>First, I took two connecting flights to get from Allentown to Miami. In a secret arrangement with my mom, we met in Florida to surprise my girlfriend, who was there with her family. The juxtaposition of freezing PA and 85 degrees Miami beach was amazing.</p>
<p><img class=" aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/IMG_3064.jpg" alt="" width="464" height="620" /></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>After a relaxing three days in paradise, I hopped on a plane and hurried back to LA. The family (and dogs especially) were beyond excited to see me. Taking full advantage of the equally beautiful weather in LA, I spent all my time outdoors hiking, beaching, and just generally soaking up all the sun I could get.</p>
<p><img class=" aligncenter" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/03/IMG_3044.jpg" alt="" width="474" height="474" /><span style="line-height: 24px;"></span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 24px;">Now that I&#8217;m back on campus, it seems as if iv brought some warmth back to campus with me! Where did winter go?!? Hopefully it doesn&#8217;t go anywhere&#8230;</span></p>
