			<div id="s-lg-content-50758571" class="  clearfix">
				<p><strong><span style="font-size:14px;">Don&#39;t forget --</span></strong></p>

<p><em><strong>Encompass Search</strong></em>&nbsp;uses <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">KEYWORD</span></strong> searching, so your search will locate records with <u>all of your search terms anywhere in the record</u>, and not necessarily in the order you want.</p>

<ul>
	<li>To search for a <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">KNOWN TITLE, enter your search in quotes</span></strong>.&nbsp; This will force Encompass Search to locate the terms together in the order you specify.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>You can <span style="font-size:14px;"><strong>combine terms</strong></span> using boolean operators like <strong>AND, OR, and NOT</strong>&nbsp;(be sure to capitalize them!)</li>
	<li>Once you have run your basic search, use the <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">FILTERS</span></strong> on the left-hand side of the results page to narrow your results by <strong>FORMAT, AUTHOR, LANGUAGE, or TOPIC</strong>.</li>
	<li>Use the <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">SUBJECTS</span></strong> hot links found within the item record under the &quot;View Description&quot; drop down. These are <strong>Library of Congress SUBJECT headings</strong>, and can be used&nbsp;to find additional resources on the same or similar topics, or of a similar <strong>GENRE</strong>.</li>
	<li>Use <strong><span style="font-size:14px;">TRUNCATION</span></strong> (i.e., shortening a word to its root or base form using a designated symbol) for words that might have more than one significant form, OR for terms that might have alternative spellings.​​</li>
</ul>

<p>For more information on Truncation or other Advanced Searching Techniques in Encompass Search see our &quot;<a href="https://libraryguides.muhlenberg.edu/usingencompass">Guide to Encompass Search</a>&quot;</p>

							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-22471745" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-50822266" class="  clearfix">
				<p>Use&nbsp;<span style="font-weight: 700;"><span style="font-size: 14px;">TRUNCATION</span></span>&nbsp;(i.e., shortening a word to its root or base form using a designated symbol) for words that might have more than one significant form, OR for terms that might have alternative spellings.​​</p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 700;">First, type as much of the word that you KNOW is correct</span>.</p>

<ul>
	<li style="margin: 0.25em 0px;">A minimum of three characters must precede any of the following symbols.</li>
</ul>

<p>​<span style="font-weight: 700;">Use an asterisk (*)</span>&nbsp;to indicate any number of letters.</p>

<ul>
	<li style="margin: 0.25em 0px;">Add to the end of a word to retrieve variant endings.</li>
	<li style="margin: 0.25em 0px;">This kind of search returns records with <strong><em>zero</em>&nbsp;or more</strong> characters in place of the truncation symbol.</li>
	<li style="margin: 0.25em 0px;">Example:&nbsp;<strong>connect*</strong>&nbsp;retrieves&nbsp;<em>connect,&nbsp;connection,&nbsp;connectivity</em>, etc.</li>
</ul>

<p><span style="font-weight: 700;">Use a pound sign (aka hashtag, octothorpe; #)</span>&nbsp;to represent a single character.</p>

<ul>
	<li style="margin: 0.25em 0px;">Replaces one character in a search.</li>
	<li style="margin: 0.25em 0px;">Example:&nbsp;w<strong>om#n</strong>&nbsp;retrieves&nbsp;<em>woman&nbsp;and&nbsp;women</em></li>
</ul>

<p><span style="font-weight: 700;">Use a question mark (?)</span>&nbsp;to match missing (zero) or up to nine characters.</p>

<ul>
	<li style="margin: 0.25em 0px;">Replaces 0 to 9 unknown characters in a search term.</li>
	<li style="margin: 0.25em 0px;">If ? is followed by a number, it replaces the specified number of unknown characters (<span style="font-weight: 700;">?n</span>&nbsp;where n= a number from 1 to 9).</li>
	<li style="margin: 0.25em 0px;">Examples:&nbsp;
	<p><strong>encyclop?dia&nbsp;</strong>retrieves&nbsp;<em>encyclopedia&nbsp;and&nbsp;encyclopaedia</em></p>

	<p><strong>res?4tion</strong>&nbsp;retrieves&nbsp;<em>respiration,&nbsp;restoration,&nbsp;reservation</em>, etc.</p>
	</li>
</ul>
