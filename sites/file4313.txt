				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/baseball-season/" title="11:03 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 27, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>You would never guess with this weather, but spring sports are finally starting. This weekend the baseball team traveled to Washington DC to open up our season. Obviously, we will not be playing on our field for a while, but to me this really marks the start of better weather. Whenever baseball season gets going, the warmer weather usually follows.</p>
<p>This weekend was a lot nicer out then any time in the recent past. I wouldn&#8217;t expect the weather to stay this way, but this weekend was the first sign of spring that we have seen in a while. I don&#8217;t think anyone would be surprised if we were to get another snow storm soon and there is no question it will be freezing again, but either way it is nice to see that spring really isn&#8217;t that far away.</p>
<p>The point is that soon enough we will be able to wear shorts and break out our summer gear again. February is just about over, and March is the time that everything should really start to change. As I said baseball is my big spring sport. Everyone has their own thing that they love about the spring. Whatever it may be, just remember that it is not that far away.</p>

				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/muhlenplays/" title="11:02 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 27, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hi all!</p>
<p>So a bit on the theatre front this semester&#8230;</p>
<p>The Muhlenberg Theatre Association just put on its first MainStage play of the semester, the Learned Ladies by Molière. Directed by Jim Peck, it was an all-female cast in a humorous play about marriage and the role of learning for women in France in the 1600s. I thoroughly enjoyed the show! The costumes were incredible, and I was laughing the entire time. Also, it was very cool to see the show done with an all-female cast, with some girls cross-dressing for the male roles. I think it&#8217;s great that Muhlenberg theatre experiments with things like that, taking risks to reach a new dimension in art.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone" alt="" src="https://scontent-b-lga.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-frc3/t1/1170702_10152217853496037_246136169_n.jpg" width="960" height="641" /></p>
<p>And now, a word about the Red Door Play Festival! This is a festival that is student directed, featuring short plays. This semester, I&#8217;ll be stage managing a play called Spotlight that my friend Ally is directing. I&#8217;m so excited, I&#8217;ve never stage managed before! We have a relatively small cast of 4 actors, and the play is about&#8230;college theatre students! It&#8217;s a comedy that will go up in April. I love the opportunities Muhlenberg has for students to direct and act and stage manage, even for kids with no experience! It&#8217;ll be an adventure I can&#8217;t wait to share with all of you.</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Emily</p>

			<div id="post-2753" class="post-2753 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/muhlensports-and-food/" rel="bookmark">Muhlensports and food</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/muhlensports-and-food/" title="4:50 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 18, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hey all!</p>
<p>Well, yes, there has been even MORE snow this past week, earning Muhlenberg not one but TWO snow days! I, for one, am sick of this snow, and can&#8217;t wait for it all to just melt.</p>
<p>But I feel as though all I&#8217;ve been talking about for the past month is the weather! So today, I want to talk about sports and food.</p>
<p>First off, sports! Muhlenberg has a beautiful Life Sports Center, complete with a weight room, cardio room, pool, several basket ball courts, a track, and some delicious smoothies. There are great classes offered, such as Zumba, spin class, and even ballet barre workout! I try to go to the gym several times a week. Like most colleges, Muhlenberg has an intramural sports program, where non-official student teams compete against each other. Recently, my friends have created a basketball team for the male basketball intramural league. With a Twitter, Facebook page, and team nicknames, they&#8217;re pretty into it! They&#8217;re not the best team, but they&#8217;re a lot of fun to watch.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/IMG_6506.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-2754 aligncenter" alt="IMG_6506" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/IMG_6506-225x300.jpg" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/IMG_6506-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/IMG_6506-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Second, food! Muhlenberg has delicious food at any time during the year. But this past week, they had a special chef, Navin Khadilkar, cook for the campus! I don&#8217;t personally like Indian food, but look at this ridiculous line! Clearly, chef Khadilkar was a hit!</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/IMG_6529.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-2755 aligncenter" alt="IMG_6529" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/IMG_6529-200x300.jpg" width="200" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/IMG_6529-200x300.jpg 200w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/IMG_6529.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 200px) 100vw, 200px" /></a></p>
<p>Well, there&#8217;s a little taste (pun intended) of what&#8217;s been happening in between snowstorms here at &#8216;Berg. Here&#8217;s to hoping the worst of winter has passed!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

			<div id="post-2741" class="post-2741 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/we-need-spring/" rel="bookmark">We Need Spring</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/we-need-spring/" title="4:49 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 18, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I&#8217;m going to guess that most of you feel the same way that I do: we need this snow to end. The first couple of times it snowed wasn&#8217;t that bad; it was even a little exciting. It didn&#8217;t seem like that much of a hassle walking to school through the snow, and classes were cancelled, which never hurts.</p>
<p>Now, it is just getting annoying. Walking to class is a struggle and teachers are forced to schedule make up classes. This snow seems to be getting in the way of big events too. Now I don&#8217;t know if you are a sports fan, but the fact that the Duke vs North Carolina basketball game was cancelled is where I draw the line. I didn&#8217;t even know that was possible.</p>
<p>At this point, it seems like every snow storm is followed by another one just a few days later. One big storm hits, and the only thing that people talk about is that there is another one coming a few days later. It just seems like there is no end in sight.</p>
<p>Just remember that eventually this will all end. Not too far down the road, it will start to warm up, and we will get to the spring. I still can&#8217;t wait for that first day that I can comfortably walk to school in shorts and a t-shirt. When that time comes, you will forget what it was like to walk through the snow to get to class, so stay tough and remember: spring always gets here.</p>

			<div id="post-2744" class="post-2744 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/the-cold-never-bothered-me-anyway/" rel="bookmark">The Cold Never Bothered Me Anyway</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/the-cold-never-bothered-me-anyway/" title="4:53 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 17, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hey everyone. I&#8217;ve got time to update finally! Berg has been under more than two feet of snow laid down over several days. I always say there&#8217;s no point in it being cold if it&#8217;s not going to snow (I lied in the title, the cold does bother me). Still, even I must admit that it&#8217;s getting a bit old. People&#8217;s cars are literally just mounds of snow in the shapes of sedans, nobody can get anywhere, and classes have been cancelled several times in the past few days. On the plus side, everything is really pretty and covered in snow.</p>
<div id="attachment_2745" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-2.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2745" class="size-large wp-image-2745" alt="The bell tower in our 3rd snowstorm this week" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-2-1024x1024.jpg" width="640" height="640" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-2-1024x1024.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-2-150x150.jpg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-2-300x300.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2745" class="wp-caption-text">The bell tower in our 3rd snowstorm this week</p></div>
<div id="attachment_2746" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-3.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2746" class="size-large wp-image-2746" alt="Just outside the door to my building" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-3-768x1024.jpg" width="640" height="853" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-3-768x1024.jpg 768w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-3-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-3.jpg 1536w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2746" class="wp-caption-text">Just outside the door to my building</p></div>
<p>Note the bench being eaten by snow in the second picture. By the end of that storm it was covered completely.</p>
<p>Aside from the snow, I&#8217;ve been doing really well. I got back some really solid grades in my Advanced Biochemistry class (which is about as scary as it sounds). I got into a black-box theater production called Viper&#8217;s Circus. It is, just like it sounds, half-play/half-circus complete with tightropes, hypnotism, knife throwing and acrobatics. Oh, and it tackles issues of power, desire, sexuality and gender because this is a private liberal arts institution and that&#8217;s just what we do. The best part? It was written by one of my good friends from my honors program, and with whom I worked over the summer in our molecular neuroscience lab. Why are two molecular neuroscientists working on a gender circus? Why are the froggy people now the theater people? Why are the Dana scholars doing acrobatics in the black box theater?! Theory of connectivity folks, theory of connectivity.</p>
<p>Also, the cast is a really awesome group of guys who are all dedicated to creating something as artsy as we can get without being cringe-worthy. It&#8217;s a dangerous game but we are dangerous gentlemen. Oh, and when we aren&#8217;t defying death, we play ninja.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-1.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2747" alt="photo 1" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-1-1024x1024.jpg" width="640" height="640" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-1-1024x1024.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-1-150x150.jpg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-1-300x300.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /> </a><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-31.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2748" alt="photo 3" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-31-1024x768.jpg" width="640" height="480" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-31-1024x768.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/02/photo-31-300x225.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
