			<div id="s-lg-content-35075924" class="  clearfix">
				<p dir="ltr" id="docs-internal-guid-b4be74c4-0ade-98f9-854c-5fcdbd3a614c">Requirements: &nbsp;Flash drive to save video recording (optional: laptop to project presentation)</p>

<p dir="ltr"><strong>Setup Notes</strong>&nbsp;</p>

<ul style="list-style-type:disc;">
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">If you wish to display a PowerPoint or other material during your recording, first attach your laptop to the digital projector using the available HDMI or VGA cable. &nbsp;Then turn on the projector, followed by your computer, and get your PowerPoint presentation ready. &nbsp;Be sure to use the menu button on the white remote to navigate the projector to the correct input.</p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">To use the optional green screen projection, push the silver switch (Left = Green light; Right = Blue Light) on the controller labeled green screen (Fig. 1).</p>
	</li>
</ul>

<p dir="ltr"><strong>How To Record</strong></p>

<ol style="list-style-type:decimal;">
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">Insert your flash drive into the USB port on top of the cabinet (Fig. 2). By plugging this in, you will initialize the system. &nbsp;At this point, the room lights should dim and studio lights turn on. You can turn on the tv monitor to see yourself while you record.</p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">The computer screen on the cabinet will tell you when the system is ready to begin.</p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">Push the silver button with the blue light to begin your recording session.</p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">When finished, push the same button again to end your recording session. &nbsp;The system will then download the video file to your flashdrive. Do not remove your flash drive from the USB port until the screen indicates it is safe to do so. If a warning message pops up after the screen has told you to remove your flash drive, ignore it.</p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">The studio lights will turn off and the regular room lights will turn back on.</p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">You may now view your video on your own or edit it as needed.</p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">If you have used the digital projector, please turn it off before leaving. The camera and monitor may remain on.</p>
	</li>
</ol>

<p dir="ltr"><strong>Troubleshooting</strong></p>

<ul style="list-style-type:disc;">
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">If nothing happens when you push the button, and the camera appears to be off (the tv monitor will display a message about there being no input), push the silver button on the top of the camera. The camera should turn on, and the tv monitor should display what the camera is recording.</p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">If nothing happens when you push the button and the camera seems to be on, try pushing the spacebar on the keyboard in the cabinet drawer.</p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">While it is possible to plug a flash drive into the projector itself, it is not recommended. Connecting a laptop is a better solution.</p>
	</li>
</ul>
