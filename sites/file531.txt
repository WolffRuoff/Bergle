										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Join Trexler Library on Tuesday, October 17th at 5:30 p.m. in the Level A Concourse as we welcome Dr. Michael Barton of Penn State-Harrisburg. Dr. Barton will be reading from</p>
<p style="text-align: center">&#8220;Glorious Recollections: J. Howard Wert&#8217;s Lost History of the 209th Regiment, Pennsylvania Volunteer Infantry, 1864-1865, including the Defense of Bermuda Hundred, the Battle of Fort Stedman and the Storming of Petersburg, with Additional Documents.&#8221;</p>

<p>Dr. Barton and his students have transcribed, edited, and compiled supporting documentation for Trexler Library&#8217;s previously unpublished Civil War regimental history, authored by historian, educator, collector, poet, and novelist John Howard Wert (1841-1920).</p>
<p>Prof. Barton received his doctorate in American Civilization at the University of Pennsylvania and was a faculty member in the American Studies program at the Harrisburg campus of Penn State University for over four decades.  He has been a Fulbright professor at the University of Copenhagen in Denmark and an invited lecturer in fifteen countries.</p>
<p>His Civil War scholarship includes the books <em>Goodmen: The Character of Civil War Soldiers</em> (Penn State, 1981), <em>The Civil War Soldier: A Historical Reader</em> (NYU, 2002)<em>,</em> co-edited with Prof. Larry Logue, and <em>The Civil War Veteran: A Historical Reader </em>(NYU, 2007) also co-edited with Logue.  He and his students and co-editors have recently published <em>The Civil War Memoir of Sgt. Christian Lenker, 19<sup>th</sup> Ohio Volunteers</em> (Xlibris, 2014), <em>Noble Sentiments of the Soul:  The Civil War Letters of Joseph Dobbs Bishop, Chief Musician, 23<sup>rd</sup> Connecticut Volunteer Infantry, 1862-1863</em> (Xlibris, 2015), and, <em>Glorious Recollections:  J. Howard Wert’s Lost History of the 209<sup>th</sup> Regiment, Pennsylvania Volunteer Infantry, 1864-1865 . . . .</em> (Xlibris, 2016).</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><em style="font-size: 16px"><img data-attachment-id="2207" data-permalink="http://trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu/trexler_blog/uncategorized/209th/attachment/barton_pic/#main" data-orig-file="https://i2.wp.com/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu/trexler_blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barton_pic.jpg?fit=1536%2C1024" data-orig-size="1536,1024" data-comments-opened="0" data-image-meta="{&quot;aperture&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;credit&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;camera&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;caption&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;created_timestamp&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;copyright&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;focal_length&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;iso&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;shutter_speed&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;orientation&quot;:&quot;1&quot;}" data-image-title="Barton_pic" data-image-description="" data-medium-file="https://i2.wp.com/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu/trexler_blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barton_pic.jpg?fit=300%2C200" data-large-file="https://i2.wp.com/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu/trexler_blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barton_pic.jpg?fit=829%2C553" loading="lazy" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2207" src="https://i2.wp.com/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu/trexler_blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barton_pic.jpg?resize=300%2C200" alt="" width="300" height="200" srcset="https://i2.wp.com/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu/trexler_blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barton_pic.jpg?resize=300%2C200 300w, https://i2.wp.com/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu/trexler_blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barton_pic.jpg?resize=768%2C512 768w, https://i2.wp.com/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu/trexler_blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barton_pic.jpg?resize=1024%2C683 1024w, https://i2.wp.com/trexlerworks.muhlenberg.edu/trexler_blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barton_pic.jpg?w=1536 1536w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" data-recalc-dims="1" /></em></p>
<p>During the sesquicentennial of the Civil War, Prof. Barton was a co-organizer of a scholarly symposium on the US Colored Troops, held in conjunction with the reenactment of the USCT Grand Review in Harrisburg.  Recently he was in charge of a symposium on Paul Kahan’s new biography of Simon Cameron, held at the Historical Society of Dauphin County, Cameron’s city home from 1865 to 1889.  Barton has also been a member of the Advisory</p>
<p>Council of the National Civil War Museum in Harrisburg.</p>
<p>A website he supervises contains the journals that Charles Rawn kept from 1830 to 1865; Rawn was an anti-slavery attorney, elderly soldier, and Harrisburg draft board member.  Another of Barton’s websites, <a href="http://old8thward.com/">old8thward.com</a>, contains research articles, photographs and maps from a neighborhood that was a playground for Civil War soldiers from nearby Camp Curtin.</p>
<p>This event is open to members of the Muhlenberg community and members of the public. Refreshments will be served.</p>
