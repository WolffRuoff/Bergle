							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Guide for writing Op-Ed pieces
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-24608874" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-56412259" class="  clearfix">
				<p>1. Be sure to make an argument. Don&#39;t just summarize details but argue for or against something. Example: &quot;Mail-in ballots should be encouraged in an election because they will increase voter participation.&quot;</p>

<p>2. Keep the op-ed short. Most sources recommend fewer than 1,000 words.</p>

<p>3. Keep paragraphs short, fewer than 4 sentences.</p>

<p>4. Begin each paragraph with a clear topic sentence that summarizes what will follow in that paragraph.</p>

<p>5. Use facts, data, statistics, and research articles as evidence in each of your paragraphs.</p>

<p>6. Know your audience. Think about the audience you will be writing for as your write your op-ed, and shape your tone and content accordingly.</p>

<p>7. Consider organizing your op-ed using the &quot;ABC Approach&quot; (source: <a href="https://www.the-learning-agency.com/insights/write-an-op-ed">The Learning Agency</a>):</p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px;"><em>Attention</em>: grab the reader&#39;s attention by saying something thought-provoking or even shocking.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px;"><em>Billboard:</em> announce your argument.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px;"><em>Context</em>: provide some brief background (history, etc.)</p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px;"><em>Demonstrate</em>: your body paragraphs, each providing key pieces of evidence that support your argument.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px;"><em>Equivocate</em>: a brief description and then discounting of the main counter-argument against your view.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px;"><em>Forward: </em>Conclusion, with impact and looking ahead.</p>

Most full text is linked directly in the database; otherwise use the Check for Full Text buttons to check for full text online or in the library.</div>

Journals include core titles from a variety of disciplines: history, economics, biology, and more.</div>
