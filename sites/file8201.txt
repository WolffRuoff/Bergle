								<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>As you know, part of your grade includes contributing to the discussion through commenting and tweeting. They should be meaningful in nature and reflect some critical thinking on the topic. Being part of the discourse is important to better understanding as well as being part of a community of thought. Not all comments are created equal, however. The focus should be on the content and be constructive or informative in nature and should not be to attack or degrade. Arguments and counterpoints are productive, but we want to strive for healthy conversation.</p>
<p>Yesterday we read an article that appeared on Medium. When Medium first launched, they actually disabled comments. As we saw yesterday, however, Medium now does have commenting and actually does comments a bit differently than most sites. By the way, Medium was started by one of the co-founders of Twitter.</p>
<p>Here is an article that argues against comments:</p>
<p><a href="http://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2015/sep/10/end-online-comments" target="_blank">http://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2015/sep/10/end-online-comments</a></p>
