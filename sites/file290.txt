<p>If you&#8217;re facing an information overload or having trouble finding information you can trust during this time, you&#8217;re not alone. </p>



<p>To combat this, Teaching and Learning Librarian Rachel Hamelers and Assistant Professor and Director of Public Health Chrysan Cronin answered some frequently asked questions about COVID-19 and offered advice on where to find trustworthy information. <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/news/2020/howtofindreliableinformationoncovid-19.html">To learn more from these Muhlenberg experts about finding reliable sources that will keep you informed as the COVID-19 situation develops, read this article.</a></p>



<p>Rachel Hamelers (Trexler Library) and Chrys Cronin (Public Health) along with Kathleen Bachynski (Public Health), Casey Miller (Anthropology), and Lufei Teng (Economics), also participated in a panel entitled: <strong>The Coronavirus Pandemic: COVID-19</strong>. This presentation looked at the COVID-19 pandemic from a variety of disciplinary perspectives, and gives a snapshot of what information we had on the pandemic on March 10, 2020. <p>This week is <a href="http://sunshineweek.org/">Sunshine Week</a>, a nationwide reminder and celebration of our access to public information via the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA). &nbsp;Passed in 1966, the FOIA requires U.S. government agencies to release their records to the public upon request.  Certain materials are categorically exempt from release, including documents involving issues of national security, an individual’s right to privacy, or internal agency management. &nbsp;However, FOIA requests provide a significant amount of information to the public, which allows for comprehensive news coverage and can increase accountability.  <a href="https://withoutfoia.tumblr.com/">The News Media for Open Government blog titled “Without FOIA”</a> provides details on these news stories. &nbsp;In order to file a request, view completed requests (including applications, status, and corresponding documentation), and search all materials by keyword, visit <a href="https://www.muckrock.com/">Muckrock</a>, a non-profit, collaborative news site and member of the Global Investigative Journalism Network. &nbsp; </p>
