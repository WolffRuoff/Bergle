					<h1 class="entry-title">Muhlensemester Two!</h1>

					<div class="entry-meta">
						<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/01/muhlensemester-two/" title="11:25 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 21, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>					</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
						<p>Hey everyone!</p>
<p>It&#8217;s been a pretty busy week back on campus. This semester, all of my work has started right off the bat. So I thought I&#8217;d share what&#8217;s on my plate for the spring!</p>
<p>My courses:</p>
<ul>
<li>Intermediate French</li>
<li>Genes, Genomes, and Society</li>
<li>Piano</li>
<li>Religion and Violence</li>
<li>Civil War, Holocaust, Crisis: Europe 1900-1945</li>
<li>Colonial Latin America</li>
</ul>
<p>This semester, since I&#8217;m undecided on a major, I thought I&#8217;d try to cover some of my general academic requirements, so that when I do pick my major I can just take those classes. All of my professors are very animated and interested in their subject, and though there&#8217;s a lot of reading and essays involved in the course work, I am excited about all of my courses.</p>
<p>In other news, this semester, in addition to my activities from last semester (Class Council, the Muhlenberg Theatre Association, Campus Delegates, and Colleges Against Cancer), I am a research assistant! I am working for Dr. Kushner in the business department. I am helping him to create the National Arts Index, which is a report put out by Dr. Kushner as part of the larger organization Americans for the Arts that examines 78 different arts indicators (such as number of Broadway tickets sold or average payroll in arts administrations) to give an overall score on the state of &#8220;arts health&#8221; in the United States. I am also helping to create the Local Arts Index, a scaled down version of the National Arts Index for every county in the country. It&#8217;s a lot of work, but since arts administration is a career path I&#8217;m considering, I don&#8217;t mind doing it. Plus, it&#8217;s very interesting to learn some of the information, like the total percentage of the population that attended a Broadway production in 2012!</p>
<p>There&#8217;s certainly a lot of work to be done this semester, both in and out of the classroom, but I&#8217;m having a great time at Muhlenberg.</p>
<p>Emily</p>
