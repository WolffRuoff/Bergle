<a name="karen-dearborn"></a></h1>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Karen Dearborn</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/dearbornk2.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-256" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/dearbornk2.jpg" alt="dearbornk2" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span class="Apple-style-span">Professor, Ballet, Composition, and Dance History; Muhlenberg Community Dance Center (MCDC) Executive Director M.F.A., Connecticut College; B.S., Radford University</span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span class="Apple-style-span">Ms. Dearborn is the founder and Director of Dance for Muhlenberg College. She has choreographed more than 80 works in concert dance, theatre, and musical theatre, including National Tours of the Tony Award-winning National Theatre of the Deaf and Equity theatre companies. As a dancer, she performed professionally for 12 years in concert dance and musical theatre productions. Her research interests include performance studies, disability studies, and dance pedagogy, and her writing has been published in the<em>Journal of Dance Education</em>and the book<em>Performing Magic on the Western Stage</em>. She has presented papers at numerous conferences including National Dance Educators Organization (NDEO), Congress on Research in Dance, and the National Dance Association. Currently she is Treasurer of the American College Dance Festival Association (ACDFA) a member of CORPS de Ballet International, NDEO, Society of Dance History Scholars and AEA. Distinguished teaching awards include the Limback and the Spira.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Contact: dearborn@muhlenberg.edu</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a name="pattie-bostick"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Pattie Bostick</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/05/bostickp.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-261" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/05/bostickp.jpg" alt="bostickp" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Lecturer, Jazz and Ballet</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Ms. Bostick has had 10 years of professional dance experience in shows throughout the United States, Europe, China, Japan, and the Caribbean. She is a certified teacher who has taught for Dance Educators of America and Dance Olympus. She also choreographs shows and special events for Confetti Entertainment. Ms. Bostick is a member of the dance faculty at both Muhlenberg College and Cedar Crest College. She is also the co-owner of a private studio, The Patricia Bostick Dance Center.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Contact: pattie.bostick@muhlenberg.edu</p>
<p><a name="lisa-busfield"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Lisa Bottitta-Busfield</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/busfield-sm_000.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-279" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/busfield-sm_000.jpg" alt="busfield-sm_000" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Lecturer, Modern and Dance Education; Ed.M., Temple University; B.A., Muhlenberg College</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Ms. Bottitta-Busfield is a 2002 graduate of Muhlenbergs Theatre &amp; Dance Department, where she received her Bachelor of Arts degree in dance. While in college, she participated annually in the American College Dance Festival (ACDFA) and made her film debut as a dancer in the independent film <em>A.K.A.</em> Ms. Bottitta-Busfield has served as a faculty member in dance for the University of Saint Francis, in Joliet, Ill., and the Indiana University-Purdue University, Fort Wayne, Ind. She has performed as a modern dancer for Dance Arts Theatre company in Fort Wayne, and has been a member of the National Dance Education Organization (NDEO) for the past ten years.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Contact Lisa: lisa.busfield@muhlenberg.edu</p>
<p><a name="susan-creitz"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Susan Creitz</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/creitzs.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-289" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/creitzs.jpg" alt="OLYMPUS DIGITAL CAMERA" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Adjunct: Improvisation and Movement;<br />
Director, Six Meters performance ensemble<br />
B.S., Trenton State College</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Ms. Creitz spent 15 years in New York City with Alwin Nikolais, Murray Louis, Norman Ader and Phyllis Lamhut. She was a faculty member of the dance and theatre departments at the University of Michigan, and she has served a guest residency at Duke University. She has held numerous guest teaching positions throughout the United States, and in the Republic of Ireland for Dublin Contemporary Dance Theatre, Wexford Arts Centre, and Galway Dance Centre. Ms. Creitz is the director of the Muhlenberg Improv Ensemble and was the choreographer for Muhlenberg theatre productions of <em>The Kafka Project</em>, <em>Cave Theory</em>, <em>Ophelia&#8217;s Tattoo</em>, and <em>Juliet and her Romeo</em>.</p>
<p><a name="heidi-austin"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Heidi Cruz-Austin</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/cruz-austin.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-270" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/cruz-austin.jpg" alt="cruz-austin" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Adjunct: Ballet</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Ms. Cruz-Austin has studied at the School of American Ballet and The Pennsylvania Ballet, where she received an apprenticeship in 1994, and joined the Company as a member of the Corps de Ballet in 1995. She has danced numerous featured roles for various choreographers, including Val Caniparoli, Ben Stevenson, Alvin Ailey, Margo Sappington, Christopher d&#8217;Amboise, and Matthew Neenan. She has also had featured roles in many George Balanchine ballets. Ms. Cruz-Austin has also danced with the Philadelphia-based company Ballet X and has performed as a guest artist through the United States and Europe. As a choreographer, she was a winner in the 2003 Ballet Builders showcase in New York City and has been commissioned to create works for Franklin and Marshall College, Bryn Mawr College, and Repertory Dance Theatre. She was a recipient of the 2004-2005 New Edge Residency at the Community Education Center of Philadelphia and a 2008 Fellowship from the New Jersey State Council on the Arts.</p>
<p><a name="megan-flynn"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Megan Flynn</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/flynn-sm_000.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-281" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/flynn-sm_000.jpg" alt="flynn-sm_000" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>

M.F.A. University of California, Irvine; B.F.A. Tulane University</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #262626; font-family: Helvetica;">Ms. Flynn is a dancer, choreographer, and teacher of ballet, </span><span style="color: #262626; font-family: Helvetica;">contemporary</span><span style="color: #262626; font-family: Helvetica;"> dance, and Pilates. As a dancer, she has worked with several companies, including David Parker &amp; The Bang Group in New York City, and EgoArt, Inc. in Cambridge, Mass.</span><span style="color: #262626; font-family: Helvetica;">She received a Professional Diploma in Dance Studies at the Laban Center in London, and was mentored by the Daghdha Dance Company in Limerick, Ireland.</span><span style="color: #262626; font-family: Helvetica;">Her performances abroad include: Edinburgh Fringe Festival, Brighton Fringe, and Resolution! Festival in the UK; The Russia Project in Moscow, St. Petersburg, and Ekaterinburg; and the Dance Improvisation &amp; Choreography Festival in Andaluca, Spain</span><span class="style1 style2">. </span><span style="color: #262626; font-family: Helvetica;">Ms. Flynn has taught in the dance departments of UC Irvine, MiraCosta College, and Milton Academy, and has served as a guest teacher for Summer Stages Dance at Concord Academy and the Boston Ballet School.</span> <span style="color: #262626; font-family: Helvetica;">As a choreographer, her solo and group pieces have been performed internationally and most recently in Philadelphia, New York City and Irvine</span>.</p>
<p><a name="gayanne-grossman"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Gayanne Grossman</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/grossmang.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-267" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/grossmang.jpg" alt="grossmang" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Adjunct: Anatomy &amp; Kinesiology for Dancers;<br />
Dance Wellness Center Director<br />
Ph.D. studies at Temple University; B.A. &amp; B.S., University of Utah</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Ms. Grossman is a physical therapist for dancers at Muhlenberg College as well as an associate instructor of anatomy and kinesiology for Temple University and Muhlenberg College dance programs. She currently serves on the board of directors and the education committee for the International Association for Dance Medicine and Science. She has authored several papers and a book chapter on subjects such as physical therapy for dancers, management of dance injuries, advocating for dance science education, and dance-specific conditioning. Ms. Grossman lectures nationally and internationally on dance medicine and science.</p>

<h2 style="text-align: center;">Madeline Hoak</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/madeline-hoak.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1538" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/madeline-hoak.jpg" alt="madeline hoak" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Adjunct: Aerial Acrobatics<br />
B.A., Muhlenberg College</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Ms. Hoak is a NYC based dancer, aerialist and teaching artist. As a company member of the Daniel Gwirtzman Dance Company (DGDC), she has performed at numerous NYC venues including Joyce SoHo, Queens Arts Museum, Museum of Jewish Heritage, City Center Studios, and the Alvin Ailey theater. Also a company member of Kinesis Project, Ms. Hoak has performed evening length works and intimate theatrical events at The West End Theater, La Mama, Judson Church, Kaatsbaan, Battery and Fort Tryon Park, and the Philadelphia Fringe Festival. In the air, she has performed with Cirque-tacular, Helium Productions, and Above &amp; Beyond Dance. A core member of Grounded Aerial, Ms. Hoak performs nationally on silks, rope, trapeze, harness, and bungee. She has showcased her own choreography at venues such as Brooklyn Arts Exchange, the Abron Arts Center, STREB, and Embody Studio.</p>
<p><a name="nicole-hockenberry"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Nicole Hockenberry</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/nicole-hockenberry.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1539" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/nicole-hockenberry.jpg" alt="nicole hockenberry" width="148" height="222" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Adjunct: Tap</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Nicole B. Hockenberry, a Pennsylvania native, has been tap dancing professionally for over 20 years. She became a performing member of A&amp;G Dance in 1991, under the artistic direction of Germaine Salsberg, and Manhattan Tap in 1992, under the artistic direction of Heather Cornell. She is the founder and director of TAP: Teach All People, which she operated from 1995 to 2006 and relaunched with her husband, Mark, in 2013. Nicole has taught and performed with organizations across the country, including the New York Foundation for the Arts, the St. Louis Tap Festival, Soul to Sole (Austin), Columbus Taps, and Third Coast Rhythm Project (San Antonio). Her choreography has been commissioned by numerous tap companies, including RPM (San Antonio), Tap Ties (Allentown), and In Motion (Chester, N.J.). Nicole spent twelve years as an adjunct professor of dance at Cedar Crest College (Allentown) and three years as a Master Lecturer at the University of the Arts in Philadelphia. Nicole and Mark also operate Crafty Nicole (<a href="http://www.craftynicole.com/" target="_blank">www.craftynicole.com</a>), an online custom-designed clay charm and pendant business that services the international tap community.</p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/JeffreyPeterson.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-273" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/JeffreyPeterson.jpg" alt="JeffreyPeterson" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>

<p><a name="tara-repsher"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Tara Repsher</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/repshert.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-292" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/repshert.jpg" alt="repshert" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Adjunct: Jazz<br />
B.F.A., Towson University</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Ms. Repsher holds a B.F.A. in dance performance and education. She received her early training from Patricia Bartholomew at Stage Door Studio. While performing with the Towson University Dance Company, Ms. Repsher was asked to perform in a restaging of Martha Graham&#8217;s <em>Panorama</em>. Her teaching experience covers many genres of dance, including ballet, modern, jazz, pointe, lyrical, musical theatre, and tap. Ms. Repsher&#8217;s choreography has been seen at the Maryland High School Dance Festivals, Northampton Community College, and Walt Disney World.</p>
<p><a name="sammy-reyes"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Sammy Reyes</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/SAMMY.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-284" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/SAMMY.jpg" alt="SAMMY" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Adjunct: Hip Hop<br />
B.F.A., University of the Arts</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">While at UArts, Mr. Reyes received the Cushman Acting Award and the ensemble theater award for his outstanding achievements in dramatic arts. After college, Mr. Reyes continued his dance training with Ronen Koresh, Rennie Harris, and Kip Martin. He was rehearsal director and dance member of Dance Theater X Project, directed by Charles O. Anderson. Mr. Reyes recently completed touring the nation with television and radio artist Raven Symone. Mr. Reyes continues to choreograph independently for numerous events including the Philadelphia Gay Pride Event at the Kimmel Center in May 2001. A highlight of his career occurred recently when he was nominated for a Barrymore Award in 2007 for Best Choreography for The Bombitty of Errors with 11th Hour Theater Company. Mr. Reyes is Artistic Director of Sanbrooka productions in Philadelphia.</p>
<p><a name="randall-smith"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Randall Anthony Smith</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2016/08/smith-randall_001.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1192" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2016/08/smith-randall_001.jpg" alt="smith-randall_001" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Assistant Professor: Modern, Jazz, African Dances &amp; Cultures<br />
M.F.A. &amp; B.F.A., University of California, Irvine</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Mr. Smith is a dancer with Armitage Gone! Dance, as well as rptiteur and assistant to choreographer Donald McKayle, having performed for McKayle&#8217;s Etude Ensemble for four years. Smith is a former ballet, modern, and contemporary jazz instructor for the Destin School of Music and Dance. Mr. Smith maintains a practice of performing, restaging and interpreting McKayle&#8217;s repertory, creating his own dance works, and teaching dance nationally. He has performed with acclaimed ballerina and choreographer Jodie Gates in &#8220;Mein Zimmer&#8221; (2012) and for jazz dance choreographer Sheron Wray&#8217;s &#8220;So Do You Think Freud Was Really Wrong?&#8221; (2011). Other recent performances include works by Bulareyaung Pagarlav, Shen We, zoe|juniper, Chrisitana Axelsen, and Amanda Selwyn, as well as &#8220;WOW&#8221; (2014), an experimental opera created by Joe Diebes, Christian Hawkey, and Adam Levin.</p>
<p><a name="teresa-sorge"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Teresa VanDenend Sorge</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/sorgetvd.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-294" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/sorgetvd.jpg" alt="sorgetvd" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Full-Time Lecturer, Modern, Dance Education, and Dance &amp; Society;<br />
Director, DanceMax Moving Company performance ensemble;<br />
Director, Muhlenberg Community Dance Center<br />
Ed.M., Temple University; B.A., Hope College</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Since 2000 Ms. VanDenend Sorge has been sharing her talents in various teaching positions including instructor of dance at Hope College in Holland, Mich.; dance instructor at Koresh Dance Company, in Philadelphia; and adjunct professor at Temple University. Teresa has performed with Aerial Dance Theatre, PUSH Physical Theatre, and InLet Dance Theatre. Teresa received her B.A. in dance performance/choreography as well as receiving her certification in dance education for children in grades K through 12.</p>
<p><a name="meredith-stapleton"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Meredith Stapleton</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/stapleton.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1540" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/stapleton.jpg" alt="stapleton" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Adjunct: Ballet, Modern<br />
B.A., Muhlenberg College</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Ms. Stapleton is a Philadelphia-based dance artist and teacher. She grew up in Harvard, Mass., training at Acton School of Ballet and performing with Commonwealth Ballet Company. In 2013, she graduated from Muhlenberg College as a dance major and women&#8217;s studies minor. She has attended many summer workshops, including Burklyn Ballet Theater, Alonzo King Lines School of Ballet, the Boston Conservatory, on scholarship with Summer Stages at Concord Academy, and Doug Varone &amp; Dancers. Meredith has performed works by Corrie Franz Cowart, Trinette Singleton, Meredith Rainey, Sean Curran, Teresa Fellion, The Bang Group, Heidi Cruz-Austin, Charlotte Boye-Christensen, and Jeffrey Peterson. She has worked professinally with DanceSpora, Nora Gibson Contemporary Ballet, Teresa VanDenend-Sorge, Megan Flynn Dance Company, DRIGGProductions, Evalina Carbonell, Sean Thomas Boyt, Ashley Sleeth, Kat Sullivan, The Naked Stark, and Antonia &amp; Artists. She rehearses her own stage, site-specific, and film projects out of Headlong Studios and Mascher Space Co-op. Meredith has taught at Happy Feet Dance Studio, Haddonfield School of Dance, and Koresh School of Dance.</p>
<p><a name="lynn-wiener"></a></p>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Lynn Schiff Wiener</h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/05/wienerl.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group33"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-298" src="http://bergdance.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/05/wienerl.jpg" alt="wienerl" width="80" height="90" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Adjunct: Lester Horton Modern Dance Technique, Ballet, and Jazz<br />
M.A., B.F.A. &amp; M.B.A., University of Cincinnati, CCM and CBA<br />
2012 Alvin Ailey American Dance Theatre Horton Pedagogy Certificate<br />
RYT-200; Certified ChildLight Advanced Yoga Instructor;<br />
Certified ChildLight Teen Yoga Instructor</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Ms. Wiener has performed with The Cincinnati Ballet, New Orleans Ballet, Tulsa Ballet Theatre, and Repertory Dance Theatre. As an Adjunct Professor at CCM, for both the college and preparatory departments, she performed and choreographed extensively for the University Dance Ensemble as well as taught ballet and Horton to university students. She has taught at Xavier University, the school for Tulsa Ballet Theatre, and DeSales University. Ms. Wiener studied, taught, and performed the Lester Horton Modern Dance Technique for more than seven years under the direction of Master Teacher, James Truitte. She has been directed by David McLain, Roman Jasinski, Frederick Franklin, Oleg Sabline, and Alexander Bennett. Lynn&#8217;s choreography has been seen on the mainstages of Lehigh University, DeSales University and recently here at Muhlenberg as the choreographer for <em>Anyone Can Whistle</em>; as well as musical theater productions for Parkland High School and ballets for Repertory Dance Theatre. Lynn joined Muhlenberg in 2008 and has been on faculty with Dolly Haltzman Dance Academy/Repertory Dance Theatre since 1996.</p>
