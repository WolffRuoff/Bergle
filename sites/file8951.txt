	<header class="entry-header">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://fys142f16.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/10/13/social-media-and-politics/" title="Permalink to Social Media and Politics" rel="bookmark">Social Media and Politics</a></h2>
	</header><!-- end entry-header -->
        
	<div class="entry-content">
					<p>Social media, particularly Twitter, has taken politics by storm. Recently, politicians have utilized social media as a way to gain publicity and to voice their opinions on current events. The craze for politicians to start using social media began in 2008 when President Obama posted on Instagram about an upcoming trip to Alaska, racking up thousands of ‘likes’ and ‘comments’ from millions of people around the world. After this, more politicians began using their own forms of social media to connect with people in attempt to gain popularity for upcoming events, such as the presidential election. For example, “Ted Cruz live-streams his appearances on Periscope. Marco Rubio broadcasts ‘Snapchat Stories’ at stops along the trail. Hillary Clinton and Jeb Bush spar over student debt on Twitter. Rand Paul and Lindsey Graham produce goofy YouTube videos. Even grumpy old Bernie Sanders has attracted nearly two million likers on Facebook, leading the <em>New York Times</em> to dub him “a king of social media” (Carr, 2015). Politicians have realized that social media platforms are a very easy way to connect with others, thus causing social media to have a “HUGE” impact on politics, as Presidential candidate Donald Trump would say.</p>
<p>Although social media can be one person’s road to fame, it can also attract unwanted attention if one is not cautious about what they say on social media. Once somebody posts a post, or tweets a tweet, it is forever online, even if they are given the option to delete it. Sometimes those posts come back to haunt the people that posted them in the first place. A perfect example of something that was thought to be forgotten is the recent video that shows Donald Trump speaking ‘locker room’ talk with colleagues which was taped over 10 years ago in 2005. Donald Trump has been hurt tremendously by this in the Presidential election. Some deem Donald Trump as a “god” on social media for the way he issues provocative messages at the perfect moments, giving his 12.4 million twitter followers something to react to, giving him an abundance of attention with every tweet he sends out. Regardless, one of the biggest dangers of social media is being careful about what you say and when you say it, because you never know when the camera might be rolling.</p>
<p>With social media being the main form of communication between people ages 16-25, politicians are smart to be utilizing different platforms in order to send their message out to people of that age range. On the edge of being able to vote, 16 and 17-year-old kids are using social media to talk to friends, connect with celebrities and in some cases, talk to strangers. It is almost impossible to think that they would not pay attention to the messages that politicians are sending out to capture their attention.</p>
<p>Some politicians may seem different in person or on live television compared to how they may seem on social media. Often times people will put their best foot forward on social media, making their social media self the best version of themselves. I have experienced this personally and have seen others do the same. There is a vast difference between somebody on social media and somebody in person. On social media, there is so much time to edit something or practice what you want to post before you post it. There are so many ways to make it ‘perfect,’ so that one can often appear different than what they may appear in the current moment. The way that social media has influenced politics is exciting, to say the least. It seems necessary for politicians to be involved in some form of social media if they want to be successful. I can only think that social media will continue to take over politics, as this might be the beginning of social media in politics.</p>

	<div class="entry-content">
					<p><span style="font-weight: 400">When one thinks of copyright law they automatically associate it with a positive. Copyright law is extremely important to preserving the public’s right to self-expression and continued ownership of individual ideas and ongoing steps need to be taken to maintain these rights.</span></p>

When it comes to politics, social media plays a big role in campaigns. The key point for politicians while campaigning is for them to try to get their message across to as many people as possible to try to earn their votes, technology allows a resource that helps candidates do just that. Now there is a flip side to this, most of the people that are being reached through the internet are the younger, less active block of voters. Reaching out to millions of people is great but who is to say that all those people you are convincing to vote for you are actually going to go out and even vote. This might be the case today, however as explained by Sharon Housley, this younger generation is going to become the older generation and technology will then play an enormous role in our elections. Social media also plays a positive impact in elections through its ability to create an effective way of fundraising. In order for campaigns to run, they need money and a lot of that money is given as support through fundraisers. On a bit of a smaller scale, websites like GoFundMe.com exist today as a place specifically for helping people raise money. Online fundraising can be very effective and it can most certainty help in the fundraising for campaigns. Now these are all some positive impacts technology has on politics, there are many ways technology can be a negative impact for a politician in particular. The saying everything you put on the internet stays there forever, is very much true and well. Everything can be dug up from the depth of the internet and brought back to life, this is also been scene in the Trump vs Clinton election. A commercial, seen a lot not only on television but on Twitter and Youtube, was made by Hillary Clinton and shows an interview done many years back with Donald trump showing him saying he does not have much respect for women. This hurts his reputation towards women tremendously and will for sure put more female votes in the hands of Hillary Clinton. The social media is also a place that spreads news very fast around the world. An audio recording of Mr. Trump has been released that has him talking about women, saying that &#8220;sometimes you just have grab them by the p***y&#8221;. This is a disgusting remark towards women and because of the internet and its social media sites, everyone is aware of Mr. Trumps remarks. As you can see, the internet can be both a helpful hand or a dagger to the heart in terms of its impact on politics.</p>

<article id="post-3799" class="post-3799 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-uncategorized">

	<div class="entry-details">
				<p>October 12, 2016<br/>
		by Zhijiang Liu (Jerries)<br/>
		<a href="http://fys142f16.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/10/12/blog-post-4-copyright/#respond">0 comments</a></p>
	</div><!-- end entry-details -->
    
	<header class="entry-header">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://fys142f16.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/10/12/blog-post-4-copyright/" title="Permalink to Blog Post #4 – Copyright" rel="bookmark">Blog Post #4 – Copyright</a></h2>
	</header><!-- end entry-header -->
        
	<div class="entry-content">
					<p>The major problems in the management of YouTube Company are that lack of a stable system that ensures that safety of legitimate content and the failure to significantly prevent the uploading of infringing material (Plagiarism Today, 2013). These problems are defined in a vast explanation of copyright problem. The legitimacy of the content uploaded by users is questionable since there is a high probability that it can be denied and yet it is original legal work. Similarly, one can upload content that is copyrighted under another user, and yet, the YouTube management can readily accept it as original work. For example, if a company or someone uploads a video that isn&#8217;t theirs. They might get subscribers and a lots of people will following them based on a video someone else did, but it is not their news. Not only  is this a wrong and illegal behavior if the real authors are not cited properly or informed, the real authors will lose the credit that they are due. Therefore, YouTube should focus on the identification of copyrighted and illegal content in order to secure its reputation.<br />
Providing a gate-pass for the creators of content to upload content that has issues with Content ID ( Which is checks to make sure the videos people upload are not the same as others)matches is one of the errors that will put the company’s operations at stake (Plagiarism Today, 2013). There are many companies that have partnered with YouTube. When YouTube subscribers incorporate copyrighted material into their creations, the companies involved might decide to withdraw their services and collaboration with YouTube. In the corporate world, collaboration makes an organization stronger but, with an error such as violation of the terms of agreement weakens its grasp in the market. Not only do the collaborating companies withdraw, but also, potential partners might shrug off and opt out of any partnerships with YouTube.<br />
Conclusively, YouTube’s policy on ContentID matches should be strict on large matches of content and exempt the minor matches. In this way, the right creators of the content will enjoy their rights to pay and the other benefits associated with their creativity. In so doing, the uploader and copyright holder dilemmas would be solved. Much as there would be few cases of violations, the Company will fully enjoy a good reputation, loyal customers and expansion of its services through partnerships.</p>
