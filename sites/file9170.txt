<h1 class="headline">Bioinformatics Central</h1>
  <p class="lead">This resource is designed to serve as a simple and direct guide for basic 
  bioinformatics investigations by students in <strong>BIO 152 Principles of Biology III</strong>
  as well as upper-level courses.</p>
  
  <h3 class="headline">What do you want to do?</h3>
  <p>Consider the scenarios below to determine which NCBI database you should use. Clicking 
  on Tips will take you to a page that provides guidance for gathering information from each 
  database and addresses common questions. Clicking on the name of the database will take you 
  directly to that NCBI database or access the <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/">NCBI homepage</a> 
  directly.</p>

    <h3 id="about" class="headline">About Bioinformatics Central</h3>
    <div class="container-fluid paddingbottom">
      <p>This resource is designed to serve as a simple and direct guide for basic bioinformatics 
      investigations by students in BIO 152 <em>Principles of Biology III</em>.  It is also 
      intended to provide a common interface that will be useful to students as they need to 
      employ various bioinformatic utilities in different upper-level electives. NCBI resources 
      include text-based references (PubMed, Bookshelf, OMIM) and biological databases 
      (Nucleotide and Protein databases, BLAST, Structure). While these applications involve 
      different search strategies and algorithms, it is our hope that combining useful 
      guidelines into one coordinated space may provide the best resource for students and 
      also support a continuing collaboration between our science programs and the library.</p>
