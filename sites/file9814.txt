	<article id="post-58" class="post-58 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-volleyball">
		<header class="entry-header">
						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/05/theres-no-place-like-home/" rel="bookmark">There&#8217;s No Place Like Home</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/05/theres-no-place-like-home/" title="9:10 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-05T09:10:49-04:00">September 5, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/gordonhs2.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-67" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/gordonhs2.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Senior Davi Gordon of the Muhlenberg volleyball team writes about the experience of playing in her home state.</em></p>
<p>Hello ’Berg family and friends, and welcome back to the beginning of a great new school year! The Muhlenberg volleyball team can surely attest to this – just as most students on campus were shutting their textbooks for the weekend, we were jet setting (and by that, I mean riding on a bus for seven hours) to Ohio!</p>
<p>As I am the only Ohio native on the team, this trip was planned especially for me (thanks Coach!). Since my first year of college, I always hoped that I would be able to play in front of a “home” crowd, and although my family members are avid watchers of the live web stream when they can’t make it out to Memorial Hall themselves, I know they had the same wish as well. So, even though we were travelling to Marietta, a small town in <a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG00142-20110903-17061.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-62" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG00142-20110903-17061-300x220.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="220" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG00142-20110903-17061-300x220.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG00142-20110903-17061-409x300.jpg 409w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG00142-20110903-17061.jpg 480w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>Southeast Ohio approximately two hours away from my real home, I couldn’t help but grin from ear to ear as I boarded the bus at 6:45am on Friday because we were headed to the Buckeye State!</p>
<p>We have 14 players on our roster this year, including seven returners (three seniors and four sophomores) and seven freshmen. This has allowed us to do a lot of scrimmaging in practice, which was something we were unable to do during our season last year with only nine players. The team is gelling really well, and despite a few minor injuries, we are getting better and better every day. Not only are our skills improving, but also between the paint renovation in the gym and our new uniforms, the volleyball team is looking good!</p>
<p>The long bus ride to Ohio was a great way for us to catch some zzzZZZzzz’s, play <em>Catch Phrase</em>, watch a few movies, dance to Pitbull and Beyoncé, and of course, do homework.  On behalf of the team, I would also like a give a shout-out to our bus driver Rafael because he was awesome! On the way to Marietta, we stopped at Moe’s and Panera to grab our meal of choice. </p>
<p>It was here that my team first faced one of the Midwest’s hardships. After surviving a mini-earthquake during preseason and a huge power outage caused by Hurricane Irene, I think</p>
<div id="attachment_63" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettabus.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-63" class="size-medium wp-image-63" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettabus-300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettabus-300x225.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettabus.jpg 400w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-63" class="wp-caption-text">Alexis Inselberg and Laurainne Ojo-Ohikuare are in the driver&#039;s seat.</p></div>
<p>my teammates thought that they could survive anything. However, they were totally taken aback by the humidity! I never realized how much more humid it is at home than in Allentown, but there is definitely a huge difference!</p>
<p>We played two matches on Friday and two on Saturday. In total, I had 13 family members attend the tournament – my parents and brother; grandparents from Florida; grandma and aunt from Cleveland; aunt, uncle, and cousins from Akron; and cousins from Pickerington. I also played against a few people who I had played club volleyball with or played against during high school. It was nice to see multiple familiar faces in the crowd, a sight that I used to take for granted. My grandpa even tried started a wave before one of the matches! </p>
<p>I know my family really enjoyed being able to see me play and spend a little time with me, and I thoroughly enjoyed their presence and company. Their support and love is greatly</p>
<div id="attachment_64" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettaseniors.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-64" class="size-medium wp-image-64" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettaseniors-300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettaseniors-300x225.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettaseniors.jpg 400w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-64" class="wp-caption-text">Seniors Brooke McQueen, Davi Gordon and Jackie Price</p></div>
<p>appreciated, and even when I’m at Muhlenberg, eight hours away from home, I know that they will always being cheering for my teammates and me!</p>
<p>Before we started our journey back to Allentown, we stopped at <em>da Vinci’s Italian Restaurant</em>, which was right over the Ohio River in West Virginia. The seniors on the team have travelled to Massachusetts, Maryland, Texas, and throughout Pennsylvania, but this was the first time we stopped in two states on one trip. The food was delicious – chicken parm, pizza, subs and pasta. After filling our stomachs, my team and coaches bid farewell to my family and hopped back on the bus for our long ride home. Even though we didn’t get to go anywhere extremely exotic or to a big city, I think that everyone enjoyed their short experience of Ohio and the Midwest!</p>
<p>Overall, we went 1-3 over the weekend, with our win coming from our first match of the tournament against Oberlin College. Although we suffered a few losses, our team and<a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettahuddle.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-65" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettahuddle-300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettahuddle-300x225.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettahuddle-399x300.jpg 399w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mariettahuddle.jpg 450w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a> coaches still have a very positive outlook for the rest of our season. We were still working out some kinks and trying different lineups, but we have definitely made great strides since our first practices and our preseason scrimmage. Our motto this year is “Kickin’ it up a notch!”, and we are planning on doing that over the next two months. GO MULES!</p>
					</div><!-- .entry-content -->

						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/02/opening-weekend/" title="7:08 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-02T07:08:22-04:00">September 2, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>It’s Friday, Friday, and everybody’s looking forward to the weekend, weekend – and looking forward to another year of Muhlenberg athletics! Seven Mule teams open up this weekend, and here’s a preview:</em></p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-46" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>There’s no better way to start a season than with a game between two NCAA Tournament teams, and there are two of them on tap this weekend.</p>
<p><strong>Football at Delaware Valley (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>Both teams earned their conference’s automatic bid to the NCAA Tournament last year, and both lost to national semifinalists (Muhlenberg to Wesley, Delaware Valley to Mt. Union). Both teams return veteran, stingy defenses, but while the Mules bring back a good chunk of their offense, the Aggies graduated nine offensive starters, including seven all-conference players. Delaware Valley won last year’s game, 27-7.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.sidearmstats.com/delval/football/">http://www.sidearmstats.com/delval/football/</a></p>
<p>Live audio (WMUH): <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/wmuh/stream.html">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/wmuh/stream.html</a></p>
<p><strong>Men’s Soccer vs. DeSales (Saturday, 6:00)</strong></p>
<p>The two Lehigh Valley school have developed a nice rivalry over the years – it doesn’t hurt that both teams are usually very good. DeSales lost to Swarthmore in the first round of the NCAAs last year, and Muhlenberg made it to the “Sweet 16.” The Mules, who also host Cortland State on Friday at 6:00, lead the all-time series 15-2-2 and have won the last four meetings.</p>
<p>Live stats (both games): <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/msoclive/xlive.htm">www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/msoclive/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video (both games): <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><em>In other action this weekend:</em></p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey at Scranton (Friday, 5:00) and Elizabethtown (Saturday, 2:00)</strong></p>
<p>For the first time since 2000, the Mules open a season with back-to-back road games. Scranton was 7-12 last year, and Elizabethtown was 14-5. </p>
<p>Scranton game live stats: <a href="http://athletics.scranton.edu/livestats/landing" target="_blank">http://athletics.scranton.edu/livestats/landing</a></p>
<p>E-town game live stats: <a href="http://www.sidearmstats.com/etown/fhockey/">http://www.sidearmstats.com/etown/fhockey/</a></p>
<p><strong>Women’s Soccer at Kean Classic (vs. Richard Stockton, Saturday, 2:00; vs. Kean, Sunday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules take on a couple of teams from the New Jersey Athletic Conference to start their season. In 2010, Richard Stockton was 13-8-2, and Kean went 9-6-4, including a 3-2 win against the Mules.</p>
<p>Live stats (both games): <a href="http://sidearmstats.com/kean/wsoc/">http://sidearmstats.com/kean/wsoc/</a></p>
<p>Live video (Kean game only):  <a href="http://www.keanathletics.com/general_news/012309live">http://www.keanathletics.com/general_news/012309live</a></p>
<p><strong>Volleyball at Marietta’s River City Classic (vs. Oberlin, Friday, 5:00; vs. Kenyon, Friday, 8:00; vs. Marietta, Saturday, 11:30; vs. Methodist, Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>The volleyball team travels to Ohio for a season-opening tournament.</p>
<p>Tournament updates: <a href="http://pioneers.marietta.edu/sports/2010/8/3/WVB_11RiverCityTournament.aspx?path=wvball">http://pioneers.marietta.edu/sports/2010/8/3/WVB_11RiverCityTournament.aspx?path=wvball</a></p>
<p>Live stats (match vs. Marietta only): <a href="http://www.sidearmstats.com/marietta/wvball/">http://www.sidearmstats.com/marietta/wvball/</a></p>
<p><strong>Cross Country at Lehigh Invitational</strong></p>
<p>All but one of the Mules&#8217; seven meets this year are in the Lehigh Valley, and this is the first.</p>
