			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/muhlenweek-1/" rel="bookmark">Muhlenweek 1!</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/muhlenweek-1/" title="11:57 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 6, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hey all! This post will be pretty long, because so many things have happened this week! I&#8217;ll break it down into four parts for you:</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 16px;">1 &#8211; Classes</span></p>
<p>Last week was the first week of classes. This semester, I&#8217;m taking Ballet 2, Theatre &amp; Society, Psychology 101, Private piano lessons, my First Year Seminar Politics of Memory, and Elementary French 2. I was originally in Macroeconomics instead of Psychology, but after sitting in the class for a day, I knew it wasn&#8217;t for me. Psych has always interested me, so I figured I&#8217;d take the intro class!</p>
<p>Classes are challenging, but I do honestly love them. My professors are all so engaged in their work, and, after some trial and error, I&#8217;ve found a homework schedule that works for me (I spend a substantial amount of time in the library). I&#8217;m about to turn in my first paper tomorrow, and I had my first quiz today!</p>
<p>2 &#8211; Auditions</p>
<p>Wednesday night, I auditioned for many of the Muhlenberg Theatre Association main stages. I was so nervous! I had separate auditions for acting, singing, and dance. The atmosphere was tense but excited, and everyone auditioning was very supportive of each other. We had had a meeting earlier that week to go over the general rules of the MTA, and from the board members to the other scared freshman, it was plain to see that it&#8217;s a very big, loving family.</p>
<p>I was fortunate enough to receive 3 callbacks based on my auditions. So more dancing, singing, and acting all over again! Here&#8217;s how freaked out I was:</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5361.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2435" alt="IMG_5361" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5361-225x300.jpg" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5361-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5361-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Okay, so I wasn&#8217;t really that scared. Mostly, it was a huge honor. Callbacks were really fun, although they were also sort of stressful.</p>
<p>Monday night, I found out that I have been cast in the ensemble of Sinternet! Sinternet is a student-written musical that first premiered during the Red Door Play festival last year. This year, it is being produced in the main stage season the first weekend of October (AHHH!). It is part of the New Voices play festival, and is a comedy with brilliant music. I am absolutely thrilled and humbled to have been cast, and can&#8217;t wait to update you all on the process!</p>
<p>3 &#8211; Allentown Fair</p>
<p>Friday night, after my first full week of classes, some friends and I took a trip to the Allentown fair! It was my first time off campus as a college student, and it was really fun. We saw and petted some animals, listened to the Luke Bryan concert from afar, and had a blast! It was the perfect way to relax after a big week. It was nice to see what the Allentown community was like, and I&#8217;ve always loved fairs. I even tried fried oreos! (Which I recommend, although you really can&#8217;t have more than one)</p>

			<div id="post-2425" class="post-2425 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/i-dont-know-about-you-but-im-feeling-twenty-one/" rel="bookmark">I don&#8217;t know about you, but I&#8217;m feeling twenty&#8230;one?</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/i-dont-know-about-you-but-im-feeling-twenty-one/" title="9:26 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 4, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Yes, I did just make a T.Swift reference. So sue me.</p>
<p>For the lazy people out there, here&#8217;s the abbreviated version of this post:</p>
<p>-P.Chem ahhh!</p>
<p>-I sing and stuff</p>
<p>-Happy birthday to me</p>
<p>Anyway, the first week of classes has come to a close, and the second is digging its heels in and rarin&#8217; to go. All of my classes seem like they&#8217;ll be pretty cool this semester, especially my modern dance class. I&#8217;ve never taken a dance class before and it feels super weird, but simultaneously really rewarding to actually be taught dance instead of making it up on the fly in any number of musicals I&#8217;ve been a part of.</p>
<p>Physical Chemistry, on the other hand, is a little less cool. Don&#8217;t get me wrong, my professor is brilliant, but calculus aren&#8217;t exactly&#8230;tangent.</p>
<div style="width: 810px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img alt="" src="http://blameitontheanalyst.files.wordpress.com/2010/11/800px-tangent-calculus-svg.png" width="800" height="570" /><p class="wp-caption-text">An actual photograph of me and not calculus.</p></div>
<p>Friday night brought with it a chance for all of Muhlenberg&#8217;s vocal groups to showcase our talents. Berg&#8217;s Got Talent is an annual event where freshmen or anyone interested in auditioning for or joining a vocal performance group can see us all perform and get a better idea of what we&#8217;re all about.</p>
<p>Live in Color, Muhlenberg&#8217;s only show choir, as well as the Gospel Choir performed, both of which I am a part of. It was a blast, and a great way to start off the semester with both of my performance groups. Following the showcase, on Saturday afternoon, all of Muhlenberg&#8217;s clubs and activities came together for the bi-annual activities fair. This is normally a pretty exciting, energetic day held outside in the Parent&#8217;s Plaza. After being moved inside due to the threat of rain, all that energy was only intensified. It was hectic, but certainly rewarding. I am looking forward to auditions for Live in Color, and, since we don&#8217;t turn anyone who wants to join the gospel family away, meeting the newest Rejoicers.</p>
<p>Lastly, but most certainly not leastly, I turned 21 this week! I had a great birthday, although it didn&#8217;t consist of what one might think a 21st birthday would. After all, I had class all day and a rehearsal to be sober for at 11pm! Work hard, play&#8230;only a little, and responsibly at that. I did go out with a friend to a local bar though, which was nice. We got to catch up and kind of touch base before the rush of the school year really picks up. Over the weekend I spent a lot of time with friends just celebrating and enjoying our temporarily light workload, and I feel ready to face this next week and whatever it may bring!</p>
<div style="width: 413px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img alt="" src="https://sphotos-b-iad.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-prn1/s403x403/1208750_10201980054841801_1098725289_n.jpg" width="403" height="302" /><p class="wp-caption-text">We&#8217;re a stunning crew. I know.</p></div>

				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/do-something-different/" title="4:44 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 1, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>There is one saying that I have heard a lot at Muhlenberg and it is to try on different hats. You may be thinking that you don&#8217;t like to wear hats, but don&#8217;t worry that is not what I am talking about. I mean that you need to go out and do something different. Being that Muhlenberg is a small school it offers benefits that bigger schools do not. The key is to take advantage of all that our school has to offer.</p>
<p>Over the course of this year go out and do something different. I&#8217;m not saying that you need to go crazy and join 6 different clubs and run for class president. Start with something small, like joining one club. There are so many different options out there and you will never know what you may like until you try.</p>
