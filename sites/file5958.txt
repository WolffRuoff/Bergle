						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/21/9-or-19/" rel="bookmark">9 or 19?</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/21/9-or-19/" title="11:56 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-21T11:56:12-04:00">September 21, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>Two Division III men&#8217;s soccer polls came out yesterday. In the one released by the National Soccer Coaches Association of America, Muhlenberg was ranked No. 19, and in the one released by D3soccer.com, the Mules were No. 9.</p>
<p>How could there be such a difference? One reason is that it&#8217;s still early in the season, and <a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/msochuddle.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-189" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/msochuddle-300x251.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="251" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/msochuddle-300x251.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/msochuddle-358x300.jpg 358w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/msochuddle.jpg 375w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>it takes a little while for the teams to sort themselves out. Another is that the two polls are conducted in very different manners, both with their quirks.</p>
<p>The NSCAA poll sticks very closely to the regions. This makes sense because Division III competition is regional in nature, and there are few inter-regional games during the season from which to form a basis of comparison. Teams are ranked 1-10 in each of the eight regions, and in general the top teams in each region are 1-8 in the poll, the second teams are 9-16, etc.</p>
<p>There are some exceptions: The No. 2 in the Mid-Atlantic on the women&#8217;s side, Johns Hopkins, is ranked No. 7 nationally, ahead of two regional No. 1s. By the way, the Mule women&#8217;s soccer team plays at Johns Hopkins on Saturday night.</p>
<p>The down side of this system is pretty obvious &#8211; teams jump around wildly in the national poll based on their regional ranking. Four teams that were ranked below the Mules last week are ahead of them this week, not because voters suddenly think they&#8217;re better, but because they moved up to No. 2 in their regions. As long as it is No. 3 in the Mid-Atlantic, Muhlenberg won&#8217;t be able to move up much past No. 15 or so.</p>
<p>The D3soccer.com poll works more like a national poll. And with national polls, it&#8217;s sometimes hard to break in, but then once you&#8217;re in it can take a while to fall out.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg was ranked 15th in the preseason poll, which was based in large part on last year&#8217;s final poll. Since the preseason poll, teams ahead of Muhlenberg have suffered losses that caused them to drop, while the Mules have inched up from 15 to 12 to 9 with their five straight wins to start the season.</p>
<p>This poll is not without its oddities. When a team loses, voters are often inclined to move it down only so many spots. Here&#8217;s an example from the women&#8217;s poll: Last week, Rochester was ranked 13th and Union received votes. Union beat Rochester, 2-1, but this week Rochester (5-1, No. 16) is still ranked ahead of Union (6-0, No. 17). The NSCAA poll has it more in the way you&#8217;d expect based on that result: Union is No. 2 in the region and No. 11 nationally, while Rochester is No. 3 and No. 18.</p>
<p>So are the Mules really No. 9 or No. 19, or none of the above? Only time will tell. Two things are for sure, though: the polls are fun to watch, and it&#8217;s better to be in them than out of them!</p>

		<div class="entry-content">
			<p>Talk about a daunting task. With just four college games under their belts, the freshmen on the Muhlenberg women’s soccer team were thrown into the fire with a game against the No. 1 team in the country, Messiah.</p>
<div id="attachment_154" style="width: 110px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/touheyhs5.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-154" class="size-full wp-image-154" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/touheyhs5.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-154" class="wp-caption-text">Touhey</p></div>
<p>And while last night’s result may not have been what the Mules had hoped for, it was an eye-opening experience for freshman Colleen Touhey, who has started all five games of her college career.</p>
<p>“It was such a faster pace,” she said. “Everyone is at a higher level; there’s no break for you. Every mistake you make is capitalized on, so you have to be sure with the ball at all times.”</p>
<p>Touhey’s classmate, Audrey Zamchiow, agreed that the tempo was a major step, or two or three, up from her high school games. But she also felt like the upperclassmen helped get the team ready as best as they could.</p>
<p>“It was a lot more intense, but the older girls really helped me adjust and prepare for what this game would be like,” Zamichow said. “I could still definitely tell the big difference compared to playing in high school, but I think it would have been a lot more shocking if they hadn’t helped us.”</p>
<div id="attachment_155" style="width: 110px" class="wp-caption alignright"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/zamichowhs3.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-155" class="size-full wp-image-155" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/zamichowhs3.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-155" class="wp-caption-text">Zamichow</p></div>
<p>The main thing both Touhey and Zamichow took away from playing the three-time national champion Falcons was a game plan for what the Mules need to do to be successful.</p>
<p>Touhey said the team has been working on playing faster, but playing against the No. 1 team in Division III showed her just how important it is to avoid and limit mistakes.</p>
<p>She was still very optimistic in spite of the loss, and said she feels the team can only grow better after facing such quality competition.</p>
<p>“Playing a game like this, although we lost, is good because we see what we need to do if we want to become a really good team,” she said. “There are so many things we can learn from this game, and now we have all season to make it happen.”</p>

		<header class="entry-header">
						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/14/ncaa-stats/" rel="bookmark">NCAA stats</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/14/ncaa-stats/" title="2:01 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-14T14:01:37-04:00">September 14, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>There’s a new way to keep close tabs on not just the Muhlenberg teams, but all Division III teams in football, soccer and field hockey.</p>
<p>Starting this week, box scores for all Division III contests in those sports will be available on the NCAA website. The NCAA has been doing this with Division I football for several years and started with Division III last year. Soccer and field hockey were added this year, with volleyball to come in the future.</p>
<p>Here are the links:</p>
<p>Football: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/mfb/mainpage.jsp?year=2011">http://web1.ncaa.org/mfb/mainpage.jsp?year=2011</a></p>
<p>Men’s soccer: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=MSO">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=MSO</a></p>
<p>Women’s soccer: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WSO">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WSO</a></p>
<p>Field hockey: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WFH">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WFH</a></p>
<p>The football page is set up a little differently, but for the other three, click on “Click here for box scores and complete team and individual statistics.” Although it is listed on the Division I line, you can get to all three divisions from there. Select III from the drop-down menu where I appears, and you get a list of all schools that play the sport. Click on any school, and you can see their links to cumulative stats and game boxes. Click on a player name in the roster to see season and career stats.</p>
<p>If you want to check up on upcoming opponents, this is an alternative to going to their school websites. And if nothing else, it&#8217;s a great way to kill some time poring over stats.</p>
<p>The NCAA continues to provide weekly rankings in these sports, as well as volleyball (<a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WVB">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WVB</a>). Although it is way too early to put any stock in national statistical rankings, it is interesting to note that the Muhlenberg football team is 28<sup>th</sup> in Division III in rushing defense after two games, and Joe Rollins of McDaniel, who will be visiting Scotty Wood Stadium on Saturday, is the second-leading rusher in the division.</p>

<p>Live video: <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><strong>Women&#8217;s Soccer at Gwynedd-Mercy (Friday, 7:00); vs. Delaware Valley (Saturday, 7:00</strong>)</p>
<p>The 1-1 Mules play back-to-back for the second weekend in a row. Saturday&#8217;s game is the home opener for Muhlenberg.</p>
<p>Live video (Gwynedd-Mercy game): <a href="http://www.gmcgriffins.com/sports/2011/9/1/GEN_0901110219.aspx">http://www.gmcgriffins.com/sports/2011/9/1/GEN_0901110219.aspx</a></p>
<p>Live video (Delaware Valley game): <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>

<p><strong>Field Hockey at Montclair State (Saturday, 6:00)</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-family: mceinline">After their Moravian game was rained out twice, the Mules head to North Jersey in search of their first win of the season.</span></p>
