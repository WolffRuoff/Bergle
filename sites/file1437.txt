	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=950" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2019-10-17T17:29:06-04:00">October 17, 2019</time><time class="updated" datetime="2019-10-17T17:30:07-04:00">October 17, 2019</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=61">Phedra Henninger</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=950" rel="bookmark">IT Protects! About campus phishing simulations</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=1" rel="category">cybersecurity</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=950#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		
<p>Phishing is not going away and cybersecurity awareness is key in helping our community recognize the tell-tale signs of a phish. In order to hone your sleuthing skills, we will be kicking&nbsp;campus phishing simulations&nbsp;into high gear with the start of monthly campaigns for faculty and staff using the College&#8217;s cybersecurity training platform KnowBe4.</p>



<p><strong>What happens if I click on the link?</strong></p>



<p>The purpose of the campaigns are to increase cybersecurity awareness. Information about individual responses will not be shared with supervisors, Human Resources, or the community. The goal of this simulation is to help you learn how to spot a phish! The more you are exposed to phishing emails that better you will be at detecting them. This is why we are increasing the frequency of our campaigns to be monthly. </p>



<p>Those who click on the link in a phishing simulation will be redirected to an informative web page (see example below) that identifies the red flags in the email. They will also be enrolled in a 10 minute training module. This training is called &#8220;Phishing Andrew&#8217;s Inbox&#8221;. It provides information on how to better identify phishing emails including the &#8220;Top Ten Characteristics of Phishing Emails&#8221; for users who aren&#8217;t confident in their knowledge on the subject. </p>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/phish-landing-1024x818.png" alt="" class="wp-image-952" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/phish-landing-1024x818.png 1024w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/phish-landing-300x240.png 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/phish-landing-768x614.png 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/phish-landing.png 1031w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></figure>



<p><strong>Remember the&nbsp;tell-tale signs of a phishing email:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Does the email contain a vague salutation, spelling or grammatical errors, an urgent request, and/or an offer that seems impossibly good?&nbsp;<strong><a href="https://support.google.com/mail/answer/8253?hl=en">Report the phish via Gmail’s built-in feature,&nbsp;</a>then click the delete button.</strong></li><li>Check the sender’s e-mail address&nbsp;to make sure it’s legitimate. Does it appears that the Support Desk is asking you to click on a link but it comes from “support@yahoo.com?” What if it’s “support@muhlenberg.edu” but it says it’s “via” another address? If it lists anything anywhere that says something other than “muhlenberg.edu?”&nbsp;<strong>Report it, then delete it</strong>.</li><li>OIT will never ask you for your password through email. Any request for one should be treated as suspicious and reported as phishing. Your password is the key to your identity, your data, and your classmates’ and colleagues’ data. Read&nbsp;<a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/Requiringauserpassword.pdf">Password Requirements for OIT Support</a>&nbsp;to learn more.</li><li>Avoid opening links and attachments from unknown senders. Get into the habit of typing known URLs into your browser. Don’t open attachments unless you’re expecting a file from someone. Give them a call if you’re suspicious.</li><li>Take extra care with mobile devices, as some of these signs are not as easily seen. </li></ul>

<p>In honor of National Cybersecurity Awareness Month, the Office of Information Technology is excited to share information on recent and upcoming security initiatives. </p>



<h3>Campus Phishing Campaigns </h3>



<p>Phishing is not going away and cybersecurity awareness is key in helping our community recognize the tell-tale signs of a phish.  In order to hone your sleuthing skills, we will be kicking <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=739">campus phishing simulations</a> into high gear with the start of monthly campaigns for faculty and staff.  <br><br>Note: People tend to be more susceptible to a phish when accessing email on a mobile device, so use extra caution when reading email on your phone. </p>



<h3>Cybersecurity training for newly hired colleagues</h3>



<p>In August 2019, we launched cybersecurity training for all newly hired colleagues. The training provides important information and tactics on how to protect yourself and the College from current threats.  This critical training will be ongoing for all new hires as part of the onboarding process. </p>



<h3>A CONTEST ! Who doesn&#8217;t love a contest?!</h3>



<p>LVAIC is running a <strong>Cyber Security Awareness Month Video and Poster Contest</strong>. Submissions are being accepted now until October 25. Open to students, faculty and staff of all&nbsp;LVAIC&nbsp;schools! Visit&nbsp;<a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="http://lvaic.org/csam" target="_blank">lvaic.org/csam</a>&nbsp;for details.&nbsp;&nbsp; </p>



<h3>Want to learn more about cybersecurity? </h3>

Support Desk emails for more tips and tricks on how to be cyber secure </li><li>Click on the KnowBe4 app in the application portal for more cybersecurity resources </li><li>Visit <a href="https://staysafeonline.org">staysafeonline.org </a>(National Cyber Security Alliance) for information on the 2019 theme &#8221; <strong>Own IT. <div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172350" rel="category">Client Support Services</a></li>

	<div class="entry-content">
		
<p>As you may know, Microsoft is ending support for Windows 7 in January 2020. We will no longer receive security updates for the operating system, which is a pretty big deal. We have been energetically replacing these machines with Windows 10 devices across campus. Each new computer deployed for the past 1.5 years has been a Windows 10 computer, and we have done a number of upgrades in-place as well. </p>



<p>Unfortunately, we simply are not moving fast enough. At the current rate, we will not be complete until the end of 2020. </p>



<p>In order to meet our very important deadline, we will be changing our policy on the Windows 10 roll-out. Whereas we have been waiting for individuals to be available before making the upgrade thus far, we are compelled to take a more assertive approach. </p>



<p>Today, we reach out to users for upgrades, then wait for a response. We have found this response to be surprisingly long in duration. We are averaging only 4-5 computers a week. Therefore, we will be waiting only 7  business days before <strong><em>your password will be reset to complete the upgrade</em></strong>. You will need to contact the Support Desk at 484-664-3566 in order to receive your new password. We estimate we&#8217;ll be able to approach 20 computers a week with this new method. Downtime for users should therefore be minimal with that kind of pace.</p>



<p>Ready for the great news? Your computer&#8217;s performance will greatly improve with the upgrade! Get ready for a reboot that happens in seconds, not minutes (upon minutes)!</p>



<p>We apologize for this rather severe change in policy, but it is critically important that we not run unsupported computers on campus.</p>

	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=907" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2019-07-01T16:47:44-04:00">July 1, 2019</time><time class="updated" datetime="2019-07-01T16:48:44-04:00">July 1, 2019</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=907" rel="bookmark">Changes to Two-Factor Authentication On-Campus</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=1" rel="category">cybersecurity</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=907#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		
<p>OneLogin, the provider for our Application Portal, is changing how it handles Two-Factor Authentication, or 2FA. As you know, we have had 2FA enabled for off-campus locations for some time. It went live for faculty last June, and for students in October. </p>



<p>As an enhanced security measure, OneLogin is eliminating the location-based exemption we&#8217;ve applied. This means we can no longer have an exception for on-campus 2FA. The requirement will be in place regardless of your location. Once you authenticate, you will not be asked for the 2nd factor again for 24 hours (while using the same browser).</p>



<p><em><strong>This is an important change</strong></em>. The use case we see most affected is in the classroom. Now, when faculty or students need to get to Canvas, they must have their phone on them, and they must 2FA into the system. </p>



<p>Under the previous arrangement, there was a very specific possibility of someone gaining unauthorized access to an account. If someone on-campus had another person&#8217;s username and password (credentials), not only could they get into the account, but could even set up a second factor on their device. This would mean the bad actor would have completely taken over the account. </p>



<p>We in OIT concluded this was highly unlikely, and that it was reasonable to trust the Muhlenberg community. Our security consultants, Greycastle Security, agreed with this assessment. Regardless, however, OneLogin is removing the option so we have to make a change. </p>



<p>We do not have a timeline for this as of yet. But, again, 2FA will be enforced from all locations, for all users, at some point in the near future. </p>

<p><strong>In this episode we talk stereotypes, power, information literacy, outreach, even MS Paint.</strong></p>



<p style="text-align:center"><strong>Get to know Jess- </strong>Jess is the librarian liaison to academic departments in the social sciences for purposes of reference, one-on-one and small group research help, citation assistance, library and information literacy instruction, and collection development.&nbsp;</p>

We hope these conversations (on four wheels) will inspire you to explore new tools, or use current tool in new ways.
