			<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/weekendpreview_small.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1812" alt="weekendpreview_small" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/weekendpreview_small.jpg" width="264" height="150" /></a>The first weekend of October includes a Hall of Fame induction ceremony on Saturday in addition to a full slate of home events.</em></p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey vs. Washington College (Friday, 7:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg&#8217;s Homecoming was two weeks ago, but this is the field hockey team&#8217;s homecoming. The Mules (3-6, 2-2) haven&#8217;t been at home in three weeks, playing five consecutive road games.</p>
<p>Their return to Scotty Wood Stadium is for a key game. Washington (3-5, 2-1) has been to the Centennial Conference playoffs each of the last three years. The Shorewomen have won the last three games against Muhlenberg to take a 12-9 series lead.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.muhlenbeg.edu/sports/fhlive/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenbeg.edu/sports/fhlive/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><strong>Volleyball vs. Washington College (Saturday, 4:00); vs. Hood (Saturday, 4:00)</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/vb_dig4acure.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1943" alt="Dig for a Cure" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/vb_dig4acure.jpg" width="600" height="214" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/vb_dig4acure.jpg 600w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/vb_dig4acure-300x107.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/vb_dig4acure-500x178.jpg 500w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a>Muhlenberg will be hosting a tri-match while Digging for a Cure: All of the Centennial Conference volleyball teams are joining forces again this year to raise money for Tanner&#8217;s Touch, a non-profit organization that raises money for pediatric cancer research. Teams are collecting pledges for each dig recorded in a preselected match.</p>
<p>The Mules&#8217; designated match is against Washington, and it&#8217;s an important one between two teams looking to reach the upper echelon of the CC. The upstart Shorewomen (12-5, 2-1) have already swept two 2012 CC playoff teams (Gettysburg and Swarthmore). With a win, Muhlenberg (9-5, 3-1) will improve to 4-1 at the midpoint of the CC season for the first time since 2001.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg leads the all-time series against Washington, 18-2. Hood, the Mules&#8217; second opponent, brings a record of 4-10.</p>

<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/croninhs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-207" alt="Nicki Cronin" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/croninhs.jpg" width="100" height="150" /></a><strong>Female Athlete of the Week &#8211; Nicki Cronin, Cross Country</strong></p>

<p><strong>Cross Country at Cedar Crest/Muhlenberg Invitational (Saturday, 10:00)<br />
</strong></p>
<p>The Mules compete in the closest thing they have to a home meet, crossing Ott Street to race for the first time in three weeks. Last year, the Muhlenberg teams swept this meet for the first time since 2008. Both Mule squads are ranked in the top 10 in the Mideast Region.</p>

<p>The two-time defending Centennial Conference champion Blue Jays kick off (or is it serve off?) the on-campus Homecoming activities. <p>McDaniel nearly spoiled Homecoming two years ago, opening up a 26-7 lead before the Mules rallied with 27 fourth-quarter points. The Green Terror beat Moravian last weekend to snap a 13-game losing streak, while Muhlenberg is coming off a 28-21 loss at Franklin &amp; Marshall. McDaniel leads the all-time series, 22-18-3, but the Mules have won the last three meetings, rolling up a 413-168 margin in total yards in last year&#8217;s 33-0 shutout.</p>
<p>Green Terror senior running back Joe Rollins will be a player to watch, and one to tackle for the Muhlenberg defense. He rushed for 219 yards last week and is the ninth all-time leading rusher in Centennial Conference history.</p>

<p><strong>Women&#8217;s Soccer vs. Johns Hopkins (Saturday, 3:00)</strong></p>
<p>Johns Hopkins (8-0) soared to the top spot of the D3soccer.com Division III rankings after scoring road wins against then-No. 1 Messiah and then-No. 5 Carnegie Mellon. The Blue Jays are 37-4 in the Centennial Conference regular season over the last four-plus years, but three of those losses were to Muhlenberg, each time with Hopkins ranked in the top 10. The Mules (3-2-1) will look to make history repeat &#8211; again. They come in with a four-match unbeaten streak and an unscored-upon streak of 380 minutes.</p>

<p><strong>Men&#8217;s Soccer vs. Gettysburg (Saturday, 7:00)</strong></p>
<p>The last home event of Homecoming matches two teams that dominated the early days of the Centennial Conference, combining to win five of the first seven titles, but both failed to make the playoffs last year. Muhlenberg (4-2) is ranked eighth in the Mid-Atlantic Region, while Gettysburg (0-5) has lost eight straight dating back to last season. Included in the Bullets&#8217; setbacks is a 3-1 loss to Neumann, who the Mules beat. Muhlenberg holds a 35-18-6 series lead and is 8-0-1 against Gettysburg since the 2005 regular season.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/msoclive/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/msoclive/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey at Johns Hopkins (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules (2-3) travel to Baltimore seeking to improve to 2-0 in the Centennial Conference for the first time in four years. Hopkins is 3-2 and opened CC play with a 4-2 win at Washington. The Blue Jays lead the series, 17-5, and have won the last three meetings.</p>

<p>Muhlenberg begins its competitive season at the annual regional tournament at Mary Washington.</p>
<p>Tournament site: <a href="http://tennislink.usta.com/tournaments/TournamentHome/Tournament.aspx?T=136819">http://tennislink.usta.com/tournaments/TournamentHome/Tournament.aspx?T=136819</a></p>
<p><strong>Women&#8217;s Golf at McDaniel Invitational (Saturday)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg&#8217;s second tournament of the year will feature mostly Centennial Conference opponents.</p>

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>The last weekend of October means falling leaves, Halloween, nasty weather in the forecast &#8211; and the fall sports seasons winding down. Two Muhlenberg teams will end their seasons tomorrow, while two others hope to continue theirs.</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-104" title="Mule Marquee Matchup" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>Women&#8217;s Soccer at Ursinus (7:30)</strong></p>
<p>This is the only game in which a Muhlenberg team can chart its own playoff course. With a win, the Mules are in &#8211; although, since the only team that can catch them (Dickinson) plays at noon, by the time this game kicks off they might be in already!</p>
<p>Muhlenberg (7-8-2, 5-3-1) is in fifth place in the Centennial Conference. Dickinson (11-5, 5-4) needs a win against Franklin &amp; Marshall and a Mule loss or tie to snatch away the final playoff spot. If the Mules win, they will face the loser of the Haverford-Swarthmore game in the first round (or Swarthmore if that game ends in a tie).</p>
<p>Although Ursinus (3-8-4, 0-5-4) is winless in CC play, it did take Haverford and Dickinson to double overtime.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg leads the all-time series, 13-6, but the Bears have won the last two meetings &#8211; including a 2010 game in Collegeville in which the Mules needed a win to stay alive in the playoff race.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://livestats.prestosports.com/ursinus/">http://livestats.prestosports.com/ursinus/</a></p>
<p><strong>Cross Country at Haverford for Centennial Conference Championships (men, 11:00; women, 12:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules compete at the 20th CC Championships. For a preview, <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/athletics/fall12/1026.html">click here</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey at Ursinus (12:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg (9-6, 4-5) closes out a season of great improvement with a chance to beat eight-time defending champion Ursinus (9-7, 5-4) for the first time ever. The Bears need a win and help to make the playoffs.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://livestats.prestosports.com/ursinus/">http://livestats.prestosports.com/ursinus/</a></p>
<p><strong>Volleyball at Ursinus (12:00)</strong></p>
<p>It&#8217;s the last match of the season for the Mules (16-11, 3-6), who have a chance to reach 17 wins for the first time since 2008. They won a total of 16 matches the last two years. Ursinus (7-16, 2-7) has lost to Muhlenberg eight straight times.</p>
<p><strong>Football at Dickinson (1:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg (5-2, 4-2) plays a road game for the first time in almost a month against a Red Devil team coming off a comeback overtime win against McDaniel. It&#8217;s been feast or famine for Dickinson (3-4, 3-3): It lost big to to nationally ranked Hobart (31-0) and Johns Hopkins (49-0), but its other five games were all decided by eight points or fewer.</p>
<p>The Mules lead the all-time series, 40-25-2, and have won four of the last five meetings, including last season&#8217;s 12-0 &#8220;Snowtober&#8221; game.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www2.dickinson.edu/sports/statistics/mfb/livestats/XLIVE.HTM">http://www2.dickinson.edu/sports/statistics/mfb/livestats/XLIVE.HTM</a></p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://www.ustream.tv/channel/reddevilsportsnetwork">http://www.ustream.tv/channel/reddevilsportsnetwork</a></p>
<p><strong>Men&#8217;s Soccer at Ursinus, 5:00</strong></p>
<p>The Mules (10-5, 5-4) enter the final day of the regular season needing a win or tie and a Johns Hopkins loss OR a win and a JHU draw to make the playoff field. The Blue Jays play at Washington at 7:00.</p>
<p>Ursinus (5-11, 1-7) has lost seven straight CC contests since opening with a win against Gettysburg and has allowed a league-worst 24 goals in conference play.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg has an all-time record of 18-0-1 against Ursinus in CC play. The tie, however, came in Collegeville in 2006 when the Mules needed a win to secure a home playoff game.</p>

						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/10/26/xc-qa/" title="9:37 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2012-10-26T09:37:30-04:00">October 26, 2012</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>Men&#8217;s cross country co-captains Jarrett Felix and Charlie Kline wrote about their recent encounter with two of the best to ever compete in the sport at Muhlenberg.</em></p>
<p>This past weekend the Muhlenberg cross country teams had a Q and A session with former Muhlenberg track and cross country standouts and Hall of Fame couple Will Elson and Karen Mount Elson. Karen had just been inducted that day into the Muhlenberg Hall of Fame, and Will was there to support her. It was special for them to take time out of their busy day to talk to the teams.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/elson-mount.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1441" title="Will Elson and Karen Mount " src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/elson-mount.jpg" alt="" width="709" height="399" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/elson-mount.jpg 709w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/elson-mount-300x168.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/elson-mount-500x281.jpg 500w" sizes="(max-width: 709px) 100vw, 709px" /></a></p>
<p>One of the quotes that Will told us was “When you’re behind, no one can see you coming.” This idea embodies the men’s cross country team’s spirit at this point in the season. Last season ended in disappointment, but behind the leadership of new coach Doug Finley, we feel that there is potential to surprise a lot of people this championship season.</p>
<p>Both Will and Karen gave us a number of tips about both preparation for a race and race strategy. Karen explained that earlier in her collegiate career, she was stressed out before big meets and was unable to overcome the pressure of the moment. By her junior year, she realized the importance of making sure to relax and laugh before the start of races to avoid getting worked up.</p>
<p>Will stressed the importance of following a routine and individualizing prerace preparation. Both ideas have been taken to heart by the men’s team; we hold a variety of prerace quirks to keep us relaxed, everything from polo shirts to pink socks.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/menxchuddle.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-1442" title="men's cross country huddle" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/menxchuddle.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="370" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/menxchuddle.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/menxchuddle-283x300.jpg 283w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>Hearing from such successful alumni has inspired our squad as we approach the two most important meets of our season. The team is looking to improve off of a sixth-place finish at Conferences on Saturday at Haverford, and aiming to dramatically upgrade our 18th-place finish at the NCAA Regional Championship in Dickinson on November 10.</p>
<p>We may be underdogs going into the postseason, but when you’re behind, no one can see you coming.</p>

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>The Muhlenberg cross country teams usually do the running while others take care of race administration, but recently the Mules were on the other side for a special cause. Junior Jackie Shulack reports:</em></p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xc_autism1.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-1422" title="cross country at Mile for Autism" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xc_autism1.jpg" alt="" width="450" height="348" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xc_autism1.jpg 450w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xc_autism1-300x232.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xc_autism1-387x300.jpg 387w" sizes="(max-width: 450px) 100vw, 450px" /></a>This past weekend the Muhlenberg cross country teams volunteered at the Miles for Autism race in Saucon Valley, which benefited The Autism Society of Lehigh Valley. As a team, it was nice to be able to give back to the community, because so often we are caught up in our own busy schedules of training, racing and managing schoolwork.</p>
<p>We helped take extra clothes from racers at the starting line and bring them to the finish line, directed runners on the course and told runners their splits during the race.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xc_autism2.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1423" title="cross country at Mile for Autism" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xc_autism2.jpg" alt="" width="375" height="288" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xc_autism2.jpg 375w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xc_autism2-300x230.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 375px) 100vw, 375px" /></a>Volunteering at the one-mile race allowed us to cheer on others who were doing what we may take for granted sometimes. One mile seems so short to us, but to the people who raced it, it may have been a huge accomplishment and I think that deserves a lot of respect.</p>
<p>One of the quotes from the race was “Before you judge a man, walk a MILE in his shoes.” This really puts things into perspective for me, and as a team I felt we were really able to support people for having the courage to start the race and avoid getting caught up thinking about their finishing times.</p>
<p>To have my team members get up on a rainy Sunday morning with smiles on their faces displayed a great deal of character, and I cannot be more proud to be a member of this team.</p>
<p><em>The volunteer work of the Mules did not go unappreciated. A few days after the mile run, the race directors sent the following e-mail to the coaching staff:</em></p>
<p>I would like to thank your team for their help in making Sunday&#8217;s event a success. They were instrumental in keeping the course managed and organized during the three one-mile races. I would also like to note that they did it in a professional manner, which was recognized by our participants.</p>
<p><em>Volunteering at this event was another highlight of what has been a memorable season for the Mules, particularly the women&#8217;s team, which is ranked seventh in the Mideast Region. Shulack wrote about that experience as well:</em></p>
<p>As a new member of this team, I could not ask for a better group of ladies to train with. The team is hardworking, focused, dedicated, and we still manage to have fun doing what we love. The team immediately made me feel welcomed and I learned quickly that they would help me through anything. It’s a nice feeling to know at the end of the day your teammates always have your back.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xcwomensteam.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1421" title="women's cross country team" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xcwomensteam.jpg" alt="" width="498" height="160" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xcwomensteam.jpg 498w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/xcwomensteam-300x96.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 498px) 100vw, 498px" /></a></p>
<p>The most important part of the season is coming up with the conference and regional meet ahead, and after all the hard work we have put in, I know we can achieve our goals. I think the best is yet to come.</p>

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>With the College on Fall Break, most of the Muhlenberg student body will be scattered around the Mid-Atlantic region and New England this weekend. So will Mule athletes.</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-104" title="Mule Marquee Matchup" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1-300x203.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="203" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1-300x203.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg 350w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>Men&#8217;s Soccer at Franklin &amp; Marshall (Saturday, 3:00)</strong></p>
<p>This is a critical matchup for two teams who were ranked 1-2 in the first regional poll of the season but now find themselves fifth and sixth in the Centennial Conference standings. F&amp;M (9-4, 2-3) is still tied for fifth in the regional rankings, while Muhlenberg (8-3, 3-2) is coming off a win against regionally ranked Drew.</p>
<p>Both teams lost 2-1 to Swarthmore and 1-0 to Haverford. The Diplomats also fell to Johns Hopkins, a team the Mules beat.</p>
<p>Expect a close game: Of F&amp;M&#8217;s 13 games, 10 were decided by one goal, and Muhlenberg has played five straight one-goal games, winning the last three.</p>
<p>F&amp;M leads the all-time series, 29-27-5, but the Mules are 4-0-1 in the last five meetings, including a 4-0 win in the 2010 CC semifinals.</p>
<p>Live stats/video: <a href="http://client.stretchinternet.com/client/fandm.portal">http://client.stretchinternet.com/client/fandm.portal</a>#</p>
<p><strong>Football vs. Gettysburg (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg returns from its bye week looking to snap a two-game losing streak. It won&#8217;t be easy against a potent Gettysburg squad that is 5-0 for the first time since 1987 and is outscoring opponents by an average of 35 points per game.</p>
<p>The Bullets are ranked in the top 15 in Division III in five statistical categories. They are third in rushing offense (326.8 ypg), fourth in scoring defense (8.2 ppg), 10th in scoring offense (43.2 ppg), 13th in total offense (489.2 ypg) and 14th in total defense (247.6 ypg). The Mules (3-2) are 11th in scoring defense (11.4 ppg) and net punting (37.1 yards per punt).</p>
<p>Gettysburg leads the all-time series, 43-32-1, but has not won in Allentown since 1996. The last time the teams played at Scotty Wood Stadium, Muhlenberg needed a last-second saving tackle on a fumble return to win in overtime, 33-27. </p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/livestats/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/livestats/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey at Franklin &amp; Marshall (Saturday, 12:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg (8-4, 3-3) is one of eight teams with two or three losses in the congested Centennial Conference standings. All of them are chasing Franklin &amp; Marshall (11-1, 6-0), which is ranked fifth in Division III and has won 11 straight since dropping its opener to Elizabethtown.</p>
<p>The Diplomats come in with a streak of six straight shutouts and have allowed only seven goals all season. The only other CC team to allow fewer than 20 is Muhlenberg (15).</p>
<p>F&amp;M leads the all-time series, 13-6-1, and has won the last three. After 4-0 games in 2009 and 2010, last year&#8217;s meeting was closer, with the Diplomats leading 2-1 at halftime and going on to win 3-1.</p>
<p>Live stats/video: <a href="http://client.stretchinternet.com/client/fandm.portal">http://client.stretchinternet.com/client/fandm.portal</a>#</p>
<p><strong>Women&#8217;s Soccer at Franklin &amp; Marshall (Saturday, 6:00)</strong></p>
<p>One aspect of this matchup would seem to favor the visiting team: Muhlenberg (6-6-1, 4-2) has shut out its last four CC opponents, while F&amp;M (2-4-4, 1-3-2) is the most offensively challenged team in the CC, with only six goals scored in 10 games.</p>
<p>The Mules enter the weekend in a four-way tie for first in the CC with 12 points, although Johns Hopkins and Gettysburg have played one fewer game. The Diplomats made the CC playoffs last season, and although they have struggled this year, they have not allowed more than two goals in a game, and they beat McDaniel, a team to which Muhlenberg lost.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg leads the all-time series, 16-4-1. F&amp;M won last year&#8217;s meeting, 1-0.</p>
<p>Live stats/video: <a href="http://client.stretchinternet.com/client/fandm.portal">http://client.stretchinternet.com/client/fandm.portal</a>#</p>
<p><strong>Cross Country at Connecticut College Invitational (Saturday); at Lafayette Invitational (Saturday)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules will split up and run in two different meets. The top runners will head to Connecticut for a rare out-of-region meet. In fact, it&#8217;s the Mules&#8217; first meet not in Pennsylvania, New Jersey, New York or Maryland since they ran at Connecticut College in 1996. For the third straight week, the Muhlenberg women are ranked seventh in the Mideast Region.</p>
<p><strong>Volleyball at Kean Invitational (Friday-Saturday)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg (12-7) takes a break from CC action and plays in its third and final tournament of the regular season. The Mules&#8217; opponents are Kean (16-6) and Alvernia (9-11) on Friday and Manhattanville (8-16) and NYU-Poly (6-19) on Saturday.</p>
