		<p><b>Yearbook Content Manager</b></p>
<p><b>Intern, Office of Communications</b></p>
<p><b>2020- 2021</b></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The Office of Communications is the liaison between the College and the media for news stories, interviews and other forms of publicity. The Ciarla yearbook is one of those other forms. What makes the Ciarla unique is the individuals who produce the publication. The Ciarla is one source of communication for Muhlenberg College where the current students tell the story. The photos, quotes, student reflections, parent senior ads and more create a unique publication for what transpired on campus during a given year. For every student on campus that year, the Ciarla documents a collection of memories that will last a lifetime. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The yearbook content manager position requires leadership qualities. The intern will be expected to demonstrate initiative in helping the student editors of the Ciarla club meet their goals for publication. As a member of the Office of Communications, the yearbook content manager will be responsible for representing the Muhlenberg community to students, parents, alumni and the general public and requires an individual that is articulate, courteous, proactive, responsible and attentive.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The yearbook content manager works under the guidance of the Ciarla advisor. Responsibilities include, but are not limited to:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Collaboration with Ciarla staff on:</span>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Design</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Photography</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Social media content</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Content writing</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Determining page content</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Meeting production goals</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Proofreading</span></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Coordinate Ciarla staff meetings</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Editing</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Recruiting student liaisons</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Outreach to student liaisons, groups and activities</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Maintaining a timeline for production</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Attending events as a representative of the Ciarla</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Attending at least one SGA meeting on behalf of the Ciarla</span></li>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Computer proficient</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Good people skills</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Pleasant personality and telephone skills</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Well organized</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Attention to detail</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Knowledge of digital photography helpful</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">A major in media &amp; communication is required for academic credit. </span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The yearbook content manager reports to the assistant to the vice president of communications, Donna Fahringer. The intern is responsible for maintaining all requirements for an internship with the Department of Media and Communication.</span></p>
<p>To apply contact: Donna Fahringer &lt;donnafahringer<span class="">@muhlenberg.edu</span></p>

		<p><strong>Lehigh Valley Hospital-17<sup>th</sup> Street </strong></p>
<p><strong>Communication Internship &#8211; Spring 2020</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Lehigh Valley Health Network’s 17th Street Campus (1627 Chew Street, Allentown) seeks a student intern for the Spring 2020 semester. The position reports to the director of school-based health &amp; the child advocacy center.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Lehigh Valley Health Network’s 17th Street campus is the network’s community neighborhood hospital located in the heart of Allentown’s West End. For more than 100 years, LVH-17<sup>th</sup> Street has been committed to caring for our neighbors and our friends, delivering high quality care and offering a full range of health care services including: emergency care; breast health services; HIV testing and treatment; hepatitis care; child and adult sleep disorders; children’s programs such as child protection medicine, the John Van Brakle Child Advocacy Center, school-based health care and preventive health education; a dental clinic; ambulatory surgery; mental health care; inpatient hospice care; rehabilitation services; and, a range of community medical practices providing primary and specialty care for the entire family. Interpreter services are available to patients for whom English is not their preferred language. Financial assistance is also offered to patients who need to be connected with medical insurance. Legal services are available around issues of public benefits, landlord-tenant disputes, repair of poor housing conditions, resolution of denied health insurance benefits, accessing food subsidies and personal safety from domestic violence.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Position responsibilities include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Researching and writing monthly blog posts featured in LVHN Daily and Weekly</li>
<li>Production of LVH-17<sup>th</sup> Street’s internal newsletter for colleagues (including all research, interviewing, writing and graphic design)</li>
<li>Partnering with colleagues in LVHN marketing to plan/coordinate social media posts of LVH-17<sup>th</sup> Street-based events, community programs, and services</li>
<li>Supporting various departments at LVH-17<sup>th</sup> Street with enhancing and standardizing internal communications</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This position is for-credit (pending academic department approval), unpaid and offers a flexible schedule. Applications will be accepted immediately. Interested applicants should submit the following to Lisa.Liddington@lvhn.org:</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>A cover letter explaining the interest in and qualifications for this role</li>
<li>A resume that details prior coursework, work experience and/or community engagement and service</li>
<li>At least one writing sample</li>
<li>The name of a faculty member who can serve as a reference, upon request</li>
</ul>

		<p><a href="https://www.sustainlv.org/act-locally/internships-with-the-alliance/current-internship-opportunities/communication/">Alliance for Sustainable Communities: Communication Internship</a></p>
<p>Communicating sustainability concepts to the community at large is a challenge, especially considering the flood of misinformation in the mainstream media. Effective communication can be quite challenging, because we often need to make scientific concepts, technical data, and moral &amp; ethical &amp; moral issues clear and engaging to the public.</p>
<p>While it is difficult to spell out in the specific activities &amp; topics months in advance, it is safe to say that interns will likely work on print, online, and in-person communications, including reports, posters, presentations, web pages, and/or news releases — and this may at times include controversial issues.</p>
<p>One challenging ‘umbrella’ topic is the extent to which media &amp; communication reinforce the unsustainable practices that are causing so many of our problems, instead of helping move to a more sustainable future. Examples of likely focus areas for 2019 include the following, all of which have technical, social-justice, and community engagement aspects::</p>
<ul>
<li>Community planning &amp; governance that often seems to benefit developers at the expense of the community</li>
<li>Education (Pre-K–12 and higher ed) that has failed to make young people ‘sustainability literate’</li>
<li>Justice systems that exacerbate problems of inequality and injustice</li>
<li>‘Business as usual’ approaches that perpetuate problems.</li>
</ul>
<p>Specific activities depend in part on what is happening in the community during the internship</p>
<p>Note: This internship is usually also available with a graphic design focus for qualified students.</p>
<p><em><strong>?</strong></em> — Send an email to internship coordinator <a href="mailto:peter@sustainlv.org">Peter Crownfield</a></p>

		<p><strong>Muhlenberg College Office of Communications</strong></p>
<p><strong>News Writer Internship/Job Description &#8211; Spring 2020</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Office of Communications seeks a student to serve as a news writer and reporter for the Spring 2020 semester. The student will serve a content-creation role and will work alongside communications staff to produce stories for both the website and printed materials. Reporting to the senior director of communications, this individual will be tasked with researching, reporting, writing, editing and publishing work in a timely fashion that adheres to the College’s style and editorial standards.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The student news writer will contribute to weekly staff and story meetings and will work collaboratively and independently on a variety of projects. In addition to work completed in the communications office, the student news writer will be assigned stories that will require attendance at events and interviews across campus with students, faculty, staff and community members. In all interactions, the student news writer is expected to serve as a representative of the Office of Communications and adhere to its standards of equity, diversity, inclusion and collegiality.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Interested students should be prepared to provide relevant writing samples and the recommendation of a faculty member who can attest to their writing and/or editing ability. Qualified applicants will be able to demonstrate the willingness and ability to communicate professionally, generate and share story ideas, respond to constructive criticism and seek opportunities for professional and personal growth.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This position may be structured as a for-credit unpaid internship (pending academic department approval) or as a paid position. Application materials should be submitted to <a href="mailto:billkeller@muhlenberg.edu">billkeller@muhlenberg.edu</a> and include the following items as a Word document, Google Doc or PDF:</p>
<ul>
<li>A cover letter explaining the interest in and qualifications for this role</li>
<li>A resume that details prior coursework, work experience and/or community engagement and service</li>
<li>At least one writing sample that demonstrates the ability to construct a narrative and displays the author’s style, tone and voice</li>
<li>The name of a faculty member who can serve as a reference, upon request</li>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p><strong>THE FOUNDATION FOR THE BETHLEHEM AREA SCHOOL DISTRICT </strong></p>
<p><strong>Position: </strong>Communications Intern</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Position Description: </strong>This is an unpaid, part-time, internship position starting in September. Hours for the position are 10am-3pm with a minimum of 15 hours per week. This however, can be modified to fit with your academic schedule. Evening hours are expected for monthly Board of Trustees meetings which are held the 3<sup>rd</sup> Tuesday of each month.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Expectations and Responsibilities</strong></p>
<p><strong><em> </em></strong></p>
<p><strong><em>Marketing/Public Relations/Social Media</em></strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Assist with proofing, editing and distribution of all printed materials.</li>
<li>Create written and graphic content for social media platforms and marketing materials.</li>
<li>Assist in developing the 2020 Social Media calendar.</li>
<li>Work with Bethlehem Area School District Media to develop promotional videos.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><em> </em></strong></p>
<p><strong><em>Administrative</em></strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Assist staff with processing of donations and acknowledgements letters.</li>
<li>Updating the donor-base system as needed.</li>
<li>Provide support for all grant recipients of Teacher Grants.</li>
<li>Provide support to the Board of Trustees and Liaisons as needed.</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong><em>Special Events</em></strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Participate in soliciting donations with consideration to final Silent Auction offering to guests.</li>
<li>Assisted in developing printed materials and theme for the 5<sup>th</sup> Celebration Dinner.</li>
<li>Support staff to ensure the Special Event timeline is adhered too.</li>
<li>Create Silent Auction tracking system.</li>
<li>Provide suggestions and examples for potential Silent Auction packages.</li>
<li>Review past check out processes and provide suggestions for improvements.</li>
<li>Assist in the planning and development of the venue, décor, and dinner program.</li>
<li>Coordinate with community partners to ensure our sponsorships goals are met.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong> </strong></p>
<p><strong>Qualifications:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Computer experience (Word, Excel, Power Point)</li>
<li>Strong organizational skills with attention to detail</li>
<li>Self-directed, enthusiastic, and creative</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The interview process will begin on August 19<sup>st</sup>.</p>
<p>Applications will be considered during the interview process, until the position is filled.</p>
<p>Interested applicants should submit resume with the email subject line “Intern Position Inquiry” to</p>
<p>Kayla Jacobs via email: kjacobs@basdschools.org</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p class="Normal"><strong><span class="tm5">Duties &amp; Responsibilities:</span></strong></p>
<ul>
<li class="Normal tm6"><span class="tm7">Develop a schedule of photography opportunities and work with summer researchers to book photo sessions</span></li>

<li class="Normal tm6"><span class="tm7">Photograph and edit images of student summer research</span></li>
<li class="Normal tm9"><span class="tm7">On occasion, write about and film students conducting summer research</span></li>
</ul>
<p class="Normal"><span class="tm7"> </span><strong><span class="tm5">Qualifications/Skills:</span></strong></p>
<ul>
<li class="Normal tm6"><span class="tm7">Excellent organization and time management skills</span></li>
<li class="Normal tm6"><span class="tm7">Strong communication skills</span></li>
<li class="Normal tm6"><span class="tm7">Familiarity with digital photography and editing</span></li>
<li class="Normal tm6"><span class="tm7">Experience with Adobe Photoshop and/or Lightroom a plus</span></li>
<li class="Normal tm6"><span class="tm7">Experience researching and interviewing unfamiliar topics</span></li>
</ul>
<p>For more information or to apply, please contact <a href="mailto:billkeller@muhlenberg.edu">Bill Keller</a>, Senior Director, Office of Communications, 484-664-3230 (tel).</p>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong>Digital Media Assistant, Communications, Office of Advancement</strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong>Muhlenberg College, Summer and/or Fall 2019</strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><u>On-site Internship Advisor:</u></strong> Heather Lavin ’08, Senior Associate Director of Advancement Communication</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><u>Goals</u></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400">The goals of Advancement’s Digital Media Assistant are to:</p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Gain an understanding of social media marketing and digital communication strategies.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Develop a comprehensive content calendar for Muhlenberg Alumni Channels on Facebook, Twitter and Instagram.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Provide student perspective in the development of young alumni programs and social media marketing.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Develop comprehensive communication campaigns that engage our online audience.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Learn to evaluate digital communications through analysis including social media insights and Google Analytics.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Execute social media marketing plan for events and mini-campaigns, including real-time photo sharing during the weekend.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Support the Sen. Associate Director of Communications in creating website content, news articles and/or emails. Training provided.</li>
</ul>
<p style="font-weight: 400">
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><u>Job Duties</u></strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Interviews in April-summer; internship begins the first week of classes in the fall.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Attend weekly meetings with the Sen. Associate Director of Alumni Relations to discuss strategy, ideas, and project feedback</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Create and implement social media marketing plans for regional alumni events, campus and alumni. This includes marketing design, social networking updates and scheduling.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Submit regular social media analytics and monthly overviews of online activity.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Archiving for our online media through collecting old and current campus photos, documents, Weekly articles and yearbook photos.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Write short news stories and content for MuhlenbergConnect.com, our alumni website.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">End of semester presentation to the Office of Advancement staff on alumni social media activity, strategy, future goals and recommendations.</li>
</ul>
<p style="font-weight: 400">Work 10-12 hours per week to meet the Media &amp; Comm. required time.</p>
