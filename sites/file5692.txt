		<p><em>Cody Antonini recently finished his career as a Muhlenberg men&#8217;s soccer player with a bang, recording a hat trick in his final game. That brought Antonini&#8217;s career total to 49 goals &#8211; the fourth-most in program history.</em></p>
<p><em>The 49 goals came over a five-year span (Antonini took the 2011 season off) and in a variety of situations. Who did he score against most? Who helped him score the most? And were his goals important ones that helped the Mules win?</em></p>
<p><em>The answer to those questions and more in this breakdown of Antonini&#8217;s 49 career goals:</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/11/antonini_anthology1.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-1477" title="Cody Antonini" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/11/antonini_anthology1.jpg" alt="" width="275" height="482" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/11/antonini_anthology1.jpg 275w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/11/antonini_anthology1-171x300.jpg 171w" sizes="(max-width: 275px) 100vw, 275px" /></a>by opponent</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Moravian &#8211; 6</li>
<li>Gettysburg &#8211; 5</li>
<li>Ursinus &#8211; 4</li>
<li>New Jersey &#8211; 4 </li>
<li>Franklin &amp; Marshall &#8211; 3</li>
<li>Dickinson, Centenary, Cortland State, Haverford, Marywood, McDaniel, Washington, Wilkes, William Paterson &#8211; 2 each</li>
<li>Baruch, DeSales, Drew, Elizabethtown, Johns Hopkins, Montclair State, NYU, Richard Stockton, Swarthmore &#8211; 1 each</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Note: Antonini scored against 23 different opponents, including all eight in the Centennial Conference.</em></p>
<p><strong>by class year</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>freshman &#8211; 5</li>
<li>sophomore &#8211; 12</li>
<li>junior &#8211; 16</li>
<li>senior &#8211; 16</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>by assister</strong></p>
