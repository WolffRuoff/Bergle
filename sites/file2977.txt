<p>We also sponsor a lot of events (Gala, dances, family programs) and run after-school activities (sports teams, classes), so there is always a lot of information to share with our audiences.</p>
<p>Sound interesting? If so, we are looking for an intern to join our Communications staff – we have had several Muhlenberg students as interns in the past, so we know the drill!</p>
<p>Whether or not you have experience in any or all of these areas, you will get an opportunity to try them out with the guidance of our Director of Communications:</p>
<ul>
<li>Publications (writing, designing and proofing newsletters and brochures, both print and electronic)</li>
<li>Media relations (identifying appropriate news items, writing releases and sending them to appropriate media)</li>
<li>Web manager (updating the site with appropriate information)</li>
<li>Internal communications (creating and producing informational flyers, both print and electronic; creating electronic communications)</li>
<li>Social media (posting appropriate news and photos)</li>
<li>Display monitors (create slides, program monitors with daily updates)</li>
</ul>
<p>Specifically, the intern will have responsibility for these projects:</p>
<ul>
<li>Create a marketing plan for two school events, using outlets like internal newsletters and flyers, news releases, advertisements, website information, social media, and online calendars</li>
<li>Develop news releases and photo features about specific school events</li>
<li>Contribute articles to at least two school e-newsletters</li>
</ul>
<p>The intern will:</p>
<ul>
<li>Report directly to the Director of Communications and work with other staff as appropriate</li>
<li>Successfully secure clearances from “Request for Criminal Record Check”, “Pennsylvania Child Abuse History Clearance”, and the FBI (the school will assume this cost)</li>
<li>Submit two letters of character recommendation</li>
<li>Work a minimum of ten hours per week <strong>(flexible schedule)</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>The intern should have these qualifications:</p>
<ul>
<li>Excellent verbal and written communication skills (including proofreading)</li>
<li>Proficiency in Microsoft Word; familiarity with Google documents is a plus</li>
<li>Digital photography and photo editing skills are a plus</li>
<li>“Low tech” skills for creating bulletin boards and other visual displays are welcome</li>
</ul>

		<h1>Editor Position for the Alternative Report for the Allentown School District &#8211; Campaign for Change (C4C), Fall Internship</h1>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>During Fall Semester 2014, one intern will work together with the Campaign for Change (C4C) staff to edit the Alternative Report for the Allentown School District. This is a comprehensive report that examines the Allentown School District (ASD) and the racial disparities within it in terms of student performance and hiring policies. This report is a direct response to the ASD Annual Report, which lacks of crucial information such as graduation rates, dropout rates, students’ reading, and math performance, and an analysis the cultural mismatch between students and teachers. Additionally to that, the intern will have the opportunity to be exposed to several activities in regards to Campaign for Change, such as nonviolent direct action tactics, community organizing processes, and tactics for advancing political goals.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>This is a non paid internship.</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Main Responsibilities</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Discuss with C4C staff the Alternative Report to the ASD (40+ pages)</li>
<li>The edition process would be section by section and would follow this steps:</li>
</ul>
<p>○      Discuss the goals of the section with C4C staff</p>
<p>○      Apply changes on the paper so the section meets its goal</p>
<p>○      Correct writing style, if necessary (must be the same throughout the whole document)</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Timing</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>The due date for applications is September 7th, 2014 (send CV and an email to gerardoct@gmail.com stating why you are interested in doing this project)</li>
<li>Internship length: September 15th to December 5th, 2014</li>
<li>The interns are expected to completely polish and edit the report.</li>
<li>They are expected to be available for follow up meetings twice per week for an hour or so.</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Equipment</strong></p>
<p>Interns are expected to use their own equipment or Muhlenberg’s equipment (laptop or pc).</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Additional Requirements</strong></p>
<p>The intern must be interested in how to advance social change and social justice, willing to learn new concepts regarding community organizing, and nonviolent direct action.  Interested students should email Gerardo Calderón at gerardoct@gmail.com.  The due date for applications is September 7th.  Student should email their resume and a paragraphs stating why they are interested in the position.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>What is Campaign for Change?</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Campaign for Change is the non-violent direct action community response to the racial barriers that are embedded in the academic environment within the Allentown School District. This group is raising awareness about the injustices currently happening in schools, and discussing with students and parents ways of addressing them.</p>
