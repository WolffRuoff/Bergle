(2011, February 25). Reporters, publicists play game year-round. <em>Variety</em>. Retrieved from <a href="http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118032485?refcatid=1985">http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118032485?refcatid=1985</a>.</p>
<p>A new development in the relationship between Hollywood publicists and news journalists has heightened to a new level, regardless of the time of year. Publicists and news journalists often have agendas of different sorts; while publicists want to promote positive images of their clients, news journalists are constantly trying to find new stories, no matter what type of light is shed on the subjects of the investigation. In years prior, however, Hollywood publicists and entertainment journalists&#8217; goals would often converge during awards season. In the period of time from October to late February, in which all eyes and ears are focused on the &#8220;awards-season beast,&#8221; publicists and reporters often came to an unspoken agreement featuring &#8220;exclusives and firsts taking a backseat to smarter analysis and better access.&#8221; According to several of Hollywood&#8217;s leading publicity honchos, however, this temporary standstill in the conflicting interests of the two groups no longer exists. Reporting on important film news related to the awards race during the actual season is now commonplace. An example of this occurred last year, when news stories regarding <em>The Hurt Locker</em> producer Nicolas Chartier being banned from attending the awards as a result of an email blasting <em>Avatar</em> quickly circulated throughout the industry in the runup to the Oscars.<a class="more-link button" href="http://mediaindustries11.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/02/26/an-armistice-between-publicists-and-journalists/">Read More</a></p>

<h1><span style="color: #000000"> </span></h1>
<p>Albiniak.P.(2011,February 14). Spencer, Levy to Head MTV&#8217;s Scripted Development.<em>Broadcasting &amp; Cable</em>. Retrieved from <a href="http://www.broadcastingcable.com/article/463937-Spencer_Levy_to_Head_MTV_s_Scripted_Development.php" target="_blank">http://www.broadcastingcable.com/article/463937-Spencer_Levy_to_Head_MTV_s_Scripted_Development.php</a></p>
<p>MTV’s scripted series recently appointed new leadership. The Network’s president or Programming David Janollari named Clay Spencer and Justin Levy the new heads. Janollari said that their combined experience makes them the perfect team to work together. Levy has worked on MTV scripted series before while Spencer is new to the network. Spencer’s diverse television career has had a successful track record; working on networks like The WB and an independent production company. Janollari and him have worked together before. Just like in the publishing world MTV is sticking to who has been successful. In demanding times for television it would not be wise to hire some brand new writer no one has heard of even if he or she looks to have good work. Janollari is hiring who he knows and who he thinks he can get good results from.</p>
