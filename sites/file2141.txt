<h2><strong><img class="alignleft" src="https://muhlenberg.meyerandassoc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/185/2017/10/069-MUHL.png" width="430" height="135" /></strong></h2>
<h3>Digital Media Assistant, Muhlenberg Advancement</h3>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left"><strong>On-site Internship Advisor:</strong> <a href="mailto:heatherlavin@muhlenberg.edu">Heather Lavin</a> ’08, Senior Associate Director of Advancement Communication</p>



<p><strong>Goals</strong></p>



<p>The goals of Advancement’s Digital Media Assistant are to:</p>



<ul>
<li>Gain an understanding of social media marketing and digital communication strategies.</li>
<li>

Develop a comprehensive content calendar for Muhlenberg Alumni Channels on Facebook, Twitter and Instagram.</li>
<li>Provide student perspective in the development of young alumni programs and social media marketing.</li>
<li>Develop comprehensive communication campaigns that engage our online audience. </li>
<li>Learn to evaluate digital communications through analysis including social media insights and Google Analytics.</li>
<li> Execute social media marketing plan for events and mini-campaigns, including real-time photo sharing during the weekend.</li>
<li>Support the Sen. Associate Director of Communications in creating website content, news articles and/or emails. Training provided.</li>
</ul>



<p><strong>Job Duties</strong></p>



<ul>
<li>Interviews in April-summer; internship begins the first week of classes in the fall.</li>
<li>Attend weekly meetings with the Sen. Associate Director of Alumni Relations to discuss strategy, ideas, and project feedback.</li>
<li>Create and implement social media marketing plans for regional alumni events, campus and alumni. This includes marketing design, social networking updates and scheduling.</li>
<li>Submit regular social media analytics and monthly overviews of online activity.</li>
<li>Archiving for our online media through collecting old and current campus photos, documents, Weekly articles and yearbook photos.</li>
<li>Write short news stories and content for MuhlenbergConnect.com, our alumni website.</li>
<li>End of semester presentation to the Office of Advancement staff on alumni social media activity, strategy, future goals and recommendations.</li>
</ul>



<p>Work 10-12 hours per week to meet the Media &amp; Comm. required time.</p>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p><strong>­­Social Media Assistant, Alumni Relations</strong></p>
<p><strong>Muhlenberg College, Spring 2016</strong></p>
<p><strong><u>On-site Internship Advisor:</u></strong> Heather Lavin ’08, Associate Director of Alumni Affairs</p>
<p><strong><u>Goals </u></strong></p>
<p>The goals of Alumni Affair’s Social Media Assistant are to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Gain understanding of social media marketing and communication strategies.</li>
<li>Develop a comprehensive content calendar for Alumni Relations’ Facebook, Twitter and Instagram accounts.</li>
<li>Provide student perspective in the development of young alumni programs and social media marketing.</li>
<li>Develop comprehensive communication campaigns that engage our online audience.</li>
<li>Write short news stories for MuhlenbergConnect.com, our alumni online community.</li>
<li>Execute social media marketing plan for events and mini-campaigns, including real-time photo sharing during the weekend.</li>
<li>Support the Associate Director of Alumni Relations in aspects of young alumni communication and department goals.</li>
<li>Write a CUE paper on a topic to be determined.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><u>Job Duties</u></strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Interviews in April-summer; internship begins the first week of classes in the fall</li>
<li>Attend weekly meetings with the Associate Director of Alumni Relations to discuss strategy, ideas, and project feedback</li>
<li>Create and implement social media marketing plans for regional alumni events, campus and alumni. This includes marketing design, social networking updates and scheduling.</li>
<li>Submit regular social media analytics and monthly overviews of online activity.</li>
<li>Archiving for our online media through collecting old and current campus photos, documents, Weekly articles and yearbook photos.</li>
<li>End of semester presentation to Development &amp; Alumni Relations staff on alumni social media activity, strategy, future goals and recommendations.</li>
</ul>
<p>Work 10-12 hours per week to meet the Media &amp; Comm. required time.</p>

	    <div class="post-meta">
		
			<span class="post-date"><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=1302" title="Religious Life Communications Intern – Spring 2015">November 10, 2014</a></span>
			
			<span class="date-sep"> / </span>
				
			<span class="post-author"><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=615" title="Posts by acorbin" rel="author">acorbin</a></span>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p><strong>Muhlenberg Chaplain’s Office</strong></p>
<p><strong> </strong>The Religious Life Communications Intern will assist the College Chaplain with event publicity and other communication for Religious Life events during the Spring 2015 semester. The intern will coordinate publicity for Interfaith Leadership Council events, including Interfaith Week 2015. The intern will also help publicize Chapel services and special events, and other religious/spiritual programming of the Chaplain’s Office. The intern will be invited to do a publicity audit and propose recommendations for more consistent and broad-reaching publicity by the Chaplain’s Office. The intern will work collaboratively with the College Chaplain, the Assistant to the Chaplain, work-study student staff and other students and staff.</p>
<p><strong>Requirements:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Intern will contribute to the hospitality of the Chaplain’s Office, ready and willing to serve students, faculty, staff and other community members of any or no faith tradition.</li>
<li>Excellent written and oral communication skills, including graphic design and social media skills</li>
<li>Intern will be self-motivated and consistently meet deadlines</li>
<li>Flexibility</li>
<li>Promptly respond to email and other communication</li>
</ul>
<p>This internship is unpaid, but may be eligible for academic credit if approved by an academic advisor and a faculty sponsor. Contact the Career Center to learn more about the internship registration process.</p>
<p>Students of any major may apply. No car is necessary.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Hours:             Average 8-12 hours per week during the academic terms.</p>
<p>Intern will meet weekly with the Chaplain.</p>
<p>Intern will Interfaith Leadership Council meetings twice each month.</p>
<p>Intern will attend monthly Religious Life staff meetings.</p>
<p>Other hours will be flexible.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Supervision:    The intern will be supervised by Callista Isabelle, College Chaplain.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>To Apply:      Send cover letter and resume to <a href="mailto:cisabelle@muhlenberg.edu">cisabelle@muhlenberg.edu</a>.</p>
<p>The internship is open until filled.</p>
