			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/big-news/" rel="bookmark">Big News</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/big-news/" title="1:23 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 21, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/swedberg/" title="View all posts by Drew">Drew</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Its been a stressful couple of weeks. Between work, work, and work there really has not been any time for other things. This may have induced some stress but behind this all was an application that was waiting to be processed&#8230;</p>
<p>The application had taken weeks to finish and was finally in the mail at the beginning of April. It has perhaps been the longest 20 days in my life waiting to hear back. Every scenario of why I would be denied played out in my mind, but finally, on this day, I have some really spectacular news.</p>
<p>In the Fall I will be studying in Prague at the Film And Television School Of Academy of Performing Arts!</p>
<p>I repeat, in the Fall I will be studying in Prague at the Film And Television School Of Academy of Performing Arts!</p>
<p>I cannot believe this. As someone who wants to work in the film industry this is&#8230;. well&#8230; there is not really words for this. FAMU is largely considered the best film school in Europe and one of the best in the world. The school was behind one of the most influential movements in film history in the Czech New Wave cinema, producing directors like Milos Forman and Vera Chytilova. I cannot talk enough about the prestige of this school and I feel so incredibly blessed to have the opportunity to study there.</p>
<p>So about the Fall. This is kind of cool and such unique situation for my family and me. In August I will be traveling to Barcelona to play basketball with my team for a week and then they will be leaving me over there. From Barcelona I will travel to Munich to spend time with family in the days between the end of the basketball trip and the start of the semester. My father&#8217;s sister married a German opera singer a while back and they will be showing me around the city. Then I will travel to Prague to study film production for 16 weeks. This is really a unique opportunity, as I have never been able to leave the country (or even the East Coast) before this. But the finances have fallen into place, and with a few greatly appreciated scholarships and some hard work over the summer I will be able to make this experience a reality for me.</p>
<p>I will certainly be missing people back here at home, from my teammates to my kids at Jefferson, but plan on staying in touch (I will be doing a pen pal program with my 1st graders while abroad) and will certainly fill people in when I return in the Spring!</p>
<p>The relief and excitement I am feeling right now is fantastic. I am ready to take this next step in my pursuit of a career in the film industry and cannot wait to get to Europe.</p>
<p>Happy Birthday!</p>
<p>Drew</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/down-the-home-stretch/" title="5:25 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 20, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I am sitting on my computer as I begin getting more and more emails about Senior Week approaching and registering for events during that week. It is hard to believe how fast college is coming to an end.</p>
<p>At this point I am not sure if I have come to terms with everything or if maybe I still haven&#8217;t comprehended that this will all be ending very soon. Of course I don&#8217;t want college to end, it has been four of the best years of my life, but I do think I have gotten myself ready for it.</p>
<p>It is still hard to tell how I will feel when graduation gets here and then comes to an end. This will be the first time in my life that I will begin a summer and there will not be a next year of college to follow. There is no denying it will be a major adjustment and will definitely feel weird for a while.</p>
<p>After some time passes there will be a new routine for me of going to work each day. It makes for longer days, but it does mean there are no more tests or papers that need to be completed. It will be a new and exciting chapter of my life.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/senior-day/" rel="bookmark">Senior Day</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/senior-day/" title="12:02 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 19, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>This past weekend for baseball we had our senior day. This is when the baseball program honors the accomplishments of all the seniors that are on the team. It is a sad day knowing the end of our baseball careers are approaching, but it is a happy occasion as we get to celebrate a career of playing our favorite sport.</p>
<p>In regards to our games it didn&#8217;t go too well. We lost both games in our double header to Gettysburg and these were very important games. I really did just take in the whole day and enjoy everything. It was a lot of fun and kind of tough knowing this will be one of the last times I will step on to our home field. I have played so many games here and can&#8217;t believe it is almost over.</p>
<p>It is hard to believe that the end is getting really close, but at the same time I am excited for all the great things that the future holds.</p>

			<div id="post-4239" class="post-4239 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/muhlenberg-honors-holocaust-remembrance-day/" rel="bookmark">Muhlenberg Honors Holocaust Remembrance Day</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/04/muhlenberg-honors-holocaust-remembrance-day/" title="12:01 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 19, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>On April 15-16 every year, not just Jews, but people all around the world, pay homage to those who perished under Hitlers reign of unimaginable evil during the Holocaust. The date of Yom ha Shoah (Holocaust Remembrance Day) was chosen because it commemorates the bravery of the Warsaw Ghetto uprising, 70 years ago.</p>
<p>To remember Yom ha Shoah, Hillel and the Muhlenberg Activities Committee had a screening of the documentary &#8220;Paper Clips,&#8221; and a conversation with the director afterwards. The film captures a small town in rural Tennessee that started a diversity project to learn about the events of the holocaust. When one of the students asked &#8220;what is 6 million, I cant imagine that&#8221; the idea came to collect 6 million paper clips in the honor of those survivors. After more and more press began covering the story, Whitwell Middle School was given over 29 million paper clips from all over the world. The students and faculty, none of whom had any connection to Judaism, became completely physically and emotionally invested in their project.</p>
<p>The question came up, what do you do with all these paper clips? The principle of the school, who was spearheading the project, suggested they try and get a german cattle car (which transported Jews to their death) to bring to Whitwell and convert into a memorial to house the paper clips. Two German journalists, the first people to cover the story, then found this cattle car, and brought it to Tennessee. Today there is a beautiful memorial site, and the students of Whitwell Middle School give tours and maintain it will love.</p>
<p>This time last year, I was touring Poland with a Jewish delegation going to the various concentration camps. On Yom ha Shoah, we marched from Auschwitz to Birkenau with 10,000 Jews and Holocaust survivors, and celebrated life while remembering those who perished. This powerful experience gave me a very personal connection to this day every year. Because of that, I was almost brought to tears by the Paper Clips story. I suggest you all see it!</p>
