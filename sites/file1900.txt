<p>The spring semester has ended and summer session has begun. Many changes have occurred over the last few months. The one constant is the support and collaborative efforts throughout the Muhlenberg College community, whether on or off campus. </p>



<p>While we are teaching, learning and working remotely, we want to make sure we stay connected and you stay informed. </p>



<p> IT Connects! blog posts will be published monthly keeping you up to date on the latest OIT news, important notices, maintenance schedules, training and professional development opportunities and more. </p>



<p><strong>Events</strong></p>



<ul><li>Weekly LIVE Google Meet on Wednesdays from 3pm-4pm<br>Start May 27. An open forum for faculty, staff and students to ask their technology questions.  <a href="https://calendar.google.com/event?action=TEMPLATE&amp;tmeid=NzZqODdlZGRwZWMyZDJqbWc0b243NzcyYWJfMjAyMDA1MjdUMTkwMDAwWiBtdWhsZW5iZXJnLmVkdV9ubjgzODRpbDYwc2gyOG1ndXRjMDhodHNmb0Bn&amp;tmsrc=muhlenberg.edu_nn8384il60sh28mgutc08htsfo%40group.calendar.google.com&amp;scp=ALL" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">The Meet link is available in the Google Calendar event</a>. You must be logged into your Muhlenberg account to access it.  </li><li>Access the OIT calendar for training opportunities, scheduled maintenance and other OIT events (Note:  You must be logged into your Muhlenberg account to access it). <a href="https://calendar.google.com/calendar/embed?src=muhlenberg.edu_nn8384il60sh28mgutc08htsfo%40group.calendar.google.com&amp;ctz=America%2FNew_York">Google Calendar link</a></li></ul>



<p><strong>Updates</strong></p>



<ul><li><strong>Extension of&nbsp;Adobe&nbsp;Creative&nbsp;Cloud&nbsp;At-Home Access</strong><br>Adobe provided temporary at-home access to Creative Cloud for impacted students due to campus closures, so that they may continue their work remotely.    Adobe has recently announced that temporary at-home access of Creative Cloud has been extended through July 6th, 2020.  </li><li><strong>G Suite</strong><ul><li><strong>Google Meet &#8211; video meetings made easy </strong><br>Advanced features available campuswide through September 30, 2020. Features include: video conference features, such as larger meetings (up to 250 participants) and recording.</li><li><strong>Google Drive shortcuts</strong><br>Create pointers to any file or folder in Drive.  Shortcuts make it easier for you or your team to find and organize files and folders in multiple Google Drives. Learn more at  <a href="https://support.google.com/drive/answer/9700156">https://support.google.com/drive/answer/9700156</a> </li></ul></li><li><strong>Workday Student</strong><br>The College is currently in the 12th month of a 32-month Workday Student Implementation project. Workday Student will provide Muhlenberg staff, faculty, and students many benefits, including a reduction in some current paper processes, quicker access to more data, and automated workflows. <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/initiatives/oit/workday/">Visit muhlenberg.edu/workday to learn more</a>.&nbsp; </li></ul>

Topics will include: communication, collaboration, security and working remotely. </li></ul>

					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172350" rel="category">Client Support Services</a></li>

	<div class="entry-content">
		
<p>As you may know, Microsoft is ending support for Windows 7 in January 2020. We will no longer receive security updates for the operating system, which is a pretty big deal. We have been energetically replacing these machines with Windows 10 devices across campus. Each new computer deployed for the past 1.5 years has been a Windows 10 computer, and we have done a number of upgrades in-place as well. </p>



<p>Unfortunately, we simply are not moving fast enough. At the current rate, we will not be complete until the end of 2020. </p>



<p>In order to meet our very important deadline, we will be changing our policy on the Windows 10 roll-out. Whereas we have been waiting for individuals to be available before making the upgrade thus far, we are compelled to take a more assertive approach. </p>



<p>Today, we reach out to users for upgrades, then wait for a response. We have found this response to be surprisingly long in duration. We are averaging only 4-5 computers a week. Therefore, we will be waiting only 7  business days before <strong><em>your password will be reset to complete the upgrade</em></strong>. You will need to contact the Support Desk at 484-664-3566 in order to receive your new password. We estimate we&#8217;ll be able to approach 20 computers a week with this new method. Downtime for users should therefore be minimal with that kind of pace.</p>



<p>Ready for the great news? Your computer&#8217;s performance will greatly improve with the upgrade! Get ready for a reboot that happens in seconds, not minutes (upon minutes)!</p>



<p>We apologize for this rather severe change in policy, but it is critically important that we not run unsupported computers on campus.</p>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>On May 3, 2017 at approximately 3:00pm, a phishing email circulated stating that a known user &#8220;has shared a document on Google Docs with you&#8221;. The content of the email stated that a known user &#8220;has invited you to view the following document&#8221; with a link to &#8220;Open in Docs&#8221;. If a user clicked on the link, they were taken to a Google hosted page asking permission for an app named &#8220;Google Docs&#8221; to access their email and contacts. By clicking &#8220;Allow&#8221; the user account was compromised, granting access to the malicious app.</p>
<p>The Support Team sent an email after the first few incidents of the phishing scam were reported. After gathering more information, we instructed users to go to <a href="https://myaccount.google.com/permissions">https://myaccount.google.com/permissions</a> to view the apps connected to their account and subsequently remove the fake &#8220;Google Docs&#8221; app, if present. In addition, the Support Team reached out to users compromised by this phishing scam.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, Google reported that within an hour of the attack, they had removed the fake application. On the <a href="https://support.google.com/mail#topic=7065107">Gmail help site</a>, Google states &#8220;We&#8217;ve addressed an issue with a phishing email claiming to be Google Docs. If you think you were affected, visit <a href="https://g.co/SecurityCheckup" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">g.co/SecurityCheckup</a>&#8220;.</p>
<p>Phishing attacks heighten awareness about the importance of securing your data and environment. Even if you know the source, if something looks suspicious, delete it. Thank you to those who reported the receipt of these phishing emails. We encourage you to contact the Support Team and Student Help Desk immediately if you receive something suspicious, so that OIT may investigate.</p>
<p>Please revisit our post <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/10/19/it-protects-working-together-to-create-a-secure-environment/" rel="bookmark">IT Protects: Working together to create a secure environment</a> for information and resources about Cyber Security.</p>

<article id="post-598" class="post post-598 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-client-support-services category-google-apps tag-cyber-security tag-email tag-google">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=598" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2017-01-20T10:50:39-04:00">January 20, 2017</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=61">Phedra Henninger</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=598" rel="bookmark">IT Protects: Gmail Phishing Scam</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172350" rel="category">Client Support Services</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172338" rel="category">Google Apps</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=cyber-security" rel="tag">cyber security</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=email" rel="tag">email</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=google" rel="tag">google</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=598#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>A highly effective Gmail phishing scam has been reported.</p>
<p>DO NOT attempt to login to your Gmail account from ANY location other than the Application Portal &#8211; http://login.muhlenberg.edu, which redirects you to https://muhlenberg-college.onelogin.com/login. If you find yourself anywhere other than the portal, DO NOT enter anything. Muhlenberg College will never ask you, either directly or via a link, to enter your credentials anywhere else.</p>
<p>If <a href="https://muhlenberg-college.onelogin.com/login">https://muhlenberg-college.onelogin.com/login</a> is<a href="https://muhlenberg-college.onelogin.com/login"> </a>not the URL in the address bar AND you do not see the login screen below DO NOT login.</p>
<p><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/onelogin.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-599" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/onelogin-300x189.jpg" alt="onelogin" width="300" height="189" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/onelogin-300x189.jpg 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/onelogin-768x484.jpg 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/onelogin-1024x645.jpg 1024w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/onelogin.jpg 1073w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>If you are asked to login to your Gmail account directly DO NOT take action. If you receive an email, even if it appears to be from a &#8220;trusted source&#8221; or known sender, DO NOT open an suspicious attachments.</p>
<p>In this particular phishing scam, users are sent an attachment that links to a FAKE Google account page. If you receive anything of this nature, DO NOT open the attachment. Contact <a href="mailto:support@muhlenberg.edu">support@muhlenberg.edu</a> immediately.</p>
