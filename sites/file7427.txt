I went into the Career Center during Quick Questions to polish it a little, and here is what I came up with:</p>
<p>&#8220;<em>I’m Felisa Wiley. I’m a Media Communications major at Muhlenberg College. I love the influential power behind social media and want to work in an industry that has the ability to shape the opinions of the masses. I’m dedicated, enthusiastic, and am looking to work in a job market that doesn’t show any signs of slowing down.</em>&#8221;</p>
<p>What do you think? I&#8217;m not sure when they&#8217;ll announce the winners but I&#8217;ll let you know the outcome!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/02/mock-tour/" title="5:40 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 17, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Last week I gave my 2nd mock tour. Before officially giving a tour to prospective students, I have to  give a tour to my Tour Guide Mentor. During this mock tour, she is to act like a perspective student and ask me questions about the college and its programs. These mock tours are just to make sure that I&#8217;m officially ready to go on out there and represent the college well.</p>
<p>My first mock tour did not go so well; I was nervous, scared, and I forgot almost everything I learned. But I told my mentor that by my 2nd mock tour, I would blow her out of the ball park and golly gee I did! The information flowed smoothly, was accurate and I made her proud!</p>
<p>My first official tour will be tomorrow at 3:30pm&#8211;I&#8217;m so excited and cannot wait!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<div id="post-3948" class="post-3948 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/02/valentines-week/" rel="bookmark">Valentine&#8217;s Week</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/02/valentines-week/" title="3:14 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 17, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Love sure was in the air last week here at &#8216;Berg! You couldn&#8217;t walk through Seegers without seeing fundraisers for different groups on campus, the tables all decorated with red and pink hearts. Even Java Joe got in the loving mood and had hearts dangling off the ceiling. Behind the information desk there were flowers, Valentine&#8217;s Day baskets, and balloons!  It was beautiful!</p>
<p>I absolutely love the week before Valentine&#8217;s Day. Everyone is so much happier, and nicer. Don&#8217;t get me wrong, people are generally nice here, but there&#8217;s something about Valentine&#8217;s Week that makes people glow a little bit more than usual. (They&#8217;re probably hoping someone will send them something, of course. I mean, aren&#8217;t we all?)</p>
<p>This year I sent a WMUH radio Valentine&#8217;s Day song for my bosses. On Valentine&#8217;s Day at the designated time we tuned into WMUH (the Muhlenberg student run radio show). I also sent a Dynamics group gram to my best friends. I had the Dynamics sing &#8220;It&#8217;s Raining Men&#8221; to my single ladies at 8 am on Saturday morning. Yup, I made sure to wake them up bright and early on a Saturday (aren&#8217;t I kind?). Then I was awakened by the same Dynamics singing to me (luckily it wasn&#8217;t until 10am). I don&#8217;t mind, if I&#8217;m going to be waken up by anyone, it better be by the Dynamics. They are my absolute favorite Acapella group here on campus (next are the AcaFellas).</p>
<p>But now Valentine&#8217;s Day is over, and I&#8217;ll have to wait a whole year until I can have the Dynamics serenade me&#8230; Overall it was a wonderful day. How was your Valentine&#8217;s Day?</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/02/baseball-is-almost-here/" rel="bookmark">Baseball is Almost Here</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/02/baseball-is-almost-here/" title="10:01 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 16, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>It is crazy for me to think about it, but right now we are less than two weeks away from our opening baseball game of the year. Next Saturday, we will be leaving for Myrtle Beach for our spring break trip. Then the next day on Sunday we will be playing our first game. We will follow that up by playing about 9 more games on our spring trip, then come home to continue the rest of our season here.</p>
<p>With the below freezing temperatures that have been crawling into the negatives, it is a little tough to believe that the season is almost here. As has been the case for most teams, we are stuck practicing inside without any chance to get on a field. The first time we step on a field will be for our first game, which everyone is really excited for.</p>
<p>Another hope we have is that the weather will be better this year than it was last year in Myrtle Beach. A year ago it rained almost every day and temperatures were in the steady 30&#8217;s for the majority of the trip. It didn&#8217;t feel like we were on any kind of spring break. Hopefully, it will be a lot nicer and soon enough we will be back on the field playing games.</p>

			<div id="post-3943" class="post-3943 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/02/muhlenberg-wrestling-at-centennial-conference-championships/" rel="bookmark">Muhlenberg Wrestling at Centennial Conference Championships</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/02/muhlenberg-wrestling-at-centennial-conference-championships/" title="9:02 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 14, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I will start this off by apologizing for such frequent post about the wrestling team and situation, but I&#8217;m just so excited by it I can&#8217;t resist. Think about it, I&#8217;m a freshman in the starting line up, coming from a tiny private school in LA, and now I&#8217;m going against some of the most well-known schools in the country, like NYU and Johns Hopkins. Give me a break, okay?</p>
<p>Going into centennial conference championships, Muhlenberg had a pretty significant advantage: health. Like I mentioned in previous post, the grind of an NCAA season puts many guys bruised and banged up this time of year. Lucky, most of our guys avoided the injury bug and came ready for a fight.</p>
<p>I came in as a major underdog in the competition. Up a weight class at 174, I was yet to win a match in the conference. My first bout, against a Merchant Marine foe, was very close the entire time, and I narrowly dropped it 3-0. Going into my second match, I was against an opponent from Ursinus whom I already faced once before. In our last meeting, he beat me 11-0. This time, he was beating me handily, 7-0, until the third period. However, I decided it was time for some late heroics. With 30 seconds less I threw him to his back and pinned him. It was the pinnacle of my career thus far.</p>
<p>My last match was another close loss, but overall the day was a great success. The team was 6th overall, the highest in years, five wrestlers came away with victories, and Jaryd Flank placed 4th, Lenny Smith placed 3rd, and Ian Gimbar placed 2nd.</p>
<p>The season still has time remaining, but let me publicly state: the mules are on the rise and a force not to be reckoned with!</p>
