			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/senior-open-house/" rel="bookmark">Senior Open House</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/senior-open-house/" title="12:47 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 20, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>As Muhlenberg has every year, this past Saturday was the senior open house. This is the chance for perspective students that have either applied to Muhlenberg, or are planning to apply, to come in and learn more about the school.</p>
<p>They start by taking a tour around campus. This is a great opportunity to see everything that the school has to offer. That is followed by a sit-in on one of two student panels. This is the chance to learn more about the school from current students.</p>
<p>I was one of the panelists this year, and some really good questions were asked. I enjoyed the chance to be a part of this, because anything I can do to help sell Muhlenberg to these perspective students is well worth it.</p>
<p>This is a pivotal time for them, and finding the right school can be difficult. I believe that this day as a whole can make a big difference in students deciding if they want to come to Muhlenberg. I can remember coming to these events when I was in high school, and they really helped me realize that this school was the right fit. I can only hope that it does the same for some other students.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/date-with-the-office/" title="12:44 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 20, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/swedberg/" title="View all posts by Drew">Drew</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Just got back from a homemade dinner, and no, I didn&#8217;t have to travel eight hours to enjoy it! This evening, I was invited to dinner and dessert with the students who diligently work in the Office of Community Engagement with me. The two wonderful women that run the office had us over to their houses: first Beth&#8217;s for a barbecue dinner and then Eveily&#8217;s for some hot apple cider and dessert. Our hosts provided us with fantastic food and entertainment, as both of them have a young child and pets to accompany their cozy homes! It also gave me a chance to meet some of the students in the office that I had not run into before, and as the office suggests, they were all wonderful people!</p>
<p>This post also gives me a chance to reflect a little on my experiences so far at Jefferson Elementary, as I recognize the brevity of just writing about dinner! Every Monday and Thursday, when I walk into the building nestled into the city, I am greeted by Sebastian, who I have mentioned before and is one of the eighteen first-graders I work with, with his hand outstretched for an expected shake, which I receive warmly as he asks me, &#8220;how you doing?&#8221;</p>
<p>This is how I start each day at Jefferson &#8211; with the incredible students there! Still plenty of shoe tying, which I doubt will slow down as it is the quickest and easiest way to grab my attention! These kids are too smart for me, I swear! Time needs to slow down; it seems to be going by so fast as basketball starts up on Tuesday! Tuesday! With that being said, I have plenty of work to get done for class before that grind kicks in!</p>
<p>So Happy Birthday!</p>
<p>Drew</p>

			<div id="post-3314" class="post-3314 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/interconnectivity/" rel="bookmark">Interconnectivity</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/interconnectivity/" title="12:43 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 20, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>One of my favorite times of the semester is when I find some connection between all of my classes. It usually happens gradually, but for some reason everything came together this week.</p>
<p>1) At the beginning of the semester, in my Principles of Argument and Reasoning class (the one I have at 8am on Mondays and Wednesdays), we were learning how to split arguments down to its most basic parts: premises, antecedent, conclusions, etc.  A few weeks later, those terms were used in my Ancient Philosophy class to describe an argument being made by one of the philosophers we were learning about (Zeno perhaps?), and I was able to accurately analyze his argument because of what I learned from the other class.</p>
<p>2) In my Ancient Philosophy class, we constantly bring up <em>a priori</em> and <em>a posterior</em> knowledge, especially when we are comparing one philosopher to the next. I first learned of those terms in that class, and then a few days later, those terms came up in my Apocalyptic Literature class. When my classmate looked confused, I was able to help her understand what the author meant (Felisa: 1, Others: 0).</p>
<p>3) On the first day of Apocalyptic Literature, my professor wrote down some terms that will be useful throughout the semester and one of them was &#8220;teleology&#8221;. Today during a discussion about Socrates, the term <em>telos</em> was brought up. I was able to pull out my notebook from my other class and use the definition from my Apocalyptic Literature class to help me understand how my Ancient Philosophy professor was using it and compare and contrast the context in which it was used.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m sure there are other small things I can think of that that I can add to my list to show how my classes are becoming more and more interconnected, but then that list will be a mile long. The connections I am able to draw always make me feel giddy. It&#8217;s as though my semester is one giant puzzle and every time I am able to make a link, it&#8217;s like I am one step closer to completing the puzzle.</p>
<p>Finding relationships between classes is something that you can really only find at a liberal arts college. Actually, it&#8217;s more than that; it&#8217;s the purpose of going to a liberal arts college. You are here to connect the dots between disciplines. You are here to discover that yes, Ancient Philosophy has some relation to Apocalyptic Literature, and yes, you can apply what you learn in Media, Theory and Methods to your Ancient Philosophy course as well&#8230; I&#8217;m still waiting on that one but it&#8217;s bound to happen before the semester ends&#8230;</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/just-one-of-those-nights/" rel="bookmark">Just one of those nights&#8230;</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/just-one-of-those-nights/" title="12:41 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 20, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/as246905/" title="View all posts by Ariel">Ariel</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Oh writers block, one of the all-too-well understood phenomena of college. I sit in the corner of the library, hoping that any minute now my brain will tell my hands to JUST WRITE SOMETHING! Wow, it’s moments like these when I realize how loud the clock on the wall is: tick tock TICK TOCK. Why am I noticing all of these new distractions in such a quite innocuous space? <em>Focus Ariel, it’s time to finish this paper!</em></p>
<p>I redirect my attention to my computer screen…still blank… except for the repetitive blinking of the pointer finger in Word. Blink blink blink. I gaze at the door, envious of those fortunate souls who are able to leave and go to bed. As my right eye begins to twitch with exhaustion, I realize that even my large cup of coffee will not be suffice in getting me through this assignment.</p>
<p>Maybe it’s time I stop writing this and get back to my paper. Thank you all for this very needed distraction.</p>
<p>‘Till next time,</p>
<p>Ariel</p>

			<div id="post-3320" class="post-3320 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/new-york-trip/" rel="bookmark">New York Trip</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/new-york-trip/" title="5:01 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 19, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>One of the major benefits of living in Allentown is that its regional location makes weekend trips to America&#8217;s most exciting cities, such as New York, Boston, Philadelphia,  etc. possible. This past Saturday, the art department organized a trip to visit some of the paramount museums in New York City. I decided to go to the Metropolitan Museum of Art, known to the locals as the &#8216;Met.&#8217;</p>
<p>Just walking up to the polarizing facade of the building is a specimen in itself. With pillars standing stories high carves with elaborate detail, I was immediately fixated. The Met sticks with a similar theme in NYC: amazingly large. As the biggest art museum in the country, the complex is over 2 million square feet and holds over 2 million works.</p>
<p>Of course, I could only see a small fraction of that in my time there, but nonetheless, it was highly rewarding. I ventured to see the European Sculpture and Decorative Arts section, Modern and Contemporary Art, and Medieval Art. All of which was fascinating in a unique way. To conclude the trip, I got some famous roasted nuts, an authentic bagel, and a delicious Starbucks coffee.</p>
<p>I am grateful for the trip and can&#8217;t wait to return to NYC!</p>
