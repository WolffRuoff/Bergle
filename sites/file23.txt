	<li><strong>Quotation marks to insist that search results contain a phrase</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>&quot;helen of troy&quot;</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>AND to insist that search results contain two or more terms</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>helen AND troy AND &quot;greek mythology&quot;</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>OR to allow the system to choose any of several terms</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>greek OR roman OR classical</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>NOT to exclude a term</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>helen NOT troy</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>Parentheses for complex searches with multiple operators</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>&quot;helen of troy&quot; AND (mythology OR legend)</li>

					</div>
							</div>
							<div id="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358401" class="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358401">
					<div id="s-lg-box-15572408-container" class="s-lib-box-container">
						<div id="s-lg-box-15572408" class="s-lib-box s-lib-box-std">
							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Too Few Search Results?  Try...
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-15572408" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-33972278" class="  clearfix">
				<ul>
	<li><strong>Breaking your topic down into its most important concepts.&nbsp;</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>Instead of searching all the words in your research question (e.g., <em>How does sleep deprivation affect college students&#39; learning?</em>), try using only the most important concepts (e.g., &quot;<em>sleep deprivation&quot;</em> and <em>&quot;college students&quot;</em> and <em>learning</em>).</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>Using broader search terms.</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>Instead of searching very specifically phrased search terms (e.g., <em>&quot;sleep deprivation&quot;</em>), try searching more general or foundational terms (e.g., <em>sleep</em>).</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>Adding synonyms for your search terms.</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>Instead of searching only one word for your concept (e.g., <em>learning</em>), try searching multiple words that express the concept you are researching (e.g., <em>learning </em>or <em>achievement&nbsp;</em>or <em>education</em>).</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>Using fewer search terms.&nbsp;</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>Instead of searching every concept in your research question (e.g., &quot;<em>sleep deprivation&quot;</em> and <em>&quot;college students&quot;</em> and <em>learning</em>), try removing some of the less important or more transferrable concepts (e.g., &quot;<em>sleep deprivation&quot;</em> and&nbsp;<em>learning</em>).</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>Adding variations of search terms.</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>Instead of searching only one variation of a word (e.g., <em>learning</em>), try using truncation symbols to find variations of the words (e.g., <em>learn*</em> will search for learn, learning, learned, etc.). (Note: most databases and catalogs recognize * as the truncation symbol, although a few use another character, such as !.)</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>Using a different search tool.</strong>
	<ul>
		<li>Instead of searching only one database or catalog, try a variety of search tools.&nbsp; <a href="http://libraryguides.muhlenberg.edu/welcome" target="_blank">Subject Guides</a> point you toward recommended resources in each subject area.</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
</ul>

		   </div>
								</div>
								
							</div>
						</div>
					</div>
							</div>
							<div id="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358402" class="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358402">
					<div id="s-lg-box-15572410-container" class="s-lib-box-container">
						<div id="s-lg-box-15572410" class="s-lib-box s-lib-box-std">
							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Too Many Search Results?  Try...
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-15572410" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-33972280" class="  clearfix">
				<ul class="bullet">
<li><b>Narrowing your topic</b>.</li>
<ul>
<li>Instead of researching a very broad topic (e.g., <em>role of the military in the Middle East</em>), try narrowing to a more specific angle (e.g., <em>role of the military in Syrian politics</em>).&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<li><b>Leaving out synonyms</b>.</li>
<ul>
<li>Instead of searching multiple words for the concept you are researching (e.g., <em>politics</em> or <em>"political parties"</em> or <em>government</em>), try searching the most specific, accurate term (e.g., <em>"political parties"</em>).</li>
</ul>
<li><b>Using subject headings or descriptors</b>.</li>
<ul>
<li>Instead of doing keyword searching, try searching with subject headings, also known as descriptors.&nbsp; Many databases and catalogs provide special terminology to more accurately and consistently describe topics.&nbsp; Using that terminology in the right way can find better results (e.g., a database may use the subject <em>"armed forces"</em> instead of <em>military</em>.)</li>
</ul>
<li><strong>Filtering your search results.</strong></li>
<ul>
<li>Instead of sifting through all the results your search turned up, try filtering your results by publication type (e.g., peer-reviewed journal articles), by date (e.g., only items published in the last 10 years), or by language (e.g., only items in English) to narrow to the most relevant information.</li>
</ul>
</ul>
		   </div>
