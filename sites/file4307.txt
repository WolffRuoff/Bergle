			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/the-joust-aka-the-craziest-and-most-overwhelming-sporting-event-of-my-life/" rel="bookmark">The Joust (aka the craziest and most overwhelming sporting event of my life)</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/the-joust-aka-the-craziest-and-most-overwhelming-sporting-event-of-my-life/" title="11:21 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 9, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/zoebriggs/" title="View all posts by Zoë Briggs">Zoë Briggs</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>When I think of a joust, I think of Medieval times, men in tights, and giant knights who ride on massive horses towards a stationary object at crazy fast speeds.</p>
<p>Well, I was right about everything, except that this is happening today, in modern times, right around the corner from where I&#8217;m living; I saw a joust in my lifetime and it was as real as it gets! The entire day was a grand event, starting with the parade and the blessing of the knights. A bunch of us were trying to get some philosophy homework done in the park (believe me, homework is ten times better when you&#8217;re surrounded by Tuscany, breathing in the fresh air and the yummy smells of pizza) when the sound of a cannon jolted us all from our reading. All of a sudden, hundreds of men in tights carrying flags with beautiful bright colors started to march past the duomo (the big church in town) leading the way to the sight of the joust. I haven&#8217;t been to a parade in ages, and I felt just like a six-year-old shouting and yelling at the procession of music and men.</p>
<div id="attachment_2977" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/paradeselfie.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2977" class="size-medium wp-image-2977" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/paradeselfie-300x168.jpg" alt="Parade selfie" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/paradeselfie-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/paradeselfie-1024x576.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2977" class="wp-caption-text">Parade selfie</p></div>
<div id="attachment_2979" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/paradepic.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2979" class="size-medium wp-image-2979" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/paradepic-300x168.jpg" alt="Men with the flags of the city" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/paradepic-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/paradepic-1024x576.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2979" class="wp-caption-text">Men with the flags of the city</p></div>
<p>A bunch of us wanted to get to the joust sight early because we had standing room tickets and wanted to get as close to the action as possible. We managed to get ourselves to the gate and immediately were overcome by masses of young Italians screaming at each other different team chants and smack talk.  I can&#8217;t even begin to describe how enthusiastic and loud these kids were.  They had so many chants and songs to scream at each other trashing the other team or supporting their own.  Jordan and I started to yell with them, having no idea what we were saying, but we managed to get a few words out.</p>
<div id="attachment_2976" style="width: 178px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Jordan.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2976" class="size-medium wp-image-2976" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Jordan-168x300.jpg" alt="Jordan with his red and green scarf to support our quad!" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Jordan-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Jordan-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2976" class="wp-caption-text">Jordan with his red and green scarf to support our quad!</p></div>
<p>Once we got into the joust sight we were transported to another world.  In the middle of the piazza there was a runway set up for the horses to run down at incredible speeds straight at the Saracen (a target that has a bullseye of sorts on one side and cattails on the other).   The piazza had beautiful tall buildings on all sides, and there were people leaning out windows yelling for their respective knights and towards the crowd beneath.</p>
<p>The joust works on a point system.  In order for a knight to win they have to hit closest to the center of the target.  1 point is furthest away and 5 points is the center.  Each quad has two knights that have one run and the highest combined total wins.  It was so cool to watch the knights charge up the hill towards the target and then smack it with their lance as it swung round and round.  I personally had know idea what was going on because A. everything was in Italian and B. I am a fairly short human being, but I could tell whether it was good or bad based on the reaction of the people surrounding me in our cheering section.  After the first two knights went, each scoring 4 points, it was our knights turn.  The crowd went wild supporting him but as he was heading towards the target he dropped his lance and was disqualified from the round.  I am pretty sure that some of the fans around me cried because they were so upset.  Because we lost all of our points from the first round it was pretty clear that we were out of contention for the prize and the mood of the crowd quickly shifted from excitement, to anger, to dead silent.  It was so sad!  At the end of the joust <span style="color: #252525;">Porta del Borgo (the blue and yellow quad) was declared the victor and the cannons sounded one more time.  </span></p>
<p>It wasn&#8217;t the best outcome but I still had an awesome time at the joust.  It was really cool to see that what I think I&#8217;ll only ever get to read about in history books is still happening today and that cities like Arezzo are keeping these traditions alive.  I loved cheering with the crowds and yelling what little Italian I knew, such as &#8220;Dai&#8221; (come on) and &#8220;Bravo&#8221; (good job) and I would definitely do it all over again.  If you want to see some parts of the joust such as the flag twirlers and the winning run by the Porta del Borgo knight then check out my YouTube videos:</p>
<p align="center"><iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/0GOVenc0tew?list=UUq46x3wghM21Ut9dCk4fh5w" width="560" height="315" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen"></iframe></p>
<p>Until next time, Ciao!!</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/theres-no-place-like-arezzo-italy/" title="11:20 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 9, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/zoebriggs/" title="View all posts by Zoë Briggs">Zoë Briggs</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<div id="attachment_2952" style="width: 235px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/photo-5-e1410014178505.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2952" class="size-medium wp-image-2952" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/photo-5-e1410014178505-225x300.jpg" alt="Gelato, aka my new boyfriend" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/photo-5-e1410014178505-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/photo-5-e1410014178505.jpg 240w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2952" class="wp-caption-text">Gelato, aka my new boyfriend</p></div>

<p>I cannot believe that in just five days I have eaten the most amazing food, met the 28 other beautiful people in our program, met Monica and Genevieve (they are basically our parents while we are here and they are the absolute best!), explored Arezzo, seen a practice for a joust, eaten so much gelato, gone to the antiques fair, performed in an open mic, and tried and failed multiple times to speak in Italian (hopefully that gets better soon).</p>
<p>There is just so much going on here, it is a little overwhelming, but I am not complaining. We arrived on Tuesday and took our bus to the villa, which is gorgeous (room tour on the way) and got settled in. I am rooming with my friend Emily from &#8216;Berg, and we already feel at home in our beautiful, bright, and giant room. The light here is incredible, especially during the sunrise and sunset. We all got to see our first Italian sunset during our first dinner with everyone and I&#8217;m pretty sure a couple of us cried.</p>
<p>Speaking of dinner the food is FANTASTIC!!!!!!!! I have never had such amazing pasta and fresh mozzarella, but I think my favorite so far definitely has to be the spinach (I know &#8220;gross spinach&#8221; but trust me it&#8217;s better than any spinach you have ever had in your entire life…EVER).</p>
<p>I feel so spoiled to be here. This morning I was walking around Arezzo with my friend Ryan, and we went to an Antiques fair. It was located in one of the parks in town, and there must have been hundreds of tents and stands where artists were selling their work, or families were selling hundreds of years old furniture that was just breathtaking. I even bought a painting from a local artist. There is so much history here no matter where you look, and it is overwhelming.</p>
<p>We are going to have our first big adventure tomorrow (Sunday) when we go into town and see the annual joust (yes a joust). Every year a knight from each quad of the city competes in a jousting contest and it is a BIG DEAL. Every night each area has been having their own events to promote their knights and get the city excited for the competition. In one of the areas they had trapeze, live bands, and a dance party. Arezzo does not joke when it comes to the joust. I will make sure to record as much as possible and hopefully my area (I don&#8217;t know the name, just that our colors are red and green) wins!! Until next time, Ciao!</p>

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Well today is the day! We somehow squished mine, Alyssa’s, and Ryan’s luggage into my tiny little car and headed to JFK for our flight to Dusseldorf Germany and then Florence! We left my house around 10:45 am and got to the airport by 12:15…five hours before our flight but it as well worth it in our minds because we were some of the first in our terminal and security was (for probably the first and last time ever) extremely quick and easy. We had to wait a little bit before boarding the plane but eventually we made it! Alyssa and I have been discussing in depth what our first legal drinks (because in Italy the legal age is 18) should be, we are leaning towards the sparkling white wine (YUM!) I don’t think that it has actually hit me that I am going to be in Italy for four months. I have never been away from home for such a long period of time but cannot wait to see what the experience brings. I am already exhausted and it is only twenty minutes into the flight but I still can’t seem to go to sleep because of the excitement.<br />
Well Hello Germany! We are currently sitting in the Dusseldorf airport waiting for our connecting flight to Florence. Poor Alyssa had to go through her luggage for the security guard and get searched but we made it! I can’t help but to hum songs from The Sound Of Music…obviously my knowledge of Germany is extremely well rounded (still so far nobody has yelled at me…yet). We have a three hour lay over and then we are Italy bound! I cannot wait to meet all the other people in the program and meet all the faculty the alumni talk about constantly back at &#8216;Berg. In just a few hours I will be landing in Florence Italy and living there for the entire semester. It is CRAZY!<br />
After 21 hours of traveling, two different planes, and three different countries we have made it to Florence! I am currently sitting on the NICEST BUS EVER, waiting to drive through Tuscany and finally see our villa that we will be living in for the next four months. Until next time, Ciao!</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/introduction-2/" title="3:46 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 5, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

For any Freshman or seniors in high school, it isn&#8217;t a lie when everyone tells you that it is the fastest four years of your life. I do think I am ready for the working world, but for now I am going to enjoy every moment of my senior year.</p>
