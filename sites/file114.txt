				<p>Three classification systems (taxonomies) of not-for-profits</p>

<p>I. IRS Sub-Sections</p>

<p>Here is a list of nonprofits by <a href="http://learn.guidestar.org/help/irs-subsection-codes" target="_blank">IRS subsection code</a> that are included in the NTEE classification system, explained further <a href="http://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/p557.pdf" target="_blank">here</a>.&nbsp;</p>

<p>II. NTEE Broad Categories and Major Groups</p>

<p>The <a href="http://nccs.urban.org" target="_blank">National Center for Charitable Statistics</a> web site describes the National Taxonomy of Exempt Entities or NTEE, a classification for nonprofit organizations recognized as tax exempt under the Internal Revenue Code.</p>

<p>Here is an <a href="https://nccs.urban.org/project/irs-activity-codes" target="_blank">introduction </a>to the concept of NTEE classification, listing the 10 &quot;broad categories&quot; that make up this classification, and the 26 &quot;major groups&quot; that fall within these categories. &nbsp;For a complete manual with in-depth description, examine the <a href="https://nccs.urban.org/publication/irs-activity-codes" target="_blank">NTEE-CC Manual</a>. NOTE: HERE IS AN <a href="https://www.yumpu.com/en/document/read/2142086/ntee-manual-national-center-for-charitable-statistics-urban-" target="_blank">ALTERNATE VERSION OF THE NTEE-CC MANUAL</a> THAT GIVES BETTER DESCRIPTIONS.</p>

<p style="padding-left: 30px;">Perhaps the single best directory of <i>U.S.</i> nonprofits on the web, <a href="http://www.guidestar.org/" target="_blank">GuideStar</a> (http://www.guidestar.org/) (password access only) offers basic directory information plus financial statements, mission and programs, goals and results, and leaders.</p>
