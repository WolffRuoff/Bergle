			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/01/starting-school-again/" rel="bookmark">Starting school again!</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/01/starting-school-again/" title="2:35 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 15, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>So here I am! The same spot I was in just 5 months ago, but this time I feel like a different person. As second semester starts, I can feel the confidence and excitement pulse through my veins. When I began classes at the beginning of last semester, I had no idea what to expect.</p>
<p>How would the classes be?<br />
Could I handle the academics?<br />
Would I be able to succeed?<br />
And, would I be happy?</p>
<p>These were important questions that used to linger in my mind, though now I have resounding answers to each of them. Last semester I figured out what I needed to do to accomplish my goals. This semester will be about reapplying those skills to build on the progress I have made thus far.</p>
<p>After concluding the first week of classes, I couldn&#8217;t be happier with the diversity and cool factor of each class. I am taking Politics of the Middle East, Microeconomics, Principles of Journalism, and a religion class called Meaning of Life. Muhlenberg has made me excited about learning. Taking notes and asking questions is a treat, not a burden. As I embark on part 2 of 8 in this journey, I look forward to sharing my perspective at least once a week!</p>
<p>Until next time,<br />
Jake</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/01/last-semester/" title="2:59 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 12, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I can&#8217;t believe it is here, the last semester of my college life. All the different times I had been told to enjoy every second of college because it moves so fast really does make sense now.</p>
<p>I remember coming to school almost 4 years ago as a freshman, not knowing anyone. I had no idea what to expect, or how things would shape out for me while I was here. It is amazing to look back and think about everything I have accomplished and all the friends I have made.</p>
<p>For now, I am trying to enjoy everything that this final semester has to offer. I don&#8217;t want to rush anything, because I know these are the times I am going to wish I could have back. There will be friends I&#8217;ll see once I graduate, but there will also be plenty of people I just won&#8217;t see anymore. It is sad to think about, but it&#8217;s the truth, people are going to be headed in different directions.</p>
<p>Don&#8217;t get me wrong I am excited for the great things coming after graduation, but this is the time I just won&#8217;t be able to get back. I must say, Muhlenberg has helped me accomplish more than I ever could have expected.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/01/back-in-new-york/" rel="bookmark">Back in New York</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/01/back-in-new-york/" title="3:15 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 10, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/as246905/" title="View all posts by Ariel">Ariel</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>After ten beautiful days in Florida, my family and I finally made it back to cold and snowy New York.</p>
<div id="attachment_3728" style="width: 215px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment.aspx_.jpeg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3728" class=" wp-image-3728" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment.aspx_-225x300.jpeg" alt="Florida" width="205" height="272" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment.aspx_-225x300.jpeg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment.aspx_-768x1024.jpeg 768w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment.aspx_.jpeg 1536w" sizes="(max-width: 205px) 100vw, 205px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3728" class="wp-caption-text">Florida</p></div>

<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment-2.aspx_.jpeg"><img class="alignnone  wp-image-3731" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment-2.aspx_-300x300.jpeg" alt="get-attachment-2.aspx" width="239" height="239" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment-2.aspx_-300x300.jpeg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment-2.aspx_-150x150.jpeg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment-2.aspx_-1024x1024.jpeg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2015/01/get-attachment-2.aspx_.jpeg 2048w" sizes="(max-width: 239px) 100vw, 239px" /></a></p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/01/closing-out-break/" rel="bookmark">Closing Out Break</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/01/closing-out-break/" title="9:46 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 8, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Winter break is now coming to an end and it is almost time to get my last college semester started (a later post will touch on my thoughts for the last semester).</p>
<p>For starters, I want to spend the end of break relaxing as much as I can. With school and baseball all going on it can be tough to find time to just sit back and relax. This is why I am taking plenty of time to lay around on the couch and watch as much TV as possible. Today I watched The Interview movie with Seth Rogan and James Franco, funny movie it&#8217;s worth seeing.</p>
<p>On Friday, we will be seeing one side of the family for my Grandpa&#8217;s 85th birthday. I can&#8217;t believe that, 85 is a great milestone and many more great birthday&#8217;s still to come. On Saturday, we will be going into New York City and seeing the other side of my family for my cousin&#8217;s engagement party. He just proposed to his girlfriend this past summer and it will be an exciting night to have everyone together to celebrate.</p>
<p>Sunday, I will be heading back to school and it will be time to get back into the swing of things.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/01/dream-to-read/" rel="bookmark">Dream To Read</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2015/01/dream-to-read/" title="6:26 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 8, 2015</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/swedberg/" title="View all posts by Drew">Drew</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Today marks a week since I&#8217;ve been back at school. We&#8217;ve played two games since returning here, going 1-1 and picking up a good conference win against McDaniel last night. We are now in the midst of some tough conference play, and with only 5 teams making the post-season tournament every game counts.</p>
<p>Even with all the focus on basketball this past week, we have found time as a team to enjoy ourselves, including our annual event we attended today (Thursday) at Jefferson Elementary School! This happens to be the same school that I work in twice a week, and after missing the kids for three weeks we were finally reunited! Not only that but I got to watch my teammates interact with the first and fifth graders, which was a treat as they enjoyed the day as much as I did!</p>
<p>We started the day reading to the kids about Martin Luther King Jr. Brandon, Tina, Abby, and I read the book and then had the kids ask any questions before we worked on placemats to give to Meals-On-Wheels that had drawings of the kids working together in a classroom (although most of them drew Brandon dunking the basketball!)</p>
<p>It was a fantastic day to say the least and I cannot wait for this semester to start so that I can work with the kids again!</p>
<p>In the meantime, here are some pictures to warm up your day!</p>
