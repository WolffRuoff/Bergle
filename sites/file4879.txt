<h2>Kestenberg Movement Profile Trainings2018</h2>
<p style="font-weight: 400">Instructor: Suzanne Hastie, MA, BC-DMT, NCC, LPC, KMP Analyst</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400">The Kestenberg Movement Profile (KMP) is a systematic and in-depth movement assessment tool based entirely on the nonverbal. The KMP was developed by Dr. Judith Kestenberg (psychiatrist and psychoanalyst) and the Sands Point Movement Study Group; it offers dance/movement therapists, clinicians, educators, childcare providers, parents &amp; those interested in nonverbal behavior a refined language as well as a psychological interpretation for what is seen in the body.</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong>Courses &amp; Workshops offer</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Alternate Route or Continuing Education as well as foundational studies in the KMP allowing one to pursue KMP Certification</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Increased understanding of the nonverbal throughout the lifespan</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Small class size; space &amp; time to move; individual attention &amp; group experientials</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Clinical applications</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400">Learning in a beautiful, tranquil setting in Pennsylvania</li>
</ul>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong>Courses:</strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><em>(the 30 hour live courses have been approved by the American Dance Therapy Association as meeting requirements for the Alternate Route R-DMT credential. And, for practicing dance/movement therapists or childcare providers in Pennsylvania, 6 CE hours per workshop &amp; 30 CE hours per course are available)</em></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em></em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><u>July</u></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>Constructing Kestenberg Movement Profiles II: Tension Flow Attributes, Pre-efforts, Efforts, Shaping in Directions, and Shaping in Planes (2 credits/30 hours/30 CE hours)</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>July 9-12, 2018</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>Fee: $850</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400">In this 30 hour course, students and clinicians will focus on tension flow attributes (affect), pre-efforts (defenses and learning style), efforts (coping style and ego strength), shaping in directions (structure for defenses, boundaries, and learning), and shaping in planes (structure for complex relationships). Movement observation, labeling, notation, and diagram construction will be taught and practiced. Clinical understanding of these patterns will be deepened and further applications to clinical work will be explored. Construction of the TFA, PE, E, SD, and SP diagrams of the profile as well as an analysis of the findings will be required (for alternate route students and those pursuing KMP certification).</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><u></u></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><u>August</u></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>Introduction to the Kestenberg Movement Profile (2 credits/30 hours/30 CE hours)</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>August 20-23, 2018</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>Fee: $850</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400">In this 30 hour introductory course, participants will learn the basic layout of the KMP, the movement patterns contained within it, and the developmental and psychological significance of the patterns. Through experiential activities, discussion, and observational practice, students will come to understand the depth, wholeness, and vision the KMP has to offer therapists and educators in their work. Clinical applications will be integrated into the course.</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><u></u></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><u>September</u></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>Constructing Kestenberg Movement Profiles I: Tension Flow Rhythms, Bipolar Shape Flow &amp; Unipolar Shape Flow (2 credits/30 hours/30 CE hours)</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>September 17-20, 2018</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>Fee: $850</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em></em></strong>In this 30 hour course, students and clinicians will focus in-depth on tension flow rhythms (developmental issues &amp; personality), bipolar shape flow (comfort with self/environment), and unipolar shape flow (attraction/repulsion to stimuli in the environment and in relationships). Movement observation, tension flow writing, labeling, notation and diagram construction will be taught and practiced. Clinical understanding of these patterns will be deepened and further applications to clinical work will be explored. Construction of the TFR, BS, and US diagrams of the profile as well as analysis of the findings will be required (for alternate route students and those pursuing KMP certification).</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong>Location for KMP Workshop &amp; Courses</strong>: Twin Ponds Integrative Health Center, West Lehigh Valley, PA. For map, directions, &amp; accommodations, go to<a href="http://www.twinpondscenter.com/">www.twinpondscenter.com</a>. Twin Ponds is only 75 minutes from Philadelphia and 2 hours from New York City.</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>Online Studies</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400">Individualized online courses of study (via skype) are available to individuals and small groups.</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>Supervision/Consultation/Independent Studies</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400">Available upon request and tailored to your interest and needs. Supervision/consultation may be arranged in person or via phone or skype.</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>KMP Certification</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400">Completion of 90 hours of coursework, a completed profile and written analysis/interpretation are first steps in pursuing certification for teaching or research purposes. For specific information, go to<a href="http://www.kestenbergmovementprofile.org/">www.kestenbergmovementprofile.org</a>.</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>KMP Color Wheel Poster</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400">This poster, which measures 11x 17, is a concise, visual representation of the KMP. Through design and color, the wholeness and complexity of the profile is captured. Further, it is a practical reference tool that can be utilized by those learning the profile as well as those who already know it.Available for purchase via credit card or Paypal. Cost of poster is $16, plus shipping (which is $8 in US and $12 outside US). If paying via PayPal, send an email to<a href="mailto:suzannehastie@aol.com">suzannehastie@aol.com</a>with your mailing address. An additional fee of $1 will be added to international Paypal purchases due to PayPals currency exchange fees.</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em></em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em>To register for courses, arrange for supervision/consultation, or to purchase a KMP Color Wheel poster, contact Suzanne Hastie at</em></strong><a href="mailto:suzannehastie@aol.com"><strong><em>suzannehastie@aol.com</em></strong></a><strong><em>or (610) 417-0831.</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em></em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><strong><em></em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400"><em><strong>Suzanne Hastie, MA, BC-DMT, NCC, LPC, KMP Analyst,</strong></em><em>graduate of Antioch/New England Graduate School, studied the KMP with Penny Lewis &amp; Susan Loman, and interned with Dr. Judith Kestenberg in the late 1980s. She has co-taught the KMP with Janet Kestenberg Amighi at Drexel University as well as independently since 2000 and started teaching the KMP via skype in 2013. Suzanne is the creator of the KMP Color Wheel poster, a concise, visual representation of the KMP. She has been integrating the KMP into her work and life for 27 years.</em></p>

<p>Dance Place is seeking college students and recent graduates interested in learning how a thriving dance center operates from the inside out. <h1 class="entry-title">Audition Notice  Carolyn Dorfman Dance is Seeking Women</h1>
</div>
</div>
<div class="heading_line"></div>
<div class="entry-content">
<p>Carolyn Dorfman Dance is auditioning for our 2016-17 Touring Season, including creation of new work, performance and teaching residencies.</p>
<ul>
<li>Full-time, Part-time, and Apprentice Positions Available</li>
<li>Paid rehearsals, performance, and teaching</li>
<li>Non-union, 30+ week contract.</li>
<li>Rehearsals are held in NYC.</li>
<li>Legal work status required.</li>
</ul>
<p>Seeking female dancers with strong modern dance technique, bold athleticism and theatrical range. Women should be between 52-57 tall. Minimum 2 years previous professional experience preferred. Dancers called back will be asked to perform a short solo.</p>
<p>The Companys ten multi-ethnic and stunning dancers tap their unique talents to present high-energy and technically demanding dance that unleashes the powerful storytelling and imagery of its visionary creator.</p>
<div class="columns-wrapper">
<div class="two-columns"><strong>AUDITION (Day 1):</strong><br />
<strong> Thursday, May 12, 2016 </strong><br />
<strong> 12:30pm-2:30pm or</strong><br />
<strong> 3:30pm-5:30pm</strong><br />
Gina Gibney Dance Studios<br />
890 Broadway, Studio 1<br />
New York, NY 10003</div>
<div class="two-columns nomargin"><strong>AUDITION (Day 2):</strong><br />
<strong> Friday, May 13, 2016</strong><br />
<strong> Callbacks 12:30pm-5:00pm</strong></div>
<div class="two-columns nomargin">Gina Gibney Dance Studios<br />
890 Broadway, Studio 1<br />
New York, NY 10003</div>
</div>
<p><strong>Dancers seen by appointment only. For an audition appointment, email: <a href="mailto:scathey@carolyndorfman.dance" target="_blank" rel="noopener">scathey@carolyndorfman.dance</a></strong></p>
<p>(And provide the following: resume, headshot, email, phone, 1st &amp; 2nd audition time slot preference, U.S. work status, and how you heard about the audition.)</p>
