	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=708" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2017-11-07T10:35:25-04:00">November 7, 2017</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=708" rel="bookmark">Workday@Muhlenberg &#8211; What&#8217;s In It For Me?</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172356" rel="category">ERP</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=708#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<div>With Workday@Muhlenberg moving along at a rapid clip, it&#8217;s time to look at some of the specific changes that we&#8217;ll see. The real question is &#8211; <em>What&#8217;s In It For Me</em>?</div>
<div></div>
<div>In this blog post we&#8217;ll highlight changes that the Human Resource Module (Human Capital Management, or HCM) presents. In truth, the bulk of changes for the community will take place with the HCM implementation on January 1. We&#8217;ve highlighted several items below.</div>
<div></div>
<div>First, the terminology can be a bit confusing. Muhlenberg uses Manager to mean exempt, salaried individuals. Staff Associates are non-exempt, hourly employees. Workday, in contrast uses Manager only for those that supervise other staff. All other employees are called &#8220;Colleagues.&#8221; One&#8217;s &#8220;Supervisory Organization&#8221; is your organization chart, basically &#8211; the structure of whom you manage, who manages you, etc. Please bear this terminology in mind as we highlight some of the new features available to Muhlenberg.</div>
<div></div>
<div>Faculty and Staff (&#8220;Colleagues&#8221; in Workday):</div>
<ul>
<li>Ability to view benefit elections</li>
<li>Ability to request time off</li>
<li>View available vacation balances</li>
<li>Apply for internal openings</li>
<li>Refer others for open positions</li>
<li>Report life events (births, marriage, divorce) and make benefits changes accordingly (including adding new dependents to insurance coverage)</li>
<li>Submit W4 forms</li>
</ul>
<p>In addition, for Managers/Supervisors:</p>
<ul>
<li>Receive and approve vacation requests electronically</li>
<li>Easily view vacation/time off scheduled by those in their supervisory organization</li>
<li>Use the recruiting module to facilitate searches and track the progression of candidates through the process</li>
</ul>
<p>Coming soon:</p>
<ul>
<li>Non-exempt staff will no longer have to use paper timesheets and instead be able to report time electronically</li>
<li>Ability to review time submitted and approve time electronically</li>
</ul>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->

	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=697" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2017-09-29T14:07:50-04:00">September 29, 2017</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=697" rel="bookmark">Workday @ Muhlenberg &#8211; September Update</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172356" rel="category">ERP</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=erp" rel="tag">ERP</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=workday" rel="tag">Workday</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=697#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>This month&#8217;s update highlights the Workday modules we will be implementing.</p>
<p>Human Capital Management, or HCM, is the Human Resources component to Workday. It will be the first such system at Muhlenberg, and as such represents a major operational benefit to the College. Having all of the data in a the same place means a single master record. Also, because HCM runs the gamut from recruiting to time tracking to benefits, it&#8217;s a full lifecycle system, from &#8220;hire to retire.&#8221;</p>
<p>In addition, we are replacing Blackbaud Financial Edge with a new, comprehensive financial product: Workday Finance. Integrated fully with the HCM module, data will flow naturally from time entered in electronic time sheets to financial statements over to our legacy payroll system.</p>
<p>Starting March 2018, we will begin implementing Workday Payroll, with a tentative launch date of January 1, 2019. This will complete the implementation of the &#8220;Core ERP&#8221; components of Workday. Again, with Worday’s centralized system, the feed from HCM to Payroll to Finance is seamless and automated. There will not be a need for integrations or moving back and forth of data.</p>
<p>Training will be provided to Muhlenberg on Workday to help everyone gain familiarity with the system and how to use it. Stay tuned as these dates/times will be released in the coming monthly updates. As always, please feel free to share any feedback or questions.</p>

<article id="post-683" class="post post-683 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-erp">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=683" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2017-08-30T09:22:11-04:00">August 30, 2017</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=683" rel="bookmark">Benefits of Workday</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172356" rel="category">ERP</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=683#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>With something the size of Workday@Muhlenberg, it&#8217;s reasonable to ask why we are making this change. Some of it is certainly based on the need to replace the aging Capstone program. But Workday provides a number of other important benefits, as well. For instance, it is built in the cloud, from the ground up, and mobile-first. This means no hardware for us to manage on our end, better resiliency and disaster recovery capabilities, and newer design and architecture. It&#8217;s also a &#8220;single line of code&#8221; &#8211; all Workday customers run the same version of the software. We are always up to date, with all the latest features, and always with a clear path to the next upgrade. Nothing we&#8217;ll do will ever block us from upgrading, as might happen with significant customization. Most importantly, Workday provides us with a unified solution for HR, Finance and Payroll (and eventually Student) with a single source of data, pulled from a definitive system of record.</p>
<p>Workday is also part of our digital transformation journey. For example, our transition away from paper to digital document repositories is a major part of what Workday offers. We&#8217;ll also see improvements in how we submit expense reports, do time-tracking and entry, and approve various requests.</p>
<p>If you have any questions or concerns about this significant project, feel free to reach out either via <a href="mailto:allanchen@muhlenberg.edu">email</a> or phone. I and the rest of the Workday@Muhlenberg team are here to help, and to make this successful. And keep an eye out of the next update!</p>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->

</article><!-- #post-## -->

			
				
<article id="post-667" class="post post-667 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-erp">

	<header class="entry-header">

			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=667" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2017-08-01T12:57:14-04:00">August 1, 2017</time><time class="updated" datetime="2017-08-01T12:58:34-04:00">August 1, 2017</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=667" rel="bookmark">Workday@Muhlenberg, an Update</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172356" rel="category">ERP</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=667#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<div>We&#8217;ve been hard at work making Workday@Muhlenberg a reality. I want to directly thank the Treasurer&#8217;s Office, Controller&#8217;s Office, and Human Resources for all the effort that is making for a successful project.</div>
<div></div>
<div>We have been relatively quiet, as far as the overall campus goes. The work thus far has been intense and quite time-consuming, but it is also fairly localized and campus-wide announcements did not make sense. With 5 months to go until our initial phase is live, it&#8217;s time for us to embark on an additional phase of the project &#8211; communications and training.</div>
<div>
<p>As you know, Muhlenberg College underwent an Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) selection process that resulted in selecting <a href="http://www.workday.com/">Workday</a> as the best system for our institutional needs. Workday is a tool built in the cloud, from the ground up and on modern architecture. It offers a unified, user-friendly, administrative solution with one set of data. We began the implementation process in February, which will include the Human Capital Management (HCM), Finance, Payroll and Student modules.</p>
<p>When making this decision to go Workday, there were many different drivers. We do not currently have an HCM product, and Human Resources will benefit greatly from its addition. Finance replaces an existing product, but Workday adds unity between its data and the rest of the system. Payroll bridges HCM and Finance. Finally, Student replaces Capstone, an aging product in a space that requires modernization and new features.</p>
<p>HCM and Finance go live in January 2018. Payroll follows a year later, and Student is targeted for mid-2019.</p>
<p>With Workday, there is one version of the software which means less maintenance, less disruptive upgrades with greater reliability and security of data. It is also built mobile-first, which will greatly increase accessibility and productivity.</p>
</div>
<div>Fundamentally, this is a project about digital transformation. Whether it&#8217;s reducing paper usage, examining existing and exploring new business practices, or opening doors to new technologies down the road such as electronic signatures, going to a powerful and modern product such as Workday has the potential to change how we get work done at Muhlenberg in significant and positive ways. Yes, it takes effort to make these a reality, but I personally have faith in this community to succeed, and I truly believe that the college will be better because of Workday in the end.</div>
<div></div>
<div>Training will be a key point in the process. For example, all Managers will need to learn how to approve time sheets, and many staff will be trained on how to do expense reports directly in the system (both of which will be huge digital transformation and paper-saving efforts, to boot). OIT is mobilizing resources to address these needs, working in concert with Attain, our project and change management partner, to build an effective curriculum. We&#8217;ll also be taking advantage of the great, existing documentation from the <a href="https://community.workday.com/ak">Workday Adoption ToolKit</a>; we won&#8217;t have to reinvent the wheel on a number of topics.</div>
<div></div>
<div>Stay tuned for more information as we move forward, here on the OIT blog.</div>

<article id="post-613" class="post post-613 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-erp tag-erp tag-workday">

	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=613" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2017-04-05T13:47:25-04:00">April 5, 2017</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=613" rel="bookmark">Workday@Muhlenberg</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172356" rel="category">ERP</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=erp" rel="tag">ERP</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=workday" rel="tag">Workday</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=613#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>This is the first post of many detailing the Workday@Muhlenberg ERP implementation project. Please access our <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/president/initiatives/oit/workday/">Workday@Muhlenberg web page</a> for the absolute latest information, as well as some history to the project, such as <a href="http://mcerp.blogs.muhlenberg.edu">the blog used during the selection process</a>. Project updates will be listed on a weekly basis on the Workday@Muhlenberg site.</p>
<p>In January of this year, we selected Workday as our ERP solution. Already an industry leader for a number of business processes in higher education, Workday has been evolving a product for student information as well. Workday is a Software-as-a-Service solution, operating purely in the cloud in their data centers, on their servers.</p>
<p>There are two major parts to the product that will have a significant impact across campus.</p>
<p>First, Workday Core ERP provides functionality for Human Resources (Human Capital Management or HCM), Finance and Payroll. These will provide new processes and automation (HCM), replace existing systems (Finance and Payroll), and provide a unified approach to these operations. Users will be able to enter hours electronically (Time Tracking) which will feed the HR system which will in turn impact Payroll, all automatically. We are slated to go live with HCM, Finance and Payroll on January 1, 2018.</p>
<p>Second, Workday Student is a wholly-new product meant to replace one&#8217;s Student Information System. In our case, that&#8217;s Capstone. Muhlenberg will continue to use Slate for Admissions and Powerfaids for Financial Aid, as those are systems best tailored to our environment and needs.</p>
<p>There are several partners involved in this large project.</p>
<ul>
<li>Workday is fundamentally the developer and seller of the software itself. It also provides high level oversight (Deliver Assurance) and training.</li>
<li>Mercer CPSG is a consulting firm that is handling actual implementation. This involves meetings with different departments on campus (Subject Matter Experts or SMEs), building our ERP instance (tenant), and keeping the overall project on schedule. They have their own Project Manager and several experts in the various functional areas that interact with our SMEs.</li>
<li>Attain is our project management firm. We brought them on board because we did not have the PM expertise in-house. Two individuals have been assigned to our project, including a senior member of their project management practice.</li>
</ul>
<p>Attain works for us and represents Muhlenberg. Mercer works with us and should be considered a third-party. Workday is the software vendor.</p>
<p>It is obvious that a great deal of coordination is required to keep all of these parts moving in sync, and we are not immune to some growing pains. However, we are committed to not only meeting our deadlines but doing so in a manner that is collaborative and cooperative.</p>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->
