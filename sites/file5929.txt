			<p>Is it October already? Indeed it is, and the Mules teams begin the second half of their 2011 seasons on a weekend filled with home events.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-104" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>Volleyball vs. Dickinson (Saturday, 11:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules dig playing at home. In their last four matches at Memorial Hall, they defeated Albright and McDaniel and were very competitive with strong Moravian and Haverford teams. Now they’ll try to even their Centennial Conference record against 2-10 Dickinson.</p>

		<header class="entry-header">
						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/18/like-father-like-son/" rel="bookmark">Like Father, Like Son</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/18/like-father-like-son/" title="3:21 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-18T15:21:21-04:00">September 18, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>If Mark Bisbing remembers the game as if it was yesterday, there’s a good reason – the game yesterday was very similar.</p>
<p>In 1980, Bisbing was a freshman center on the Muhlenberg football team that pulled off a comeback for the ages. Playing their first home game of the season a week after winning at Franklin &amp; Marahall, the Mules trailed Johns Hopkins 38-14 entering the fourth quarter. They scored 27 points in the last 11 minutes, including the winning touchdown on an untimed down after time expired, for a 41-38 win.</p>
<p>Yesterday, Muhlenberg (in its home opener a week after winning at F&amp;M) again stunned a visiting team from Maryland by scoring 27 points in the fourth quarter. This time the victim was McDaniel, which took a 26-7 lead early in the second quarter and seemed to be in complete control, only to fall 34-26.</p>
<div id="attachment_170" style="width: 110px" class="wp-caption alignright"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/bisbinghs.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-170" class="size-full wp-image-170" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/bisbinghs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-170" class="wp-caption-text">Garrett Bisbing</p></div>
<p>Mark Bisbing’s son, Garrett Bisbing, is the current Mules’ starting right guard and blocked an extra point in yesterday’s game.</p>
<p>“You can play for many years without going through a game like that,” said Mark. “That I got to play in a game like that, and now have Garrett play in a game like that – that’s pretty neat.”</p>
<p>Muhlenberg has scored more than 27 points in a quarter on five occasions since the 1980 game, but not in the fourth quarter. The last comeback from more than two touchdowns came in 2007, when the Mules trailed Dickinson 17-0 before winning 29-24, but in that game they had closed the gap to 17-16 by halftime.</p>
<p>How do the two epic comebacks compare? The circumstances in the 1980 game made that comeback a little more improbable. Muhlenberg recovered two onside kicks during the <a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/bisbingmcd.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-171" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/bisbingmcd-246x300.jpg" alt="" width="246" height="300" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/bisbingmcd-246x300.jpg 246w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/bisbingmcd.jpg 315w" sizes="(max-width: 246px) 100vw, 246px" /></a>rally and got the benefit of two penalty calls on the final drive, one a personal foul after a fourth-down sack and the other a pass interference call in the end zone on the last play.</p>
<p>“I’ve heard that story a few times,” said Garrett, a junior. “I have a friend who goes to Hopkins. He was over at my house this summer and my dad was telling us about the 1980 game. Jim Margraff, the Hopkins coach, was their quarterback then.”</p>
<p>On the other hand, yesterday’s comeback seemed to come out of nowhere.</p>
<p>“Yesterday’s comeback stunned me because of the sheer way we stunk up the joint in the first three quarters,” said Mark, who was the third-string center in 1980 but got to play the whole fourth quarter after the two players ahead of him suffered injuries. “I didn’t think we had it in us.”</p>
<p>“I was trying to stay positive, thinking, we just have to score,” said Garrett. “Everything about it was just unbelievable.”</p>
<p>Mark watched the game from the stands with his mother  and a teammate from the 1980 team. As the comeback unfolded, both turned to him at various times, independently of each other, and said “this is just like Hopkins in 1980.”</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_1161.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-172" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_1161.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="291" /></a>The 1980 football team was the last coached by Frank Marino, and this was the first home football game for Muhlenberg since his passing in January. It was also the first football game at Scotty Wood Stadium since “Frank Marino Field” was added to the scoreboard.</p>
<p>“Frank would have been going nuts,” said Mark. “He would have loved it.”</p>
<p>If it happened twice, it could happen a third time, right? So if it’s the year 2042, and you’re on a visiting team that has a big lead on Muhlenberg after three quarters, and if the Mules have a lineman named Bisbing … let’s just say you can plan on a long bus ride home.</p>

						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/14/ncaa-stats/" rel="bookmark">NCAA stats</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/14/ncaa-stats/" title="2:01 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-14T14:01:37-04:00">September 14, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>There’s a new way to keep close tabs on not just the Muhlenberg teams, but all Division III teams in football, soccer and field hockey.</p>
<p>Starting this week, box scores for all Division III contests in those sports will be available on the NCAA website. The NCAA has been doing this with Division I football for several years and started with Division III last year. Soccer and field hockey were added this year, with volleyball to come in the future.</p>
<p>Here are the links:</p>
<p>Football: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/mfb/mainpage.jsp?year=2011">http://web1.ncaa.org/mfb/mainpage.jsp?year=2011</a></p>
<p>Men’s soccer: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=MSO">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=MSO</a></p>
<p>Women’s soccer: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WSO">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WSO</a></p>
<p>Field hockey: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WFH">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WFH</a></p>
<p>The football page is set up a little differently, but for the other three, click on “Click here for box scores and complete team and individual statistics.” Although it is listed on the Division I line, you can get to all three divisions from there. Select III from the drop-down menu where I appears, and you get a list of all schools that play the sport. Click on any school, and you can see their links to cumulative stats and game boxes. Click on a player name in the roster to see season and career stats.</p>
<p>If you want to check up on upcoming opponents, this is an alternative to going to their school websites. And if nothing else, it&#8217;s a great way to kill some time poring over stats.</p>
<p>The NCAA continues to provide weekly rankings in these sports, as well as volleyball (<a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WVB">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WVB</a>). Although it is way too early to put any stock in national statistical rankings, it is interesting to note that the Muhlenberg football team is 28<sup>th</sup> in Division III in rushing defense after two games, and Joe Rollins of McDaniel, who will be visiting Scotty Wood Stadium on Saturday, is the second-leading rusher in the division.</p>

	<article id="post-125" class="post-125 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-football">
		<header class="entry-header">
						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/13/records-are-made-to-be-broken/" rel="bookmark">Records Are Made to Be Broken</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/13/records-are-made-to-be-broken/" title="1:07 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-13T13:07:52-04:00">September 13, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>Jim Skidmore was sitting at home in Sea Girt, N.J., on Saturday when he received a series of mysterious phone calls.</p>
<p>“They were all saying ‘the record is broken,’ and I didn’t know what they were talking about!” he said</p>
<p>What they were talking about was Dan Deighan’s 95-yard touchdown pass to Isaiah Vaughn to seal the Muhlenberg football team’s 34-13 win at Franklin &amp; Marshall. It was the longest pass play in Mule history, eclipsing the 90-yard pass Skidmore threw to Gene Reed against Albright in 1951.</p>
<p>(<a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/vaughn95td.wmv">watch video of the Isaiah Vaughn touchdown</a>)</p>
<p>Although that game was nearly 60 years ago, Skidmore remembers the play very well.</p>
<p>“The first two passes I threw that day were for touchdowns – one for each team,” he recalled. “The first pass was in the right flat. The ball got tipped and a guy on the other</p>
<div id="attachment_127" style="width: 194px" class="wp-caption alignright"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/skidmore-old.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-127" class="size-medium wp-image-127" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/skidmore-old-184x300.jpg" alt="" width="184" height="300" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/skidmore-old-184x300.jpg 184w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/skidmore-old.jpg 350w" sizes="(max-width: 184px) 100vw, 184px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-127" class="wp-caption-text">Jim Skidmore then ...</p></div>
<p>team ran it back.”</p>
<p>Skidmore was a sophomore then in his first year on the varsity after helping the 1950 freshman team to an undefeated season. Freshmen were not eligible to play varsity in those days.</p>
<p>“Here I was, the underclassman. And when I got back into the huddle, all the upperclassmen told me not to worry about it. So I called a pass.”</p>
<p>The play was called “flood left.” Muhlenberg had two receivers line up to the left, with Reed split out to the right. According to the game report, Reed, who passed away earlier this year, caught the ball at the 22 and took off to the end zone with a single Albright defender in futile pursuit the whole way.</p>
<p>“The irony is that because of the encouragement you’re given by others, you try again immediately,” said Skidmore. “I do believe that the lessons we got on those athletic fields were tremendous foundations for life.”</p>
<p>It is a life that has taken Skidmore many interesting places. He played football for the Marine Corps after graduating from Muhlenberg and had professional opportunities with the Packers and Giants, but he decided to “go make a living.” Now 78, he is the chairman</p>
<div id="attachment_128" style="width: 251px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/SKIDMORE.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-128" class="size-medium wp-image-128" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/SKIDMORE-241x300.jpg" alt="" width="241" height="349" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-128" class="wp-caption-text">... and now</p></div>
<p>of Science Management, an international professional services business. He serves on the boards of 13 public companies and is a member of Muhlenberg’s Board of Trustees. He was appointed by Muhlenberg president Randy Helm to the Fitness and Wellness Committee.</p>
<p>“I really care a lot about the school,” he said.</p>
<p>Sure, but how does he feel about getting knocked out of the record book after all these years?</p>
<p>“I think you realize that anytime you set some kind of record, you know it’s going to be broken. I’m elated for Dan Deighan. I think it’s terrific.”</p>
