	<div class="entry-details">
				<p>November 4, 2016<br/>
		by Rosie Martin<br/>

	</div><!-- end entry-details -->
    
	<header class="entry-header">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://fys142f16.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/11/04/digital-natives-vs-digital-immigrants/" title="Permalink to Digital Natives vs Digital Immigrants" rel="bookmark">Digital Natives vs Digital Immigrants</a></h2>
	</header><!-- end entry-header -->
        
	<div class="entry-content">
					<p>Digital Natives  was coined by Mark Prensky in 2001 (Vodanovich, Sudaram, Myers, 2010). “It is more specifically defined as those who have grown up in a  world where the use of information and communications technology is pervasive, ubiquitous and where information and communication technologies are used in an organizational and non organizational context” (Vodanovich, Sudaram, Myers, 2010). I have always found the topic of digital natives to be incredibly fascinating. The reason for this is because last year I started babysitting three boys that lived next door to me. I remember thinking to myself the task was going to be incredibly difficult because of the level of activity required to babysit three little boys. To my surprise it was actually incredibly easy. All they wanted to do was play on their I-pads. When I was younger I was constantly outside and I am considered to be a digital native. Now with the younger generation they are even more involved with technology than I was. In a way I found it sad that because their lives are so immersed with technology they may never understand the things that I came to love in my childhood. This leads me to think what are some of the things I missed out on in my childhood.</p>
<p>“Digital immigrants were not born into the digital world; rather they have learnt to engage with it in their adult lives” (Vodanovich, Sudaram, Myers, 2010). The major difference between the two is that digital natives were moulded by technology from a very young age. Digital Immigrants were people who later in life were immersed in the technology culture (Vodanovich, Sudaram, Myers, 2010). The skill set of the two can be completely different because of this. For example most digital immigrants are more comfortable with face to face communication or talking on the phone with someone then digital natives are (Vodanovich, Sudaram, Myers, 2010). This means that it is fair to assume that digital immigrants have better social skills than digital natives. Digital natives however are seen as more tech savvy then most adults. For example my five year old cousin is a lot better at using an I-pad than my Dad will ever be. I also found out from personal experience that digital immigrants seem to be more satisfied with the technology they have rather than yearning for the next best thing. My brother, sister and I always want the new phone coming out. However, my Mom and Dad still have the I-phone four and do not want to get a new upgrade. I think this goes back to the way technology has moulded us. We grew up in a generation were technology was constantly evolving and the next best thing was always considered the best. On the contrary my parents grew up without the same technology and do not value its importance as much as we do.</p>
<p>Vodanovich, S., Sundaram, D., &amp; Myers, M. (2010). Research Commentary: Digital Natives and Ubiquitous Information Systems. <i>Information Systems Research,</i> <i>21</i>(4), 711-723. Retrieved from http://www.jstor.org.muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/stable/23015639</p>

					<div class="control-box-wrapper">
<p>            The proliferation of new technology has brought to the national forefront a new form of bullying. Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying is a more insidious, prevalent and difficult to trace form of abuse and the number of cyberbullying incidents seem to be growing in epidemic proportions. With the burgeoning advancements in technology, people now have the ability to instantaneously communicate with others through various technological mediums&#8211;internet, text messaging, social networks, and video games. Consequently, this provides multiple forums for disseminating information and an outlet for hurling anonymous insults and harassing comments. Is there not a way to combat cyberbullying? Everywhere she turned she was confronted with images of swastikas taunting her about her Jewish heritage and death threats all because she was appointed to this incredibly prestigious position.</p>
<p>The mental and emotionally consequences suffered by countless numbers of cyberbullying victims are resulting long lasting effects. In today’s age technology allows a tormentor to go where bullies where afraid to go in the past. A crisis exists and it needs to be stopped.  If nothing is done soon, increasingly more and more people will be directly affected by this everyday creating a culture where you are forced to conceal your true feelings for fear of public scrutiny, ridicule and humiliation.</p>

	</header><!-- end entry-header -->
        
	<div class="entry-content">
					<p>Do you remember when people would talk face to face, make plans by asking another person if they had plans over the weekend, and interact in ways that did not include digital technology? Personally I do not remember, because I am a digital native and was essentially born with an iPhone in my right hand. Growing up in a society where technology was constantly changing, it was easy for me to adapt to the latest form of technology because I was surrounded by transforming technology, and in order to survive and do well in the society I was growing up in, I had to get used to the use of digital electronics. At a young age, I had learned to speak with people and feel comfortable interacting with others in face-to-face circumstances, but with the development of social media, email, instant messaging and texting, I knew that it was not going to be long before technology took over the world of communication.</p>
<p>I started using social media as a way to communicate with my peers in middle school. Since nobody had a cell phone, we would use email to send each other instant messages, mapping out our weekend plans, and where we would go after school. Not long after that, my friends and I would text each other, as it was easy to send a quick message and then go back to what you were doing. Before I could realize, the way that I communicated with others was permanently changed. I felt myself constantly checking my cell phone for any new text messages, repeatedly checking Facebook to see if any of my friends had posted a new status update. “Let’s use our imagination a bit. If the internet, mobile devices, and games were taken away from us, how would we feel? I know many of us would feel like a part of our life is missing due to technology now being a necessity in our everyday life.” When I did not have my phone on me, or I was not using a source of social media, I felt naked, disconnected, and as if I was missing out, and still feel that way today when I do not carry around my cell phone with me.</p>
<p>I have recognized over time that technology has taken away valuable communication skills from me, and that it is more difficult to hold a conversation with somebody for an extended period of time without checking my phone for new notifications. I also find myself preferring to make plans over text, rather than face-to-face communications. I have also realized that it is more difficult for myself and others to be as social and outgoing as we can potentially be because we are too dependent upon technology. Moving forward I plan on depending less on technology not because I am too dependent, but because it is affecting me as an individual and holding me back from reaching my full potential as a person.</p>

They are nothing but a great waste of time, money and energy.However, in my humble opinion, it is meaningless to debate whether or not computer game is detrimental,since computer game itself is neither good nor bad; it is totally at our disposal.Being an indispensable side-product of IT industry, computer game has been burning its way into the fabric of social life. Obviously, the appliance of three dimensions and special sound effects has turned the plain gaming process into a visual and audio feast that we have never been to before. In addition, the brand new experience of playing another role in games is so vicarious and gratifying that when playing, people tend to immerse themselves thoroughly into the virtual world. It can definitely be a good entertainment and recreation, as long as it is taken in moderation. Computer-gaming binge is absolutely dangerous, but in that case, it is not the games to blame.<br />
Although many people doubt the advantages of computer games, if used wisely computer games can actually be used in educational purposes, for example typing games to allow students to learn typing English or Chinese. Also another advantage of computer game is that the games may be played together among friends or family members, which may improve the relationship between family members and allow parents to know more about their children on their hobbies and interests.<br />
However computer games if misused can also have very bad effects on human beings. Firstly spending long time in front of the computer screen will bring to various health problems, such as eye sore, back pain and headache. Also if people spend too much time on computer games rather than the work they should do, for example school work for students and projects and presentations for employees, it may have bad effects on their real life and social life status.<br />
It is true for everything that it is beneficial if used wisely, therefore although computer games are widely said to be bad for people, it can still be used as a good tool if used wisely.</p>
