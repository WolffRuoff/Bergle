			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/muhlenrehearsals-and-sickness/" rel="bookmark">Muhlenrehearsals and sickness&#8230;</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/muhlenrehearsals-and-sickness/" title="8:51 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 13, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hey everybody! It&#8217;s been another eventful week here for me. My life pretty much consists of classes, rehearsals, and homework&#8230;with some fun of course!</p>
<p>I don&#8217;t have much to report on this week, because unfortunately, I was sick&#8230;but I did have my first visit to the campus health center! I was expecting much more of a typical school nurse type of place, but it operates a lot like any other doctor&#8217;s office. You enter, sign in, wait for the nurse practitioner, then you go back and tell the nurse what&#8217;s going on, and then the doctor comes and says what they&#8217;re gonna do! I was very impressed with the professionalism. I was prescribed some antibiotics for my &#8220;muhlenplague&#8221; (some sort of cough/cold/sinus/thing), and I&#8217;m happy to say that I&#8217;m feeling much better (although there is a lingering cough).</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5418.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2468" alt="IMG_5418" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5418-225x300.jpg" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5418-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5418-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>(the examination room, featuring my water bottle)</p>
<p>The production I&#8217;m in, Sinternet, is going very well! Today we ran through the entire show for the first time. It&#8217;s hard to believe that we&#8217;ve only had 10 days of rehearsal, and harder still to know that we open in 3 weeks! I was just fitted for my costume today. When I was sick, I sat our of a rehearsal, so I do have a picture for you!</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5421.jpg"><img alt="IMG_5421" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/IMG_5421-225x300.jpg" width="225" height="300" /></a></p>
<p>For anyone who doesn&#8217;t know, Sinternet is about when a teacher, frustrated that his students would rather pay attention to their laptops than his lectures, makes a deal with Satan to destroy the internet. But that&#8217;s all the summary you get, no spoilers! The music is really incredible and even student written! It&#8217;s a very fun, creative show to work on with a truly talented cast.</p>
<p>Well, that&#8217;s pretty much it! I&#8217;m loving Muhlenberg and I just turned in my first real paper today, and have my first quiz tomorrow! I can&#8217;t believe it. I really feel like a verified college student, as if I wasn&#8217;t already!</p>
<p>Till next time,</p>
<p>Emily</p>

			<div id="post-2457" class="post-2457 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/purified-protein-pleasure-politics/" rel="bookmark">Purified Protein Pleasure Politics</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/purified-protein-pleasure-politics/" title="8:35 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 12, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hey guys. Just a quick update today.Â It&#8217;s currently 3 in the morning here in Allentown, but I&#8217;m wide awake so I figured I&#8217;d put out a post. It&#8217;s probably a better idea than doing a problem set or working on my research at this hour (not that I don&#8217;t also do those in the middle of the night all too often).</p>
<p>First order of business is the center for ethics kick-off event. The CFE is a faculty-run center which organizes campus-wide discussions, talks, and other events concerning a given theme. This year the theme is Sex Ethics and Pleasure Politics. Prior to the event, which was a panel of faculty and students who were meant to open up the discussion on these issues, the senior Dana Scholars were invited to a dinner with the panelists to hear what they had to say and offer our insights. The center pairs nicely with the senior Dana forum which is a class on the same basic topic as the center for ethics which culminates in novel research projects.</p>
<p>The dinner was lovely, but the event is what was truly interesting. There was much debate and discussion. Many thought-provoking data were gathered from the audience ourselves, who were able to participate anonymously in the discussion via text-messages displayed as polls on the projection screens. I think a lot of us were honestly surprised by the total number of students and faculty in attendance. There must have been several hundred people gathered into Miller Forum to hear the panel consisting of the college&#8217;s president, a student rep, an athletic director, a multicultural director and an ethicist and psychologist.</p>
<div style="width: 970px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img alt="" src="https://sphotos-b-lga.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-frc1/1236071_10202049204170491_1613909657_n.jpg" width="960" height="244" /><p class="wp-caption-text">Apparently Ginelle, my fellow blogger, is in this picture somewhere making a face. What can I say? She caught me bloggin&#8217;</p></div>
<p>The event went on for an hour and a half and included student protests, fierce debates and a very open and exciting beginning to this series of talks and events. I am definitely looking forward to being an active part of the CFE programming this semester and next as the Dana Forum class continues to interface with big questions concerning the admittedly broad and intimidating topic.</p>
<p>Live In Color (Muhlenberg&#8217;s show choir), along with the a capella groups on campus has been holding auditions and callbacks all week. We finally have a list of a few star students we want in our group, but the question remains&#8230;who else wants them? It will be an exciting next couple of days before final cast lists go up, that&#8217;s for sure.</p>
<p>On a side note, you all should be ready for at least a few froggy pics on their way soon. The lab just got some new amphibian members. I&#8217;m pushing for &#8220;Icarus, Jose, and Special K&#8221; to be their names but my co-worker insists that they are not girly enough. (In all fairness we do only work with female frogs).</p>
<p>Lastly, today in my Experimental Biochemistry class we had a lot of ups, downs, and waits. It seemed as though the science gods were not smiling on us today. Lots seemed to go wrong, but thanks to the expert guidance of our fearless leader/professor, we were able to salvage the protein we were attempting to purify out of E.Coli. It was quite like actual lab work, in that we had to think on our feet and deal with problems as they arose. The positives to all that are that 1)We were still able to solve the problems and get our protein and 2) All the wait time and backpedaling allowed me to get some shots of my classmates in all their protein purifying glory.</p>
<div style="width: 730px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img alt="" src="https://sphotos-a-lga.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-ash3/1235157_10202049208570601_1364513361_n.jpg" width="720" height="960" /><p class="wp-caption-text">Kelly loves uncharacterized protein. No seriously. LOVES IT.</p></div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div style="width: 730px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img alt="" src="https://sphotos-a-lga.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-ash3/1235291_10202049206250543_1318913013_n.jpg" width="720" height="960" /><p class="wp-caption-text">Kat and Cyrus are busy recording their data in their electronic lab notebooks, a new method of data collection and recording being tested out by our class.</p></div>
<p>Lastly, I was recently announced as tour guide of the month, so you can be sure that my tours are seriously top-knotch. That being said, I&#8217;m giving tours on Friday and Monday at 10am, so if you&#8217;re interested, come by at those times and ask for me! (Seriously, everyone else would just be a let-down(not really, all of the tour guides are pretty amazing(they learned from the best(that&#8217;s me))))</p>
<p>I&#8217;ll keep you guys up to date with all of the aspects of my hectic life, from live in color and gospel choir to touring, research, class, applying to grad schools, the center for ethics and of course my ongoing crusade to dress up our statue of Henry Melchior Muhlenberg as spiderman in a sombrero. Until then, stay constantly curious about my life to a questionably awkward extent!</p>

			<div id="post-2447" class="post-2447 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/tutoring/" rel="bookmark">Tutoring</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/tutoring/" title="8:34 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 12, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/DSC00011-e1378689694766.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2446" alt="DSC00011" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/DSC00011-e1378689694766-168x300.jpg" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/DSC00011-e1378689694766-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/DSC00011-e1378689694766-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a></p>
<p>This binder represents one of the most useful tools that Muhlenberg has to offer, tutoring. Every tutor at this school receives this binder on the day that they go to train and become a tutor. I have been on both sides of this process as a tutor and a tutee, and it is well worth doing. It is a great step to take for any student that is struggling in a class, needs extra help, or wants to simply make themselves feel more confident in what they are doing.</p>
<p>Some people think that getting a tutor means they aren&#8217;t as smart as other people. This is not true and it is important to remember that everyone learns differently. Some students can learn things right away, and others need to go over things a lot of times before it makes sense to them. When this is the case it helps that much more to have someone there that already did well in the class. This way they can be there to steer you in the right direction.</p>
<p>Here are the easy steps to follow if you are interested in getting a tutor:</p>
<p>1. Start by talking to your teacher first. This is not a necessity, but teachers usually like to hear about this kind of thing.</p>
<p>2. Go to the Academic Resource Center in the basement of Seegers.</p>
<p>3. Ask the reception desk for the tutoring papers (they will tell you what you need to fill out and bring back to them).</p>
<p>4. Wait to hear the name of your tutor.</p>
<p>5. Get in touch with the tutor and figure out a time to meet.</p>
<p>6. This last one is more of a helpful tip. Find a place that you feel most comfortable in and meet at that place at the same time every week. By doing this you will get into a good rhythm and it will help you out in the long run.</p>
