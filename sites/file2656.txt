<h5>About the West End Alliance</h5>
<p>The West End Alliance is a non-profit organization whose purpose is to stimulate revitalization in the West End Theatre District of Allentown by envisioning and catalyzing positive change among residential, commercial, governmental and institutional entities. The organization seeks to promote the development of the West End of Allentown as a local residential neighborhood center by enhancing, diversifying and supporting the businesses and services to provide a walkable, pedestrian-friendly community atmosphere. Building on the area’s culturally rich and ethnically diverse composition, the West End Alliance strives to create a welcoming mecca for residents, college students, entrepreneurs and artisans.  The West End Alliance is comprised of a volunteer board, committee members and a part-time employee, the neighborhood coordinator.</p>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p>Internships at the National Book Foundation</p>
<p>The National Book Foundation, presenter of the National Book Awards, welcomes applications from individuals seeking internships at the center of an established and active literary nonprofit. Interns assist with a variety of tasks and programs, including the National Book Awards, 5 Under 35, BookUp, the Innovations in Reading Prize, as well as administrative, marketing and social media projects.</p>
<p>Internships at the National Book Foundation include a robust introduction to the world of literary non-profits, with exposure to aspects of programming, development, community engagement and operations. Our small and dedicated staff ensures that every intern receives the mentorship and experience necessary to progress in the arts or publishing industry.</p>
<p>We are especially interested in hiring a Marketing &amp; Communications intern, who can dedicate their experience to our growing social media platforms. A background in marketing and/or social media is preferred.</p>
<p>Our internships operate on a seasonal calendar, and require a minimum of 15 hours a week. Applications for internships are due one month before the start of the season applied for.</p>
<p>Fall Internships: September &#8211; December<br />
Spring Internships: January &#8211; May</p>
<p>Summer Internships: May &#8211; August<br />
General responsibilities include, but are not limited to:</p>
<p>Assisting with National Book Award entries and procedures</p>
<p>Helping with the coordination of National Book Week events and related materials</p>
<p>Receiving and sorting National Book Award Ceremony RSVPs and related correspondence</p>
<p>Supporting the Educational Manager by maintaining records and orders for BookUp materials</p>
<p>Contributing to the National Book Foundation’s social media outlets, as well as content creation for the Foundation’s website and Tumblr</p>
<p>Editing of press releases and marketing materials</p>
<p>Assisting at events outside working hours, including the National Book Awards ceremony</p>
<p>Qualifications: We are looking for a responsible self-starter who is hard-working, detail-oriented, organized, and able to multi-task. Excellent oral and written communication skills required. Having a passion for reading and literature is a must.</p>
<p>Compensation: This is an unpaid internship, though a local travel stipend is available. National Book Foundation internships are also eligible for college credit.</p>
<p>To Apply for An Internship: Send resume, cover letter, and a list of ten favorite contemporary books to jobs@nationalbook.org with the subject line Internship as well as the season applying for.</p>
<p>The National Book Foundation is committed to inclusivity and does not discriminate on the basis of race, religion, ethnicity, age, gender identity, sexual orientation, socio-economic status, nationality, marital status, parental status, military service, or disability.</p>

<p>The San Francisco Bay Area Program provides students with academic, experiential learning and professional experiences in new media technologies, film, art, and ecology that are unique to the San Francisco Bay area. Internships are available in Berkeley and San Francisco.</p>

<p><a href="http://www.movingimage.us/about/internships#SUMMER" target="_blank">http://www.movingimage.us/about/internships#SUMMER</a></p>
<p><strong>SUMMER 2015</strong><br />
June 8 – August 14</p>
<p>The Museum offers full-time, paid summer internships. Under the supervision of department heads, interns have the opportunity to learn about the full range of Museum operations. Undergraduates entering their senior year in fall 2015, college graduates, and graduate students are eligible to apply.</p>
<p>(Additional full-time paid summer internships in <a title="DIGITAL" href="http://www.artsintern.org/momi---marketing-and-publicity-intern.html">Digital Media</a> and <a title="MARKETING" href="http://www.artsintern.org/momi---marketing-and-publicity-intern.html">Marketing and Publicity</a> are available through the Arts Intern program. <p>The Museum’s offices are open Monday through Friday, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.</p>
<p><strong>Collection Curatorial</strong>: Intern will assist with researching a new, major acquisition in the Museum’s collection, and with general research support for recent and pending acquisitions.</p>
<p><strong>Development</strong>: Intern will assist with the administrative needs of the development and membership department, including but not limited to research, data entry, and mailings, as well as event planning.</p>
<p><strong>Film</strong>: Intern will assist in research, preparation, and implementation of film screenings and discussion programs, and with various web projects and publications. Intern will also assist with research for installations and exhibitions.</p>
<p><strong>Institutional Archive</strong>: Intern will assist with a comprehensive inventory of the Institutional Archive, which contains documents, photographs, brochures, and products created by the Museum throughout its 30 year history.</p>
<p>To apply, complete the online application form at <a title="APPFORM" href="https://docs.google.com/a/movingimage.us/forms/d/17DZmGT8xPJIA_NhdVWQzB7UVFwkaYWLgsP-Rmnug0HE/viewform?formkey=dDhBNG5SUXhkZjhGWlFoUzFaMzZrcHc6MQ">movingimage.us/intern2015</a>, then email the following documents to <a title="INTERNSHIPSEMAIL" href="mailto:internships@movingimage.us" rel="external">internships@movingimage.us</a>:</p>
<p>A <strong>single PDF</strong> that includes: 1) a cover letter indicating your department of interest and 2) your resume. One letter of recommendation from a current or past professor or employer, which may be submitted with the rest of your materials or separately.</p>
<p>Applications must be received no later than March 30.</p>
<p>Finalists will be interviewed in person or by phone. Successful applicants will be notified no later than May 6.</p>
<p>The Museum of the Moving Image is an equal opportunity employer and encourages qualified candidates from all backgrounds to apply</p>

		<p><b>Arts Management Internship Opportunity<br />
Mt. Gretna (Harrisburg/Lancaster/Hershey), PA</b><br />
Gretna Music, south-central Pennsylvania’s premier presenter of world-class concerts, is offering full-time and/or part-time internships in arts management for summer 2015.  The successful candidate(s) will have the opportunity to work with Gretna Music’s management in customer service, public relations, promotions, corporate and community relations, artist relations, and marketing.  Gretna Music will be presenting 10-15 classical music, jazz and other concerts in its 40th Summer Festival in 2015 in Mt. Gretna, PA.  Concerts are presented from June through September.</p>
<p>A sample list of responsibilities could include order processing, writing press releases and other promotional materials, artist hospitality, researching and writing program notes, box office, and general office support.  We will tailor the final job description to fit the successful applicant’s interests and skills.  This position is particularly suited for students with interest in arts management, business administration, writing, communications, public relations, marketing and /or music.</p>
<p>Applicants must be at least 18 years of age, and should have good interpersonal, writing, research, and design skills.  An ability to learn quickly and apply existing knowledge and experience to new situations is especially valued.  Your own housing, reliable transportation and willingness to work weekends are required.  A stipend of up to $2,000 is included (based on a full-time internship).  The internship runs from May 26 through August 14, plus weekends through September 13.</p>
<p>To apply, send a resume, writing sample, and three references to Carl Kane at kanec@etown.edu.  Application deadline is March 15.  For further information, call 717-361-1508.</p>
<p>About Mt. Gretna:  situated just 20 minutes from Harrisburg, Hershey and Lancaster, Mount Gretna is a 100+ year old summer resort community offering a broad range of summertime recreation and cultural activities.  The region is a popular summertime tourist destination, with easy access to the Washington DC, Philadelphia, and New York areas.  Nearby attractions include Hershey Park and Pennsylvania Dutch country.  Gretna Music’s administrative offices are located in Elizabethtown, PA, approximately 20 minutes from Mt. Gretna.  Very reasonable living costs and friendly people will make for a special summer experience.</p>

	    <div class="post-meta">
		
			<span class="post-date"><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=1144" title="iDreamMachine production, writing, and outreach">January 6, 2015</a></span>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<div style="color: #000000">
<div>
<p>iDreamMachine is seeking interns to work on one of three teams; outreach, production, and writing. The out reach team will be responsible to help grow the audience for the project through a myriad of channels. The production team will assist in preparation for the production and in production, and the writing team will help write the web series and social media story-line.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>What will the interns walk away with? The opportunity to work with one of the pioneers of the transmedia movement, the knowledge base of how productions will be produced in the future, and an industry credit for work done on the project.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The story for <i>The Great WTYT 960 Billboard Sitting Contest </i>will be told across multiple story telling vehicles; a web series, feature film and play. The web series and play is about the four contestants survival on the billboard. The movie is about the radio station&#8217;s survival. The web series and movie is slated to go into production in August and the play will be mounted at the Bucks County Playhouse in October then NYC in November. Check out <i><a href="http://wtyt960.com/" target="_blank">http://wtyt960.com</a></i> to get a feel for the project.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
</div>
<p>Zeke Zelker a Lehigh Valley based filmmaker is the founder of iDreamMachine a multi-facetd entertainment company that produces projects that provokes thoughts, stir emotions and <span class="Apple-style-span">inspires ideas. </span>Zeke has a number of films to his credit, (<i>InSearchOf, Loggerheads, Southern Belles)</i> is in the midst of producing a cross platform, multi-media extravaganza <i>The Great WTYT 960 Billboard Sitting Contest</i>, about a radio station who hosts a billboard sitting contest. Zeke has been on the bleeding edge of the transmedia movement where instead of simply creating a linear storyline, he creates story worlds.  He produced the first transmedia project at the Sundance Film Festival, <i>Pandemic</i> and most recently produced part of David Cronenberg’s <i>Body Mind Change</i>.  Zeke is the creator of Lehigh Valley Art Wars &amp; Art Spark, live events that get artists out of the studio, enabling them to earn money and gain exposure.</p>
</div>
<div style="color: #000000">
<p><span class="Apple-style-span"> </span></p>
</div>
<div style="color: #000000">
<p>Deadline for spring internships is Jan. 20 and for summer internships May 1. Please send a resume and letter of interest which should include some background information on what makes you unique, along with some of your experiences. If you&#8217;re interested in writing for the production, send a 5 page script sample or a 1 page short story.</p>
</div>
<div style="color: #000000">
<p>&nbsp;</p>
</div>
<div style="color: #000000">
<p>Please send info to <a href="mailto:info@wtyt960.com" target="_blank">info@wtyt960.com</a> and put &#8220;intern&#8221; in subject line.</p>
</div>
