		<h1>What is Zotero?</h1>
<p><a href="http://www.zotero.org">Zotero</a> (<a href="http://www.zotero.org">http://www.zotero.org</a>) is a free and easy-to-use tool that allows you to collect, manage, and cite research and other sources. It can either be used as a Firefox add-on or as a standalone client on your computer.</p>
<h1>Zotero How To</h1>
<p>Zotero provides excellent documentation to help you get started installing and using it.  Refer to the &#8216;Documentation&#8217; section of their website and review the pages that may benefit you in understanding how to use Zotero effectively.</p>
<p>Of particular help to get started, refer to:</p>
<p><a href="https://www.zotero.org/support/quick_start_guide">The Quick Start Guide</a><br />
<a href="https://www.zotero.org/support/screencast_tutorials">Screencast Tutorials</a></p>
<h1>Digital Activity</h1>
<p>Now that you&#8217;re familiar with Zotero and what it can do, you can install it for your own use, save a source for Thursday&#8217;s class to Zotero, and then post that source on the blog. Be sure to assign a category of &#8216;Digital Activity&#8217; as well as tags.</p>
