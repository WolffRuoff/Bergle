And why should they when the DVR makes it so easy to avoid them?  But the Turner Company, a cable unit owned by the media conglomerate Time Warner, is developing a new strategy to save commercials from falling to their death (ouch!).  The company has undergone a four year effort to create a new kind of advertising strategy that will offer advertisers on the cable networks TBX, TNT, and True something that many others do not: a strong link between the commercial and the TV show that the consumers are viewing. 				<p>Fernandez, Jay A. (2011, April 14). Roundtable: the writers behind summer&#8217;s biggest blockbusters (exclusive video). <em>The Hollywood Reporter. </em>Retrieved from <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/risky-business/roundtable-writers-summers-biggest-blockbusters-177491">http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/risky-business/roundtable-writers-summers-biggest-blockbusters-177491</a><a href="http://mediaindustries11.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/04/2558435938_dc8097fd79.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-1106" src="http://mediaindustries11.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/04/2558435938_dc8097fd79-300x260.jpg" alt="" width="184" height="111" /></a></p>
<p>With the summer fast approaching, the pressure on Hollywood screenwriters is beginning to mount. Summer blockbusters or &#8220;tentpoles&#8221;, while not necessarily containing the same topics or plotlines, tend to share a lot of the same elements. Much like the season itself, summer blockbusters are designed by filmmakers and marketed by studios to &#8220;make you cheer, make you gasp, and make you laugh,&#8221; according to Ehren Kruger, screenwriter of <em>Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen, </em>in a Hollywood Reporter roundtable discussion with Hollywood screenwriters. These blockbusters are not typically designed to capture the attention of Oscar voters; instead, studios pour countless amounts of money into these films&#8217; budgets in order to regain profits and more. &#8220;These movies are greenlit now based on pre-visualized animated sequences that are the action sequences or moments that are really designed to wow the audience or stand out in a trailer,&#8221; said Kruger. Kruger&#8217;s quote exemplifies how summer blockbusters are designed first and foremost to appeal to a widespread audience. In order to accomplish this, studios look at past moneymakers and formulas that tend to appeal to the masses, and often design these summer blockbusters with these precedents in mind. Fundamentally, the studios are out to maximize profits when making these blockbusters. The main objective isn&#8217;t always necessarily making a film with quality of the highest caliber. Instead, money is what makes the industries continue churning out these massively-budgeted films year after year.<a class="more-link button" href="http://mediaindustries11.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/04/14/summer-blockbusters-cross-promotions-happy-meal/">Read More</a></p>

				<p>The Associated Press (4, April 2011). Pandora Discloses a Privacy-Related U.S. Inquiry On Apps. <em>The New York Times.</em> Retrieved from <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/04/05/technology/05pandora.html?_r=1&amp;scp=1&amp;sq=pandora%20radio&amp;st=cse">http://www.nytimes.com/2011/04/05/technology/05pandora.html?_r=1&amp;scp=1&amp;sq=pandora%20radio&amp;st=cse</a></p>
<p>Cecillia Kang. (8, April 2011). Web firms face increased federal scrutiny over Internet privacy. <em>The Washington Post. </em>Retrieved from <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/business/economy/web-firms-face-increased-federal-scrutiny-over-internet-privacy/2011/04/06/AFvREp1C_story.html">http://www.washingtonpost.com/business/economy/web-firms-face-increased-federal-scrutiny-over-internet-privacy/2011/04/06/AFvREp1C_story.html</a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Internet radio, such as Pandora, used with linking pin power roles such as the smartphone mobile industry, was recently subpoenaed by the government on the terms of illegal distribution of personal information.  When a user logs into an internet related radio site on their smartphone, their private information is given to that certain company to be distributed to their client power role, the advertisers.  Using Pandora, (although other internet related radio websites have been included in this court hearing) a federal grand jury served this subpoena in order to justify that these services disclose users personal information such as telephone number, gender, personal interests, and other information, illegally.</p>
