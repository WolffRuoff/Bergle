		<div class="info">
			<span class="date">January 26th, 2010</span>
			<span class="author"><a href="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/ltaub/" title="Posts by Lora Taub-Pervizpour" rel="author">Lora Taub-Pervizpour</a></span>						<span class="comments"><span>Comments off</span></span>

			<p><span style="font-family: Lucida Grande;font-size: small"> </span></p>
<h2 style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt"><span style="font-family: Lucida Grande;font-size: small"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-273" src="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/01/ishot-4.jpg" alt="ishot-4" width="251" height="258" /></span><strong>The 7th Annual LVAIC Social Research Social Justice  Conference<br />
April 8-9, 2010<br />
Muhlenberg College</strong></h2>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt"><strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">The Social Research Social Justice Conference provides students within the Lehigh Valley a forum for sharing innovative, excellent research addressing some of the</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">most challenging issues of social justice in both contemporary and historical contexts.  Now in its seventh consecutive year, SRSJ provides important opportunities for students from campuses across the Lehigh Valley to come together in critical conversations about the social justice issues that matter to them most. We encourage collaborative exploration among students, faculty, and community members of the role of academic research and creative work in the creation of more just and human societies.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">We encourage the submission of proposals to present research papers, poster sessions, media, arts and cultural productions, performances, and exhibits dealing with all aspects of social life &#8211; institutions, communities, movements, identities -and connected to issues of social justice. The conference has an interdisciplinary focus, with panels converging student work from varying disciplines and areas, such as women and gender studies, history, political science, media and communication, sociology and anthropology, education, religion, theater, art, African-American studies, public health, nursing, environmental studies, international affairs, psychology and other pertinent fields that explore social justice.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify">
<p style="margin-bottom: 0pt;margin-top: 0pt;text-align: justify"><span style="color: #ff0000"><strong>Deadline</strong></span>: Students interested in presenting their work should submit an abstract of no more than <span style="color: #ff0000"><strong>250 words by February 1, 2010</strong></span>. Your abstract must explain how your work connects to social justice. Make sure to include your name, institutional affiliation, faculty mentor, email address, and a title of the work to be presented at the conference. You may submit abstracts electronically to srsj@muhlenberg.edu, by fax at 484-664-3993, or by hard copy to co-directors Susan Kahlenberg or Kate Ranieri in the Department of Media and Communication at Muhlenberg College, 2400 Chew Street, Allentown, PA 18104. Questions may be directed to: Susan Kahlenberg (484) 664-3483 or Kate Ranieri (484) 664-3484 or by e-mail to srsj@muhlenberg.edu.<span style="font-family: Lucida Grande;font-size: small"> </span></p>

		<h2><a class="title" href="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2009/12/07/health-communication-students-take-community-action/" rel="bookmark">Health Communication Students Take Community Action</a></h2>
		<div class="info">
			<span class="date">December 7th, 2009</span>
			<span class="author"><a href="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/ltaub/" title="Posts by Lora Taub-Pervizpour" rel="author">Lora Taub-Pervizpour</a></span>						<span class="comments"><span>Comments off</span></span>
			<div class="fixed"></div>
		</div>
		<div class="content">
			<p>Students in Dr. Kahlenberg&#8217;s Health Communication course implemented two public information campaigns to raise awareness of social smoking and addictive smoking, as part of the Great American Smokeout.</p>
<p>Students hosted a smoke free event at the Tavern on Liberty so that students would contemplate healthy sociability.  The Tavern offered drink and food specials, and students hosted a raffle with donated prizes from area businesses; raffle tickets could be purchased or attained through donation of smoking paraphernalia. Funds raised will be donated to the local chapter of the American Cancer Society.</p>
<p>Addicted smokers were featured, literally, in the first annual Health Communication College Student Fashion Show, which showcased the physical effects that smoking has on the body and appearance.  Special effects make up was used to age the models as they progressed through life as a smoker.  The campaigns objective was to raise awareness of the short term health effects, and carbon monoxide testing and treatment options available at Muhlenberg College were provided, along with a screening of the feature film, Thank you for Smoking.</p>
<p>The campaigns were conceptualized, designed, and implemented by Health Communication students, guided by social marketing principles, theories, and methodologies examined throughout the semester.</p>

		<h2><a class="title" href="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2009/11/17/public-talk-tonight-on-networked-progressive-media/" rel="bookmark">Public Talk TONIGHT on Networked Progressive Media</a></h2>
		<div class="info">
			<span class="date">November 17th, 2009</span>
			<span class="author"><a href="http://mediacom.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jpooley/" title="Posts by jpooley" rel="author">jpooley</a></span>						<span class="comments"><span>Comments off</span></span>

		<div class="content">
			<p style="clear: both">Reminder: The department&#8217;s annual Visiting Honors Lecture is TONIGHT! Jessica Clark, director of the Future of Public Media project at American University’s <a href="http://www.centerforsocialmedia.org/">Center for Social Media</a>, will deliver a public talk on networked progressive media: </p>
<p style="clear: both"><strong>&#8220;Beyond the Echo Chamber: Reshaping Politics Through Networked Progressive Media”</strong></p>
<ul style="clear: both">
<li>Miller Forum, <a href="http://wapp.verizon.net/bookmarks/bmredir.asp?region=all&amp;bw=dsl&amp;cd=7.0unattached&amp;bm=ho_central">Moyer Hall</a></li>
<li>7pm</li>
<li>Reception to follow</li>
<li>Free and open to the public</li>
</ul>
<p style="clear: both">In less than a decade, a new breed of progressive media projects—from the Huffington Post to Air America to Brave New Films—have captured huge, nontraditional audiences, and helped to shape public debate and policy discussion. In “Beyond the Echo Chamber,” Clark discusses the political potential of social networks, new video platforms and citizen media. </p>
<p style="clear: both">Clark directs the <a href="http://www.centerforsocialmedia.org/">Center for Social Media</a>’s Future of Public Media project at American University. She is the author, with Tracy Van Slyke, of the forthcoming <a href="http://wapp.verizon.net/bookmarks/bmredir.asp?region=all&amp;bw=dsl&amp;cd=7.0unattached&amp;bm=ho_central"><em>Beyond the Echo Chamber</em></a> (New Press, 2010). Clark has been researching, creating, writing and fighting for independent media for more than a decade, at outlets and organizations like <em>In These Times, LiP Magazine, </em>The Media Consortium and the Independent Press Association. She blogs at <a href="http://www.buildtheecho.net/">www.buildtheecho.net</a>. </p>
