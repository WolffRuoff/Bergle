				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/10/back-in-black-2/" title="10:35 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 17, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Sorry for that haitus I&#8217;ve been subjecting you folks to. I&#8217;m sure you were hanging on the edge of your seats, awaiting my next installment. For this I apologize. I feel rather like George RR Martin in this moment, with millions of fans waiting to drink in my next words.</p>
<p>In any case, stuff has been happening in my life. You know, people have been saying things, events have been happening, places have been visited. In the most recent past, I went to see Civic Theatre&#8217;s production of Spring Awakening last night. Civic is a local theater just a few blocks from Berg, and I know a lot of the cast members. It was really an amazing experience. It has been too long since I&#8217;ve been in or seen theatre, and given that I attend Muhlenberg, there&#8217;s really very little excuse for that.</p>
<p>On a similar note, I&#8217;ve been cast in one of Muhlenberg&#8217;s Red Door productions, a show called Scuba Lessons. I know I have barely enough time for my work and extracurriculars as it is, but I really was itching to get back on the stage, so look forward to some updates on that as the rehearsal period begins.</p>
<p>I really ought to update more often because otherwise I run into awkward moments like this when I have to try to take stock of my last week or two and really see what was important about them that you guys might want to hear about.</p>
<p>As a partial remedy, I&#8217;ve decided to offer a bit of advice to all of you out there who are considering colleges and starting to figure out where your applications are going out to. I would suggest that above all, the most important part of deciding on a college that you really love is the atmosphere. I&#8217;m not talking about how ambient the lighting of the campus is, but rather the feeling of being there on campus as someone who is part of campus, rather than just visiting. Take a moment after tours and info sessions (which you should totally still do, by the way) and just stand on campus and look around. See if the students are the kinds of people you feel like you could be around for the next four years, imagine yourself as a student on that campus. 9 times out of 10, your gut feeling is going to tell you whether or not the school is right for you. The rest is logistics (not that money, location, majors, and prestige are negligible!).</p>
<p>And as another part of remedying the awkward gap between what I can fit into a blog post and what has been going on for me in the past few weeks, I&#8217;ll leave you guys with a few snapshots, and let my phone&#8217;s camera roll tell some stories.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/10/the-happiest-5k/" rel="bookmark">The Happiest 5K</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/10/the-happiest-5k/" title="10:32 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 17, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/ginellewolfe/" title="View all posts by Ginelle">Ginelle</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Last weekend, I did the Color Run! It&#8217;s a 5K that I have been wanting to do for a while now, so when I found out that there was going to be one in Allentown that actually ran through campus, I signed up as soon as I could. Basically, it&#8217;s a 5K where you get a different colored powder sprayed at you at each checkpoint. At the end, everyone gets color packets and have a celebration party! &#8230;So everything just ends up looking like one big tie-dye project.</p>
<div id="attachment_2538" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/1382099_10151700530158907_156910384_n.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2538" class="size-medium wp-image-2538" alt="The Before Picture: taken at the pink checkpoint" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/1382099_10151700530158907_156910384_n-300x200.jpg" width="300" height="200" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/1382099_10151700530158907_156910384_n-300x200.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/1382099_10151700530158907_156910384_n.jpg 960w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2538" class="wp-caption-text">The Before Picture: taken at the pink checkpoint</p></div>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/10/fall-break/" rel="bookmark">Fall Break</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/10/fall-break/" title="1:46 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 10, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>It&#8217;s finally that time of the school year where we have our first break. It is the first time that most people will be going home since the summer, which if you ask me feels like months ago. There will be many different breaks throughout the year, Thanksgiving, Easter, Spring Break, and Winter Break, just to name a few.</p>
<p>One thing most people will agree on is Fall Break is definitely not the best time off that we have. For one thing it is getting colder, and there won&#8217;t be as many chances to get outside. It is also one of the shorter breaks with only two days off. Another problem is that a lot of our close friends won&#8217;t be home because they are still at school. Lastly most people will still have work to do over break.</p>
<p>You may be thinking to yourself that I am just showing all negatives and that there are no positives to Fall Break. Well, you are wrong; this all leads into why Fall Break is a great thing.</p>
<p>Take this chance to go and spend some valuable time with your family. For many, this is the first time seeing them since the summer. Go out and do something fun together &#8211; there will be plenty of time to see your friends when you are home for Thanksgiving. Another great thing is the chance to go sleep in your own bed, or lay on your favorite couch by the TV. You also have the chance to get some good meals, whether it is home cooked or going to your favorite restaurant.</p>
<p>Although it may not be long, enjoy Fall Break. Even if you have work to get done, take the chance to sit back and relax in the comfort of your own home.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/10/getting-sick/" rel="bookmark">Getting Sick</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/10/getting-sick/" title="8:33 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 2, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Now here is a topic that no one wants to talk about until it is happening to them. Let&#8217;s face it everyone is going to have that day or week where they just feel terrible. Especially as a Freshman, you are subject to so many different germs in so many different places. For one, there are the classrooms, and in most of them there are no windows and the doors are closed. Enough said. This is the same case with the library and other various closed in places that don&#8217;t get a lot of air flow. Here are a few helpful tips to keep you on your feet feeling great:</p>
<p>1. Have a hefty medicine drawer in your room with everything you may need. I have one of these and just a few examples are Advil, Mucinex decongestant, Tums and many other things that can help any ache or pain you have. With all of these things you will be able to stop something before it can start.</p>
<p>2. Wash your hands as much as you can. This is something you should always do before you eat, and using hand sanitizer is not a bad idea either.</p>
<p>3. GO to the Health Center. They are here for a reason and that is to help you get better. If you are really sick it is a good idea to stop by there.</p>
<p>4. Lastly, if you feel really sick, don&#8217;t go to class. As much as you may not want to miss the material it will make you feel worse, and it will get other people sick as well. Stay in bed and get some rest.</p>
<p>You may choose to take my advice or not, that is up to you. These are just some helpful tips that really do work. This is what I always do and it has kept me going for over two years at this school.</p>
