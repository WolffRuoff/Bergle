			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/12/dublin-or-the-coolest-figuratively-and-literally-place-youll-ever-visit-where-the-people-are-almost-too-kind-to-believe/" rel="bookmark">Dublin! Or the coolest (figuratively and literally) place you&#8217;ll ever visit where the people are almost too kind to believe</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/12/dublin-or-the-coolest-figuratively-and-literally-place-youll-ever-visit-where-the-people-are-almost-too-kind-to-believe/" title="6:10 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">December 17, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/zoebriggs/" title="View all posts by Zoë Briggs">Zoë Briggs</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>So Arezzo is over.  Like I don&#8217;t live there anymore over.  BUT that doesn&#8217;t mean that the adventure is.  When Ryan, Alyssa and I were booking out flights for coming to Italy and going home, we decided that we would want a few extra days in Europe after our program ends.  This ended up being perfect because the other big theatre abroad program is in London and it ends after ours did in Italy.  This means that we not only got to see our friends, but also mooch off of free housing and tour guides.</p>
<div id="attachment_3659" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Dublin.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3659" class="size-medium wp-image-3659" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Dublin-300x168.jpg" alt="Dublin" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Dublin-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Dublin-1024x576.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Dublin.jpg 1920w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3659" class="wp-caption-text">Dublin</p></div>
<p>BUT, before we got to head to London we decided to take a day and go to Dublin, Ireland, because why not?  Sitting in the Florence airport writing this as a reflection I do not regret a thing, however sitting in the Milan airport five days ago after having to say goodbye to the villa and Arezzo and then heading straight to a night in the airport, I was not feeling it in the moment.  Both Ryan and I almost imploded a couple of times out of frustration and pure exhaustion.  Food for thought and perhaps some advice, when planning a trip, don&#8217;t over extend yourself and don&#8217;t think that it&#8217;s every a good idea to sleep in an airport multiple nights in a row (unless, you know you&#8217;re twenty and abroad and only have four days to see the world before going home, then I send you all my well thoughts and good luck)</p>
<p>We were both absolutely exhausted after our night in the train station and airport, but as soon as we landed in Dublin we both seemed to get a second wind.  We walked out of our gate and were both immediately excited for the adventures of the day.  I think a big part of this was just how nice everyone was.  We asked multiple people for help on how to get into the city, how to purchase a bus ticket, and where we should travel to and everybody was extremely helpful and seemed really glad to help us.  We immediately felt welcome and excited to go out and explore.</p>
<div id="attachment_3662" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/ThePrezHouse.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3662" class="size-medium wp-image-3662" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/ThePrezHouse-300x168.jpg" alt="The Irish White House" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/ThePrezHouse-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/ThePrezHouse-1024x576.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/ThePrezHouse.jpg 1920w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3662" class="wp-caption-text">The Irish White House</p></div>
<p>Ryan and I decided to purchase tickets for the hop-on-hop-off bus because we had such a limited amount of time in Ireland.  It turned out to be a really good decision.  Not only did we get to escape the cold, we also got to see pretty much everything in the city and then choose what we wanted to see more in depth later on.  My favorites had to be Trinity College and the Guinness Factory (sorry Dad, but don&#8217;t worry I got you souvenirs!)  Dublin is a beautiful city and there is so much to do!</p>
<div id="attachment_3660" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Guiness.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3660" class="size-medium wp-image-3660" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Guiness-300x168.jpg" alt="The Guinness Factory" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Guiness-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Guiness-1024x576.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Guiness.jpg 1920w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3660" class="wp-caption-text">The Guinness Factory</p></div>
<p>We ended up getting dinner at this really cute burger place and then headed out to go gift shopping for our families.  After freezing for as long as we could we headed back to the airport for our second night out in the world before heading to London.  This experience was much better than our first night of traveling, mostly because we took over the only table in McDonalds with an outlet and had some pretty solid wifi.  After stirring through a couple hours of sleep we headed back into the airport to take off for London which was a whole other set of adventures.</p>
<div id="attachment_3661" style="width: 178px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/RyanCold.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3661" class="size-medium wp-image-3661" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/RyanCold-168x300.jpg" alt="Chilly Ryan" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/RyanCold-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/RyanCold-576x1024.jpg 576w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/RyanCold.jpg 1080w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3661" class="wp-caption-text">Chilly Ryan</p></div>
<p>Until Next Time, Ciao!</p>

			<div id="post-3583" class="post-3583 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/12/paris-or-one-of-those-trips-you-dream-about-and-dont-think-will-happen-until-it-happens/" rel="bookmark">Paris, or one of those trips you dream about and don&#8217;t think will happen until it happens</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/12/paris-or-one-of-those-trips-you-dream-about-and-dont-think-will-happen-until-it-happens/" title="9:50 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">December 11, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/zoebriggs/" title="View all posts by Zoë Briggs">Zoë Briggs</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Way way way way way back in September when the roomie and I were talking about our adventures that we really wanted to go on and what places we wanted to see and both of us were really interested in going to Paris.  Now Paris has always been one of my bucket list places.  I felt like I grew up with people telling me how magical Paris was, how spectacular of a city it was, how I had to go.  Did I think it would happen?  Eventually, yes but I didn&#8217;t expect to be able to go at the age of twenty with one of my best friends and do it all in less than 48 hours.</p>
<div id="attachment_3633" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Lockbridge.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3633" class="size-medium wp-image-3633" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Lockbridge-300x168.jpg" alt="Love Lovk Bridge" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Lockbridge-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Lockbridge-1024x576.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Lockbridge.jpg 1920w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3633" class="wp-caption-text">Love Lock Bridge</p></div>
<p>Emily and I started our adventure by leaving the villa late Friday night after finishing up our movement finals and saying goodbye to the music students (they were leaving even later that night/very early Saturday morning) and headed to the Arezzo train station that we have gotten to know so well.  I have to say I was not as excited for this trip as I was for Copenhagen, even though I knew I was going to have fun, and I think that&#8217;s because I knew it was the last weekend trip I would be able to take while studying in Italy.  I knew that as soon as I got back I would have just one week left and then it was home.  I was also exhausted after working endlessly on my final all week.  BUT we were determined to have fun and see as much as possible.</p>
<div id="attachment_3631" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/meandluvre.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3631" class="size-medium wp-image-3631" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/meandluvre-300x300.jpg" alt="Me and the Louvre" width="300" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/meandluvre-300x300.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/meandluvre-150x150.jpg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/meandluvre-1024x1024.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3631" class="wp-caption-text">Me and the Louvre</p></div>
<p>We once again got to the airport super late at night and settled in for some tv show binging and Facebook stalking before we were able to check in around 4am and get to our 6am flight.  I hate to say it, but I now think that I am a pro when it comes to surviving off of literally no sleep and then walking for 6 or 7 hours around a city I don&#8217;t know and managing to still function like a human being.  (I don&#8217;t know if that&#8217;s a good or bad thing..)</p>
<p>As soon as we stepped off of the metro literally steps away from Notre Dame I knew it was all worth it.  All of the pictures, all of the movies, all of the google searches can not do Paris justice.  It is ABSOLUTELY BEAUTIFUL!!!!!!!!!  We walked into Notre Dame and were immediately overcome by the sheer massiveness of the church and then of course itsbeauty.</p>
<div id="attachment_3629" style="width: 178px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/NotreDame.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3629" class="size-medium wp-image-3629" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/NotreDame-168x300.jpg" alt="Notre Dame" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/NotreDame-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/NotreDame-576x1024.jpg 576w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/NotreDame.jpg 1080w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3629" class="wp-caption-text">Notre Dame</p></div>
<p>Another really great thing about Paris is that it is super easy to navigate.   We literally ran into the Love Bridge (Love Lock Bridge?&#8230;the bridge with all of the locks on it)  and then continued stumbling upon Paris until we ended up at the Louvre.  There was a bit of drama getting our tickets (if you are a student you get in for free, don&#8217;t let them tell you they don&#8217;t and if they insist go to another window) but eventually it worked out and we entered into the biggest museum I have ever been in in my entire life.  Not only are there like a bajillion rooms, there are multiple wings, WINGS! and not surprisingly its super easy to get lost, like really easy.  So after spending way to much time in the Egypt exhibit (which is usually my favorite but not after an hour and a half of going in circles) we finally made it out and headed to some of the most famous works of art that call the Louvre home.</p>
<div id="attachment_3632" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Louvre.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3632" class="size-medium wp-image-3632" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Louvre-300x168.jpg" alt="Louvre" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Louvre-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Louvre-1024x576.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Louvre.jpg 1920w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3632" class="wp-caption-text">Louvre</p></div>
<p>My personal favorite was the Winged Victory statue which is magnificent!  But of course we made sure to see the Mona Lisa, because who goes to the Louvre and doesn&#8217;t see the Mona Lisa?  What was actually the best part about seeing the Mona Lisa (yes the painting was beautiful, but it was definitely overshadowed) was seeing the mass of people surrounding it and literally fighting to get a selfie with the portrait (yes, not even picture, a selfie)  It was hilarious!</p>
<p>After the Louvre we decided it was time for a nap.  Yeah I know, I was in Paris and decided to nap, but is was NECESSARY FOR ME TO SURVIVE!!  We headed to our hostel (which was literally a block away from the Louvre)  and settled in for a nice little hour long nap.</p>
<p>After being rejuvenated we headed down the Seine river to the Eiffel Tower!  Ugh I cannot express the beauty!  We got to the tower after the sun had set so it was all beautiful and lit up.  It was literally a beam of light towering (see what I did there) over the rest of the city.  We decided not to go up because a. the line was crazy long and b. I&#8217;m currently in the broke phase of abroad life (trust me, it&#8217;s a real thing).</p>
<div id="attachment_3634" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/eiffle.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3634" class="size-medium wp-image-3634" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/eiffle-300x300.jpg" alt="Eiffle Tower" width="300" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/eiffle-300x300.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/eiffle-150x150.jpg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/eiffle-1024x1024.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3634" class="wp-caption-text">Eiffle Tower</p></div>
<p>We went to dinner at a really cute and small pub before heading to Shakespeare and Co. and then heading back to the hostel for bed.  It was an exhausting trip but so incredibly worth it!  I still have to pinch myself when I am going on these trips and am in these amazing places.  I know I have been saying in almost every blog since fall break that it is going by so quickly and that I don&#8217;t want the end to come but it has never been as true as it is right now.  Why?  BECAUSE I LEAVE IN LITERALLY FOUR DAYS!  UGH NO!!!!   I still have a few trips that stand between me being abroad and going home but still, where did the time go and why can&#8217;t I control it?</p>
<div id="attachment_3635" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Eiffelselfie.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3635" class="size-medium wp-image-3635" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Eiffelselfie-300x300.jpg" alt="Eiffel Selfie" width="300" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Eiffelselfie-300x300.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Eiffelselfie-150x150.jpg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/Eiffelselfie.jpg 960w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3635" class="wp-caption-text">Eiffel Selfie</p></div>
<p>Until next time, Ciao!</p>
