					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Going home was absolutely surreal. Upon arrival at LAX on Sunday, I was greeted with 78 degree warmth, blue skies, and the open arms of my family. It doesn&#8217;t get much better than that; however, when I got home, I quickly smelled fresh baked pumpkin muffins. So yeah, it does get better than that.</p>
<p>Each day was like a time transport back to summer, when the days were low stress and high reward. Spent working out or going to the beach, Thanksgiving break back home was the exact rejuvenation I needed heading into finals week at Muhlenberg.</p>
<p>On the day of Thanksgiving, 25 of my relatives come over just as they do annually, and we feasted together. My mom had spent the prior four days cooking a myriad of delicious meals for us all to enjoy.</p>
<p>For the last 6 years, I have had at least one cousin in college, but this year four of us were returning home. Despite being the newest Gordon to have a homecoming, the persistent questions about college life didn&#8217;t faze me. I was just excited to be back with the people I love and missed so dearly.</p>
<p>Now time to rock it on finals!!</p>

<p style="color: #2b2b2b">But on the Wednesday before break I left for New Hampshire. The weather was looking alright in Philly around 12:15am, right before I get on to the packed Amtrak for Boston. The rest of the trip is spent in the dark, and eight hours later, in Boston, there is still no sign of snow.</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">We are now in New Hampshire, the time is 10am on Wednesday, and snow is still nonexistent. As we pull into my driveway however, it begins&#8230;</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">First a smallÂ flurry&#8230;</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">Soon the ground is coated in a frost&#8230;</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">And then we get hit&#8230; hard&#8230; the end result being seventeen inches of</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">Pure</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">White</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">SNOW!</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">Many of my friends lost power for Thanksgiving dinner, my family being one of the extremely lucky few to avoid the stress.</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">Maybe I brought the luck&#8230;</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">But probably not, since I had to turn around 48 hours later and drive back out to Allentown&#8230;</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">And now here I am, with two weeks of school left&#8230;</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">And no stress&#8230;</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">Just kidding! (of course)</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">Bring on the finals!</p>
<p style="color: #2b2b2b">Happy birthday!</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/12/the-dining-hall/" rel="bookmark">The Dining Hall</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/12/the-dining-hall/" title="4:52 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">December 4, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>My favorite place on campus, besides Java Joe, is the Dining Commons&#8230;</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3605" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/photo-768x1024.jpg" alt="photo" width="640" height="853" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/photo-768x1024.jpg 768w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/photo-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/photo.jpg 1536w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></p>
<p>I usually try to sit at the fireplace every time I&#8217;m in here but there&#8217;s always someone who beat me to it.</p>
<p>I love coming in here, setting up my work and just being in here for hours.</p>
<p>I get work done, I have unlimited coffee, unlimited fro-yo, unlimited french fries, and everything else my heart usually desires. Whenever I haveÂ to study, one of the things I do when I procrastinate is get food, I may not even be hungry, but I&#8217;d rather be eating than studying. However, when I get to the Dining Commons I&#8217;m surrounded by food so there&#8217;s really no need to have to leave to get food, and it&#8217;s there at my disposal. On top of that, I was able to eliminateÂ on of the main ways I procrastinate which allows me to focus more on getting work done.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s a win-win.</p>
<p>A win for my stomach and a win for my brain.</p>
<p>The Dining Commons has its rush hours (lunch around 1pm and dinner around 6pm) but for the most part it&#8217;s very slow-paced and relaxing. There are also many places to study in there. There&#8217;s upstairs for those who do not want to be distracted by anyone. There&#8217;s the cozy fireplace. There are tables along the patio that allow you to people watch in between your studying. There are 2-top tables that guarantee that one of your friends will not plop there things down since (presumably) all of your notes and books will already be taking up the space. And if you want to do group study sessions there are 8-top tables and longer tables usually used by the sororities, fraternities or sports teams (but they are available for everyone else as well). But wherever you choose to study in the Dining Commons you will have unlimited food and beverages which is amazinggggggggggg and the main reason why many other people choose to study in here as well.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Good luck on finals everyone,</p>
<p>FelisaÂ <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/12/photo.jpg"><br />
</a></p>
