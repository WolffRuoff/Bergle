			<p>Hello Blog Readers!</p>
<p>Welcome to the 2017 Sedehi Diversity Project blog! My name is Mikaela Esposito and I am this year&#8217;s project manager which basically means that I am here to welcome you on this journey with us, and update you on all of the exciting, tiring, life-changing things we are getting up to over here on campus. We have actually been back at Muhlenberg and hard at work for a little over two weeks now, so here&#8217;s a quick update of everything we have accomplished, discovered, experienced, stressed about, etc. over the past two weeks:</p>
<p>Week One: July 21st-July 28th, 2017</p>
<p>Now, I can&#8217;t speak for all the ensemble members here, but I know that I was extremely anxious to move in on July 21st for the rest of the summer (mainly because we would have to cook for ourselves in our Village suites and as the ensemble very well knows, I can barely make a good bowl of cereal!) because I knew how special and important this project is to not only all of us, but Muhlenberg College as well. We had and have pretty big shoes to fill as the project has always been phenomenal and groundbreaking in the past, so I was overwhelmingly nervous to start working on this year&#8217;s production. Additionally, most of us in the ensemble were acquaintances or just friendly with each other, hardly any of us best friends, and now we would be living together and working every night on something so controversial and opinionated and personal! Needless to say, I was scared. Excited, but scared. However, as move in day began and we helped each other move all of our stuff into the dorm buildings (through a lot of sweat, I might add) I could tell that this summer was going to be great. There&#8217;s something about carrying boxes and bags and hampers and furniture in together that bonds a group of people, you know?</p>
<p>We decided that we were going to start rehearsal the night after we moved in so that we could dedicate that first night to being with each other and bonding as an ensemble, as the work we had ahead was going to require us being very comfortable and open with each other. So, of course, we ordered Chinese takeout and stayed up until the late hours of the morning chatting in the downstairs suite about Muhlenberg, our experiences, and how we were feeling about starting this project. I soon found out I wasn&#8217;t the only one to have some nerves, but that collectively, we were all so pumped to be here and working on something that means so much to all of us.</p>
<p>The next day we began our rehearsal process. Throughout the summer we had been doing our interviews with the students who had volunteered (as project manager let me tell you, organizing interviews with Muhlenberg students who have crazy busy schedules was a FEAT and on more than one occasion I thought this project was going to kill me&#8230;but I&#8217;m not dramatic!) and Bree B., being the amazing director that she is, required us to have our transcriptions of the interviews finished before our first rehearsal so we could get right to work. The first step of the process was interviews so&#8230;check. Next step? Transcribing the interviews&#8230;check. Step three: picking quotes from the transcriptions as an ensemble; quotes we think are interesting, important, and relevant for the story we are trying to tell with this project. This part of the process was time consuming, repetitive, and definitely got a bit silly at times, particularly with how late our rehearsals went. As Bree O. would say, &#8220;Welcome to SDP After Dark.&#8221;</p>
<div id="attachment_362" style="width: 179px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/146.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-362" class="size-medium wp-image-362" src="http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/146-169x300.jpg" alt="" width="169" height="300" srcset="http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/146-169x300.jpg 169w, http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/146-576x1024.jpg 576w, http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/146-624x1110.jpg 624w, http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/146.jpg 750w" sizes="(max-width: 169px) 100vw, 169px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-362" class="wp-caption-text">After one of our first rehearsals, hanging out in the upstairs suite together!</p></div>
<p>Throughout this week, our time mostly consisted of going through our transcriptions and finding quotes, doing some last minute interviews, transcribing them, and then starting step four of the process: categorizing the quotes. Basically in order for the script to have a structure, we needed to categorize the quotes based on what they were talking about so we could organize the script by these different topics. This is the step before the actual script building process, which hold onto your hats, is coming in week two.</p>
<p>Even though we spent a lot of time working, we also spent a LOT of time having fun which for us means EATING. One thing I&#8217;ve learned about this ensemble (besides the fact that everyone is hilarious, incredibly bright, and kind, loving people) is that we all really appreciate some good food. Highlights include celebrating Bree O.&#8217;s 21st birthday with some ice cream cake, late night chocolate chip cookie baking in the upstairs suite, family dinner in the multi cultural center, and late night trips to Applebee&#8217;s for half priced appetizers!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_364" style="width: 235px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/133.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-364" class="wp-image-364 size-medium" src="http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/133-e1502313435941-225x300.jpg" alt="" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/133-e1502313435941-225x300.jpg 225w, http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/133-e1502313435941-768x1024.jpg 768w, http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/133-e1502313435941-624x832.jpg 624w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-364" class="wp-caption-text">Getting ready to cut open Bree&#8217;s 21st birthday cake!</p></div>

<p>Week Two: July 29th-August 4th, 2017</p>
<p>I&#8217;m still in denial that it&#8217;s August and we&#8217;re already 9 days in. Classes start in 3 weeks and I haven&#8217;t even thought about buying textbooks, waking up for morning classes, or managing my dining dollars yet. Time flies when you&#8217;re creating a production, am I right ladies?</p>
<p>Week two began with our very first work call with Paul Theisen in the blackbox theater which was super fun! We got to help the Wild cast take down their set and began discussing ideas towards what we were going to do with our own production. Everyone was incredibly kind and helpful to us, especially because some of us were very out of our element (I was asked to use a drill and I think the terrified look on my face was Drew&#8217;s very clear cue to take over for me). We had two more work calls after that setting up the lights and the risers, which were broken up quite nicely with impromptu McDonald&#8217;s lunches and Domino&#8217;s dinners bought for us generously by the one and only Robin Riley-Casey. The way to our hearts, friends, is definitely through food. Just putting that out there.</p>
<p>Our productive streak was absolutely not disrupted by our fun meals together, though. We went head first into step four of the process, categorizing and listening to Bree B.&#8217;s &#8220;ambiance music&#8221; as we did so. Step five followed quickly after with printing out the category sheets and cutting out each individual quote. The purpose of this is to move the quotes around within their own category to create a sort of conversation among them, so that when we combine the categories to make a script, it&#8217;s as if there are many conversations within one giant conversation about Muhlenberg. After many late nights, paper cuts, and sweat/tear stained script pieces we finally had an order, structure, and&#8230;.script! After we had it all typed up, it came to a whopping 53 pages and took over an hour to read. We&#8217;ve since done lots and lots and LOTS of editing, &#8220;snipping&#8221; away for hours, but all that fun stuff will be revealed in week three&#8217;s post ðŸ™‚</p>
<div id="attachment_368" style="width: 179px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/193.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-368" class="size-medium wp-image-368" src="http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/193-169x300.jpg" alt="" width="169" height="300" srcset="http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/193-169x300.jpg 169w, http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/193-576x1024.jpg 576w, http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/193-624x1110.jpg 624w, http://sedehidiversityproject.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2017/08/193.jpg 750w" sizes="(max-width: 169px) 100vw, 169px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-368" class="wp-caption-text">Hidden beneath all the notebook paper and category folders was our rough draft of the script! Hundreds of tiny slips of paper we worked over for so many hours :&#8217;)</p></div>
<p>Thanks for reading this BEAST of a post, everyone! Not only will you have an update waiting for you here from me each week, but the ensemble members and our director Bree B. will be visiting here as well and sharing their experiences with you! For now, I leave you with our first SDP Video Blog with footage from the first two weeks. It&#8217;s getting there, my friends. What a special time in life we&#8217;re experiencing together and what a wonderful project we get to share! Talk to you soon!</p>
