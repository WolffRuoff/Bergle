			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/11/dream-on/" rel="bookmark">Dream On</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/11/dream-on/" title="1:46 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 12, 2010</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<div>
<p>Something in my heart makes me feel good whenever I hear  about students here at berg taking classes in many different  disciplines. For instance, my friend who is taking biology and chemistry  has also decided to sate his academic hunger with a philosophy class.  Another friend of mine here at berg is a creative writing major, and yet  I was talking with her today about the test she had in her robotics  class. One thing I think a lot of students forget is that learning is  supposed to be fun. Even though that sounds corny, I’ll stand by it.</p>
<p>Maybe it isn’t the student’s faults that they learn towards a grade.  After all, that is what the system encourages. Some classes reward  regurgitation of memorized material. Luckily here at berg I haven’t had  the misfortune of sitting in a class that doesn’t stimulate genuine  learning. Sometimes I feel as though students in today’s society are so  obsessed with getting good grades, that we lose sight of the true beauty  that is learning something for yourself. Economics is interesting until  you grade me on it, so is history and so is mathematics.</p>
<p>It’s about the time of year here at Berg that students begin to  register for the fall semester and that’s that kind of stuff I have on  my mind. I want to take courses that I’m honestly interested in. Remind  me of that when I’m flailing in a history course next semester please.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/10/let%e2%80%99s-do-the-time-warp/" rel="bookmark">Let’s Do the Time Warp</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/10/let%e2%80%99s-do-the-time-warp/" title="4:49 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 28, 2010</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<div>
<p>I cannot believe how fast this semester is going! It feels  like a few weeks ago that the semester started, but it’s a little more  than half over already! Just this week I signed up for auditions for the  three spring semester mainstage productions, made an appointment to  meet with my advisor about spring semester class registration and got an  Email from the biology secretary saying my October timesheet was due. I  guess time flies when you’re having fun, although you’d think it would  feel longer since I don’t spend very much of my time here sleeping.</p>
<p>Yes, things have gotten quite busy around here, although mostly for  the better. I’m a part of three choirs and after attending the latest  MTA (Muhlenberg Theater Association) meeting, I’m in the runnings for  three productions. I originally began my time here thinking I would just  dabble in theater here and there, where and when I could. However,  after seeing Polaroid stories, and feeling the heat of the lights on my  face at the Gospel Choir’s first gig, I can’t imagine all but drifting  from the world of theater. I need it. It seems to go that you don’t know  what you’ve got ’till it’s gone. I hadn’t actually realized what  theater meant to me until I faced a deficit of the performing arts in my  future. I couldn’t imagine it.</p>
<p>No, mom, this isn’t me confessing that I’ve thrown my hopes for  biochemistry out the window. In fact, if anything, my love for the  micro-level sciences has grown even more recently as I attended a  nano-tech and a biochem lecture recently. Here, my two greatest passions  can coalesce wonderfully. Although the path is quite difficult (the  science majors seem to be having a general breakdown this week for  unknowable reasons), I feel motivated by myself and my passions. Let’s  see if I feel so determined once finals roll around.</p>
</div>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/10/this-is-college/" rel="bookmark">This is College</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/10/this-is-college/" title="5:00 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 21, 2010</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>It’s been a while since my last post and I have accumulated quite a few  photos that I just never got the chance to upload. So here is is, an  honest (perhaps slightly tongue-in-cheek) college collage. Try saying  that 5 times fast. (Wait, I just tried it, it’s not that hard).</p>
<div id="attachment_139" style="width: 510px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/AntelopeHalo.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-139" class="size-full wp-image-139" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/AntelopeHalo.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="667" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/AntelopeHalo.jpg 500w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/AntelopeHalo-224x300.jpg 224w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-139" class="wp-caption-text">Little did the bio majors know, the resident Antelope head is quite the party animal.</p></div>
<div id="attachment_140" style="width: 510px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/ChrisSurfing.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-140" class="size-full wp-image-140" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/ChrisSurfing.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="375" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/ChrisSurfing.jpg 500w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/ChrisSurfing-300x225.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-140" class="wp-caption-text">I assure you no college students were hurt in the depicted activities.</p></div>
<p>Although there’s plenty to do around campus and most students will tell  you that they rarely find themselves in their dorms, occasionally a  group of us do gather perchance. It’s rather difficult to cram 15 people  into any one dorm room, so we the residents of Prosser are occasionally  privy to general hallway hijinks.</p>
<div id="attachment_141" style="width: 510px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/CupNoodlesandMe.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-141" class="size-full wp-image-141" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/CupNoodlesandMe.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="375" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/CupNoodlesandMe.jpg 500w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/CupNoodlesandMe-300x225.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-141" class="wp-caption-text">This is me, and those are my cup noodles.</p></div>
<p>As a college student, I am  devoted to keeping a well rounded diet. I’ve already consumed enough  sodium to last me for a couple years.</p>
<div id="attachment_142" style="width: 510px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/IMG_1531.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-142" class="size-full wp-image-142" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/IMG_1531.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="375" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/IMG_1531.jpg 500w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/IMG_1531-300x225.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-142" class="wp-caption-text">These are boxers. </p></div>
<p>It doesn’t worry me that they were laying on the  stairs. It worries me that I walked past them without questioning their  presence. This, is college.</p>

			<div id="post-145" class="post-145 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/10/a-public-intellectual/" rel="bookmark">A Public Intellectual</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/10/a-public-intellectual/" title="11:34 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 7, 2010</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Spent the night trying to meet a deadline, squeeze our complicated lives into a headline.</p>
<p>I try to keep this blog from becoming just a log of what I did  during the week. While the objective of the blog is of course, to give  you all a glimpse into the lives of a Muhlenberger such as myself, I  think I ought to give you insight into the mind of a ‘Berg student as  well. After all, just telling you what I did will hardly give you a good  idea of what Muhlenberg is, what a Muhlenberg student is. I’d imagine  all over the country there are thousands and thousands of college  students figuring out laundry, studying for midterms and struggling with  Organic Chemistry or Latin or some other very collegiate sounding  course. However, Berg students aren’t like those students. Why? Because  we don’t struggle with those things? No, we do. We’re just far superior  to any other college’s students because we’re really cool, really  friendly and generally awesome.</p>
<p>What I thought I would talk about in this post is (I imagine you  moving your cursor to that back button…”Oh no, he’s going to ramble  about something”, well I promise some interesting pictures at the end of  this post) the various campus events that are set up by all different  groups at the college. What I find really fascinating about this new  label that is “college student” is a certain level of something that  tastes like respect. It’s more like, being taken more seriously. The  Center for Ethics hosts speakers like Jeffrey Ball from the Wall Street  Journal to hold open talks about the issues surrounding climate change  and the IPCC.</p>
<p>Although some classes offer credit for attendance of academic  lectures, they are never offered through a class for the sole purpose of  that class. I’ve listened to a free verse poet who speaks out as an  activist for LGBT rights, an environmental journalist from the WSJ, and a  Florida-based researcher of the construction of the 40s ribosome  subunit all because I genuinely wanted to hear what they had to say.</p>
<p>In my first year seminar, we’ve discussed the effects of  surroundings (aesthetics, that is) on a person’s aspirations, ambition  and success in the long run. By surrounding me with intellectual  opportunities to learn because we want to, we are presented with the  position of public intellectuals. We are the ones who, in the future,  will ask the tough questions, and then answer them. I personally have  verily enjoyed the opportunity to think about something and to learn  about a topic not because I have to, but because I get to. The love of  learning is easier to see.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/JustTapItTop.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-146" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/JustTapItTop.jpg" alt="" width="407" height="375" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/JustTapItTop.jpg 407w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/JustTapItTop-300x276.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 407px) 100vw, 407px" /></a>Part of Muhlenberg College’s campaign to promote promiscuity?</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/JustTapItFull_01.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-147" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/JustTapItFull_01.jpg" alt="" width="200" height="315" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/JustTapItFull_01.jpg 200w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/12/JustTapItFull_01-190x300.jpg 190w" sizes="(max-width: 200px) 100vw, 200px" /></a>Ohhhh. Part of Muhlenberg College’s campaign to go green!</p>

			<div id="post-149" class="post-149 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/09/a-sea-of-600/" rel="bookmark">A Sea of 600</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2010/09/a-sea-of-600/" title="1:47 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 28, 2010</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<div>
<p>How does one maintain one’s individuality when suddenly  thrust into a pool of 600 new people they’ve never met? (and that’s just  the freshman class) Lately I’ve noticed that in order to make friends, a  lot of new college freshmen tend to be generic versions of the same  person and lose their individuality. I believe it’s important to  maintain individuality. Now having made it mostly through the trial  period of friendship-forging and meeting so many people for the first  time, I’m glad I was my usual, slightly idiosyncratic self. Everybody  seems to find his or her niche just fine. What’s more, everyone here  seems pretty nice to me.</p>
<p>Speaking of finding one’s niche, mine seems to be anything that ends  in choir. I auditioned and am now a member of the Gospel Choir, the  Show Choir, and the Church Choir. I’ve yet to determine if there is  another choir group on campus, but I think three is just fine for now.</p>
<p>I also started work study last week. I work in the biology  department as a slave (haha, just kidding). I do work for various  professors around the department. I think it’s cool that professors  trust students (even a freshman like me) with making solutions and media  for their classes and whatnot. Of course, whenever I’m micro-pipeting  something out or putting a batch of agar in the autoclave, I like to  pretend I’m doing high-level research and that every move I make is  critical to the success of the mission I’ve been handed. (Hey, you’ve  gotta do something to keep your mind busy whilst washing beakers and  racking hundreds of pipet tips. I’ve got an active imagination.) All in  all it’s a pretty sweet work study job. My friends work at the  information desk or in the library and pull obscene hours like 11pm to  one in the morning. I work whenever it fits my schedule. Although, let’s  be serious, it’s not like I’m sleeping at 1. Actually, I’m writing  this, at 1. Well, I do have an 8:30 Calculus class that demands my full  attention. Until next time. adieu, adieu, to you and you and you.</p>
</div>
