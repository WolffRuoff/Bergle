			<p>Is it October already? Indeed it is, and the Mules teams begin the second half of their 2011 seasons on a weekend filled with home events.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-104" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>Volleyball vs. Dickinson (Saturday, 11:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules dig playing at home. In their last four matches at Memorial Hall, they defeated Albright and McDaniel and were very competitive with strong Moravian and Haverford teams. Now they’ll try to even their Centennial Conference record against 2-10 Dickinson.</p>
<p>The Mules also dig pediatric cancer research. Muhlenberg volleyball and the Centennial Conference are working together this year to raise money for Tanner&#8217;s Touch, a non-profit organization out of Westminster, Md., that raises awareness and funds to support pediatric cancer research. The Mules (who also play Mount St. Mary at 3:00) will collect pledges and donations for every dig they record in the match against Dickinson.</p>
<p>Live stats (Dickinson match only): <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/vblive/topview.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/vblive/topview.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video (Dickinson match only): <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey vs. Dickinson (Friday, 7:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg will try to make it two wins in a row and capture its first Centennial Conference win of the season. Dickinson is also 0-3 in the CC.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/fhlive/xlive.htm">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/fhlive/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><strong>Cross Country at Paul Short Run (Friday, 12:15 men and 1:00 women)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules compete in one of the largest meets in the nation as they continue to prepare for the Centennial championship meet.</p>
<p>Results page: <a href="http://www.paulshortxcrun.com/PaulShort/PSLayer.php">http://www.paulshortxcrun.com/PaulShort/PSLayer.php</a></p>
<p><strong>Football vs. Susquehanna (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>

			<p>Homecoming is a time for all generations of the Muhlenberg family to come together, and that certainly was the case last Friday.</p>
<p>While on campus for her 50th Reunion, Louise (Niebaum) Bogan met with current members of the Mule field hockey team on Friday morning, showed them a slide show, and then attended their game on Friday night.</p>
<p>Louise Niebaum was a member of the 1959 and 1960 field hockey teams &#8211; the first two at Muhlenberg. The 1959 team won all four of its games without giving up a goal. The next year, Muhlenberg again went undefeated, with a record of 5-0-1. </p>
<p>The program did not lose a game until its fifth season of existence and helped establish the winning tradition of Muhlenberg field hockey. In 52 seasons of play entering this season, the Mules had a winning percentage of .605.</p>
<p>Click the link below to view the slideshow. Of particular note in the old pictures are the hairstyles (of course), the uniforms, the sticks and the cars around the field (Muhlenberg played its first games on what is now the site of Trexler Library). Things have changed quite a bit in the last 50 years!</p>

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>There’s a new way to keep close tabs on not just the Muhlenberg teams, but all Division III teams in football, soccer and field hockey.</p>
<p>Starting this week, box scores for all Division III contests in those sports will be available on the NCAA website. The NCAA has been doing this with Division I football for several years and started with Division III last year. Soccer and field hockey were added this year, with volleyball to come in the future.</p>
<p>Here are the links:</p>
<p>Football: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/mfb/mainpage.jsp?year=2011">http://web1.ncaa.org/mfb/mainpage.jsp?year=2011</a></p>
<p>Men’s soccer: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=MSO">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=MSO</a></p>
<p>Women’s soccer: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WSO">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WSO</a></p>
<p>Field hockey: <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WFH">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WFH</a></p>
<p>The football page is set up a little differently, but for the other three, click on “Click here for box scores and complete team and individual statistics.” Although it is listed on the Division I line, you can get to all three divisions from there. Select III from the drop-down menu where I appears, and you get a list of all schools that play the sport. Click on any school, and you can see their links to cumulative stats and game boxes. Click on a player name in the roster to see season and career stats.</p>
<p>If you want to check up on upcoming opponents, this is an alternative to going to their school websites. And if nothing else, it&#8217;s a great way to kill some time poring over stats.</p>
<p>The NCAA continues to provide weekly rankings in these sports, as well as volleyball (<a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WVB">http://web1.ncaa.org/stats/StatsSrv/rankings?sportCode=WVB</a>). Although it is way too early to put any stock in national statistical rankings, it is interesting to note that the Muhlenberg football team is 28<sup>th</sup> in Division III in rushing defense after two games, and Joe Rollins of McDaniel, who will be visiting Scotty Wood Stadium on Saturday, is the second-leading rusher in the division.</p>

			<p><em>A look at what&#8217;s coming up this weekend in the world of Mule sports:</em></p>

						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/02/opening-weekend/" rel="bookmark">Opening Weekend</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/02/opening-weekend/" title="7:08 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-02T07:08:22-04:00">September 2, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>It’s Friday, Friday, and everybody’s looking forward to the weekend, weekend – and looking forward to another year of Muhlenberg athletics! Seven Mule teams open up this weekend, and here’s a preview:</em></p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-46" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>There’s no better way to start a season than with a game between two NCAA Tournament teams, and there are two of them on tap this weekend.</p>
<p><strong>Football at Delaware Valley (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>Both teams earned their conference’s automatic bid to the NCAA Tournament last year, and both lost to national semifinalists (Muhlenberg to Wesley, Delaware Valley to Mt. Union). Both teams return veteran, stingy defenses, but while the Mules bring back a good chunk of their offense, the Aggies graduated nine offensive starters, including seven all-conference players. Delaware Valley won last year’s game, 27-7.</p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.sidearmstats.com/delval/football/">http://www.sidearmstats.com/delval/football/</a></p>
<p>Live audio (WMUH): <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/wmuh/stream.html">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/wmuh/stream.html</a></p>
<p><strong>Men’s Soccer vs. DeSales (Saturday, 6:00)</strong></p>
<p>The two Lehigh Valley school have developed a nice rivalry over the years – it doesn’t hurt that both teams are usually very good. DeSales lost to Swarthmore in the first round of the NCAAs last year, and Muhlenberg made it to the “Sweet 16.” The Mules, who also host Cortland State on Friday at 6:00, lead the all-time series 15-2-2 and have won the last four meetings.</p>
<p>Live stats (both games): <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/msoclive/xlive.htm">www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/msoclive/xlive.htm</a></p>
<p>Live video (both games): <a href="http://www.livestream.com/muhlenberg">www.livestream.com/muhlenberg</a></p>
<p><em>In other action this weekend:</em></p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey at Scranton (Friday, 5:00) and Elizabethtown (Saturday, 2:00)</strong></p>
<p>For the first time since 2000, the Mules open a season with back-to-back road games. Scranton was 7-12 last year, and Elizabethtown was 14-5. </p>
<p>Scranton game live stats: <a href="http://athletics.scranton.edu/livestats/landing" target="_blank">http://athletics.scranton.edu/livestats/landing</a></p>
<p>E-town game live stats: <a href="http://www.sidearmstats.com/etown/fhockey/">http://www.sidearmstats.com/etown/fhockey/</a></p>
<p><strong>Women’s Soccer at Kean Classic (vs. Richard Stockton, Saturday, 2:00; vs. Kean, Sunday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules take on a couple of teams from the New Jersey Athletic Conference to start their season. In 2010, Richard Stockton was 13-8-2, and Kean went 9-6-4, including a 3-2 win against the Mules.</p>
<p>Live stats (both games): <a href="http://sidearmstats.com/kean/wsoc/">http://sidearmstats.com/kean/wsoc/</a></p>
<p>Live video (Kean game only):  <a href="http://www.keanathletics.com/general_news/012309live">http://www.keanathletics.com/general_news/012309live</a></p>
<p><strong>Volleyball at Marietta’s River City Classic (vs. Oberlin, Friday, 5:00; vs. Kenyon, Friday, 8:00; vs. Marietta, Saturday, 11:30; vs. Methodist, Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>The volleyball team travels to Ohio for a season-opening tournament.</p>
<p>Tournament updates: <a href="http://pioneers.marietta.edu/sports/2010/8/3/WVB_11RiverCityTournament.aspx?path=wvball">http://pioneers.marietta.edu/sports/2010/8/3/WVB_11RiverCityTournament.aspx?path=wvball</a></p>
<p>Live stats (match vs. Marietta only): <a href="http://www.sidearmstats.com/marietta/wvball/">http://www.sidearmstats.com/marietta/wvball/</a></p>
<p><strong>Cross Country at Lehigh Invitational</strong></p>
<p>All but one of the Mules&#8217; seven meets this year are in the Lehigh Valley, and this is the first.</p>

						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/08/30/field-hockey-preview/" rel="bookmark">Field Hockey Preview</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/08/30/field-hockey-preview/" title="6:56 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-08-30T18:56:32-04:00">August 30, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/08/deangelischs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-23" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/08/deangelischs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Senior Courtney DeAngelis writes about the outlook for the 2011 Mule field hockey team:</em></p>
<p>First, I want to welcome all the freshmen who are reading this to the ‘Berg!  And to the upperclassmen, I hope you’re all as happy to be back as I am. I’m pleased to report that the Muhlenberg field hockey team was seemingly unfazed by the earthquake, hurricane and the power outage across campus, and still managed to soldier through preseason.<strong>  </strong>But, aside from those calamities, things have been running smoothly for us so far.   </p>
<p>Now to be perfectly frank, for those of you who followed us last season, you already know it was a rough one. There are fewer things that are more frustrating than to not perform at your level of play, and this was a recurring issue for us. Nonetheless, I am completely confident that our team will bounce back from last year and experience success. We do NOT want a repeat of last season, and we’re determined to ensure that this does not happen. </p>
<div style="width: 260px" class="wp-caption alignright"><img src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/08/todisco.jpg" alt="" width="250" height="326" /><p class="wp-caption-text">Jaclyn Todisco celebrates after a Mule goal in last week&#039;s scrimmage against FDU-Florham.</p></div>
<p>We’re not going to dwell on the past, or linger on what should have, could have, or would have been; what happened, happened, and all of the upperclassmen just want to move forward. As a senior, I’m especially grateful to have another shot to prove to the conference what we’re really capable of, and we’re hoping to really shock everyone this season.</p>
<p>Another big change this season is our size &#8211; with the addition of thirteen freshmen, we’re up to 32 girls! I’ll be perfectly honest and admit that I was initially a bit nervous about having such a large class, and how it would change our team dynamic. They were like a small army coming in the first day, and my biggest concern was how I’d ever remember all of their names. </p>
<p>Fortunately, spending ten (give or take a few) hours a day together for a week straight provided plenty of opportunities to get to know everyone, and with the help of some good old fashioned name games and team bonding activities, everyone seems to be clicking really well.</p>
<p>So all in all, we’re ready to move on and surprise the conference.  We know we’re competing against some of the top DIII programs in the nation, but we’re ready for the challenge and we’re eager for our first game this upcoming Friday. </p>
<p>Hope to see you all there at some home games this year!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
