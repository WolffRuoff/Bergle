				<p>What a week! Oh wait, we&#8217;re still sitting on mid-afternoon Thursday. Right. For me, Thursday is the top of the ninthÂ when you&#8217;re the home team. If you&#8217;ve knocked out your homework at this point, then you only have half an inning left before you win the game, but if you have to get a few things in before class starts, then you might be going into extras. Sadly your relief pitcher is a caffeinated beverage and sometimes you just can&#8217;t help but make the call to the bullpen.</p>
<p>Hold the phone though! I haven&#8217;t even introduced myself!</p>
<p>My name is Drew Swedberg and I am a sophomore from central New Hampshire: you know, the state that exists in the United StatesÂ (you would be surprised as to the amount of times I&#8217;ve been asked if I&#8217;m from Canada). Although the drive is far, the journey has been scenic, and despite the weather and the people beingÂ vastly different from what I grew up around, not a lot has changed for me in college. I still play organized basketball for my school. I still work with elementary school kids. I am still in love with film. Who am I kidding though. EVERYTHING HAS CHANGED.</p>
<p>I achieved a long-term goal of mine by walking on to the men&#8217;s basketball team, and now, in less than a year, I will be playing basketball with my team in Barcelona! This gave meÂ the opportunity to practice with a Special Olympics team last year, a fantastic experience to say the least, and needless to say, I have enjoyed every second around the supportive athletic community. And I swear, I did not block this shot&#8230;</p>
<div id="attachment_3031" style="width: 970px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/10264334_10152357179703221_4349596715896905182_n.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3031" class="wp-image-3031 size-full" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/10264334_10152357179703221_4349596715896905182_n.jpg" alt="One of the Special Olympic athletes shooting the ball in front of the blogger with both his hands up as if he is about to block the shot." width="960" height="640" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/10264334_10152357179703221_4349596715896905182_n.jpg 960w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/10264334_10152357179703221_4349596715896905182_n-300x200.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 960px) 100vw, 960px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3031" class="wp-caption-text">Playing some light defense.</p></div>
<p>My work with children has transformed from hanging out with a fourth grader for half an hour when I was in high school, to working in twoÂ elementary schools twice a week for three hours. I have always been at a loss for words for the experience I had last year through the America Reads program, which I found while working for the <a title="Office of Community Engagement - Muhlenberg College" href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/community-service/">Office of Community Engagement</a> (which I will be talking about extensively this year as it shapes many of my experiences at college). It would be an understatement to say it changed my life. Rather, it reached out to my heart and my soul in a way that no planned experience ever could. You will most certainly be hearing more about the kids and my encounters with them this year, as I just handed in all my clearances and get back to it next week!</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/1622391_505426046270317_873852774800821088_o.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-large wp-image-3032" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/1622391_505426046270317_873852774800821088_o-1024x685.jpg" alt="Class of first graders and the blogger in a group photo" width="640" height="428" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/1622391_505426046270317_873852774800821088_o-1024x685.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/1622391_505426046270317_873852774800821088_o-300x200.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/1622391_505426046270317_873852774800821088_o.jpg 1780w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a></p>
<p>I am also one of the co-partners of a student film production group on campus. A lot of the work I do for them is in cinematography, but this year one of my classes, Video Production (did I forget to mention I am a double major with film studies and English?), has given me the opportunities to develop one of my original ideas with a group of two other students into a script that is at this point, four weeks into the semester, almost done. From there we will cast actors, set up a shot list, and begin shooting a film that will receive an &#8216;A&#8217; if it is ready to be submitted to a filmÂ festival!</p>
<p>I believe I have exhausted my blogging and introductions for the day, and I hope that you have a better idea of who I am and what I will be bringing to the fantastic world of blogs this year!</p>
<p>Before I go I want to explain my sign off. I am not in the education department here at Muhlenberg and despite my fantastic experiences within elementary schools, I am not at school to become a classroom teacher. I am, however, taking an education class, and in the class, Education Psychology, I heard this wonderful story of an educator that only heightened my appreciation and admiration for teachers. Every day, this teacher would sing happy birthday with his class, not because he had 365 students, but because he was building this appreciation for each day. Every day is special. Every day is wonderful. So without further ado!</p>
<p>Happy birthday!</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/fall-baseball/" rel="bookmark">Fall Baseball</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/fall-baseball/" title="3:24 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 18, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>For baseball, our main season is in the spring. We also have our fall season, which is still competitive, but nothing like the spring. We are allowed to have about 16 practices, so it is always nice to get the chance to go out and play. During this fall season, we get the chance to have one game day against another team. Every year, we play against Lehigh University in 3 separate 6-inning games.</p>
<p>This Sunday, we will be heading to Lehigh for our annual trip. It is always exciting to get the chance to go out and play, especially against a Division I school that is really good competition for us. After this game, we will have another week of practices, and then we are done until February roles around.</p>
<p>During the winter, everyone gets in and works out, but it is a huge difference with the amount of extra time we have. Our spring season is a lot of fun, but it can get pretty hectic. It&#8217;s nice to have a couple of months off to relax and just enjoy college. That way when the spring comes we are all well rested and ready to make a run at a Conference Championship.</p>

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>As a member of the Cardinal Key Society (CKS) here at Muhlenberg, one of the requirements is working as a student representative at different events during Homecoming Weekend.</p>
<p>Are you familiar with the Cardinal Key Society?</p>
<p>No?</p>
<p>WHAT?</p>
<p>Well here&#8217;s a little bit about us&#8230;</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">We were founded on May 28, 1940, so we&#8217;ve been around for a while. The main goal of the Cardinal Key Society is to maintain lifelong relationships with alumni. We alsoÂ work to develop traditions and instill school spirit among Muhlenberg students. We work with the Alumni Relations office and collaborate on events such as Homecoming, Honors Convocation, graduation and events throughout the year.</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">Homecoming Weekend is one of our biggest weekends of the year! Each Key is assigned to work at multipleÂ events and because I am a member on the executive board (Events and Community Service Chairperson #represent) I had the honor ofÂ filling in the gaps where Keys were needed.</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">(You can meet the rest of us keys by clicking <a title="here" href="http://www.muhlenbergconnect.com/s/1570/index.aspx?sid=1570&amp;gid=2&amp;pgid=865" target="_blank">here</a>)</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">My Friday started at 7:45am for set up and ended at 6pm at the HMMS and Circle of 1848 dinner, a dinner that honors donors whoÂ who name the College as a beneficiary in their estate plan, through their will, life insurance policy, etc. (P.S. Shoutout to all those who have ever donated money to Muhlenberg College &#8212; you are all amazing, and this college would not be the same, or be able to offer the services it does for its students if it weren&#8217;t for you).</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">On Saturday, my day started at 6:45am for setup and I worked at the Homecoming Fair, the Class of 1989 dinner and then my final task was to drive alumni to and from their cars after the Alumni dance ended (meaning I didn&#8217;t finish working until about 10:45pm&#8211; however, I got to drive a golf cart (a.k.a my weekend bat mobile) around so I&#8217;m not complaining; I love driving golf carts &#8211; no one can ever understand the joy that driving a golf cart brings me.)</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">On Sunday I got to sleep in and didn&#8217;t have to report to duty with the rest of the exec- board until 9am for cleanup (driving my lovely bat mobile around again&#8230; ) and I worked at the Hillel Bagel Brunch. That was my final assignment, which meant I finally got to take a nice needed nap.</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">If this weekend was busy for me, imagine how busy it was for those who work in the Alumni Relations Office. They put so much time, effort and energy into this weekend, it amazes me. And even after working so hard, they are always smiling and so happy! To learn more about the events that the Alumni Relations Office puts on and what alumni are up to, click <a href="http://www.muhlenbergconnect.com/s/1570/start.aspx?sid=1570&amp;gid=2&amp;pgid=61&amp;cid=160" target="_blank">here</a>!</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">Homecoming Weekend was an absolute success &#8212; seeing everyone return to campus, reunite with old friends and have a great time makes all the work everyone puts in WORTH IT! My favorite part is hearing the stories about the good ol&#8217; days and being able to hear first-hand accounts about how the college has changed so much. I cannot wait until I return to campus 50 years from now and sit down at a dinner with a Cardinal Key member and tell my stories. Just thinking about returning to campus after I graduate makes me so happy and I still have another year before that even happens.</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">Well, I&#8217;m still recovering and still exhausted so I&#8217;m going to go to sleep now&#8230;</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">Until next time,</p>
<p class="bodytext" style="color: #000000;" align="left">Felisa W</p>
