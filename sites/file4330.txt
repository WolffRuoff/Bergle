			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/03/danielle-in-dublin/" rel="bookmark">Danielle in Dublin</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/03/danielle-in-dublin/" title="4:11 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">March 11, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/ml24513/" title="View all posts by Megan">Megan</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Last week one of my close friends, Danielle, came to visit! She also happens to be a fellow blogger for Muhlenberg, so please check out her blog [just click the tab above that says Danielle] for more details on the trip! Here are just some of the highlights:</p>
<div id="attachment_2035" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/daniairport.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2035" class="size-medium wp-image-2035" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/daniairport-300x200.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="200" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/daniairport-300x200.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/daniairport.jpg 604w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2035" class="wp-caption-text">Our first picture in Dublin. Picking Danielle up at the airport!</p></div>
<p><strong>Cliff Walk (Greystones-Bray) </strong>After getting breakfast at Bewley&#8217;s Cafe, we took a train to Greystones, a small town by the water, to do a cliff walk that ends in Bray, another small fishing town. The walk was incredible with breathtaking views! We were lucky and had a beautiful (still cold, but not windy) day!</p>
<div id="attachment_2032" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin-1.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2032" class="size-medium wp-image-2032" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin-1-300x200.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="200" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin-1-300x200.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin-1.jpg 604w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2032" class="wp-caption-text">Danielle and I on the cliff walk between Greystones and Bray.</p></div>
<div id="attachment_2033" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin3.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2033" class="size-medium wp-image-2033" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin3-300x200.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="200" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin3-300x200.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin3.jpg 604w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2033" class="wp-caption-text">SPOTTED: Mr. Mule enjoying the view overlooking the water.</p></div>
<p><strong>Kilmainham Gaol/Book of Kells </strong>Both of these are must see&#8217;s in Dublin; however, we never actually got to see them. Funny story. First, we went to the Book of Kells in Trinity, but they had to shut down because they had an emergency maintenance issue that needed to be fixed. So then we went to Kilmainham Gaol, which is a famous old jail in Dublin, only to find out that all of their tours were booked. Regardless of the epic fail that seemed to be that day, we ended up having a lot of fun laughing and exploring parts of the city that we hadn&#8217;t seen before. I have realized this even more so abroad, that the events that happen unplanned end up being the memories I love the most.</p>
<p><strong>National Leprechaun Museum </strong>Our friends, Jimmy and Nina, were also in town visiting, so we all went to the National Leprechaun Museum. It was the most interesting place I&#8217;ve ever been. It was a guided, interactive tour through different stories about leprechauns. One of the rooms had giant furniture to make you feel as small as a leprechaun, while another room had a giant rainbow made of string. It was definitely entertaining to say the least.</p>
<div id="attachment_2034" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin6.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2034" class="size-medium wp-image-2034" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin6-300x200.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="200" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin6-300x200.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/dublin6.jpg 604w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2034" class="wp-caption-text">[from left to right] Myself, Danielle, and Jimmy sitting on the giant furniture listening to leprechaun stories.</p></div><strong>Queen of Tarts/Butler&#8217;s Chocolate Cafe </strong>I thought it was important that I take Danielle to my favorite sweet shops in town. My two favorites, which I have already mentioned several times throughout my blog because I&#8217;m obsessed, are Queen of Tarts and Butler&#8217;s Chocolate Cafe. I am no longer the only one obsessed though because I&#8217;m pretty sure Danielle would agree that the tarts and cookie hot chocolate are equally the most amazing things you will ever have.</p>
<p>These are just a few of the many things we got to see and do together in Dublin. The whole week was so much fun!! I am loving my time here, but I also miss my friends back home in Muhlenberg so it was nice to see her! Also, it&#8217;s funny because the one thing that I have been craving and missing the most while abroad is a GQ, which is a place to get food on Muhlenberg&#8217;s campus, giant chocolate chip cookie. <p>THURSDAY, to reward myself for writing my paper a week in advance, I took a solo trip into the city for the afternoon. I ate lunch at my favorite cafe, Bewley&#8217;s, followed by hot chocolate at Butler&#8217;s Chocolate Cafe as I walked around St. Stephen&#8217;s Green and Trinity College. It was a beautiful, relaxing day! Also, Jena and I made burritos for our Irish friends. They had never had a burrito before and really enjoyed them!</p>
<p>FRIDAY, Catt and I went to a concert. Walk the Moon, a band from Ohio, was playing in a small venue in the city. It was an amazing concert and a fun night out!</p>
<div id="attachment_1994" style="width: 234px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/02/walkthemoon.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1994" class="size-medium wp-image-1994" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/02/walkthemoon-224x300.jpg" alt="" width="224" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/02/walkthemoon-224x300.jpg 224w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/02/walkthemoon.jpg 717w" sizes="(max-width: 224px) 100vw, 224px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1994" class="wp-caption-text">Walk the Moon lead singer, Nick, and myself after the concert.</p></div>

We also had cookie hot chocolate that had actual cookies in it. It was soo amazingly good! For dinner, Jena and I made crepes with our Irish friends. They call them pancakes, but they are super thin and more of what we would call a crepe. They were so tasty!</p>
