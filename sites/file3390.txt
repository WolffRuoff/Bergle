					<h1 class="entry-title">Introduction</h1>

					<div class="entry-meta">
						<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/08/introduction/" title="9:20 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">August 20, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>					</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
						<p>I am glad to say that this will be my first of many posts to come in the future. I will be writing about a lot of different events, activities, stories, topics, and thoughts that I have over the coming year. Before I get into any of that, I would like to take some time to introduce myself to you and let you know more about the person that will be blogging to you.</p>
<p>I am going into my junior year here at Muhlenberg and could not have been happier with the way the first half of my college career has begun. I am a double major in business and accounting, and I am a member of the baseball team. Like so many people at Muhlenberg, I am just another New Jersey kid that found myself at what I believe to be such a great college choice.</p>
<p>We are now just a week away from getting back to school, with classes right around the corner. To the returning students, it is that time where we once again say bye to all our friends from home and look forward to seeing all of our friends at school. To the incoming freshmen, it is a strange time of mystery and confusion, but when in doubt, remember that there are so many people going through this just like you.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m not going to lie, it is a sad time saying goodbye to the people that you have spent so much time with over the last few years, but the important thing is to keep in mind the amazing journey that is ahead of you. Take advantage of everything that Muhlenberg has to offer you. If you get to school and feel nervous and out of place, good; that is how everyone feels. This is a life changing time for you, but I promise if you give things a chance it will only get better. College really does go fast so be sure to enjoy all of the amazing things that will be coming your way.</p>
