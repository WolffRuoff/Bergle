					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>This past week was a pretty typical week of college. I am just starting to get in the groove of the semester and work is starting to pick up! There was a really pretty snowfall a few days ago&#8230;although, I truly could have dealt without the wind gusts while walking to class.</p>
<p>Friday I went to an a capella concert in the Red Door, an event space in the student union. It was packed and it was really fun to go with my friends. 			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/01/bittersweet-goodbyes/" rel="bookmark">Bittersweet Goodbyes</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/01/bittersweet-goodbyes/" title="4:19 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 29, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/ml24513/" title="View all posts by Megan">Megan</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>My weekend visit to Muhlenberg was bittersweet! It was so nice to see all of my friends, especially those who were abroad last semester, but also a little sad to leave. I began to think about all the spring traditions I&#8217;m going to miss while abroad this semester. Then my friend Jena, who is also going on the program, reminded me how incredibly lucky we are to feel a little sad about leaving for the semester. I know that sounds strange to say that we are slightly sad to be going to Europe, but the fact that we are and are only leaving Muhlenberg for one semester, only to return in the fall for senior year just shows how much we really love this place and our friends here. All of this being said, as much as I am clearly obsessed with Muhlenberg, I am also incredibly stoked for Dublin! I got a chance to hear stories from my friends who were abroad last semester. Every single one of them had amazing things to say about the countries they studied in and visited.</p>
<p>While on campus this weekend, I wanted to experience things that I could only find in Allentown at Muhlenberg. So on Friday night, my friends and I went to the Red Door to hear some a capella. One of Drew University&#8217;s a capella groups joined with two of our a capella groups, Inacchord and Dynamics, to have a free concert. Let me tell you, I LOVE a good a capella concert, especially at Muhlenberg. We have such talented singers! The Red Door was packed! Every seat was taken, so people sat and stood anywhere they could! One of my friends who I went with was Danielle, a fellow blogger, but we also ran into Ginelle, another blogger.</p>
<div id="attachment_1842" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_08932.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1842" class="size-medium wp-image-1842" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_08932-300x224.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="224" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_08932-300x224.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_08932-1024x764.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1842" class="wp-caption-text">My friend Naomi Leslie singing a solo for the Dynamics at the a capella concert.</p></div>
<div id="attachment_1841" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_09011.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1841" class="size-medium wp-image-1841 " src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_09011-300x300.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_09011-300x300.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_09011-150x150.jpg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_09011-1024x1024.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_09011.jpg 1936w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1841" class="wp-caption-text">(from right to left)<br />Danielle, Ginelle, and I in the Red Door. <p>On Sunday afternoon, Jena and I said our official Muhlenberg goodbyes to our friends and headed back to her house in Philly. Her parents took us out to a nice dinner in Philly, followed by a quick driving tour of the city to all of its historic locations. It was a really nice way to relax with all the pre-leaving jitters. After helping Jena pack up the last of her suitcases, we are both all officially set to go abroad Tuesday night! So the next time I&#8217;ll be writing I&#8217;ll be on the other side of the pond in Ireland! See you later America!</p>
<div id="attachment_1843" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_0932.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1843" class="size-medium wp-image-1843 " src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_0932-300x300.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_0932-300x300.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_0932-150x150.jpg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_0932-1024x1024.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/01/IMG_0932.jpg 1936w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1843" class="wp-caption-text">Our friends Maddie and Ali saying goodbye in the cold air as Jena and I drove away.</p></div>

			<div id="post-1823" class="post-1823 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/01/off-the-major-path/" rel="bookmark">Off the Major Path</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/01/off-the-major-path/" title="4:39 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 28, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>One of the great things about a liberal arts college, especially Muhlenberg, is that there are an array of courses that anyone can take and count towards the general academic requirements needed to graduate. While some people don&#8217;t love them, I have been surprised by the impact some of the classes and teachers have had on me throughout my college career. I have loved most of my professors and classes but I just wanted to name a few that were not classes towards my majors in business or theatre but still had a major impact.</p>
<p>1. <strong>Shakespeare with Dr. Coppa</strong>&#8211; I loved having this class with Dr. Coppa. She is so passionate when she teaches and really makes you invested and interested in whatever topic she is discussing. I mean, she made me enjoy the Shakespearian histories and want to study them in depth. It takes a great teacher to do that.</p>
<p>2. <strong>FYS with Dr. London</strong>&#8211; My first year seminar was nothing like I thought it was going to be. I expected it to be lecture style and a class to learn about college writing. But it was so much more than that. With my class, we explored through the arts what inspired us. We even put on a performance at the end of the semester. It was such a creative way to learn and a great class to have as I was transitioning into college.</p>
<p>3. <strong>Italian with Sra. Zanchettin</strong>&#8211; I knew I had to take a language and I wanted to explore something other than Spanish which I had been taking for years. I took a risk and had a blast learning a new language. Sra. Zanchettin really made the class interactive and fun while making sure the class was progressing quickly. It sparked my love for the Italian language and it helped me pick Italy as the country I studied abroad in.</p>
<p>4. <strong>Tap with Shelley Oliver</strong>&#8211; I have always loved tap but throughout my four years at Muhlenberg, I have been able to learn so much about a new style of tap I had never really explored. Along with that, Shelley makes tap fun and is really willing to help anyone who puts in the effort. If anyone is looking for a fun workout, I would recommend tap 100 percent.</p>
<p>There are so many other classes that I really enjoyed but these are a couple that I would recommend to anyone who wants to explore something new.</p>
