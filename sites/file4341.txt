					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>This week, I decided to tag along with a couple of friends who were doing a Sociology project having to do with breaking the class system. They decided to have one person walk around the Farmers Market in a pink snuggie. Here&#8217;s a video of <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cIPDV3Xs2pk&amp;feature=youtu.be">Noah modeling his pink snuggie.</a></p>

They were delicious!)</p></div>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/10/i-get-to-vote-for-the-next-president/" rel="bookmark">I get to vote for the next president?</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/10/i-get-to-vote-for-the-next-president/" title="9:09 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 4, 2012</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>With the election just about a month away and the presidential debate being televised this week, there is a lot of talk about the presidential candidates and the election all over campus. There is even a series of events leading up to the election on campus called the Muhlenberg College 2012 Election Series. Click here for more information about those.</p>
<p><a title="2012 Election Series" href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/pdf/main/academics/polisci/Electionseriesbrochurefinal.pdf">http://www.muhlenberg.edu/pdf/main/academics/polisci/Electionseriesbrochurefinal.pdf</a></p>
<p>These are ways to help educate, inform and discuss the topics surrounding the election coming up in November.</p>
<p>One event that I went to also happened to be the first theatre and dance production of the semester called &#8220;44 Plays for 44 Presidents,&#8221; and it is just what it sounds like. It is 44 plays for each president of the United States.  There are 44 productions of this show happening all over the country, and Muhlenberg was picked as one of the places to put the production on. Unlike the history classes we took in high school, this show was very fun, fast-paced and informative as well. When I walked away from the production, I felt like it showed the audience where we came from as a nation and to think about that fact that by voting, there is the opportunity to affect who becomes our nation&#8217;s next leader.</p>
<div id="attachment_1303" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-1-.jpeg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1303" class="size-medium wp-image-1303" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-1--300x199.jpeg" alt="" width="300" height="199" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-1--300x199.jpeg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-1-.jpeg 960w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1303" class="wp-caption-text">Picture of the 44 Plays Production. Pictures by KeneK photography</p></div>
<div id="attachment_1304" style="width: 209px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-2-.jpeg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1304" class="size-medium wp-image-1304" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-2--199x300.jpeg" alt="" width="199" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-2--199x300.jpeg 199w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-2-.jpeg 639w" sizes="(max-width: 199px) 100vw, 199px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1304" class="wp-caption-text">Picture of the 44 Plays Production. Pictures by KeneK photography</p></div>
<div id="attachment_1305" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-3.jpeg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1305" class="size-medium wp-image-1305" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-3-300x199.jpeg" alt="" width="300" height="199" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-3-300x199.jpeg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/44-plays-pic-3.jpeg 960w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1305" class="wp-caption-text">Picture of the 44 Plays Production. Pictures by KeneK photography</p></div>
<p><iframe src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/SfkE-TilxbI?rel=0" frameborder="0" width="560" height="315"></iframe></p>
<p>At the end of the production, the cast members asked the audience that if they had not registered to vote that they do so. I am going to ask the same of everyone reading this blog as well. If you are not registered to vote, please do so. <strong>You can register in the Community Engagement office until October 9<sup>th</sup>, which is the deadline to register to vote for the upcoming election.</strong> Get informed, know the facts, register and go out and vote on Election Day. You have an amazing opportunity to impact who our next president will be so don’t throw that right away but choose to register and vote instead.</p>

			<div id="post-1296" class="post-1296 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/10/karibu-mwezi-wa-kumi-welcome-october/" rel="bookmark">Karibu mwezi wa kumi! (Welcome October!)</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/10/karibu-mwezi-wa-kumi-welcome-october/" title="1:11 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 3, 2012</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/edward/" title="View all posts by Edward">Edward</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<div id="attachment_1297" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/DSC00642.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1297" class="size-large wp-image-1297" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/DSC00642-1024x768.jpg" alt="Travel Lecture!" width="640" height="480" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/DSC00642-1024x768.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/10/DSC00642-300x225.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-1297" class="wp-caption-text">An Ecolo-man-icy class (Ecology, Management, Policy)</p></div>
<p>Hey there everyone. Happy October! I can&#8217;t believe September is already over. Things have been going great over here in Tanzania. I think I might have got a little stomach bug, but I&#8217;m fairly confident it isn&#8217;t Ebola so, hey, that&#8217;s good news!</p>
<p>Classes have been going pretty well. Got a B+ on my first Ecology paper and an A+ on my Swahili Language and Culture composition (One might say my Kiswahili is &#8220;Nzuri sana&#8221;). The assignments keep rolling in and this week and the next promise to be busy ones, with two environmental policy papers, a worksheet and exam in Swahili and a poster session for wildlife management!</p>
<p>This Friday is our expedition into Ngorongoro Crater, which is the trip I&#8217;ve been looking forward to the most. The term &#8220;field trip&#8221; really takes on a whole new meaning here. We&#8217;re sincerely taking trips into the field to do field work!</p>
<p>Above is a picture of where we had our lectures this morning. It was an awesome cross-disciplinary kind of thing where the Ecology, Wildlife Management, and Environmental Policy professors each took turns giving parts of the lecture about the issues facing the area that we were in. The view was stunning and almost made up for 3 hours of lecture.</p>
<p>Hopefully I&#8217;ll be updating after Ngorongoro with pictures of all the super rare game I spot (or maybe just a bunch of pictures of wildebeests, if the game drive stinks).</p>
