<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Stress Awareness Month</strong></span></p>
<p>April is Stress Awareness Month! It’s time to deal with your stress so that you can be successful in the final few weeks of school. With finals looming, its easy to get overwhelmed. Here are some tips to help you deal with your stress:</p>
<ul>
<li>Get outside and get active! Whether it’s going for a walk or a run, just get out there and get your endorphins flowing.</li>
<li>Try yoga. The deep breathing techniques and poses help reduce stress.</li>
<li>Listen to music. Research has shown that music can relieve stress by triggering biochemical stress reducers.</li>
<li>Laugh! Laughing can reduce the physical effects of stress, such as fatigue.</li>
<li>Chew gum. Research has shown that the act of chewing gum can reduce cortisol levels, lowering stress levels.</li>
<li>Kiss someone! Science suggests kissing releases chemicals that eases hormones related with stress, like cortisol.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Relay for Life</span></strong></p>
<p>In May 1985, Dr. Gordy Klatt walked and ran for 24 hours around a track in Tacoma, Washington, ultimately raising $27,000 to help the American Cancer Society fight the nation’s biggest health concern, cancer. A year later, 340 supporters joined the overnight event. Since those first steps, the Relay For Life movement has grown into a worldwide phenomenon, raising nearly $5 billion to fight cancer. Donate or join a Relay for Life team here at Muhlenberg!</p>

<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Beating Spring Allergies</strong></span></p>
<p>About 40 million people in the U.S. have some type of “indoor/outdoor” allergy. Research shows that there is a genetic component to allergies. They typically develop during your childhood, but in some people they develop later, after exposure to certain environmental factors such as diesel particulate. One way to beat spring allergies is to treat them early! Spring pollen starts much earlier than people think, so if you take seasonal allergy medications you should start taking them in mid- to late-February. Another way to beat spring allergies is to avoid areas with high pollen and mold, especially when you are exercising. For example, opt for tennis on cement courts instead of going golfing. Also, even though fresh air is good for you, if you have really bad allergies you might be better off exercising inside when the pollen count is high. For those of you that have glasses or contacts, choose glasses over contacts, or wear sunglasses to protect you eyes. Finally, simply washing your hands, rinsing your eyes, and showering can get rid of pollen and mold spores that you come into contact with when you are working outside.</p>

<p>Send Silence Packing is a nationally recognized traveling exhibition of 1,100 donated backpacks, representing the number of college students lost to suicide each year.  The program is designed to raise awareness about the incidence and impact of suicide, connect students to needed mental health resources, and inspire action for suicide prevention.  At each exhibit of  Send Silence Packing 1,100 backpacks are displayed in a high-traffic area of campus, giving a visual representation of the scope of the problem and the number of victims. Muhlenberg has been chosen to host Send Silence Packing this year!</p>
