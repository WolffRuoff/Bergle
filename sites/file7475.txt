					<div class="entry-content">
				<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00006-e1377455550510.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2377" alt="DSC00006" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00006-e1377455550510-168x300.jpg" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00006-e1377455550510-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00006-e1377455550510-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00007-e1377455604657.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2378" alt="DSC00007" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00007-e1377455604657-168x300.jpg" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00007-e1377455604657-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00007-e1377455604657-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00005-e1377455513272.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2376" alt="DSC00005" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00005-e1377455513272-168x300.jpg" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00005-e1377455513272-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC00005-e1377455513272-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>When it comes to the library there&#8217;s the good, the bad, and the ugly. This is the place where work is plentiful and something inside of you cringes at the sight of it. It is where people are reminded of the mid terms and finals that came before them. The bottom line is as empty as this place looks now, it won&#8217;t be like this much longer. Soon this place will be filled with everyone from any grade and any major. People will come and go at all times of the day and night in their sweat pants and flip flops.</p>
<p>For most people like myself attempting to do work in the room just won&#8217;t work. Whether you live in an off campus house, mile house, Benfer, an apartment, Walz, Brown, Prosser, or anywhere else, doing work right next to a bed is next to impossible. That two hour nap is usually a result of telling yourself you were only going to lay down for a few minutes.</p>
<p>When it comes to studying and getting work done there is one tip I can give to help make things a little easier, go out and get some kind of exercise. I am not saying that you should go lift some crazy amounts of weight, just do something. A light jog or even a nice walk can really help get the blood flowing in your body. After sitting around crammed at a desk all day, it is always a good idea to get your muscles stretched out. This is a great way to break up a long day of studying and will really help you re focus for that big test at the end of the week. In the end just remember one important thing, less than 120 days until winter break.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

			<div id="post-2361" class="post-2361 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/08/introduction/" rel="bookmark">Introduction</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/08/introduction/" title="9:20 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">August 20, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I am glad to say that this will be my first of many posts to come in the future. I will be writing about a lot of different events, activities, stories, topics, and thoughts that I have over the coming year. Before I get into any of that, I would like to take some time to introduce myself to you and let you know more about the person that will be blogging to you.</p>
<p>I am going into my junior year here at Muhlenberg and could not have been happier with the way the first half of my college career has begun. I am a double major in business and accounting, and I am a member of the baseball team. Like so many people at Muhlenberg, I am just another New Jersey kid that found myself at what I believe to be such a great college choice.</p>
<p>We are now just a week away from getting back to school, with classes right around the corner. To the returning students, it is that time where we once again say bye to all our friends from home and look forward to seeing all of our friends at school. To the incoming freshmen, it is a strange time of mystery and confusion, but when in doubt, remember that there are so many people going through this just like you.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m not going to lie, it is a sad time saying goodbye to the people that you have spent so much time with over the last few years, but the important thing is to keep in mind the amazing journey that is ahead of you. Take advantage of everything that Muhlenberg has to offer you. If you get to school and feel nervous and out of place, good; that is how everyone feels. This is a life changing time for you, but I promise if you give things a chance it will only get better. College really does go fast so be sure to enjoy all of the amazing things that will be coming your way.</p>

			<div id="post-2364" class="post-2364 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/08/muhlenpacking-and-introduction/" rel="bookmark">Muhlenpacking and Introduction</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/08/muhlenpacking-and-introduction/" title="9:20 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">August 17, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hello! My name is Emily, and this fall marks my debut at college!</p>
<div id="attachment_2365" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a style="line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC_9631.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2365" class="size-medium wp-image-2365" alt="DSC_9631" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC_9631-300x199.jpg" width="300" height="199" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC_9631-300x199.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/DSC_9631-1024x680.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2365" class="wp-caption-text">Me, enjoying a beautiful day at the National Zoo! I really like elephants.</p></div>
<p>I am so excited to begin my freshman year at &#8216;Berg in&#8230;6 days (wow, is it that soon?). I&#8217;m from the D.C. area on the Virginia side, and, at 3.5 hours away, Muhlenberg is pretty far from home. Apart from my roommate and a few kids from Facebook, I really don&#8217;t know anyone attending Muhlenberg. It&#8217;s a little intimidating, but I can&#8217;t wait to make new friends. I&#8217;m a pretty undecided student, and my interests are varied, from theatre to math to psychology, and I&#8217;ll be exploring a bit before I declare any major. I am also a Dana Scholar. I&#8217;m not quite sure what activities I&#8217;ll be involved in on campus, mostly because I&#8217;m wondering how I can fit everything in!</p>
<p>With school in only 6 days (and I&#8217;m leaving in 5), you&#8217;d think I&#8217;d be prepared. I think I&#8217;m finally done shopping, but my packing situation is in what I&#8217;d like to call phase 1&#8230;</p>
<div id="attachment_2366" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/IMG_5245.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2366" class="size-medium wp-image-2366 " alt="IMG_5245" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/IMG_5245-300x225.jpg" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/IMG_5245-300x225.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/IMG_5245-1024x768.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2366" class="wp-caption-text">I hope I have enough boxes for all of this&#8230;</p></div>
<p>This summer, I&#8217;ve been pretty busy. I traveled to Paris with my family, which was lovely (although it was clear that my French level is minimal, so it&#8217;s a good thing I&#8217;ll be taking French this semester&#8230;). Then I worked at a Jewish summer camp during the day, and rehearsed a production of Grease at night (I played Patty Simcox, head cheerleader). Apart from that, I&#8217;ve been spending time with my friends and family before we go our separate ways. It&#8217;s been one of my favorite summers ever, a perfect way to end my life in Virginia before starting in Allentown.</p>
<p>While I&#8217;m excited to leave, I will admit that I&#8217;m somewhat anxious. Muhlenberg, clubs, classmates, college in general&#8230;it&#8217;s all a little intimidating. Luckily, Muhlenberg has been making the transition easier. Everyone I&#8217;ve talked to, whether student or staff has answered all of my questions and generally been very welcoming. I&#8217;ve got pre-college jitters, and I&#8217;m eager to see what Muhlenberg has in store for me.</p>
