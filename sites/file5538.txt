			<p><em>With the spring sports season just about wrapped up, we will be taking a closer look at Muhlenberg‚Äôs senior student-athletes.</em></p>
<p><em>In the coming days, the MuhlBox will feature some of the highlights from the traditional senior question and answer profiles that run with the programs for Senior Day.</em></p>
<p><em>We start with women&#8217;s tennis, which beat Ursinus, 8-1, in its Senior Day match. Seniors¬†Emily Robbins and Lauren Wink were honored before the match.</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/robbinshs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1112" title=" Emily Robbins" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/robbinshs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Emily Robbins<br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>What‚Äôs the most memorable moment from your time with the tennis team? </strong>One of my favorite memories is a bus ride last year with the four seniors. They knew how to keep us all laughing, but get us ready for the match at the same time. I also enjoy our team dinners (especially at Wegmans) before important matches. üôÇ<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>Who‚Äôs your favorite pro player? </strong>I love watching Roger Federer play because of how hard he works and his strategy in matches. I have seen him live a few times, and he is incredible.<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>How have you been able to balance your time to make the Dean‚Äôs List the past two semesters? </strong>I really focus on my schoolwork and make that a priority. I enjoy spending time with my friends and family, which is also motivation for me to get my work done.<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>What made you choose psychology as your major?</strong> I took Intro to Psychology with Dr. Rudski and was very intrigued by the information we were learning. I ended up finishing my major in three semesters because I enjoyed my psychology classes that much.<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>What are your future plans?</strong> I intend to use the next year to do research and apply to graduate school in 2013 in neuropsychology to do concussion research.<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>What kind of person is Lauren Wink?</strong> She works hard, but also knows how to have fun. She is a great friend and truly enjoys spending time with the team. Even though she‚Äôs student teaching this semester, she joins us at practice whenever she can, which shows her dedication.<strong></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/winkhs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1113" title="Lauren Wink" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/winkhs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Lauren Wink</strong></p>
<p><strong>What made you decide to join the team as a junior? </strong>My parents always told me to live my dreams. With their motivation and encouragement, I was able to work hard and join the team as a junior. I always played tennis with my family, but never competitively. I was an avid soccer player growing up, and unfortunately soccer and tennis were the same season in high school. The summer after I had back surgery, I shied away from competing in physical contact sports and increased my time spent playing tennis. It was at that time that I decided to take lessons and try out for the team at Muhlenberg. 						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/05/02/where-have-you-gone-joe-dimaggio/" rel="bookmark">Where Have You Gone, Joe DiMaggio?</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/05/02/where-have-you-gone-joe-dimaggio/" title="12:19 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2012-05-02T12:19:54-04:00">May 2, 2012</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredoniahs.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-1102" title="Chris Manfredonia" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredoniahs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="151" /></a>Over the last 24 games of the 2012 season, Muhlenberg second baseman Chris Manfredonia hit .320, raising his average from .260 to .299.</p>
<p>A solid end to a promising first season of collegiate baseball, especially for someone who missed most of his senior season in high school due to a back injury, but nothing extraordinary.</p>
<p>Except for the fact that Manfredonia had at least one hit in each of those 24 games.</p>
<p>Manfredonia broke a 25-year-old school record for longest hitting streak. But the streak was so inconspicuous that it flew under even his own radar until he surpassed the previous mark of 17 games in the second game of the Gettysburg doubleheader.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredonia3.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1103" title="Chris Manfredonia" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredonia3.jpg" alt="" width="250" height="468" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredonia3.jpg 250w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredonia3-160x300.jpg 160w" sizes="(max-width: 250px) 100vw, 250px" /></a>&#8220;After the Gettysburg game a couple of guys came up to me and told me that I broke the record for longest hitting streak,&#8221; said Manfredonia. &#8220;I thought they were joking.&#8221;</p>
<p>Manfredonia had exactly one hit in 18 of the 24 games. In 15 of the 24 games, his first hit came in the sixth inning or later &#8211; including the record-breaking single against Gettysburg, which came on a two-strike pitch in the ninth. That was one of five ninth-inning streak-extenders.</p>
<p>True to form, Manfredonia made sure his streak would carry over into 2013 by going 1-for-6 in each game of Sunday‚Äôs season-ending twinbill vs. Johns Hopkins. He beat out a slow roller to third with the bases loaded in the eighth inning of the opener and went opposite field for his only double of the season in the sixth inning of Game 2.</p>
<p>By comparison, Kelly Kline of the Muhlenberg softball team had a school-record 25-game hitting streak this season in which she batted .512. Joe DiMaggio hit .406 in his Major League Baseball-record 56-game streak in 1941.</p>
<p>This wasn&#8217;t so much &#8220;Joltin&#8217; Joe&#8221; as it was &#8220;Consistent Chris.&#8221;</p>
<p>‚ÄúI just try to not get too low, stay on an even keel and play the game with a consistent approach,‚Äù said Manfredonia.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredonia4.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-1104" title="Chris Manfredonia" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredonia4.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="405" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredonia4.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/manfredonia4-222x300.jpg 222w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>Of course, you can&#8217;t get hits without putting the ball in play, and Manfredonia did plenty of that. He struck out 10 times in his 50 pre-streak at-bats but whiffed only 11 times in 97 at-bats during the streak.</p>
<p>‚ÄúI was striking out a little more early in the year than I would have liked,‚Äù he said. ‚ÄúSo I tried to get fastballs early in the count, make hard contact and put the ball in play.‚Äù</p>
<p>A big Yankees fan, Manfredonia gained a new appreciation for DiMaggio&#8217;s streak, which would take almost another full year for him to approach.</p>
<p>‚ÄúIn the Major Leagues you play every single day; here you play only four days a week,‚Äù he said. ‚ÄúIt‚Äôs hard to stay healthy to be able to hit in 56 straight games, and you have to get lucky once in a while. I can‚Äôt even imagine doing that.‚Äù</p>
<p>One of the ironies of Manfredonia‚Äôs record streak is that before it got going, he was on his way to a different school record. He had six sacrifices, one short of the record, halfway through the season, but as the hits kept coming and he moved up in the order, the bunt sign stopped coming. Another unfortunate irony is that most of his streak coincided with a team losing streak that turned the program‚Äôs best start in 84 years into a 19-21 final record.</p>
<p>So would Manfredonia, the would-be king of sacrifices turned king of hitting streaks, be willing to sacrifice his streak for a few more wins next year?</p>
<p>‚ÄúAny day!‚Äù he said.</p>

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>When she traveled to Italy with the Muhlenberg women‚Äôs basketball team during the summer of 2010, Alexandra Chili fell in love with the culture and European way of life (especially the croissants and cappuccino).</p>
<p>Now, she wants to make another trek across the pond. But this time she hopes the trip will be primarily about business.</p>
<p>After completing one of the most accomplished careers in both Muhlenberg and Centennial Conference history, the two-time All-American was invited to the 2012 Women&#8217;s Final Four Combine held in late March in Denver.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/chilicombine.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-1096" title="Alexandra Chili" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/chilicombine.jpg" alt="Alexandra Chili" width="250" height="482" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/chilicombine.jpg 250w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/chilicombine-155x300.jpg 155w" sizes="(max-width: 250px) 100vw, 250px" /></a>There, Chili took the next step in a journey she hopes will end in a place not normally associated with Division III athletes: professional basketball.</p>
<p>Chili was part of a group of about 80 players from all NCAA divisions, as well as former college players, who took part in a series of drills and games played in front of scouts from the WNBA and several European professional leagues.</p>
<p>And just like she had during her time in a Mule uniform, Chili stood out from the rest, thanks in large part to her lethal jump shot.</p>
<p>The school and conference career three-point leader for men or women, Chili knocked down 21 of her 25 attempts from beyond the arc, more than any other player at the combine. Then when teams were picked for the games, she found herself alongside players from conferences like the Big Ten and Pac 12.</p>
<p>And after taking a short while to get used to the thin air in the Mile High City, she felt that she more than held her own despite being one of just a handful of Division III players invited to the event.</p>
<p>‚ÄúYou could really notice the elevation difference,‚Äù she said. ‚ÄúBut I feel like I played pretty well. I was a little nervous at first, but once we got going I didn‚Äôt really think about that.‚Äù</p>
<p>Chili played so well, in fact, that she‚Äôs already garnered interest from teams in Israel and Italy. Although there have not been any official offers yet, she received positive feedback from the combine‚Äôs director, who told her she had the ability to play professionally.</p>
<p>Now comes the hardest part for Chili, who made a habit of scoring points quickly: waiting. She‚Äôs done all she could to leave a positive lasting impression for the scouts, and as she gets ready for finals week, she‚Äôs anxiously hoping for the phone call that could come as early as today, or not until August or September.</p>
<p>But as she was during her entire career, Chili is prepared. After she dons her cap and gown and graduates in two-and-a-half weeks, she will continue working to improve her game. She has a summer job lined up in case she doesn‚Äôt hear back soon, but she‚Äôs also put her long-term plans temporarily on hold so she can chase her dream.</p>
<p>It‚Äôs not every day that an athlete is given the opportunity to make a living playing the sport they love. Then again, considering she‚Äôs the only player in conference history, male or female, to score 2,000 career points, Chili has shown she‚Äôs not like every other player.</p>
<p>Now she‚Äôs ready for the opportunity to take her game to the next level. All that she needs is a call.</p>
<p>‚ÄúI had never really thought about playing professionally until this season,‚Äù she said. ‚ÄúBut when I looked into it, I thought it might be pretty cool. It would be a great to get to live in Europe and experience the culture. I‚Äôll probably never have a chance like this again, so I want to make the most of it.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúHopefully I‚Äôll get a call soon; between that and graduation, it would really be time to celebrate.‚Äù</p>

	<article id="post-1077" class="post-1077 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-mens-basketball category-womens-basketball">
		<header class="entry-header">
						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/04/24/basketball-special-teams/" rel="bookmark">Basketball Special Teams</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/04/24/basketball-special-teams/" title="3:15 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2012-04-24T15:15:52-04:00">April 24, 2012</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/Special-Olympics.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-968" title="Special Olympics - Division III" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/03/Special-Olympics.jpg" alt="" width="220" height="238" /></a>NCAA Division III is engaged in a partnership with Special Olympics this year. According to the NCAA Website, the purpose of the partnership is to improve the lives of Special Olympics athletes through their involvement with Division III student-athletes and to foster a mutual learning experience between Division III student-athletes and Special Olympics athletes.</em></p>
<p><em>Last month, we had an <a title="A ‚ÄúSpecial‚Äù Saturday" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/03/20/a-special-saturday/">article </a>on four Muhlenberg athletes participating in a Special Olympics bowling event. Recently, the Mule basketball teams got to help out at a Special Olympics practice session. Junior Kate Clawson (who also participated in the bowling event) and sophomore Leeann Lanza wrote the following reports:</em></p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Kate Clawson</span></p>
<p>I was so excited to go to the Special Olympics basketball practice because I knew there would be many familiar faces, having just gone to a bowling event a few weeks prior. But I was just as excited to meet new athletes.</p>
<p>Just as I had expected, one of the friends I made there, Tarre, was super excited to see me and catch up. All of the other athletes were so happy to have us there, and you could tell on all of our faces that we were more than happy to be there too. We started off by stretching with them, then split the group into four lines and had relay races with dribbling. This was really fun for everyone because some of the Muhlenberg athletes also got to participate.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball1.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1079" title="Special Olympics basketball" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball1.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="160" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball1.jpg 500w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball1-300x96.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a></p>
<p>Next we split up into stations, for dribbling, passing, shooting and games. My group, which included junior Hunter Forsman and freshman AJ Dean, did passing. It was funny because some of the athletes thought passing was too easy, because some are very skilled, but we explained to them that even college athletes warm up and practice our passing.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball2.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1080" title="Special Olympics basketball" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball2.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="207" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball2.jpg 500w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball2-300x124.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a></p>
<p>Each group had so much fun, and the shooting group with the enthusiastic Austin Curry and Ryan Lagomarsino was definitely the loudest. It was great seeing all the athletes and teammates having fun together.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball3.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-1081" title="Special Olympics basketball" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball3.jpg" alt="" width="250" height="364" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball3.jpg 250w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball3-206x300.jpg 206w" sizes="(max-width: 250px) 100vw, 250px" /></a>We continued this by playing 3v3 games. This was my favorite part because I got to see the athletes really in their element. Their effort, teamwork and enthusiasm were so tremendous to see. Each time one of the athletes scored, the biggest smile came across their face, and high fives were a must. I loved watching my teammate Caitlyn excel. It seemed like she couldn‚Äôt miss!</p>
<p>At the end we huddled together in the middle, and just like after a practice at Muhlenberg, said MULES! on the count of 3. I think it was important to bring everyone together and have that sense of community, because not only did the Special Olympics athlete learn things from us that night, but we also learned about the heart, joy and courage that the Special Olympics athletes take pride in.</p>
<p>Hopefully we will be a part of this experience a few more times before the semester ends!</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Leeann Lanza</span></p>
<p>Our trip to help out with the Lehigh Valley Special Olympics team at Trexler Elementary School was one I will never forget. As soon as we arrived, we were greeted with excitement and enthusiasm by a few players who just could not wait to start playing. Both the men‚Äôs and women‚Äôs Muhlenberg basketball teams were there and ready to share everything we know about the game with players of all ages.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball4.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1082" title="Special Olympics basketball" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball4.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="221" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball4.jpg 500w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball4-300x132.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a></p>
<p>When we split into different stations, a few of my teammates and I, along with some of the men, taught the players some tricks about defense. Getting to help different groups of the players with defense and rebounding was so much fun because it turned into a one-on-one competition between all of us!</p>
<p>After our first practice with players of all ages, I had the opportunity to stay with our men‚Äôs team and scrimmage with the Special Olympics competitive men‚Äôs team (who will play in a tournament at Kutztown for the regional championship in May). This was a really fun experience for me getting to hang out with our men‚Äôs team and also getting to make some new friends. The Special Olympics organization allows these players to participate in high levels of competition while still conveying the importance of having FUN with the game!</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball5.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1083" title="Special Olympics basketball" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball5.jpg" alt="" width="400" height="285" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball5.jpg 400w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/04/SO_basketball5-300x213.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 400px) 100vw, 400px" /></a>I know that I speak for all of the Mules who got the opportunity to take part in this practice when I say that we really enjoyed ourselves and learned a lot from this experience. I think a new appreciation for basketball can be gained when playing with or even just watching some of these players. Their spirit and positive attitudes are just the motivation we need to get excited for our own season next winter. Hopefully we will all be able to get together again soon so we can cheer each other on!</p>

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>It‚Äôs the middle of April ‚Äì taxes are due, the Titanic has been down for 100 years, and even though softball and men&#8217;s lacrosse have the weekend off,¬†there‚Äôs plenty of Muhlenberg sports action.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-104" title="Mule Marquee Matchup" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee1-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>Women‚Äôs Tennis vs. Washington (Friday, 3:30)</strong></p>

Washington (5-3) and Muhlenberg (3-3) are in the middle of a five-team pack that includes Haverford (6-2), Dickinson (5-2) and Gettysburg (4-3). Those five teams are likely competing for three playoff spots, with Johns Hopkins and Swarthmore setting the pace at 6-0.</p>
<p>The Shorewomen are ranked eighth in the Atlantic South Region but have struggled since losing two of their top four players to injuries. Muhlenberg is coming off its most decisive win of the season, 8-1 against Ursinus.</p>
<p>Washington has won the last four meetings, including one in the 2009 CC playoffs. Last year‚Äôs 7-2 match featured many close sets.</p>
<p><strong>Tennis vs. Johns Hopkins (Saturday, 1:00); at McDaniel (Sunday, 12:00)</strong></p>
<p>A busy weekend of tennis for the Mules includes teams at opposite ends of the CC standings. For the women, Johns Hopkins is 6-0 in the CC and ranked eighth in Division III; McDaniel is 0-8 in the CC. For the men, 16th-ranked Hopkins is 6-0 and McDaniel is 0-5. The Muhlenberg men have not lost to McDaniel since 1982.</p>
<p><strong>Baseball vs. Swarthmore (Friday, 3:30); at Gettysburg (Saturday, 12:30 doubleheader)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules will try to halt their five-game skid at home against Swarthmore. Muhlenberg has not lost at home to the Garnet since 1995, extending the streak last year on a walkoff home run. The Mules then travel to Gettysburg, whom they swept last year.</p>

<p><strong>Women‚Äôs Lacrosse vs. Bryn Mawr (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>After three straight one-goal losses, the Mules are looking to take out their frustrations against a Bryn Mawr team that has not won a CC game since 2000.</p>
