<h3>IT Protects: Be aware of a current Gmail Phishing Scam</h3>
<p>A highly effective phishing scam is targeting Gmail users. DO NOT login to your account from any location other than our <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">Application Portal</a>. <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2017/01/20/it-protects-gmail-phishing-scam/">Learn more about this scam</a>.</p>

<h3>G Suite: Frequently Asked Questions</h3>
<div class="content-add05">
<p dir="ltr">This list is derived from engagement during training, as well as, support inquiries. We will be adding to this resource, as well as, the <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/gsuite/documentation/">G Suite documentation library</a> and <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/gsuite/videos">video library</a>.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Why am I still receiving emails in GroupWise?</strong><br />
Email will continue to flow into GroupWise. Gmail is the only email from which you should send mail. Emails sent to any muhlenberg.edu address within GroupWise will NOT flow to Muhlenberg Gmail accounts. We are asking people to use GroupWise as a cross-reference only, reporting any anomalies. If you wish to forward any emails from GroupWise (or grouped as attachments), you must forward to your Google Test Domain Address which is username@muhlenberg.edu.test-google-a.com. For example janesmith@<a href="http://muhlenberg.edu.test-google-a.com/">muhlenberg.edu.test-google-a.com</a> Use it for no other purpose except forwarding your mail to Google.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Is there a restriction on the number of email addresses I send to in a single email? Does it matter if they are internal or external email addresses?<br />
</strong>G Suite limits the amount of mail one user can send. Limits restrict the number of messages sent per day and the number of recipients per message.</p>
<p dir="ltr">If users exceed a G Suite email sending limit, they’ll see an error message, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>You have reached a limit for sending email.</li>
<li>You reached a Gmail sending limit.</li>
<li>You exceeded the maximum number of recipients.</li>
</ul>
<p>After reaching one of these limits, a user can&#8217;t send new messages for up to 24 hours. However, they can still access their G Suite account and receive incoming email. The user can also access their other Google services. After this suspension period, sending limits are automatically reset and the user can resume sending mail.</p>
<p dir="ltr">To learn more about these restrictions read “<a href="https://support.google.com/a/answer/166852?hl=en">Gmail sending limits in G Suite</a>”</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>How do I access email Groups (formerly distributed lists in GroupWise)?</strong> <br class="kix-line-break" />Distributed email lists are now referred to as &#8220;Groups&#8221;. The ability to add Groups to our muhlenberg.edu directory is not an available feature &#8211; they will not auto-populate as you type if it is the first time you are sending to that Group. They are, however, available in the Groups apps, currently visible based on membership.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Note: Once you email your Group, they will be added to your Contacts and will auto-populate.</p>
<p dir="ltr">We are currently working on a new group management model. Departments &amp; offices will be contacted regarding the management of their group&#8217;s membership.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Supporting documentation: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/gsuite/MuhlenbergGroups.pdf">Contacting Muhlenberg Groups (formerly distribution lists)</a>‌</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Do I need to logout of G Suite apps?</strong> <br class="kix-line-break" />Yes. There is no imposed timeout on G Suite apps. While we have a 15 minute timeout on the Application Portal, you are still logged into G Suite. It is important that you logout of G Suite (and other apps) accessed via the Application Portal if you wish to end the session. This is critically important when accessing your account on a public computer.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Supporting documentation: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/gsuite/LoggingoutofGSuiteapps.pdf">Logging out of GSuite apps</a></p>
<p><strong>Can I send an email from an alias?<br />
</strong>If you want to always send from your other address, you&#8217;ll need to change both your default &#8220;From&#8221; and &#8220;reply-to&#8221; address. If you only change the &#8220;From&#8221; address, replies will go to your original Gmail address by default.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Supporting documentation: <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/gsuite/Sendingemailfromanotheraddressoralias.pdf">Sending email from another address or alias</a>‌</p>
<p><strong>I sent an email to myself and did not receive. Why?</strong><br />
By default, Google does not send an email sent to an alias to your inbox &#8211; Google presumes you do not want the clutter of such emails. If you sent a message to yourself using an alias and NOT your official muhlenberg email address (<a href="mailto:firstnamelastname@muhlenberg.edu">firstnamelastname@muhlenberg.edu</a>), it will not be delivered to your inbox. For example, johnsmith will work but jsmith will not. However, this email will appear in your Sent mail and All Mail. If you wish to send emails to yourself and see them in your inbox, please use your official email address, not an alias.</p>
<p><strong>When I click on an email address in a browser window or MS Office application, I am kicked to GroupWise. How do I get this work in Gmail?<br />

<article id="post-558" class="post post-558 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-printing-and-wepa">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=558" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-12-19T16:04:51-04:00">December 19, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=558" rel="bookmark">Update on Print Quota Usage</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172331" rel="category">Printing and WEPA</a></li>
								<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=558#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Now that fall term has completed, we&#8217;ve grabbed the latest print usage data. I want to be clear about one thing &#8211; this data will not change our quota. The same policy is in place as before &#8211; $75 standard quota for full time students with the option to supplement with a once-per-semester $50 if there is a &#8220;print-heavy&#8221; course. But it&#8217;s worth looking at the data.</p>
<p>I want to present the results as close to the exact academic term as possible, without any skewing from post-finals printing (small volume as that is, admittedly). I originally broke apart use pre and post finals, but there actually wasn&#8217;t as much of a spike as one might expect from exams, so I&#8217;m keeping all the data lumped together. The biggest comparisons are from last term, when we first rolled out WEPA and did not have a quota.</p>
<p>First, the overall quota usage chart. This is the amount of quota used, by student. Note that the numbers on the x-axis are just a count of users, and are not Berg IDs or anything else that ties a specific amount of quota usage with a specific student. The user population includes Wescoe students.</p>
<p><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Fall-2016-Print-Usage.png"><img class="alignnone wp-image-559 size-full" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Fall-2016-Print-Usage.png" alt="fall-2016-print-usage" width="653" height="414" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Fall-2016-Print-Usage.png 653w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Fall-2016-Print-Usage-300x190.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 653px) 100vw, 653px" /></a></p>
<p>You&#8217;ll notice a few things right off the bat. First, the graph is very linear. R2 is .905. This compares to an R2 of only .63 in Spring. The lower score in the spring was because we saw a dramatic and non-linear rise in usage at the extreme right. The heaviest of users printed far more than others. A smooth graph is neither &#8220;good&#8221; nor &#8220;bad&#8221; but I am highlighting that usage did not have the same spike as in the Spring. I think this does mean a more egalitarian printing environment &#8211; it&#8217;s not 5% of users accounting for 50% of all printing.</p>
<p>Some stats are below. Note that &#8220;quota&#8221; means &#8220;however much money we loaded to given user.&#8221; For most students this was $75. For Pell-eligible students it was $120, and for 115 students there was an additional $50 added as a &#8220;supplement&#8221; for print-heavy courses (note: this is an option for all students, once per term). While the good news is that this let&#8217;s us measure sufficiency of quota, the bad news is that I can&#8217;t directly gather an &#8220;average dollar amount spent&#8221; for the entire student community.</p>
<ul>
<li>90th percentile was 74.3% quota usage</li>
<li>17% of students did not print at all</li>
<li>27% of students used less than 5% of quota</li>
<li>76% of students used less than 50% of quota</li>
<li>1.7% (43 students) used up 100% of their quota</li>
<li>Equivalent of 693,433 single-sided Black and White pages printed based on total amount spent (compared to 1,119,405 equivalent pages in Spring)</li>
</ul>
<p>So what does this mean? We certainly don&#8217;t think it means that all but 43 students had &#8220;enough&#8221; print quota. We realize that some rationing did happen and that tough choices had to be made. But we also see that a large number of users did not use up all of the quota allotted, and more than 3/4 did not even use up half of their quota. Our hope is that this means fewer of those tough calls had to happen because we chose an appropriate quota amount.</p>
<p>The biggest number, though, is the decrease in printing from Spring. Instituting a quota, even with a supplement available to all students and concessions based on Pell-eligibility, decreased printing by a huge margin compared to Spring. And our ultimate goal with the quota is about sustainability and reducing paper usage on campus. As we&#8217;ve said before &#8211; decreasing student printing is only half of the equation, and conversations with faculty are ongoing. But we have to start somewhere, and we&#8217;re off to a great one.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=538" rel="bookmark">Getting to Google in 20  Million Short Steps</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=170064" rel="category">Communication &amp; Collaboration</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172338" rel="category">Google Apps</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=g-suite" rel="tag">g suite</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=538#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Getting the campus to Google &#8211; which involves moving nearly 20 million emails &#8211; is no small task. We thought we&#8217;d shed some light on the actual process.</p>
<p><strong>Final cut-over will be on December 27, 28, or 29</strong>. This is a few days before &#8220;G-Day&#8221; on January 2 when we&#8217;ll have workshops, training, and a number of other support options. But moving ahead a few days early is necessary &#8211; we need to make sure everything moves over correctly and time to make any needed course corrections.</p>
<p>First, a few reminders:</p>
<ul>
<li>data/old emails will not be migrated for student accounts</li>
<li>all faculty/staff old emails <em>not in an archive</em> will be migrated</li>
<li>GroupWise will keep working after December 29, but only as a back-up (see below)</li>
<li>The <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">Application Porta</a>l will your access path to Google Suite (Muhlenberg Network Username for login, usually firstnamelastname for faculty &amp; staff or ab123456 for students)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>GroupWise Archives</strong></p>
<p>GroupWise offers an &#8220;archive&#8221; feature that allows one to move emails out of one&#8217;s &#8220;live&#8221; mailbox into an offline space. This saves on quota, among other benefits. We have discouraged using this feature in preference to simply increasing quota, so most likely you aren&#8217;t using it. In fact, if you don&#8217;t distinctly remember configuring and turning on your archive, you almost certainly aren&#8217;t using it. Technically, however, if you have emails in your archive, they will <em>not</em> migrate over. We can only migrate emails in your live mailbox. If you have questions, contact the Support Desk.</p>
<p><strong>GroupWise Moving Forward</strong></p>
<p>As mentioned, GroupWise should only be used to cross-reference your GMail account during the first few days following the switch-up. Emails sent within the system &#8211; from one campus user to another &#8211; stay entirely internal. They never get off campus, and therefore never reach Google. This means that your Google inbox will not receive any email you send from GroupWise to another member of the college community. So it&#8217;s still there, it still works, but please don&#8217;t use it unless you have to.</p>
<p><strong>Migration</strong></p>
<p>The actual migration process has taken place in iterative steps. We did a first, large migration of all emails in the entire system up to those dated August 31, 2016 almost 2 months ago. For some users this took close to a month to complete. We then ran multiple successive migrations covering emails from shorter and shorter periods of time, slowly catching up to the latest emails. We&#8217;ve already run 2 migrations to get to 10/31. In a couple of weeks we&#8217;ll get to 11/30. We&#8217;ll probably run 3 to 4 more migrations in smaller and smaller chunks of time until we run just 1 day&#8217;s worth on 12/28 and then flip the switch.</p>
<p>All told, we&#8217;ll have spent 3 months on migrating email alone.</p>
<p><strong>What you can do to get ready for G Suite</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/11/23/gsuite-info-sessions-announced/">Attend a G Suite Information Session</a></li>
<li>Visit our Application portal at <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">login.muhlenberg.edu</a> to ensure you are able to login successfully.</li>
<li>Learn about G Suite on lynda.com (available via the application portal) and by visiting the <a href="https://gsuite.google.com/learning-center/" target="_blank">G Suite Learning Center</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Actual Cut-Over</strong></p>
<p>The switch itself is nearly instantaneous after the last migration. By the end of the day of 12/28, you will see G Suite among the options in the Application Portal. Simply click here and you&#8217;ll be routed to G Suite. It&#8217;s single sign-on so no need to log in again.</p>
<p>You&#8217;ll see a welcome screen the first time, but after that you can go straight into G Suite.</p>

<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">

		<p>Welcome to the first issue of our monthly newsletter! Our hope is to provide updates on our projects, address any concerns that have arisen since the last article, and speak towards the challenges facing information technology at Muhlenberg and in higher education.</p>
<p>Not all posts will be the same length with the same types of content, and it&#8217;s possible that we&#8217;ll miss the deadlines now and then. But these are our goals, along with advancing transparency into our operations and communication between OIT and the college community.</p>
<p><a href="#news">IT News</a><br />
<a href="#spotlight1">Project Spotlight: Google Suite for Education</a><br />

<h3 id="news">First, some IT News</h3>
<p>We&#8217;ve had a number of major announcements since the start of the fall. We&#8217;ve launched a new <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/10/a-single-application-portal-for-muhlenberg-web-services/">Application Portal</a> and released <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/09/13/qualtrics/">Qualtrics</a> <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/q_logo_banner-300x135-1.png"><img class="size-medium wp-image-490 alignleft" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/q_logo_banner-300x135-1-300x135.png" alt="q_logo_banner-300x135" width="300" height="135" /></a>as a new campus-wide survey software solution, for instance. <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/10/lynda-com-our-new-online-training-resource/">Lynda.com</a> comes to campus, as well, as a new powerful training tool for the whole community. We also went live across campus with the new Canvas Learning Management System, and have started on projects involving Zoom video conferencing <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu"><img class="alignright wp-image-492 size-medium" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-300x197.jpg" alt="lynda-com-logo" width="300" height="197" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-300x197.jpg 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-768x504.jpg 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-1024x671.jpg 1024w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-1200x787.jpg 1200w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo.jpg 1600w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>among many others. Back in June, we launched a new version of the iMuhlenberg mobile app that will bring some new capabilities to our mobile efforts.</p>
<p>A big piece of news is the <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/category/projects/printing-and-wepa/">institution of a print quota</a>. With sustainability in mind, we&#8217;ve started a new system whereby the first $75 of printing is provided &#8220;free&#8221; to all full-time students, with printing after that on a fee-per-page basis. We&#8217;ve already seen a marked dip in print usage this September, and hope that it&#8217;s a result of greater awareness of what and when to print. We are still working on classes or other training materials on digital note-taking and similar tools.</p>
<p>Finally, October was Cybersecurity Awareness Month. We have a <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/10/19/it-protects-working-together-to-create-a-secure-environment/">new blog post</a> about all the efforts we&#8217;re making for data security, including bits on encryption, dealing with Personal Identifying Information, and how to spot phishing scams.</p>
<h3 id="spotlight1">Project Spotlight: Google Suite for Education</h3>
<p>Google Suite for Education, formerly Google Apps for Education, is still on pace for a January 1 launch for all of campus. We&#8217;ve been relatively quiet on this project so far, as we&#8217;ve been in a &#8220;steady state&#8221; of testing, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-488 alignright" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-300x158.jpg" alt="g-suite-logo" width="300" height="158" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-300x158.jpg 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-768x404.jpg 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-1024x539.jpg 1024w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-1200x632.jpg 1200w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo.jpg 1900w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>but things will start to get &#8220;noisier&#8221; quite<br />
soon. We&#8217;ll have announcements on training, documents comparing features and tools between GroupWise and Google, and various other items intended to ease the transition.</p>
<p>Note that while faculty &amp; staff accounts will be fully migrated, with all existing GroupWise emails shifting over to the Google environment, students will receive an empty, pristine inbox. GroupWise will still be available through Spring term, and students can forward any needed emails to themselves from the old system. This decision is due to limitations on Google&#8217;s end on how much data we can migrate during our window of time.</p>
<p>OIT will be fully on Google by the end of October, so that we are actively working in the environment. Training for the college will start mid-November and run through the end of the semester. Moving email data takes multiple weeks, and we&#8217;ll start doing that for faculty and staff as soon as we can (there are a number of measures to make sure we have everything accurate right to the last minute before cut-over, so that nothing will be missed). Then we&#8217;ll modify some records and settings on the network and be ready to go in Google Suite when everyone returns for the new year.</p>
<p>During the first phase of implementation, training will focus on providing a smooth transition from GroupWise to Google Suite.  We want to ensure that the necessary information is available so that users can successfully perform their daily tasks.  Topics will include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>GMail – composing emails, moving from folders to labels, connecting with a mobile device</li>
<li>Calendar – scheduling events, creating reminders, creating new calendars, shared calendars &amp; settings</li>
<li>Contacts – Groups, Directory</li>
</ul>
<p>Training will be ongoing after the initial phase of implementation. Our team will continue to offer learning opportunities focused on the collaborative capabilities offered with the Google Suite. Topics will include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Drive &#8211; uploading files, sharing files and folders, version control</li>
<li>Docs/Sheets/Slides &#8211; creating files, concurrent editing</li>
<li>Groups &#8211; Host discussions, create forums, organize meetings and events</li>
<li>Integration of Google with Canvas and other systems</li>
</ul>

<article id="post-508" class="post post-508 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-phone-system tag-mitel tag-phone">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=508" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-11-01T09:17:34-04:00">November 1, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-12-20T16:48:31-04:00">December 20, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=61">Phedra Henninger</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=508" rel="bookmark">What to expect while you are expecting the new phone system</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172334" rel="category">Phone System</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=mitel" rel="tag">mitel</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=phone" rel="tag">phone</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=508#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>OIT is in the midst of the installation of the new campus wide phone system, based on Mitel. We want to provide further clarity about the project and additional details about what you can expect during this transition.</p>
<p><strong>Project Goals</strong></p>
<p>The goal of the first phase, to be completed in 2016, is to replace our aging legacy architecture, which is past its maintenance life and viability in a crisis. Detailed information about the early planning stages of this project are posted on our <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/09/new-phone-system/">IT blog</a>.</p>
<p>The new system provides the same capabilities you have today, as well as some popular, easy-to-use new features. After the initial installation is complete campus wide, we will be expanding the range of supported features and options. Your new Mitel phone is just the beginning.</p>
<p><strong>What to Expect</strong></p>
<p>There is an enormous amount of behind the scenes work accomplished by three different teams. This takes place over a three-week period prior to switching over each campus location to the new system; each week, we start the cycle over again for the next building.</p>
<p>The last time we replaced the phone system was 17 years ago. We will need access to the wall jacks and cables, often behind your desks.  This may require moving books, paperwork, and so on.  We are on tight schedules, and apologize if we ever seem hurried. We will strive to do the work with minimum disruption. Your understanding is greatly appreciated.</p>
<p><strong>Timeline<br />
</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>1-3 weeks prior to cut-over &#8211; Wiring work</strong>
<ul>
<li>Our wiring partner, Westside Hammer, will upgrade existing wiring or repair/improve wall jacks prior to the phones being placed.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>1-2 weeks prior to cut-over &#8211; Network upgrades</strong>
<ul>
<li>OIT staff will install and configure new network equipment in wiring closets. This may result in an early morning interruption of service, depending on your desktop configuration. We usually get these done prior to 7am. If we expect an extended outage, we will communicate that to you.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>1 week prior to cut-over &#8211; Placing the new phones</strong>
<ul>
<li>Staff from Frontier Communications, our phone partner, will set up your new phone. Your new Mitel phone is NOT ready for use; it still needs to be programmed and tested.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Cut-over day</strong><strong>– Go live</strong>
<ul>
<li>The specific day your new phone goes live, or is <strong>cut-over</strong><em>, </em>will be communicated in an e-mail when the date has been scheduled. We usually know about a week ahead of time and will provide that date as early as we can. Dates have been posted on the <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/31/phone-system-installation-schedule/">IT blog</a>, but are subject to change as we move across campus. We will be on-site making sure the new phones work. Documentation basic phone functionality, including how to setup your voicemail, will be provided in a hard copy at the time of service and is <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/documentation/Mitel-3300-training-guide.pdf">available online</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>&#8220;Who are these people?&#8221;<br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>OIT Staff (Sergio Maciujec, Stephen Small, and Joe Shigo)</strong></p>
<p>They are doing site surveys to assess what work needs to be done. They are also installing new network equipment in each building to prepare the infrastructure for the new phone system. In many cases they will be speaking with you personally to address any concerns you have and/or better understand your phone needs.</p>
<p><strong>Westside Hammer (Todd Hanzl and his team)</strong></p>
<p>They are preparing the building wiring to accept the new phones, which use a technology called &#8220;power over Ethernet&#8221; or POE. Some buildings required more work than others.</p>
<p><strong>Frontier Communications (Jim and Steve and their team)</strong></p>
<p>They are the Mitel experts and are deploying, configuring and generally doing the work of installing the new phone system.</p>
<p><strong>Frequently Asked Questions</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>What is &#8220;Cut-over&#8221;?</strong><br />
This is the process of actually “moving” your line from the old system to the new. It is very simple and should only take a few minutes per desk. The new phones can already accomplish most functions &#8211; we just need to route incoming calls to them. The only things we need to do are make the actual change in our database, then a few test calls to make sure everything is working.</li>
<li><strong>How can I access my voicemail?</strong><br />
Voicemail will work as soon as the phone is live. Because they are separate systems, though, you will need to remove all voicemails (listen and delete) from the old system before the cut-over. You may do so by dialing <a href="tel:4846643000">484-664-3000</a> to access the messages on the old system.New voicemail will start working immediately, but you won&#8217;t be able to retrieve messages until you go through the activation process, which includes setting a new PIN. The temporary PIN is “1111”.</li>
<li><strong>What type of support will be provided?</strong><br />
Members of OIT and Frontier will be visiting offices to answer any questions and do on-the-spot training throughout the day. We will also provide documentation on the basic functionality of the phone and it is <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/documentation/Mitel-3300-training-guide.pdf">available online</a>. Additional training materials are being developed and will be released at a later date.</li>
<li><strong>What are the new features?</strong>There are many new features with the phones, but some notable ones are:
<ul>
<li>Voicemail to email &#8211; receive messages straight to your inbox for listening on the go, on your phone, etc. This is on by default.</li>
<li>Twinning &#8211; concurrent ringing of your desk phone to your cell phone. We have to configure this, and you have the ability to turn it on and off at will. If you are interested in this feature, please contact <a href="mailto:support@muhlenberg.edu">support@muhlenberg.edu</a>.</li>
<li>The new system gives us a great deal of additional functionality and control that will allow for easier management, easier support for people changing offices or new faculty and staff coming on board.</li>
<li>There are many other features, as well. Our plan is to slowly implement these over time to those interested. For example, there are powerful desktop and mobile software clients that some may be interested.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<article id="post-489" class="post post-489 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-client-support-services tag-cyber-security tag-passwords tag-support tag-virus-scan">
	<!-- Grab the featured image -->
	
	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=489" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-10-19T12:06:52-04:00">October 19, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-10-19T12:21:14-04:00">October 19, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=61">Phedra Henninger</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=489" rel="bookmark">IT Protects: Working together to create a secure environment</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172350" rel="category">Client Support Services</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=cyber-security" rel="tag">cyber security</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=passwords" rel="tag">passwords</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=support" rel="tag">support</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=virus-scan" rel="tag">virus scan</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=489#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>October is National Cyber Security Awareness Month. This week’s theme is “Cyber from the Break Room to the Board Room” which focuses on the protection of personal and organizational data in the workplace. What better time to mention some of the processes already in place that contribute to protecting our digital work environment.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Weekly Virus Scan<br />
</strong>IT runs a weekly virus scan using Kaspersky anti-virus software. A weekly reminder is sent via email. Please remember to <strong>leave your computer on Wednesday nights</strong>. We encourage you to turn off your monitor.  Since launching Kaspersky in 2013 we’ve seen a dramatic decrease in virus outbreaks, and your cooperation is needed to keep this up.</li>
<li><strong>Managing College owned devices</strong>
<ul>
<li>College-owned Windows devices are managed using ZENworks. This tool, in use for many years, helps us deploy and update applications remotely and configure computers. The <strong>Zenworks Application Window</strong> allows the user to install select campus standard software (e.g., Office 2016).</li>
<li>College-owned Apple devices are managed using JAMF Casper Suite. This management software allows the College to supply applications and configuration settings on a large scale, improving the maintenance and security of these devices. In addition, this software includes a self-service application that allows the user to install select campus standard software (e.g., Office 2016).</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Here are simple ways you can personally help secure your data and work environment.</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Increase personal awareness</strong>
<ol>
<li>Lock your computer<br />
Perform a simple <strong>ctrl+alt+delete</strong> and lock your computer when leaving your desk.</li>
<li>Our department works diligently to filter out SPAM and phishing emails, but we can’t catch them all. If you question the validity of an email and/or see a suspicious attachment do not open the email, particularly the attachment. If someone is asking for personal information DO NOT share it. Be vigilant in the art of protecting your digital environment. Remember: OIT will NEVER ask for your username and password in an email, and will always direct you to one of our standard sites to login (e.g. – <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu" target="_blank">http://login.muhlenberg.edu</a>).</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li><strong>Strong passwords</strong><br />
The Office of IT (OIT) requires that passwords used to access any Muhlenberg College system be strong. It is advisable that all passwords used to access any system, even those not associated with Muhlenberg (e.g. Facebook, Google), be strong. Read IT’s “<a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/oit/about/policies_procedures/strong-passwords.html" target="_blank">Guidelines for Strong Passwords</a>”.</li>
<li><strong>Update and reboot</strong><br />
While we centrally manage College owned devices, providing security and the ability to rollout updates; it’s a healthy practice to check for Windows Updates on a regular basis. If you are not sure how to check for updates, contact <a href="mailto:support@muhlenberg.edu">support@muhlenberg.edu</a> for assistance. In addition to performing Windows Updates, make sure you reboot your computer on a regular basis. A simple reboot can clean up a whole host of issues that have resulted from multiple processes running for an extended period of time.</li>
<li><strong>Backup your data<br />
</strong>Institutional data should be stored on the Network. If you are storing any data locally on the device itself, please remember to back it up. If the device is corrupted during its lifecycle and needs to be rebuilt, any data stored locally will not be retrievable.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Some upcoming cybersecurity projects</strong></p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Encryption<br />
</strong>Over the next 6 months, we will be rolling out “full disk encryption” to select computers across campus. Users must enter an additional password to access data on a computer or even boot it up in some cases. Without this credential, the computer is effectively useless. Not only is this best practice in general, but a lost but encrypted computer is not considered a data breach. Best of all – it’s only an additional password and nothing else required of the user. All college-owned laptops will be encrypted first, then moving onto all computers of any kind that access covered data.</li>
<li><strong>Improved Windows Update Procedures<br />
</strong>We are looking into new, more reliable and less intrusive ways to update Windows machines.</li>
<li><strong>Cybersecurity training<br />
</strong>Topics such as how to avoid phishing scams, what is included under Personal Identifying Information, and how to use password managers will be covered in a series of short, easy-to-consume training videos. We hope to roll these out over the remainder of the academic year and the make it an ongoing offering.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Additional resources on Cyber Security</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.dhs.gov/topic/cybersecurity" target="_blank">Department of Homeland Security</a></li>
<li><a href="http://securingthehuman.sans.org/resources/newsletters/ouch/2016" target="_blank">OUCH! Security Awareness Newsletter</a> (securingthehumans.sans.org)</li>
<li><a href="https://staysafeonline.org/" target="_blank">StaySafeOnline.org</a></li>
</ul>
