			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/the-beginning-of-the-ends/" rel="bookmark">The Beginning of the End&#8217;s</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/the-beginning-of-the-ends/" title="2:46 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 5, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>During this past week as we get ready for fall break, the baseball team completed our fall season. Usually when the fall ends it is a nice chance to relax and enjoy the winter. This is when our schedules open up and we get to enjoy some well deserved time off. Nobody bats an eye at the thought of the fall ending, but this year it brings a much different feeling.</p>
<p>For four years we have gone out and competed throughout the fall and each year there is a different group of seniors that have graduated and are no longer there. I can remember getting off the bus my freshman year for our first practice. I was so nervous with no idea who anyone was or what I could expect at practice. I can remember all the upperclassmen at the time and all I could think about is how I wanted that to be me. As the years have gone by I have gotten myself into a position where I get to be one of those guys and I have enjoyed every second of it.</p>
<p>This also makes something else very real, this is the beginning of the different ends that will take place this year. This is the last ever fall practice I will have and this seems to be a theme that will keep popping up throughout the year. The last winter break, spring break, baseball game, or last day in general.</p>
<p>It is a sad thought that you can&#8217;t really let yourself think about. The last few years of my life have been centered around college and I can&#8217;t believe this is the last one. At this point I have learned to simply enjoy every moment of this final year.</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/sos/" title="2:45 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 5, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Have you ever been so stressed out that while you&#8217;re editing your essay in the middle of the Dining Commons, you just start crying?</p>
<p>No?</p>
<p>Just me?</p>
<p>Okay, perfect, guess I stand alone with this one then&#8230;</p>
<p>Since it&#8217;s Fall Break, professors kind of slam us with work, and this week, it wasn&#8217;t just a slam, it was more like a 5 lb brick being dropped from a 10 story building and landing on my head.</p>
<p>And it&#8217;s not because I wasn&#8217;t prepared.</p>
<p>Every weekend I finish my homework for the following Monday, Tuesday and Wednesday since I know I&#8217;m extremely busy those days and won&#8217;t be able to dedicate any time to do any other work.</p>
<p>Then professors decided to change a few assignments Sunday evening (the assignments I already completed the day before) and then a few others even decided to add new assignments that were not originally put on the Syllabus (since it would be &#8220;beneficial&#8221; to the topic we were on).</p>
<p>Sure, the new articles I had to read and write responses for, and the new essay I was assigned, were all possibly beneficial for my classes, they just weren&#8217;t beneficial for my mental stability.</p>
<p>I live an organized life.</p>
<p>I have a schedule.</p>
<p>I live by that schedule.</p>
<p>And I complete everything ahead of schedule so I can have time to take a breather and de-stress since I know how much I can handle and strategically arrange my schedule so that I do not get overwhelmed.</p>
<p>So when things get switched around and are completely out of my control, I freak out a bit and end up crying in the middle of the Dining Commons. (It was quite embarrassing, actually.)</p>
<p>I should probably take some time to learn how to handle stress in a different way, but wait&#8230; I have too much work to do so I guess I&#8217;ll have to add that to my schedule for next week.</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>If I survive the rest of this week,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<div id="post-3200" class="post-3200 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog tag-freshman tag-muhlenberg">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/the-first-month-of-college-in-summary/" rel="bookmark">The first month of college in summary</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/10/the-first-month-of-college-in-summary/" title="9:35 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 1, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Ever since I stepped on that plane to Philadelphia Airport on August 20th, I knew my life would change forever.</p>
<p>I recognize that a statement that broad could seem hyperbolic or even dramatic, but my flight out of LA was a one-way ticket&#8230; at least until Thanksgiving. While I sat on that 6-hour venture, I was highly aware of the world I was leaving, but with an optimistic outlook. After scrolling through old pictures, videos, and even ancient text conversations, I was ready for Muhlenberg.</p>
<p>Even then, I didn&#8217;t exactly understand what it meant that I was &#8220;ready,&#8221; I just felt it.</p>
<p>A couple days and goodbye&#8217;s later, I was alone, about to go to begin in the so called &#8220;real world.&#8221;</p>
<p>Well, I wasn&#8217;t actually alone, because by some divine coincidence, I met my five best friends on the first weekend.</p>
<p>All of us sat for dinner on the Sunday night before class started; without saying much, it was clear by our faces each of us had a hybrid of nervousness and excitement. This is it. Tomorrow is the day we start college.</p>
<p>I knew after two days, school was going to be hard. I envisioned countless late nights and long papers. But for some reason, that didn&#8217;t scare me. I may have been overwhelmed, but I knew this is what I signed up for.</p>
<p>After completing my first few essays, attending a couple parties, and several hours of hard wrestling, I felt myself beginning to settle into a rhythm.</p>
<p>No longer was I committed to a regimented 7-hour day of classes 8-3. Now I had maximum of 2 and a half hours of class a day and a wrestling workout. The rest was for me to fill in with&#8230; Anything.</p>
<p>For me, &#8220;anything&#8221; doesn&#8217;t include video games, Netflix, or even much hanging out, though. All day I&#8217;m on the move. Studying for this thing, preparing for that, meeting with a professor or writing assistant, and periodically eating. The freedom, as you would assume, is liberating and empowering. The confidence to know that I am truly capable of accomplishing a task independently has made all the work beyond worth it.</p>
<p>And it&#8217;s not like it&#8217;s ALL work.. This is college after all. I&#8217;m having plenty of fun, seriously, so don&#8217;t worry if you&#8217;re a perspective student or parent and think Muhlenberg is scary. It&#8217;s actually awesome, I promise. And I can even say with confidence that I am a better person because of the last month.</p>
<p>Until next time,<br />
Jake</p>

			<div id="post-3187" class="post-3187 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/fall-break-2/" rel="bookmark">Fall Break</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/fall-break-2/" title="2:53 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 29, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I love Fall Break! It&#8217;s such a strategically placed mini-vaca. By this time every year, everyone is annoyed with one another, everyone is stressed out with the amount of work that has been done and the work that will need to get done, and on top of that, everyone just wants to go home and cuddle with their dogs *cough* I mean see our parents and siblings *cough*.</p>
<p>If they&#8217;re luck enough to go back home&#8230;<br />
Unfortunately, I won&#8217;t be going home this break. It doesn&#8217;t make any sense anyway&#8230; Fall Break is really just a long weekend. Why on Earth would I fly all the way down to St. Thomas for that?</p>
<p>So, I&#8217;ll be here on campus. Thankfully the Office of Residential Services offers housing for students who live a certain distance away from campus during these breaks.</p>
<p>I love staying on campus by myself. It&#8217;s so quiet, there&#8217;s no one around and I feel like I have the entire campus to myself (because I basically do). As much as I love people, I also love being on my own. I love being able to read a book on the grass with my music playing, knowing that I won&#8217;t get interrupted by one of my acquaintances or friends. I enjoy being able to go to the park by myself and reflect on how my semester has been and not have people interfere in any way, shape or form. It&#8217;s nice being able to sit and think and dream and oft conspire&#8230; (Get it? The Alma Mater? haha! Muhlen-joke! Not funny? Okay&#8230; I tried&#8230;)</p>
<p>Fall Break gives my mind a well-deserved (if I may say so myself) break from everyone else. And I can also catch up on some sleep and Netflix episodes.</p>
<p>Can&#8217;t wait until Friday!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa W</p>
