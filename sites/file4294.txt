			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/saturday-means-wrestling/" rel="bookmark">Saturday means wrestling</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/saturday-means-wrestling/" title="7:55 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 10, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>All week we (wrestlers) just like anyone else, know Saturday is coming. For most, the day represents relaxation and entertainment. Saturday is when you get to take a break from all the stress and difficulties that weigh down the conscious during the week.</p>
<p>Not for us wrestlers. Not even close actually. Saturday for us is the day to step on a mat with another college wrestler and fight to achieve our goals. To describe a match any other way than a fight would be misguided as well. While we respect our opponent fully, he is trying to take away the victory that we work tirelessly to attain. No competitor in their right might would ever give up that claim of success without first surrendering blood, sweat, and tears.</p>
<p>I rise in the wee hours of the morning, 4:46am on this occasion, to begin the physical preparation to compete. The night has been largely restless and sleepless because my mind has been racing far to fast to drift off. My mouth is dry from thirst and my belly growls with aggression, but my visualization of winning distracts such discomforts.</p>
<p>I make my way to the wrestling room to check weight. I know that I have to be under 165 to even be eligible to take the mat, but I honestly do not know where I am at. That unknowingness is one of the most daunting and eery feelings. I step on the scale&#8230; The three dotted lines move in a wave length pattern, giving enough time to say a short prayer or allow an unwanted stream of consciousness to creep in.<br />
&#8230;165.4</p>
<p>Half a pound &#8220;over weight.&#8221; A feeling of disappointment and slight anger rush through my veins. 20 minutes of the bike and relentless spitting later, mind you, it has just broke 5am, and I am slightly &#8220;under weight&#8221; at 164.9.</p>
<p>Us seven freshman taking the mat today are ready to go. Yes, seven. While most teams have at least 30 guys, bigger wrestling rooms, and more coaches, we frankly don&#8217;t care. Because that is the beauty in wrestling, it is just you and the other guy out there. I know how hard I worked, and I doubt the opponent soon to be in front of me did as well in equal caliber. Therefore, I have nothing to worry about.</p>
<p>The bus ride to Ursinus College, is the calm before the storm. Few words are said as some of us stare out the window in thought and others take naps. This is the time to gather a wandering psyche and check the readiness of the mind.</p>
<p>I don&#8217;t know what is going to happen out there, but I do know that I am ready. Even if I don&#8217;t fully believe it, I have an obligation to convince myself it is true.</p>
<p>We arrive. In what seems like no time, the multitude of teams populate the mat. Division 1 powerhouses such as Naval Academy, American University, Franklin and Marshall, and Columbia, among others, are present.</p>
<p>&#8220;Bout 53, Jake Gordon, Muhlenberg report to mat 3&#8221;</p>
<p>This is my time, I got this- I repeat in my head. As I look across to see my opponent, I notice that I am wrestling a division 1 kid from Frankin and Marshal.</p>
<p>The action begins and is relentless. He gets the first takedown, I&#8217;m down 2-0. Then I escaped and get my own takedown, I&#8217;m up 3-2. In a flurry of action lasting over 10 minutes, I came out on top. I won! My first win in the books, and against a D1 guy!</p>
<p>I&#8217;m elated, this feels surreal. What an experience. This makes every moment of brutal practice, lifting, conditioning, and cardio, worth it. This is why I do it.</p>
<p>I have a long road ahead to climb, but getting over this hump has meant the world.</p>

			<div id="post-3439" class="post-3439 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog tag-freshman tag-halloween tag-muhlenberg">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/halloweekend-at-muhlenberg/" rel="bookmark">Halloweekend at Muhlenberg</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/halloweekend-at-muhlenberg/" title="8:36 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 2, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Halloween is one of the most anticipated &#8220;holidays&#8221; of the entire year. While few actually know the historical significance of the annual fall celebration, the societal expectation embraced by all is to to dress up. In fact, Halloween incites so much excitement here, that instead of partaking in costume festivities only on the 31st of October, students plan three outfits for the Thursday, Friday, and Saturday of the weekend. This phenomenon is actually not unique to this school, as I was made aware through various forms of social media that my friends around the country were doing the same.</p>
<p>Given the theatrical and artistic foundations of Muhlenberg, it is no surprise students compile both clever and sophisticated costumes. For example, I saw a variety of imitated animals, television stars, food items, and even pop cultural characters from decades ago (leave it to berg students to be extremely ironic with costumes).</p>
<p>Unfortunately for me, I could not go out on Thursday or Friday because I had a wrestling tournament on Saturday. However, the one night I did go out was amazing and adequately hyped. Let&#8217;s start the countdown until next year!</p>
<p>Til next time!<br />
Jake</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/course-schedule-already/" rel="bookmark">Course Schedule Already?</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/course-schedule-already/" title="1:12 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 2, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/as246905/" title="View all posts by Ariel">Ariel</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>The course schedule for next semester is up! Students franticly log onto capstone to see what’s being offered.</p>
<p>“Will my favorite professor be teaching the class I really want to take?”</p>
<p>“I hope that awesome GAR (general academic requirement) fits into my schedule this year!”</p>
<p>“I’m a senior; shouldn’t I be able to sign up for ALL the classes I want next semester?”</p>
<p>“How am I last to register AGAIN?”</p>
<p>As I hear my peers ponder the new and exciting opportunities next semester will bring, I can’t help but take this time to reflect. Next semester I will be studying abroad in Israel. Don’t get me wrong; I couldn’t be more excited for the adventures to come! I am however, beginning to realize that this will be the first semester I don’t even need to look at the course schedule. I don’t get to compare my schedule to my friends’ schedules. I don’t even need to go through the stress of worrying which classes will fit with other classes. Even though I’m sure that being abroad will present many novel and unique opportunities, my dwindling days at Muhlenberg is something I always will cherish.</p>
<p>&#8216;Till next time,</p>
<p>Ariel</p>

			<div id="post-3413" class="post-3413 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/halloween-weekend/" rel="bookmark">Halloween Weekend</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/halloween-weekend/" title="1:11 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 2, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>First things first: Muhlenberg is not a party school. So if you&#8217;re a perspective student reading this thinking about applying and you&#8217;re into partying, Muhlenberg lacks in that department. </p>
<p>To make up for it though, The Muhlenberg Activities Council does a fantastic job at providing things for students to do during the weekends. A few weekends ago, students were offered discounted tickets for Dorney Park, and this weekend students have opportunities to go to a corn maze, to a hockey game to support the Mules and even see the Lehigh Valley Phantoms at the PLL Center. On campus, there will be a Rocky Horror Picture show (it&#8217;s a tradition; you have to go your freshman year), there will be student performers and (as a Halloween special) there will be zombie caricaturist (cool right?).</p>
<p>Now I know I just said that Muhlenberg is not a party school, and talked about the other opportunities there are on campus. But all things change during Halloween Weekend. If there&#8217;s one weekend where you will be finding parties all over the place, it&#8217;s this weekend. And it&#8217;s one of my favorite weekends, not because of the parties, but because I get to dress up in different costumes and play around with my makeup and hair. This year I am going to be Little Red Riding Hood (Thursday), Sandy from Grease (Friday) and a witch (Saturday)! Yes, you did read 3 different costumes. It&#8217;s not a requirement, but I personally judge those who do not wear different costumes every night besides it&#8217;s so much fun being creative. I know that a witch isn&#8217;t creative, but I already had the hat from freshman year, and I refuse to spend money on costumes this year. I&#8217;m so excited for the festivities to begin!</p>
<p>Have a Happy Halloween weekend everyone! Stay safe, have fun and enjoy the candy!</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>
