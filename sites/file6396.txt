          <h1 class="entry-title ">The F in RJF &#8211; Sophie Weber</h1><div class="entry-meta"><a href="http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?m=20191118" title="12:11 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date updated" datetime="18 Nov, 2019">November 18, 2019</time></a> &nbsp;in <a class="" href="http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=211844" title="View all posts in RJ Fellows Senior Blog"> RJ Fellows Senior Blog </a> <span class="by-author"> by <span class="author vcard author_name"><a class="url fn n" href="http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1845" title="View all posts by rileyminkoff" rel="author">rileyminkoff</a></span></span> </div><hr class="featurette-divider __before_content">        </header>
                  <section class="entry-content ">
              <p><span style="font-weight: 400">The F in RJF not only stands for Fellows, but also for Family. Within this program comes a group of extremely diverse and inquisitive students, and I could not ask for a better group with which to spend my years at Muhlenberg College. My name is Sophie Weber, and I’m a junior and a psych major here at Muhlenberg. My time in the RJ Fellows program has been shorter than most, unfortunately. While most students have four years of learning and self-exploration at Muhlenberg College, I came in with the understanding that I <a href="http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/rjswBER-BLOG.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group1431"><img class="size-medium wp-image-1433 alignright" src="http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/rjswBER-BLOG-300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/rjswBER-BLOG-300x225.jpg 300w, http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/rjswBER-BLOG-768x576.jpg 768w, http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/rjswBER-BLOG.jpg 960w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>would only experience three short years of my undergraduate experience. I’m fortunate enough to be in a 5-year program studying Occupational Therapy, but that total of five years only includes three at Muhlenberg. The RJF senior capstone course that I’m in right now is with an entirely different class people than I came into college with, and that has allowed me to explore new and meaningful relationships in this program. I appreciate the sense of community, academic exploration, and family that this program has provided to its members over the years.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">My first impression of the RJ fellows program was sealed in an envelope and delivered to my home in Bucks County, Pennsylvania. My choice and my ability to attend Muhlenberg College stems in part from that letter. I had already been excited about being granted entry into the college, but was genuinely ecstatic when I received a letter stating that I had also been granted entry into the RJ Fellows program. A lot of my middle school and high school career was filled with shadowing, volunteering, and gaining experience in the world of service, so having the opportunity to continue that throughout college as well was pivotal in my final commitment decision. In addition to the opportunities that I was to be granted through this program in terms of academics and extra programs and trips, receiving extra academic and financial support throughout my undergraduate years allowed for me to attend Muhlenberg and thrive.<br />
</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Pre-orientation was and always will be one of my favorite memories from my undergraduate experience at Muhlenberg. I was one of a few dozen students in my class year that had the opportunity to move onto campus early, and it changed the course of my college experience so early on. While I don’t remember exactly what the group of 12 or so of us did with our pre-orientation leaders, I have <a href="http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/RJ2WEBERBLOG.jpg" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group1431"><img class="size-medium wp-image-1432 alignleft" src="http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/RJ2WEBERBLOG-300x169.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="169" srcset="http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/RJ2WEBERBLOG-300x169.jpg 300w, http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/RJ2WEBERBLOG-768x432.jpg 768w, http://rjfellows.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2019/11/RJ2WEBERBLOG.jpg 960w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>the friendships that I’ve formed to remind me of the first memories we formed as students together. I’d like to elaborate on the first moment that I can fully remember from my pre-o (from after, actually): I remember sitting with my new friends under the arch between Shankweiler and Trumbower as parents said goodbye to their children in parents plaza on move-in day for the rest of the freshman. Parents and their children alike were crying, and there were around 7 of us, parent-less—our parents had moved us in almost a week prior—and excited for the beginning of orientation, hoarding pita chips and cookies from the little tables set up for the parents and incoming students. In that moment, we decided to call ourselves the “RJ orphans,” which made sense because we were some of the only students there without parents! As minor and insignificant as that may seem, we all bonded in that moment and continued to build strong and deep friendships.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">That group of people changed the way I view college friendships, and while my groups of friends have shifted, my RJs have remained the roots of my other relationships. We may not all keep in touch, as some members of my year have transferred, but I know that I will always have these people to rely on in times of need, just as they can also rely on me. They have truly seen me grow throughout our time together in college, and I can confidently say that they have been a cause for growth in my life, especially academically. In our first year seminar freshman year, we all had the opportunity to discuss an important topic—though not entirely of our choosing—and learn more about each others’ values and ideals. Though the idea of wilderness is not the most exhilarating topic in the world for many, I always enjoyed walking into that class and seeing the joy and excitement on my peers’ faces as we reunited after a few short days of being apart. Our professor also had a huge impact on how we all enjoyed the class. Matt is a theater professor at Muhlenberg, and though that does not align with all of our interests, we all shared such meaningful conversations and fleeting moments of fun that I would say that we all had become friends—even more so than we already had before—after finishing our time in this class. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400"> Well the group of people that I am studying with in the RJ fellow senior capstone is not the same as the group of people that I came into school with, they are equally as curious, intelligent, and creative. I will say that I was fairly intimidated entering a group that had already been formed three years prior, but I can say that I was welcomed with open arms and I do feel as though I am a part of this group of RJs. I appreciate the creativity that this course allows, as we are in charge of leading our own discussions, choosing our own topics, and coming up with a group project that will hopefully benefit the Muhlenberg and Allentown community in some way. I love that I have been surrounded by such inspiring students for the last three years at Muhlenberg in the RJ fellows program, and these are but a few of those people. Being a part of this group of students is challenging me to become a more focused and hard-working student and person.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">This program has added so much to my college career, and I will never be able to thank the staff, other students in the program, and Rita and Joe enough for all they have contributed to my academic experience. The lessons I’ve learned, the values I hold, and the friendships that I have made are all things that I will carry with me for the rest of my life. I will always appreciate this program and what it has provided for me. I know that I will continue to be creative, curious, and a productive member of every community I will become a part of throughout my life both during and after college. This family is something that I will always appreciate, and I hope to accomplish more and more as I grow.</span></p>
