							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Is my article Peer-Reviewed?
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-15571886" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									<div class="">
                        <ul id="s-lg-link-list-36757528" class="s-lg-link-list s-lg-link-list-10"><li class="">
                        
                    <div id="s-lg-content-46333208" class=""><a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://ulrichsweb.serialssolutions.com/"  target="_blank"  onclick="return springSpace.springTrack.trackLink({link: this,_st_type_id: '16',_st_content_id: '46333208'});">UlrichsWeb Global Serials Directory</a> <div id="s-lg-database-desc-46333208" class="s-lg-database-desc">"Ulrichsweb is an easy to search source of detailed information on more than 300,000 periodicals (also called serials) of all types: academic and scholarly journals, e-journals, peer-reviewed titles, popular magazines, newspapers, newsletters, and more."</div>
                    </div></li>
                        
                            </ul>
                            </div>
			<div id="s-lg-content-33972048" class="  clearfix">
				<p>Journal articles that are peer-reviewed have been critiqued by other experts before publication.</p>
<p>To determine if an article is peer-reviewed or not, you need to check if the <strong>journal </strong>in which the article was published is peer-reviewed. You can do this by using the database <a href="http://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://ulrichsweb.serialssolutions.com/">Ulrich's web</a>. For instance, in the following citation:</p>
<p>Barthlott, W, Wiersch, S, Čolić, Z, &amp; Koch, K 2009, 'Classification of trichome types within species of the water fern Salvinia, and ontogeny of the egg-beater trichomes', <i>Botany</i>, 87, 9, pp. 830-836.</p>
<p><em>Botany</em> is the journal title. Using <a href="http://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://ulrichsweb.serialssolutions.com/">Ulrich's web</a>, type in the name of the journal, in this case&nbsp;<em>Botany.</em> (Do NOT enter the title of the article.) If the journal is listed as "refereed" (another word for peer-reviewed), your article is in a peer-reviewed journal. Therefore, most articles in this journal will be peer-reviewed. Exceptions to peer-reviewed articles in a peer-reviewed journal include news items, commentaries, and letters to the editor.</p>
<p>In some databases, such as <em>Academic Search Complete </em>, you can click on the name of the journal and the database will list information about the journal, including if the journal is peer-reviewed or not. Other databases allow you to limit to see only results that are peer-reviewed. To determine if you can limit to peer-reviewed journals, look in the database's help section.</p>
