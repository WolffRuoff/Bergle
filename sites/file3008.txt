		<p><b>Yearbook Content Manager</b></p>
<p><b>Intern, Office of Communications</b></p>
<p><b>2019</b></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The Office of Communications is the liaison between the College and the media for news stories, interviews and other forms of publicity. The Ciarla yearbook is one of those other forms. What makes the Ciarla unique is the individuals who produce the publication. The Ciarla is one source of communication for Muhlenberg College where the current students tell the story. The photos, quotes, student reflections, parent senior ads and more create a unique publication for what transpired on campus during a given year. For every student on campus that year, the Ciarla documents a collection of memories that will last a lifetime. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The yearbook content manager position requires leadership qualities. The intern will be expected to demonstrate initiative in helping the student editors of the Ciarla club meet their goals for publication. As a member of the Office of Communications, the yearbook content manager will be responsible for representing the Muhlenberg community to students, parents, alumni and the general public and requires an individual that is articulate, courteous, proactive, responsible and attentive.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The yearbook content manager works under the guidance of the Ciarla advisor. Responsibilities include, but are not limited to:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Collaboration with Ciarla staff on:</span>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Design</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Photography</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Social media content</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Content writing</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Determining page content</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Meeting production goals</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Proofreading</span></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Coordinate Ciarla staff meetings</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Editing</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Recruiting student liaisons</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Outreach to student liaisons, groups and activities</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Maintaining a timeline for production</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Attending events as a representative of the Ciarla</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Attending at least one SGA meeting on behalf of the Ciarla</span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">Job qualifications:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Computer proficient</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Good people skills</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Pleasant personality and telephone skills</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Well organized</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Attention to detail</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Knowledge of digital photography helpful</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">A major in media &amp; communication is required for academic credit.</span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">The yearbook content manager reports to the assistant to the vice president of communications, Donna Fahringer. The intern is responsible for maintaining all requirements for an internship with the Department of Media and Communication.</span></p>

Democracy Now! Internships are paid, 20 hour per week, temporary work placements to help students, people early in their careers and career changers to gain entry-level skills and experience in the fields of outreach, social media, education, fundraising, translation and archiving in a non-profit setting. All internships take place at our Manhattan studio where we produce our daily TV and radio news hour.</p>
<h3>Eligibility Requirements</h3>
<p>Applicants must have completed a high school diploma, <span class="caps">GED</span> or equivalent and must have a valid US work authorization.</p>
<p>Democracy Now! is an equal opportunity employer. People of color, people with disabilities, women and <span class="caps">LGBTQ</span>-identified people are encouraged to apply.</p>
<h3>To Apply</h3>
<p>Review the Internship Descriptions below to choose which department you’d like to work with. Candidates may only apply for one internship per session. <a href="https://www.democracynow.org/about/jobs/internships">Click here for more information</a>.</p>
<p>Interns may work in one of five departments:</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jomr/">Archives Intern</a>.</strong> Archives interns are responsible for cataloging videos, turning paper and tape records into digital media and processing all incoming news feeds. The individuals must have experience working with Mac OS X and Office.</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jtom/"><em><u>Democracy Now!</u></em> en Español</a>.</strong> This internship involves translating English content to Spanish, updating social media accounts and assisting with efforts to grow a Spanish-speaking audience. Individuals must be bilingual and have basic journalism skills.</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jomi">Development &amp; Outreach</a>.</strong> Interns in this area are responsible for maintaining the donor database and processing contributions. Familiarity with Microsoft Word and Excel is preferred.</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jomg/">Education Intern</a>.</strong> Interns in this area invite educators and their classes to visit the set and use the broadcast as a part of their curricula. Previous experience with teaching is beneficial for the role.</p>
<p><strong>– <a href="https://democracynow.recruiterbox.com/jobs/fk0jo6k/">Digital Intern</a>.</strong> Digital interns produce content for <em><u>Democracy Now!</u></em> social media platforms and are responsible for updating social media accounts. Knowledge of social media and basic journalism skills are required.</p>
<p>To apply, send a one page resume, a cover letter, and two work or academic references to the email address specified in the appropriate internship description. In your cover letter, please address these questions:</p>
<ol>
<li>How did you discover Democracy Now!?</li>
<li>What are your goals in seeking out this internship?</li>
<li>What makes you a good candidate for this internship?</li>
</ol>

<p><a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/wmuh/">WMUH-FM</a>, the radio station of Muhlenberg College, has two internship opportunities for the Spring 2019 semester.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Audio Production</h3>
<p>WMUH is seeking an audio production associate to produce, record and edit audio content for broadcast and online distribution. Current projects include working with Muhlenberg Theatre and Dance, Student Engagement and the Living Writers Series to create, engineer and air monthly programming. Knowledge of audio editing software and recoding techniques is preferred.</p>
<p>Responsibilities include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Working with the General Manager to establish and execute a calendar for interviews and programs</li>
<li>Recording and editing original programs for broadcast and distribution</li>
<li>Uploading content to Soundcloud</li>
<li>Preparing and editing syndicated content for broadcast</li>
<li>Creating legal station IDs, liners, and promotional spots</li>
</ul>
<h3>Web Designer/Developer</h3>
<p>WMUH is planning to revamp our web presence and is looking for a web designer/developer to help with wmuh.org. Ideally this site will be ready to go live to coincide with our annual membership drive which begins April 8, 2019. Working knowledge of WordPress preferred. Graphic design skills a plus.</p>
<p>Responsibilities include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Working with the General Manager and student executive board to establish site style guide and content requirements</li>
<li>Coordinating with Muhlenberg Instructional Designer to create site prototype</li>
<li>Creating and developing content for site</li>
<li>Updating site as needed</li>
</ul>
<p>For more information, or to apply, contact <a href="mailto:wmuh@muhlenberg.edu">Paul Krempasky</a>.</p>

The Theatre District borders on the Muhlenberg College campus and extends from 17th to 22nd streets (east to west) and Washington to Liberty streets (north to south).</p>
<h5>Specific responsibilities may include:</h5>
<ol>
<li>Updating the business database</li>
<li>Assisting with the creation of a business brochure</li>
<li>Helping to re-establish the website, including content and design</li>

<h5>About the West End Alliance</h5>
<p>The West End Alliance is a non-profit organization whose purpose is to stimulate revitalization in the West End Theatre District of Allentown by envisioning and catalyzing positive change among residential, commercial, governmental and institutional entities. The organization seeks to promote the development of the West End of Allentown as a local residential neighborhood center by enhancing, diversifying and supporting the businesses and services to provide a walkable, pedestrian-friendly community atmosphere. Building on the area’s culturally rich and ethnically diverse composition, the West End Alliance strives to create a welcoming mecca for residents, college students, entrepreneurs and artisans.  The West End Alliance is comprised of a volunteer board, committee members and a part-time employee, the neighborhood coordinator.</p>
