		<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/weekendpreview_small.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-1812" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/weekendpreview_small.jpg" alt="weekend preview" width="264" height="150" /></a>A volleyball tournament, two home women&#8217;s soccer games and a home field hockey game make for a busy weekend at Muhlenberg, while football and men&#8217;s soccer travel north.</em></p>
<p><strong>Volleyball hosts Buttermaker Tournament: vs. FDU-Florham (Friday, 3:00); vs. Rutgers-Camden (Friday, 7:00); opponents TBD (Saturday, 10:00 and 2:00)</strong></p>
<p>The 17th Buttermaker Tournament features visiting teams from as close as Bethlehem (Moravian) and as far away as Winchester, Va. (Shenandoah). Allegheny, Baruch, DeSales and Elizabethtown round out the nine-team field.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg (3-1), coming off a strong showing at Gettysburg last weekend, is seeking its first Buttermaker title since 2005. Each of the last two years, the Mules went 2-0 on the first day of the tournament but 0-2 on the second</p>
<p><a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/vblive/topview.htm">Live stats</a> (all Muhlenberg matches) | <a href="http://portal.stretchinternet.com/muhlenberg/">Live video</a> (all Muhlenberg matches) | <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/athletics/buttermaker/buttermaker14.html" target="_blank">Tournament page</a></p>
<p><strong style="color: #373737;">Women’s Soccer vs. Delaware Valley (Friday, 7:00) and vs. Gwynedd Mercy (Saturday, 6:00)</strong></p>
<p>The regionally ranked Mules (2-0) play home games on back-to-back days for the second straight weekend. Last weekend, they defeated Wilkes and Marywood to open a season with two shutout wins for the first time since 1999.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg has never started with more than two shutout wins; in 1991, their first varsity season, the Mules blanked their first four opponents, but one of the games was a scoreless draw.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg scored 2-0 wins against both Delaware Valley (1-1) and Gwynedd Mercy (1-2) last year.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/wsoclive/xlive.htm">Live stats</a> | <a href="http://portal.stretchinternet.com/muhlenberg/">Live video</a></p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey vs. Elizabethtown (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>Fresh off its win against third-ranked Montclair State, Muhlenberg hosts another team that has not shied away from tough competition: Elizabethtown opened the season with one-goal losses to No. 8 Franklin &amp; Marshall and No. 12 Messiah.</p>
<p>Elizabethtown (14-6 last year) is part of an opening four-game stretch for the Mules against teams that combined for a .783 winning percentage in 2013.</p>
<p>The Blue Jays hold a 9-6-2 series lead and have won the last three meetings, scoring two goals in a span of less than three minutes to turn a 1-0 second-half deficit into a 2-1 victory last year.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/fhlive/xlive.htm">Live stats</a> | <a href="http://portal.stretchinternet.com/muhlenberg/">Live video</a></p>
<p><strong>Football at Wilkes (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg opens its 113th season of football with an interesting matchup against Wilkes. Although the teams haven&#8217;t played each other since 2009, there will be plenty of familiarity: The Colonels&#8217; new head coach is Trey Brown, the quarterbacks coach and special teams coordinator for the Mules for the last four years.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg returns most of its starting lineup from a 2013 squad that went 8-3 and earned an ECAC bowl bid. The Mules, ranked in the top 20 by Lindy&#8217;s and Sporting News, were picked second in the Centennial Conference preseason poll.</p>
<p>Wilkes, 4-6 last year, was eighth in the Middle Atlantic Conference preseason poll.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.sidearmstats.com/wilkes/football/" target="_blank">Live stats</a> | <a href="http://wilkesathletics.ezstream.com/broadcasts/index.cfm?fuseaction=usrbrd&amp;broadcasterid=60611&amp;mobi=0" target="_blank">Live video</a></p>
<p><strong>Men&#8217;s Soccer at Marywood (Saturday, 4:00)</strong></p>
<p>The regionally ranked Mules (2-0) hit the road for the first time this year in a rematch of last year&#8217;s ECAC Tournament first-round game, which Muhlenberg won, 2-0.</p>
<p>Wilkes was the last opponent for both teams. Muhlenberg won, 4-0, and Marywood (0-1-1) lost, 4-1.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.sidearmstats.com/marywood/msoc/" target="_blank">Live stats</a></p>
