				<p>Hey everyone!</p>
<p>I am writing from my library at my school, Umbra, right now and I am so excited to finally have internet to tell you about my first couple of days in Perugia. I took probably the longest flight I have taken to date, which was fun, but cramped, to Rome and then a bus to Perugia. We spent the first night in a hotel, which was rough, because I spent the day fighting jetlag, but exciting because I spent the day meeting new people that are just as excited as me to learn and immerse themselves into the Italian culture.</p>
<p>The next day, we got to see where we will be staying for the next couple of months. It is absolutely stunning. I love it. 			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/01/a-new-year-and-a-new-adventure/" rel="bookmark">A New Year and A New Adventure</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/01/a-new-year-and-a-new-adventure/" title="1:44 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 3, 2012</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I am surrounded by mounds of clothes in piles that are starting to just become a huge mess. I check the TSA website almost every day. While most people really want snow, I have my fingers crossed that until Jan 6<sup>th</sup>, the snow won’t fall. I am in the packing and repacking stages of my departure process. I am leaving for Italy in four days for my semester abroad. I am incredibly nervous and so excited at the same time. I am spending a semester at the Umbra Institute in Perugia, Italy to study, learn and explore the culture around me.</p>
<p>Although I have traveled out of the country before, I was with a group of people I knew well. This time, I am flying solo. There is a part of me that is a little nervous for what I may find on the other side of the ocean but I think that it is really just turning into more excitement than anything. In the real world, there will be times when I will have to start new and challenging things on my own and I think this experience will help me learn more about that.</p>
<p>I had an amazing semester at Muhlenberg with so many opportunities, and now I am about to start on a once in a lifetime journey to Perugia. I am starting to meet some people from other schools who are going to the same program. They seem really nice and they are in the same boat as I am in terms of being the only person from their school to go to this program.</p>
<p>Well, its back to folding, sorting and weighing my suitcases to make sure they meet the TSA requirements because I want to make sure everything is up to code and ready to go for Thursday. I am so happy that I am getting this opportunity and that it is about to begin. I am open to the experiences to come. I am excited for what is ahead. I am ready to go.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/11/the-home-stretch/" rel="bookmark">The Home Stretch&#8230;</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/11/the-home-stretch/" title="10:53 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 30, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/eliana/" title="View all posts by eliana">eliana</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I am writing from the lovely Seegers Union couches right next to Java Joe&#8217;s. This is my home. For this entire semester this has been my home base. During the week this is where I do most of my studying, socializing, eating, and sleeping (just kidding!). These weeks are the craziest of the year. Only a couple of weeks left of classes which means major studying for exams, putting together presentations, and meeting with partners. Slowly but surely everything is coming together. I hope that my Herculean strength will continue to power me through to success. Last week I had Thanksgiving break as a brief break from Muhlenland. I flew out of the lovely and quaint Lehigh Valley International Airport, which is only twenty minutes away from campus, and flew into O&#8217;Hare despite some weather worries.</p>
<p>Thanksgiving was the first time I have been home all semester. I love it at Muhlenberg, but there is seriously no place like home. It was so nice to see my family, friends, and of course my beloved dog, Beatrice. The Thanksgiving meal was delicious, of course, but I am personally very fond of Black Friday shopping. Even Beatrice joined in the fun!!!</p>
<div id="attachment_779" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/DSCN03381.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-779" class="size-medium wp-image-779" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/DSCN03381-300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/DSCN03381-300x225.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/DSCN03381-1024x768.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-779" class="wp-caption-text">Beatrice models her new boots!</p></div>
<p>But of course, I was back on the plane to Allentown in no time at all, headed back to Muhlenland. I am back on my beloved couches. The fire is burning in Seegers, giving off a lovely seasonal ambiance. The beautiful winter wreaths are beginning to be put up all over campus. The Christmas music is soothing coming from the Java Joe&#8217;s stereo and makes everyone&#8217;s mood a little brighter, so even though we are all working really hard, there is a lightness and a joy that is undeniable.</p>
<p>Today I took a brief few minutes of my day to take in the Linda Stein exhibit in the Martin Art Gallery. The Martin Art Gallery exhibits diverse provocative art shows in its intimate space in order to expose students, faculty, and staff to professionals in the art world free of charge. Linda Stein&#8217;s exhibit is titled &#8220;The Fluidity of Gender: Sculpture by Linda Stein&#8221;. The sculptures are wearable body suits made out of leather and other materials. The garments are incredibly striking and intricate, and each one makes a different statement about the role of women in society.</p>
<div id="attachment_780" style="width: 231px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/linda_stein.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-780" class="size-medium wp-image-780" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/linda_stein-221x300.jpg" alt="" width="221" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/linda_stein-221x300.jpg 221w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/linda_stein.jpg 241w" sizes="(max-width: 221px) 100vw, 221px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-780" class="wp-caption-text">One of Linda Stein&#39;s incredible pieces!</p></div>
<p>I feel so lucky to be exposed to the arts on and off campus through Muhlenberg. I am so lucky to go to a school where I have the arts at my fingertips. When I am home, I love going to museums in Chicago, so I am very grateful to have a mini Art Institute across the street from where I live!</p>
<p>That&#8217;s all for now! I&#8217;m going avoid the rain and continue do work from the comfort of my couches.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/11/giving-thanks/" rel="bookmark">Giving Thanks</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/11/giving-thanks/" title="9:32 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 29, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>As I am home for Thanksgiving, filling myself with food, seeing family and yes doing Black Friday shopping, I am reflecting on all the many things that I am thankful for. I think about all the people, friends and family, who are in my life and I am thankful for each and every one of them. I have also had amazing opportunities over the last semester and I am quite thankful for all the things I have gotten to do and experience.</p>
<p>The week before Thanksgiving break, I had the opportunity to be in a performance of Moving Stories. Moving Stories is a dance performance where dance majors get the opportunity to choreograph a piece in full production on the Baker stage. I was the understudy for the tap piece by a senior Jeremy Arnold. The piece was called ANEW and it was amazing. It was about the way classical music and something opposite such as hip hop are viewed as different styles and classes of art and he wanted to comment on the fact that it is all art and to have people appreciate the melding of both. Another student, Andy Zepp, even DJed onstage during the performances. Even as the understudy, Jeremy was so kind and allowed me to perform in one of the shows. It was such a cool experience and I got to tap with amazing tap dancers too.  Here is a picture of all of us together before a performance.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/ANEW-blog-pic-.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-742" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/ANEW-blog-pic--300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/ANEW-blog-pic--300x225.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/ANEW-blog-pic-.jpg 960w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>Also, last Friday night, I went to an event called Help ODK CAN HUNGER. ODK is a leadership society on campus and they were hosting an event with a lot of musical performances throughout the night and getting donations to help fight hunger. There is a video below of two musical performances.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8Cse9YJ99q4">ODK CAN HUNGER BENEFIT</a></p>
<p>I hope everyone had a wonderful Thanksgiving break. Now back to school for the last couple weeks of classes and finals!</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/11/26-2/" rel="bookmark">26.2</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/11/26-2/" title="12:32 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 14, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>So this weekend I witnessed a marathon&#8230;of theatre. Or at least that is what it felt like. From friday at 3pm to saturday at 3pm, more than 40 people participated in Marathon Theater. Marathon Theatre is 24 hours of theatre. That is right. 24 hours. They stayed up all night having singing and theatre groups perform, playing improv games and many other things for 24 hours. I admire them because after 20 mins in the cold, I had to go inside. Here is a video of some of the highlights from the 24 hours.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hBv3OdSnFgQ">Marathon Theater</a></p>
<p>I also went into New York City on saturday which was so much fun. If you are in a theatre class, you go to see a show each semester and saturday we saw Cymbeline at Barrow Street Theatre. The show was amazing and we had some time to eat a nice lunch in the city. It is great how close NYC and Philly are to Muhlenberg because it gives access to amazing theatre, art and many other things. So with all the theatre I got to see this weekend, it did feel a little bit like a marathon but I loved every second of it. Here is a video of NYC!</p>
<p><a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HVGtBp95Fro&amp;feature=channel_video_title">NYC! </a></p>
