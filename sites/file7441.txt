			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/this-weekend/" title="6:40 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 22, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Muhlenberg&#8217;s Activity Council usually hosts the weekend events on campus for students to get involved in. This weekend students can go play backlight mini golf or go to a comedian. Students can also perform at a Spoken Word Poetry event. I&#8217;m not sure if that&#8217;s run by MAC, but it&#8217;s something to do. There&#8217;s also some porting events going on. Apparently the Muhlenberg soccer team has won many games this semester and made into the NCAA. Which is amazing(?). I know absolutely nothing about sports, but I&#8217;ve asked a few people so I wouldn&#8217;t be as ignorant and that means that our team is one of the best in the country, which is pretty dang cool. Still haven&#8217;t made it out to a soccer game, but I am going to a basketball game later tonight (GO MULES!)</p>
<p>You heard that correctly, ladies and gentlemen, yours truly will be going to her very first basketball game at Muhlenberg. Which is slightly pathetic&#8230; I will admit. I&#8217;m a junior and have never attended a basketball game. Oopsie, missed the memo.</p>
<p>Anyway the reason for this blog post is to talk about Muhlenberg&#8217;s Got Talent, every year &#8216;Berg&#8217;s Got Talent is put on by the class council. I only went to support my friend, and then I stayed because the 2 MC&#8217;s were so hysterical and had my falling out my chair in laughter (not really, but they were so funny). People tap danced, people sang (duh! It&#8217;s Muhlenberg&#8230; of course people would be singing), people performed original songs (all of them were about love which got annoying after a while. I mean, there are more important things in the world than love, for instance, Nutella crepes and bacon pizza), people rode large unicycles and then some guy did some cool magic tricks and another guy did some giant hoop circus thingy that I do not know how to describe.</p>
<p>As usual, people were amazing. 			<div id="post-3522" class="post-3522 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/a-casual-organ-concert-in-one-of-the-oldest-buildings-in-arezzo-with-an-instrument-from-the-1700s/" rel="bookmark">A casual Organ concert in one of the oldest buildings in Arezzo with an instrument from the 1700&#8217;s</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/a-casual-organ-concert-in-one-of-the-oldest-buildings-in-arezzo-with-an-instrument-from-the-1700s/" title="12:28 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 21, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/zoebriggs/" title="View all posts by Zoë Briggs">Zoë Briggs</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Remember my last post?  Remember the two music students singing O Holy Night?  Remember the kid playing the piano in that video?  Well that&#8217;s Evan who is multi-talented, insanely cool, and has become one of my best friend at the Accademia!  You all know the drill, meet Evan!!</p>
<div id="attachment_3526" style="width: 178px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/11/MeandEvan1.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3526" class="size-medium wp-image-3526" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/11/MeandEvan1-168x300.jpg" alt="Me and Evan in Assisi a few weeks ago!" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/11/MeandEvan1-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/11/MeandEvan1-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3526" class="wp-caption-text">Me and Evan in Assisi a few weeks ago!</p></div>
<p>Evan came to the Accademia to study Organ and Piano, and as someone who shares a wall with him and his piano during the thousand of hours he practices (I&#8217;m not exaggerating) I can a test to the fact that he is an incredible musician.  This past week he had an Organ concert in town to show us what he has been working on all semester with his teacher (an expert in Organ Music and its history).  We all headed into town after class and were not only serenade by absolutely beautiful music, but also got to see one of the oldest buildings in Arezzo, Santa Maria delle Pieve, a magnificent church which is also the home of an organ from the 1700&#8217;s.</p>
<p align="center"><iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/77GsB3bt5Rs?rel=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>
<p>Evan played a bunch of pieces for us throughout the night and they were all amazing!  That kid astounds me with how much he practices and how much his work pays off.  He is just one example of the talent that I have been surrounded by here in Italy and I will be heartbroken when he leaves the villa in just a week, but I know that I will be seeing him again and am so lucky to have had this experience with him and everyone here in the villa.</p>
<p>Until next time, Ciao!</p>

			<div id="post-3524" class="post-3524 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog tag-thanksgiving">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/why-im-excited-to-go-home-for-thanksgiving/" rel="bookmark">Why I&#8217;m excited to go home for Thanksgiving</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/why-im-excited-to-go-home-for-thanksgiving/" title="12:27 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 21, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I left my house packed and ready for college on August 19th, just over three months ago. And unlike most of my east-coast-native friends, I have yet to return to my Los Angeles room since this semester has began. Three months is a long time not to be home, and I miss it. Don&#8217;t get me wrong, I love Muhlenberg. I love my classes, my friends, wrestling, the campus&#8230; etc. My mild homesickness has nothing to do with the school, it just has to do with wanting to go back to the place I know so well.</p>
<p>1. <strong>Food</strong>&#8211; The Muhlenberg food is phenomenal. It wows me daily with its flavor and quality. Regardless though, nothing beats mom&#8217;s home cooked meals. My whole life I have been spoiled with a myriad of fine-dining quality foods, cooked from my kitchen, by my talented mother. So, I can&#8217;t wait to see what she has in store for Thanksgiving this year as head chef.</p>
<p>2. <b>Family- </b><span style="color: #000000;">Yes, I am friends with all my aunts, uncles, cousins, parents, and sister, on Facebook. So, on a </span>daily<span style="color: #000000;"> basis I get to read their thoughts and posts, and see their pictures, but Facebook with never be able to replicate or </span>replace<span style="color: #000000;"> the deep bond that all branches of the Gordon family have. We have all of our quirks and </span>idiosyncrasies that makes the family unique and special. I am counting down the hours until all of us can reunite and catch up.</p>
<p>3. <strong>Weather- </strong>While the weather change is exciting, it hasn&#8217;t taken long until the 25-30 degree Allentown days have gotten old, and everyday 70 and sunny of back home is merely a memory. While home, I plan to hike, run, bike, go to the beach, go to the park, and generally take advantage of the famous LA weather that I can now appreciate so much more.</p>
<p>4. <strong>My bed- </strong>This one is pretty self explanatory. I am going to crawl under the covers in my bed for the first time in months! Can&#8217;t Wait!</p>
<p>I will report back post-Thanksgiving with updates and pictures!</p>

			<div id="post-3397" class="post-3397 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/mainstages-and-blackboxes/" rel="bookmark">Mainstages and Blackboxes</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/mainstages-and-blackboxes/" title="11:45 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 20, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Muhlenberg has some seriously talented students. I say this every time I attend a show. The people are simply OUTSTANDING. I try my hardest to see every performance I can go to (usually I have some friend, or aquaintance) and every time I leave, I leave in awe.</p>
<p>I can&#8217;t sing, I can&#8217;t dance, and I can&#8217;t act, so whenever there are people who can do all three and are good at it, I start fan-girling and ooh-ing and awh-ing.</p>
<p>This weekend, the MainStage production was &#8220;Anyone Can Whistle&#8221;. If anyone knows the main actress, the Mayoress? Can you please tell her that SHE. IS. AMAZING. and that she did a FANTASTIC job! She BLEW ME AWAY. After the performance I could not stop RAVING about her. I think her name is Samantha State (does anyone know her name? I would tell her myself but I lost the booklet)&#8230; but someone, anyone, if you know her, please please please please please tell her that she was absolutely, undoubtedly, positively remarkable?</p>
<p>My mentee for the Emerging Leaders Program, Evan B, was also in it and my oh my, I&#8217;m so proud of him. He&#8217;s a freshman and in his first semester got on a MainStage. That is incredible. I always say that freshman who get into main stages are the future elite of the Muhlenberg Theatre Association and things can only go up from here&#8230; People will know who he is, how talented he is and he&#8217;ll be complimented behind his back for his performances and he&#8217;ll never know it. Can someone please say Muhlenberg celebrity? That&#8217;s right, my mentee, Evan B, a star in the making. And it was his birthday Monday so once again you did an outstanding performance this week in &#8220;Anyone Can Whistle&#8221; and I hope you had a relaxing birthday.</p>
<p>This weekend, there were also Studio Productions, which <span style="color: #333333;">are produced, directed, designed, and run by the students. My best friend, Sarah M was the lead in &#8220;The Song of Death&#8221; by Tawfiq al-Hakim and she was sheer perfection. The intensity, the tears, the heartache, I felt it all. Everyone was raving about her and she even got pulled aside (twice!)  in the Dining Commons by someone&#8217;s mother who watched her performance. You go girl!<br />
</span></p>
<p>The talent here at Muhlenberg is enough to have you secretly hate yourself for not being able to hold a tune or dance to a beat. And on top of that, it&#8217;s so competitive. There&#8217;s always that few who tend to hog the spotlight and the rest sadly go unnoticed, but almost everyone here are incredible singers, actors and dancers. And if they don&#8217;t get on a MainStage, there are Studio Productions, Red Door Plays, Acapella groups, improv groups, dance groups and so many more groups and places for them on campus to broaden their artistry.</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>
