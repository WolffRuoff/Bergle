			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/400ft-deep/" rel="bookmark">400ft Deep</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/400ft-deep/" title="3:16 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 25, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Today I ended up 400 ft deep and 1800 ft inside of Mahanoy Mountain.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<hr />
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>I went on a trip to the Pioneer Tunnel in Ashland, PA!</p>
<p>It was a Center for Ethics field trip that students had to opportunity to go on with the speaker from the night before, Erik Loomis. As soon as I heard about this trip I was so excited. I&#8217;m not sure why&#8230; I was warned that temperatures were going to drop to 50 degrees (I hate the cold). I knew it was going to be grimy and dirty since it&#8217;s a coal mine (I don&#8217;t know that much about mines, but that seemed obvious).</p>
<p>Why on Earth would I be excited about going on a coal mine tour? Who knows! It&#8217;s one of the many mysteries of Felisa that no one, including myself, will ever solve. I asked some of my friends to join me, but no one seemed interested.</p>
<p>When I got to the bus I was overjoyed when I saw some of my Emerging Leader munchkins getting on the bus! (People I knew! Yay!)</p>
<div id="attachment_3135" style="width: 568px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/10685588_10152710448093866_4495366887708317335_n.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3135" class="wp-image-3135" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/10685588_10152710448093866_4495366887708317335_n.jpg" alt="Emerging Leaders take on Pioneer Tunnel!" width="558" height="761" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/10685588_10152710448093866_4495366887708317335_n.jpg 704w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/10685588_10152710448093866_4495366887708317335_n-220x300.jpg 220w" sizes="(max-width: 558px) 100vw, 558px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3135" class="wp-caption-text">Emerging Leaders take on Pioneer Tunnel!</p></div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>But back to this coal mine. The ride to the coal mine actually took longer than the tour itself but we had the opportunity to get pulled 1800 feet into the mountain by a mine car, and then we went on a walking tour while the miner-guide explained how the coal was mined, how the mine was built, the tools and techniques the miners used, how the conditions were back in the day, and we learned all about Anthracite coal.</p>
<div id="attachment_3121" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1268.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3121" class="wp-image-3121 size-large" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1268-768x1024.jpg" alt="Going in...." width="640" height="853" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1268-768x1024.jpg 768w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1268-225x300.jpg 225w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3121" class="wp-caption-text">Going in&#8230;.</p></div>
<div id="attachment_3127" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1274.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3127" class="wp-image-3127 size-large" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1274-768x1024.jpg" alt="IMG_1274" width="640" height="853" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1274-768x1024.jpg 768w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1274-225x300.jpg 225w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3127" class="wp-caption-text">It was so dark down there&#8230;</p></div>
<div id="attachment_3132" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1279.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3132" class="wp-image-3132 size-large" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1279-1024x1024.jpg" alt="Found myself a mule amongst the Mules... get it? (Mule)enberg? Bad joke? Okay... moving on..." width="640" height="640" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1279-1024x1024.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1279-150x150.jpg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/IMG_1279-300x300.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3132" class="wp-caption-text">Found myself a mule amongst the Mules&#8230; get it? (Mule)enberg? Bad joke? Okay&#8230; moving on&#8230;</p></div>
<p>The theme for the Center for Ethics program this year is Civility and Disobedience. Erik Loomis&#8217;s talk the night before gave students a quick history of the resistance between the people who worked in the coal mines and those who were in charge. Apparently there are still disputes going on today with a &#8220;war on coal&#8221;. So it seemed fitting that the Center for Ethics would include a field trip to the PA coal mine country (good thinking!). For more Center for Ethics events and talks, click <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/cfe/upcoming_events.html#Civility" target="_blank">here</a> and to learn more about the &#8220;war on coal&#8221;, I found this <a href="http://www.newyorker.com/news/john-cassidy/obamas-war-on-coal-is-worth-fighting" target="_blank">great article</a> that summarizes it better than I can.</p>
<p>Overall, it was a great experience. Just as dirty and grimy and cold as I thought it was be, but equally as cool. Now I can say that I&#8217;ve been in a coal mine, and that there&#8217;s no need for me to ever return to one!</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/two-days-two-fantastic-guests/" rel="bookmark">Two Days, Two Fantastic Guests</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/two-days-two-fantastic-guests/" title="3:31 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 24, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/swedberg/" title="View all posts by Drew">Drew</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>For two consecutive nights, our community has been blessed with two spectacular guests. RJ Mitte, the actor that plays Walt Jr. on the critically acclaimed AMC series Breaking Bad, and Ibiyinka Aloa, the Nigerian arts ambassador for the United Nations, both stopped in for a little chat. Both events were free, so I felt obligated to go as a student of the college.</p>

They both had an incredible stage presence as well as a ton of advice to give the audience! I couldn&#8217;t be happier that I had the opportunity to attend both of these, and I look forward to soaking up more knowledge this year!</p>
<p>Happy birthday of course!</p>
<p>Drew</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/late-night-thoughts/" title="1:34 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 23, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<p>The 3am stroll from the library to the dorm is a lonely one. A roughly 7-minute walk that all students here must have done at some point. Most sane students have been long asleep by now, though on this particular Monday night&#8230;or Tuesday morning I guess, the college grind has inflicted it&#8217;s will on me more harshly then usual.</p>

<p>My thoughts are flowing something like this-</p>
<p>&#8220;Maybe this is like a &#8220;second wind&#8221; that my mind is going through right now, like a marathon runner on mile 20&#8230;why else would I have any form of energy at this ungodly hour.. I wonder if GQ is still open, I&#8217;m hungry&#8230; That&#8217;s a no&#8230;hmmm.. This is pretty cool being the only one around right now&#8230;Is college really about the work, or about the work habits that can be later applied to a job.. That would give me a good reason not to be as sad if I bombed that Econ test&#8230; Oh god, Is there a person over there&#8230;nope just a tree&#8230;&#8221;</p>
<p>Okay.. It&#8217;s clear to me now I really need to sleep. Until next time,<br />
Jake</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/monday-night-football/" rel="bookmark">Monday Night Football</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/monday-night-football/" title="10:34 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 22, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Tonight, the Jets play on Monday Night Football, and yes, I will be going to the game. As I get ready to leave, there are a few things that I begin to realize. The biggest thing is that I am not nearly as excited for this type of game as I would be when I was younger. As a kid there is no better feeling then getting to go to a night game and watch your team play. The excitement of staying up late and having this awesome activity for a Monday night is the best.</p>
<p>Now it has hit me that I would be so much happier going to watch a normal Sunday afternoon game at 1. The first reason is that I lose an entire Monday. Being that it is the first day of the week it is key for getting a lot of work done. I have to leave school early and I won&#8217;t get back until after midnight. All of this means one thing: I can&#8217;t get any work done on Monday. The thrill of getting home late and not sleeping that much just isn&#8217;t as good as it once was.</p>
<p>After thinking about all of this, I realize that it is important to just enjoy this day. People love the chance to get to go to a football game, let alone a prime time game like this. It may not have the same thrill as when I was a kid, but this is my favorite team and one of my favorite places to be. That&#8217;s why even though I would rather the team play on a Sunday, I will enjoy every second of this awesome day.</p>

			<div id="post-2995" class="post-2995 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/bye-muhlenberg-im-joining-the-circus/" rel="bookmark">Bye Muhlenberg, I&#8217;m joining the circus</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/bye-muhlenberg-im-joining-the-circus/" title="8:56 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 22, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/zoebriggs/" title="View all posts by Zoë Briggs">Zoë Briggs</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>This past week was our first full week since we have started classes here in Arezzo, and it has been both exhausting and exhilarating. Our four main courses while we are here at the Accademia are Movement, Italian, Voice, and Philosophy, but these past two weeks we have actually had a few more classes with guest teachers.  We took a Tarentella class with a guest artist from town, and then had the opportunity to take a couple of juggling classes.  I have always wanted to know how to juggle but haven&#8217;t had the materials, or the time for a class before, but here there are juggling balls galore and our teacher Sam is awesome, really passionate about what he does, and loves to share his knowledge! It&#8217;s only been two classes and about a week and a half of practice time, but I am already obsessed and am in a studio whenever I have a free moment throwing and dropping and throwing (but mostly dropping) those three balls that seem to escape me now, but I am DETERMINED to get it before I leave here. I have actually gotten better and cannot wait for our third class next week.</p>
<div id="attachment_3080" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Lifts.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3080" class="size-medium wp-image-3080" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Lifts-300x300.jpg" alt="Besides juggling, Sam also taught us some cool acro" width="300" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Lifts-300x300.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Lifts-150x150.jpg 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Lifts.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3080" class="wp-caption-text">Besides juggling, Sam also taught us some cool lifts</p></div>
<p>Going along with the circus theme, I have also officially attended my first ever aerial acrobatics class. One of the MFA students (the Accademia has a masters program) Rihanna and I were chatting sometime in the first couple of days and aerial came up and I told her how we have classes at Muhlenberg, but I haven&#8217;t been able to take one yet. She immediately got super excited and told me that she has been taking aerial forever and even takes classes in town. Well of course after I found that out I knew that I had to give it a go and I finally did this past Friday!</p>
<p>There is a studio in town that has everything from trampolines to the trapeze. The studio offers a free aerial class to beginners every  so often, and this weekend was one of those times! I gathered a few friends from the program, and we all headed into town excited and a little nervous to have our first experience climbing up the silks and spinning in the air.</p>
<p>We got to class, and as soon as we walked into the building, we were overwhelmed by the amount of gymnastic equipment and super talented gymnasts using it all.  There were two guys practicing their gymnastics, and we found out that they are actually a part of the Joustra (the joust I talked about a little while ago) and that they train all year to be able to perform. After a very brief and barely coherent conversation (there was a little bit of a language barrier), we finally got to start our class!</p>
<p>I took a whole bunch of embarrassing pictures and a few videos:</p>
<div id="attachment_3076" style="width: 178px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Aerial-class-upside-down.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3076" class="size-medium wp-image-3076" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Aerial-class-upside-down-168x300.jpg" alt="Just hanging around" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Aerial-class-upside-down-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Aerial-class-upside-down-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3076" class="wp-caption-text">Just hanging around</p></div>
<div id="attachment_3077" style="width: 178px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Jillian-Aerial.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3077" class="size-medium wp-image-3077" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Jillian-Aerial-168x300.jpg" alt="Jillian doing some Aerial" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Jillian-Aerial-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/Jillian-Aerial-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3077" class="wp-caption-text">Jillian doing some Aerial</p></div>
<div id="attachment_3078" style="width: 178px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/asia-aerial.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3078" class="size-medium wp-image-3078" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/asia-aerial-168x300.jpg" alt="Asia being a star" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/asia-aerial-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/asia-aerial-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3078" class="wp-caption-text">Asia being a star</p></div>
<p align="center"><iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/pyFxiSF8Atw" width="560" height="315" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen"></iframe></p>
<p>After aerial, we were exhausted. I&#8217;m still sore, but we had to get ourselves together because the next day was the Accademia Dell&#8217;arte&#8217;s tenth birthday and there was a huge celebration at the villa.  A bunch of the faculty and staff and even a few alumni came back.  It was an evening of songs, dance, and a lot of food!  We even sent out ten paper lanterns into the night sky to represent each year the accademia has been around.  It was a really special moment and made me think about how lucky I am to be here and have this experience.  Until next time, ciao!</p>
