			<p><em><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/weekendpreview_small.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-1812" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/08/weekendpreview_small.jpg" alt="weekend preview" width="264" height="150" /></a>The first full weekend of Centennial Conference play features two playoff rematches.<br />
</em></p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey at McDaniel (Friday, 7:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg (4-1) begins its quest for a second consecutive playoff berth with a trip to Maryland. The Mules come in with a four-game winning streak and have allowed only three goals &#8211; tied for the fewest in the CC &#8211; in five games.</p>
<p>McDaniel (4-2) brings a three-game winning streak into its CC opener. The Green Terror has not made the playoffs since 2006 and had a 3-7 CC record &#8211; its worst since 1999 &#8211; last year.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg leads the all-time series, 12-9, and has won the last two meetings. The last game played in Westminster was a wild back-and-forth affair in which the Mules came from behind twice before winning, 4-3 in overtime.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.mcdanielathletics.com/sports/fh/2014-15/boxscores/20140919_ztba.xml">Live stats</a> | <a href="http://www.mcdanielathletics.com/livevideo">Live video</a></p>
<p><strong>Volleyball vs. McDaniel (Saturday, 12:00); vs. Cedar Crest (Saturday, 4:00)</strong></p>
<p>Fresh off its win against defending CC regular-season champion Franklin &amp; Marshall on Wednesday, Muhlenberg hops right back into the fire against a McDaniel team that it faced &#8211; and beat &#8211; in the CC semifinals last year.</p>
<p>The Mules (11-2) have won four in a row and lead the CC in kills per set (11.89), hitting percentage (.254), assists per set (11.09) and points per set (16.2). McDaniel (9-2), coming off a five-set loss to 13-1 Catholic, is the top blocking team in the CC, averaging 2.11 blocks per set.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg leads the all-time series, 15-8, and has won the last four meetings, including both last year.</p>
<p>The final match of the day pits Muhlenberg against 1-6 Cedar Crest. A win against McDaniel would put the Mules at 2-0 in the CC for the first time since 2002. A sweep would give them their best record (13-2) through 15 matches since 1993.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/sports/vblive/topview.htm">Live stats</a> | <a href="http://portal.stretchinternet.com/muhlenberg/">Live video</a></p>
<p><strong>Men&#8217;s Soccer vs. McDaniel (Saturday, 4:00)</strong></p>
<p>This is one of five games that open the 2014 CC men’s soccer season, and the early non-league results point to a highly competitive campaign ahead. 						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/17/eye-of-the-keeper/" rel="bookmark">Eye of the Keeper</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/17/eye-of-the-keeper/" title="10:40 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-09-17T10:40:13-04:00">September 17, 2014</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>Even an emergency surgery couldn’t prevent the record start of the Muhlenberg women’s soccer team.</p>
<p>The regionally ranked Mules have set a program record for longest unbeaten streak to start a season – six, following a 1-1 tie with Lebanon Valley last night. Defense is a big reason why: through games of last weekend, Muhlenberg was one of only 12 teams in Division III that had played at least five games and given up fewer than two goals.</p>
<p>Not bad for a team that had to change goalies unexpectedly.</p>
<div id="attachment_2376" style="width: 110px" class="wp-caption alignright"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/mermelsteinhs.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2376" class="size-full wp-image-2376" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/mermelsteinhs.jpg" alt="Jen Mermelstein" width="100" height="150" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2376" class="wp-caption-text">Jen Mermelstein</p></div>
<p>Junior Jen Mermelstein started the first two games of the season, recording shutouts in both. On the night of Tuesday, September 2, she went to bed with a stomach ache. When the pain became too great in the middle of the night, she went to the hospital.</p>
<p>Not long after, she underwent surgery to have her appendix removed.</p>
<p>That was Wednesday, two days before the Mules’ next scheduled game. Sophomore Tori Gonzalez got the news in class when a teammate texted “good luck” to her.</p>
<p>Although Gonzalez had no previous game experience at Muhlenberg, she was a logical choice to step into the starting role.</p>
<p>Mermelstein was visited by some teammates in the hospital, and the topic of her replacement came up. “We felt if someone was the most game ready, it was Tori,” she recalled.</p>
<div id="attachment_2375" style="width: 110px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/gonzalezhs.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2375" class="size-full wp-image-2375" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/gonzalezhs.jpg" alt="Tori Gonzalez" width="100" height="150" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2375" class="wp-caption-text">Tori Gonzalez</p></div>
<p>Gonzalez has rewarded her teammates’ faith in her, allowing only two goals in her first four starts.</p>
<p>“I think she’s done a really good job,” said Mermelstein. “She didn’t have any time to mentally prepare [to be the starter]. She’s stepped up to the challenge.”</p>
<p>Maybe “rising up to the challenge” would be a better way to put it. Gonzalez had her own way of mentally preparing – she watched all four “Rocky” movies the night before and the day of her first start.</p>
<p>“I love those movies,” she said. “That got me pumped up.”</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/mermelstein_GK.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2374" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/mermelstein_GK-300x297.jpg" alt="Jen Mermelstein" width="300" height="297" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/mermelstein_GK-300x297.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/mermelstein_GK-150x150.jpg 150w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/mermelstein_GK-302x300.jpg 302w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/mermelstein_GK.jpg 353w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>Gonzalez says she was nervous for about the first 20 minutes of her first start, against Delaware Valley, but then her years of playing goalie all came back to her and she began to feel more comfortable. Her confidence and comfort level have continued to increase since then.</p>
<p>Still feeling the effects of her surgery, Mermelstein attended Gonzalez’ second start, a game against Gwynedd Mercy on Saturday, September 6. It was Mermelstein’s 20<sup>th</sup> birthday, and her presence was a sort of present for Gonzalez.</p>
<p>“I liked having Jen there a lot,” she said. “She’s very stable mentally and was able to help me out when I was a little bit nervous.”</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/gonzalez_GK.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2373" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/gonzalez_GK-300x288.jpg" alt="Tori Gonzalez" width="300" height="288" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/gonzalez_GK-300x288.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/gonzalez_GK-312x300.jpg 312w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/gonzalez_GK.jpg 350w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>Mermelstein returned to classes on September 9 and ran with the team for the first time this week. Her legs are fine, but she needs to have less pain in her upper body to be able to dive like a goalie. She hopes to return to action next week as the Mules get into the heart of their conference season.</p>
<p>And who will be the starting goalie then?</p>
<p>“I want my spot back,” admitted Mermelstein. “But if Tori is more deserving of it at the point when I come back, then why switch?”</p>

<p><em>This week marks the beginning of Centennial Conference play for most teams.</em></p>
<p><strong>Soccer:</strong> It&#8217;s been a great start for the Mule soccer teams. The women are 5-0, tying the best start in program history, while the men are 4-0 and received votes in last week&#8217;s NSCAA Division III poll.</p>
<p>Muhlenberg is one of only four schools in Division III whose men&#8217;s and women&#8217;s soccer teams are undefeated and untied at this stage in the season. The others are Luther, Messiah and Tufts.</p>

<p>The 11 CC women&#8217;s teams have a combined record of 33-18-2 (.642), with Dickinson, McDaniel and Haverford joining Muhlenberg in the undefeated ranks. And Johns Hopkins (5-2) is ranked in the top 20 in Division III.</p>
<p>The CC men&#8217;s teams have done even better, going 9-0 on Saturday to improve their combined record to an amazing 38-8-2 (.813). Franklin &amp; Marshall, Dickinson, Johns Hopkins, Gettysburg, Swarthmore and Muhlenberg are all receiving votes in at least one of the Division III polls.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/boll_pow.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2370" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/boll_pow-180x300.jpg" alt="boll_pow" width="180" height="300" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/boll_pow-180x300.jpg 180w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/boll_pow.jpg 270w" sizes="(max-width: 180px) 100vw, 180px" /></a>Volleyball:</strong> The 10-2 Mules, off to their best start in 20 years, have a huge opening week of CC play. On Saturday, they welcome McDaniel in a rematch of last year&#8217;s CC semifinal, won by Muhlenberg.</p>

<p>Muhlenberg went 3-1 at Neumann&#8217;s tournament over the weekend, and it was announced yesterday that junior Katherine Boll was named to the all-tournament team. It was the third straight all-tournament nod this season for Boll, who leads the CC in blocks (38) and is second in hitting percentage (.371).</p>
<p><strong>Field Hockey:</strong> The CC &#8230; you guessed it &#8230; is strong in field hockey too, with every team except 0-6 Swarthmore at .500 or better. Muhlenberg (3-1) opens at McDaniel (3-2) on Friday night.</p>
<p>The Mules&#8217; success this season has been fueled by their defense, which is very apparent if you look at their scores. They have allowed only three goals in four games; the last time they started a season giving up no more than one goal in their first four games was 1991.</p>
<p>The defense looks even more impressive when you look at the opponents&#8217; scores. Take away the Muhlenberg games, and Scranton, Montclair State, Elizabethtown and Drew have combined to score 64 goals in 17 games, an average of 3.8 goals per game.</p>

				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/14/an-83-year-climb/" title="2:21 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-09-14T14:21:35-04:00">September 14, 2014</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>One of the big plays in yesterday&#8217;s Homecoming football win against Franklin &amp; Marshall was an 83-yard touchdown run by freshman Max Cepeda. But that wasn&#8217;t the only significant thing about the number 83.</p>
<div id="attachment_2366" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/palladino_nick.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2366" class="size-full wp-image-2366" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/palladino_nick.jpg" alt="Nick Palladino Class of 2017 Quarterback Allentown (N.J.) High School" width="300" height="389" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/palladino_nick.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/palladino_nick-231x300.jpg 231w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2366" class="wp-caption-text">Nick Palladino<br />Class of 2017<br />Quarterback<br />Allentown (N.J.) High School</p></div>
<p>The 42-7 victory improved the Mules&#8217; all-time record to 497-497-41. It&#8217;s the first time they&#8217;ve been at .500 since 1931 &#8211; 83 years ago.</p>
<p>The Mules defeated St. Joseph’s, 7-0, in the fourth game of the 1931 campaign to put their all-time record at 115-114-22. They lost their next game to Ursinus to fall back to .500 and dropped the following contest to Lehigh to slip under .500.</p>
<p>And that is where they stayed for the next 83 years &#8211; until yesterday. Muhlenberg’s low-water mark came midway through the 1998 season, when a 34-31 loss at Johns Hopkins dropped its all-time record to 61 games under .500.</p>
<p>The Mules have made up that ground in fast order, going 114-53 (.683) over their last 167 games to get back to .500.</p>
<p>So where do they go from here? They can move above .500 with a win at McDaniel this Saturday. All-time win number 500 is on the horizon. The school record for games over .500 (10, when the 1927 season ended with an all-time record of 104-94-21) is within reach in the near future if the teams goes at its current pace.</p>
<div id="attachment_2365" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignright"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/palladino_ferdinand.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2365" class="size-full wp-image-2365" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/palladino_ferdinand.jpg" alt="Ferdinand Palladino Class of 1932 Quarterback Allentown (Pa.) High School" width="300" height="389" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/palladino_ferdinand.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/palladino_ferdinand-231x300.jpg 231w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2365" class="wp-caption-text">Ferdinand Palladino<br />Class of 1932<br />Quarterback<br />Allentown (Pa.) High School</p></div>
<p>The current Muhlenberg squad certainly does not want to go the way of the 1931 team, which represented the start of a downward slide for the program. That team finished with a record of 2-8 and scored only 51 points all season &#8211; a total the 2014 Mules needed only six quarters to surpass.</p>
<p>But the teams are linked, not only by their all-time record at one point during the season, but also by an incredible coincidence that stands as further proof that truth can be stranger than fiction: One of the members of the 1931 Muhlenberg football team was a quarterback from Allentown High School named Palladino.</p>

<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/fbbig_MB072414.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2336" alt="The Uniform Project - Football" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/fbbig_MB072414-300x170.jpg" width="300" height="170" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/fbbig_MB072414-300x170.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/fbbig_MB072414-500x284.jpg 500w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/fbbig_MB072414.jpg 780w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>It&#8217;s no surprise that the uniforms of the first Muhlenberg football team in 1900 looked more like rugby jerseys. The game probably more resembled rugby than what has evolved into football. And just like the sport, the uniform designs have evolved.</p>
<p>For a good portion of the sport&#8217;s history at Muhlenberg, the uniforms were pretty generic and free from distinguishing markings. In the 1922 picture below, both teams are wearing dark jerseys and dark helmets. That must&#8217;ve been rough for the TV and radio announcers of the day. Muhlenberg is the team with stripes on its sleeves. We think.</p>
<p>Big numbers on the jerseys first showed up around 1935. The 1958 helmets had numbers on them, the first time they had any elements other than stripes.</p>
<p>In 1977, the kicking Mule logo first appeared on the helmets, and it has remained there for most of the last 37 years. Since 1997, the helmet design has remained unchanged except for a change from flat grey to metallic silver and the number and width of the stripes (the Mules went with the &#8220;Ohio State&#8221; look for a period before switching back to a single red stripe).</p>
<p>As for the uniforms, the word &#8220;Mules&#8221; first appeared on the front of the jerseys in 2003. And last fall, 113 years after Muhlenberg first fielded a varsity football team, the word &#8220;Muhlenberg&#8221; made its debut on the uniforms.</p>

The Mules also had a noteworthy season from the standpoint of uniforms, sporting at least five different uniform types during the season (&#8220;old&#8221; black and red jerseys, new black jerseys and red and white libero tops).</p>
<p>The players on Muhlenberg&#8217;s first varsity volleyball team in 1979 wore jerseys with pinstriped sleeves. The program has been through multiple style changes since, ranging from collared shirts to mesh shirts to long-sleeved shirts, from the name on the front to the name on the back, and from loose-fitting running shorts to tight-fitting biker shorts.</p>
<p>Volleyball is the latest sport to be highlighted in the Uniform Project. Previous sports were <a title="The Uniform Project - Track &amp; Field" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/06/21/the-uniform-project-track-field/" target="_blank">track and field</a>, <a title="The Uniform Project - Field Hockey" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/06/28/the-uniform-project-field-hockey/">field hockey</a>, <a title="The Uniform Project - Men's Soccer" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/07/19/the-uniform-project-mens-soccer/" target="_blank">men’s soccer</a>, <a title="The Uniform Project - Baseball" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/07/03/the-uniform-project-baseball/">baseball</a> and <a title="The Uniform Project – Women’s Soccer" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/07/10/the-uniform-project-womens-soccer/">women&#8217;s soccer</a>. Next Thursday we&#8217;ll have the latest in the series.</p>

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/wsocbig_MB071014.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2280" alt="The Uniform Project - Women's Soccer" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/wsocbig_MB071014-300x170.jpg" width="300" height="170" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/wsocbig_MB071014-300x170.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/wsocbig_MB071014-500x284.jpg 500w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/07/wsocbig_MB071014.jpg 780w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>Women&#8217;s soccer is one of the youngest varsity sports at Muhlenberg. <p>Women&#8217;s soccer was elevated to varsity status in 1991 and won a postseason tournament (ECAC Mid-Atlantic) in just its eighth season. The Mules have had only three head coaches in their 23-year existence &#8211; and not many more uniform styles. From 1994 to 2005, the Mule logo appeared on the left side with the number either on the right or centered, and since 2006, it&#8217;s been the word Muhlenberg, in various styles, across the top.</p>
<p>Previous projects have featured the sports of <a title="The Uniform Project - Track &amp; Field" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/06/21/the-uniform-project-track-field/" target="_blank">track and field</a>, <a title="The Uniform Project - Field Hockey" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/06/28/the-uniform-project-field-hockey/">field hockey</a>, <a title="The Uniform Project - Men's Soccer" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2012/07/19/the-uniform-project-mens-soccer/" target="_blank">men’s soccer</a> and <a title="The Uniform Project - Baseball" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/07/03/the-uniform-project-baseball/">baseball</a>.
