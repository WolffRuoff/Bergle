			<div id="post-2487" class="post-2487 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/getting-muhlenvolved/" rel="bookmark">Getting Muhlenvolved</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/getting-muhlenvolved/" title="4:25 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 23, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>It&#8217;s crazy to think that I&#8217;ve only been here for a month! College has been treating me well, and there are so many opportunities that didn&#8217;t exist in high school. I&#8217;ve been trying to get involved with as many things as possible without getting overwhelmed, and I&#8217;ve actually been doing a pretty good job! Here&#8217;s a rundown of what I&#8217;m currently involved in:</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="line-height: 16px;">Classes! Obviously, isn&#8217;t that why I&#8217;m here? My classes are all going well, the introductory affect has worn off and the real quizzes, essays, and tests are setting in. I&#8217;m glad they eased us into it a bit, and I&#8217;m really thankful I took AP classes in high school, because they actually prepared me to read 30 pages of 3 different subjects a night and retain the information.</span></li>
<li>Colleges Against Cancer. This is a club that I&#8217;m in, and I&#8217;m really enjoying it. The club does many different things, from planning Relay for Life to educating members on different types of cancers. Last meeting, we made cards for kids with pediatric cancer in Philadelphia hospitals.</li>
<li>Class Council. This is something that just had its first meeting, but seems really fun. Basically, class council is the social chair of each class. Each class is involved with planning both events for their class, and the upperclasses are in charge of more school-wide events (for example, the sophomore class is in charge of planning the auction at family weekend). I&#8217;m going to be planning an event for the class of 2017 in November, and I couldn&#8217;t be more excited!</li>
<li>Sinternet! We&#8217;re only 9 days away from opening, ahh! Starting this week, we&#8217;ll have rehearsals every night from 7-11 pm, and longer on the weekends. The show is pretty put together, so we&#8217;re going to be adding costumes and lights and cleaning up vocals and choreography.</li>
<li>Campus delegates. Starting soon, I will be hosting potential students for the day, and, in the spring, overnight. I&#8217;ll be showing them what Muhlenberg is like, taking them to classes with me, and introducing them to my friends! This is really exciting for me, I can&#8217;t wait to meet potential students and show them everything that&#8217;s wonderful about Muhlenberg.</li>
</ul>
<p>That&#8217;s about everything I do on a regular basis! I&#8217;m pretty busy, but I still have time to order pizza with my friends, attend the homecoming game (go Mules!) and, thankfully, to study! I can&#8217;t wait to see what the next month has in store for me.</p>
<p>Always,</p>
<p>Emily</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/homecoming-weekend/" rel="bookmark">Homecoming Weekend</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/homecoming-weekend/" title="10:16 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 18, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/DSC00012.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-2478 alignleft" alt="DSC00012" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/DSC00012-168x300.jpg" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/DSC00012-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/09/DSC00012-576x1024.jpg 576w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>As most people know, this weekend is homecoming weekend. For a lot of people, this is a time to see teammates, classmates, and, most importantly, friends that have graduated. This is a big time for alums who take this chance to come back to their beloved school.</p>
<p>The activities of home coming weekend are centered around the many sporting events that are happening all around campus. If you were sitting around hoping for an itinerary of all the home games this weekend, then you are in luck. Here it is:</p>
<ol>
<li>The Women&#8217;s volleyball team starts us off with their match against Johns Hopkins at 1:00.</li>
<li>Next we have the football team taking on McDaniel at 2:00.</li>
<li>Right after that, we have the Women&#8217;s soccer game against Johns Hopkins at 3:00.</li>
<li>The night cap and our final game of the day is the Men&#8217;s soccer game against Gettysburg at 7:00.</li>
</ol>
<p>These games are only magnified by the fact that each sports team is facing a conference opponent. The Centennial Conference is very competitive, and every game means so much. That also proves how important it is to get a great fan base to support our school. I am not telling you to go to every game, because that is just about impossible. All I am saying is if you have a little free time, stop by the field or court and check out one of the games. One thing I know is that all of us athletes here at school love the chance to play with a big crowd behind us. Let&#8217;s get some wins this weekend, and GO MULES!</p>

			<div id="post-2475" class="post-2475 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog tag-arezzoitaly-abroad-theater">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/abroad-in-arezzo/" rel="bookmark">Abroad in Arezzo!!</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/09/abroad-in-arezzo/" title="10:14 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 18, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/kcmorse/" title="View all posts by KC Morse">KC Morse</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I&#8217;m here in Arezzo Italy! I&#8217;ve been here for almost two weeks now, and I&#8217;m finally settled in to my room and my schedule here.</p>
<p>First of all it&#8217;s BEAUTIFUL here, everywhere you look there is gorgeous view of Â Tuscany. My schedule is such that I have about 3, 2-hour classes a day Monday through Friday and breakfast, lunch and dinner throughout the day. So it&#8217;s a pretty tight schedule, but I love it! I&#8217;m taking a Movement Class (which is kind of a mix of Modern dance and Yoga), a Voice and Speech class, a Philosophy of the Arts class, and Italian! Starting Monday we will have our first Commedia Class, which is a certain type of theater that we are focusing on here in Italy!</p>
<p>Every class has been SO wonderful and I&#8217;ve already learned so much! Our Movement teacher is Claudia; she is German, and she is so talented, OH MY GOD! She makes everything look so effortless and beautiful, it&#8217;s amazing. Voice and Speech is taught by Kevin (if any of you got to see last year&#8217;s production of Bartholomew Fair, it was directed by Kevin). He is a hilarious British man who does lots of fun and hard exercises with us so that we can learn how to project and scream and cry and laugh on stage without straining our voices. Philosophy is taught by a Syrian woman (whose name I can&#8217;t remember!), and so far it is a very interesting class. I&#8217;m lucky to have a class with some differing point of views on our readings, and we always have a great discussion! For example: we had a wonderful class discussion on whether Comedy was &#8220;better&#8221; than Tragedy! We came to the conclusion that both are needed in a show or any piece of art to be successful; so that when dealing with tragic matters comedy can lighten the mood and keep the audience interested, or having tragic matter in comedies to keep a piece grounded. Lastly Italian; I&#8217;ve taken two semesters of Italian at Muhlenberg, so I am in the second level Italian class with 6 other people from Muhlenberg. A few people have taken Italian recently and a few (like myself) took it freshman year, so this week has been lots and lots or review for us, but so far it&#8217;s going well!</p>
<p>We eat breakfast, lunch and dinner Monday- Friday here in the villa and then have time on the weekends to explore other places or the town of Arezzo!Â The food here is INCREDIBLE! Seriously, I&#8217;m not sure I will ever be able to leave here because the food just won&#8217;t be the same! Everything here is just so fresh; the pasta, the pizza, the meat, the vegetables &#8211; it&#8217;s seriously crazy good. But what I have found to be the most beloved of all the food here is&#8230;.NUTELLA! Yes the wonderful hazelnut and chocolate mix that becomes nutella is actually more amazing (if that&#8217;s possible) here than in the USA, and everyone here agrees! We have it for breakfast, lunch and dinner, usually on bread, but my friends and I have started having it with bananas for dessert (sort of healthy right?).</p>
<p>Well that&#8217;s all for now! I&#8217;m visiting the beach town of Viareggio Italy this weekend, so I&#8217;ll have some fun updates from there! Until next time ðŸ™‚</p>
