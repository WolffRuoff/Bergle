<h2><strong><img class="alignleft" src="https://muhlenberg.meyerandassoc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/185/2017/10/069-MUHL.png" width="430" height="135" /></strong></h2>
<h3>Digital Media Assistant, Muhlenberg Advancement</h3>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left"><strong>On-site Internship Advisor:</strong> <a href="mailto:heatherlavin@muhlenberg.edu">Heather Lavin</a> ’08, Senior Associate Director of Advancement Communication</p>



<p><strong>Goals</strong></p>



<p>The goals of Advancement’s Digital Media Assistant are to:</p>



<ul>
<li>Gain an understanding of social media marketing and digital communication strategies.</li>
<li>

Develop a comprehensive content calendar for Muhlenberg Alumni Channels on Facebook, Twitter and Instagram.</li>
<li>Provide student perspective in the development of young alumni programs and social media marketing.</li>
<li>Develop comprehensive communication campaigns that engage our online audience. </li>
<li>Learn to evaluate digital communications through analysis including social media insights and Google Analytics.</li>
<li> Execute social media marketing plan for events and mini-campaigns, including real-time photo sharing during the weekend.</li>
<li>Support the Sen. Associate Director of Communications in creating website content, news articles and/or emails. Training provided.</li>
</ul>



<p><strong>Job Duties</strong></p>



<ul>
<li>Interviews in April-summer; internship begins the first week of classes in the fall.</li>
<li>Attend weekly meetings with the Sen. Associate Director of Alumni Relations to discuss strategy, ideas, and project feedback.</li>
<li>Create and implement social media marketing plans for regional alumni events, campus and alumni. This includes marketing design, social networking updates and scheduling.</li>
<li>Submit regular social media analytics and monthly overviews of online activity.</li>
<li>Archiving for our online media through collecting old and current campus photos, documents, Weekly articles and yearbook photos.</li>
<li>Write short news stories and content for MuhlenbergConnect.com, our alumni website.</li>
<li>End of semester presentation to the Office of Advancement staff on alumni social media activity, strategy, future goals and recommendations.</li>
</ul>



<p>Work 10-12 hours per week to meet the Media &amp; Comm. required time.</p>

		<p><strong>Development &amp; Marketing Intern</strong> <strong>Job Description</strong></p>
<p>Accountable to: Director of Development &amp; Marketing</p>
<p>General Description: Internships are available in Development &amp; Marketing year-round. This internship is appropriate for candidates studying communications, marketing, public relations, event coordination, or related areas. Interns are not required to be enrolled with an educational institution or program at the time of internship. This internship is unpaid. Intern may receive academic credits if applicable.</p>
<p><strong>Job responsibilities</strong> will vary based upon focus area of internship. Responsibilities may include any of the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Creative writing of web content, articles for e-mail newsletter, etc.</li>
<li>Press releases and related communication with the news media</li>
<li>Photography</li>
<li>Public speaking – communication with other Zoo departments</li>
<li>Development of Zoo membership program</li>
<li>Data entry</li>
<li>Office administrative tasks (typing, filing, folding, collating, mailings, etc.)</li>
<li>Development of sponsorship proposals</li>
<li>Communication with sponsors including sponsorship fulfillment</li>
<li>Phone work (incoming and outgoing)</li>
<li>Planning and execution of events, activities, and Zoo rentals (day and evening)</li>
<li>Direct contact with Zoo visitors and general public in a promotional capacity</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Required Knowledge and Skills:</strong> High school diploma or GED with minimum two years in a college program (or equivalent experience) in communications, creative writing, marketing, events, or any similar course of study.  A working knowledge of basic office computing (MS Word, Excel) is required.  Scheduling requirements are flexible.  Credit is available at respective educational institutions; all credit requirements will be met, but intern is responsible for coordinating internship with educational institution.  Interns are not required to be enrolled in an academic program and may elect to intern on an individual basis for job experience only.</p>
<p>To apply, please mail resume and internship application form to:<br />
Lehigh Valley Zoo<br />
Attention: Director, Development &amp; Marketing<br />
5150 Game Preserve Road<br />
P.O.Box 519<br />
Schnecksville, PA 18078<br />
or email <a href="mailto:skline@lvzoo.org">skline@lvzoo.org</a></p>

		<p>The Civic Theatre of Allentown has posted several internships for the <strong>2014-2015 school year.</strong></p>
<p><strong>ARTS MARKETING INTERN</strong></p>
<p><b>Description:</b></p>
<p>Civic Theatre of Allentown is looking for a qualified intern to join our marketing/advertising team. Our theatre is constantly working to market our live theatre, film and education programs. We need an intern to help us create and execute new and innovative marketing campaigns that appeal to new and current patron. This internship will also include assisting in the securing of advertisers and sponsors for the theatre&#8217;s various publications.</p>
<p>This intern should be prepared to work in a team environment, and will finish the internship with confidence, having gained experience in Art Marketing.</p>
<p><b>Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Assist in the creation of signage, circulars, e-mail campaigns, on line promotion, etc.</li>
<li>Assist in the distribution or delivery of marketing materials</li>
<li>Perform analysis of marketing and sales data</li>
<li>Maintain database of marketing contacts</li>
<li>Provide support to social media efforts</li>
<li>Create advertising and marketing relationships with local businesses.</li>
<li>Assist in the design and layout of theatre publications.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Requirements:</b></p>
<p>Civic Theatre is looking for an undergraduate student who is majoring in Marketing, Advertising, Arts Management or Public Relations. This person should have excellent verbal and written communication skills, with extensive knowledge of Word, Excel and social media. Desktop Publisher experience is a bonus, and will be considered when choosing the best applicant for this internship position.</p>
<p><strong>Contact:</strong></p>
<p>Amanda Mitchell via email at Amanda@civictheatre.com  This unpaid internship requires that you submit a resume and cover letter.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong></strong><strong>ARTS ADMINISTRATION INTERN</strong></p>
<p><strong>Description: </strong></p>
<p>Civic Theatre of Allentown is looking for an intern to join our team and learn the ropes of managing a local Non-Profit Theatre in the midst of a hectic and exciting production schedule. The intern hired for this position should expect to be involved in all aspects of theater management.</p>
<p>This intern will work closely with each member of the Civic Theatre team including but not limited to the Managing Director, Artistic Director, Box Office Manger, Volunteer Coordinator and Development Director. This is a team of art enthusiasts who care deeply about producing quality work. Interns will learn the ins and outs of theater management, helping to produce and watching some of the best theater and film this area has to offer.</p>
<p><b>Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Perform background research on upcoming films and live theatre productions</li>
<li>Assist with implementation of new education programs</li>
<li>Assist with documentation and analysis of each live theatre event or film</li>
<li>Answer patron phone calls</li>
<li>Take ticket orders</li>
<li>Enroll Theatre School students</li>
<li>Develop promotional materials</li>
<li>Perform online marketing and promotion</li>
<li>Lead volunteer initiatives</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Requirements:</b></p>
<p>Excellent research, written and oral communication skills, first-rate organizational skills and attention to detail are important. The intern needs to be able to work independently with minimal supervision. A strong interest in entering a position in the non-profit sector&#8211;the arts, theater, museums&#8211;is vital. Computer skills with proficiency in Word and Excel are necessary, website and social media experience preferred.</p>
<p><strong>Contact</strong>:</p>
<p>Amanda Mitchell via email at Amanda@civictheatre.com.   This unpaid internship requires that you submit a resume and cover letter.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>DEVELOPMENT INTERN</strong></p>
<p><strong>Description: </strong></p>
<p>Civic Theatre of Allentown is looking for an intern to work closely with our Director of Development. Development is a vital department in a non-profit theatre. This intern will develop skills fundamental to non-profit fundraising and arts administration through hands on work. They will also join a team of art enthusiasts who care deeply about producing quality work.</p>
<p><strong>Responsibilities:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><span class="Apple-style-span" style="line-height: 18px">Assist with the cultivation and stewardship of individual and institutional donors</span></li>
<li><span class="Apple-style-span" style="line-height: 18px">Learn industry best practices for annual fund communications, including solicitations and acknowledgements, as well as grantwriting fundamentals and reporting</span></li>
<li><span class="Apple-style-span" style="line-height: 18px">Help prepare for and staff large scale benefit and small-scale cultivation events as well as Board and staff meetings.</span></li>
<li><span class="Apple-style-span" style="line-height: 18px">Aid the department with research projects and develop strong database skills.</span></li>
<li><span class="Apple-style-span" style="line-height: 18px">Carry out crucial administrative support, including filing, correspondence, and database management.</span></li>
<li><span class="Apple-style-span" style="line-height: 18px">Provide excellent customer service to an audience of patrons, donors, and high-level volunteers.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Requirements:</strong><br />
Excellent research, written and oral communication skills, first-rate organizational skills and attention to detail are important. The intern needs to be able to work independently with minimal supervision. A strong interest in entering a position in the non-profit sector&#8211; the arts, theater, museum&#8211; is vital. Computer skills with proficiency in Word and Excel are necessary, website and social media preferred. Interest in non-profit career opportunities and a passion for the arts highly encouraged.</p>
<p><strong>Contact:</strong></p>
<p>Amanda Mitchell via email at Amanda@civictheatre.com.   This unpaid internship requires that you submit a resume and cover letter.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

		<p><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/images.jpeg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1000" alt="images" src="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/images-300x80.jpeg" width="300" height="80" srcset="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/images-300x80.jpeg 300w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/10/images.jpeg 408w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>The Jewish Federation of the Lehigh Valley seeks interns for <strong><em>two positions for the spring 2014</em></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>MARKETING DEPARTMENT</strong></p>
<p>In today’s economy, it’s not enough to earn a bachelor’s degree from a well-respected university. To compete with thousands of others vying for the same marketing, journalism and non-profit jobs, you need practical, hands-on experience.  That’s where we come in. At the Jewish Federation of the Lehigh Valley, we produce a monthly newspaper and countless marketing pieces including print advertisements, event invitations, postcards, e-newsletters, posters and mailings. And we need YOU.</p>
<p>As a marketing intern, you’ll gain experience in a variety of areas, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>Writing</li>
<li>Reporting</li>
<li>Editing</li>
<li>Graphic Design</li>
<li>Web Content Management</li>
<li>Social media</li>
<li>Marketing</li>
<li>Non-Profit Management</li>
<li>Event Planning</li>
<li>Photography</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>We’re looking for an enthusiastic, creative intern who brings some of these skills to the table but is also willing to learn. Are you interested in finding out how a newspaper is produced from start to finish? Then this is the place for you. Are you interested in learning how to market an event from conception to execution? Then this is the place for you.  Contact Stephanie Smartschan, Director of Marketing at the Jewish Federation of the Lehigh Valley, 610-821-5500, ext 328 or stephanie@jflv.org by <strong>December 1, 2013</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>DEVELOPMENT INTERN</strong></p>
<p>Gain practical, hands-on experience working in the non-profit development arena and make a difference in the lives of Jews here in the Lehigh Valley, in Israel and around the world.</p>
<p>The Jewish Federation of the Lehigh Valley is seeking a motivated intern who can jump into projects quickly and is a real team player. Strong writing skills and good communication skills are a must.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Duties of the development intern will include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Event management with special focus on the annual golf tournament planned for June 9, 2014</li>
<li>Corporate sponsor solicitation</li>
<li>List and database management</li>
<li>Donor research</li>
<li>Marketing support</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Interested applicants should submit resumes to Judy Diamondstein, assistant executive director, at <a href="mailto:judy@jflv.org">judy@jflv.org</a> no later than <strong>December 1, 2013</strong><strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>About the Jewish Federation of the Lehigh Valley</b></p>
<p>Together with our donors, the Jewish Federation of the Lehigh Valley works to make our community and the world a better place. Acting on our core values of compassion, charity, generosity and responsibility, we care for people in need and inspire Jewish life and learning.</p>
<p>The Jewish Federation is a non-profit organization serving the needs of the Allentown, Bethlehem and Easton Jewish communities. Our offices are located at 702 N. 22nd St., Allentown. Find out more at www.jewishlehighvalley.org.</p>
