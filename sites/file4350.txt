			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/11/snowy-halloween/" rel="bookmark">Snowy Halloween</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/11/snowy-halloween/" title="10:43 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 2, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>There was a wintery blanket of snow covering the entirety of Muhlenberg this past weekend. I think bizarre is the right word for what it felt like waking up last Saturday to see snow covering the ground with Halloween only a couple days away. Regardless, it looked beautiful outside however bizarre it was.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/DSC00030.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-688" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/DSC00030-300x168.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/DSC00030-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/DSC00030-1024x575.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>This past week/weekend has been full of wonderful activities surrounding Halloween. Earlier last week, there was an event called Trick of Treat through the halls. This is when faculty and staff bring their kids, dressed up in costumes, to the freshman dorms and trick or treat from door to door. All of the kids are so cute and it is a really fun event.</p>
<p>This weekend also happened to be parent’s weekend. There were tons upon tons of events happening because of that. Last Friday night there was a talent show that gave parents and other students a taste of all the performance groups on campus. I got to perform with my tap group Copasetic Rhythms and that was so much fun. It was also great to see how many talented groups are on campus. There are so many ways to express through dancing, singing and many other forms so it was nice to see some of them on Friday. There is a picture of the tap group I am in below. 🙂</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/copes-pic.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-689" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/copes-pic-300x201.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="201" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/copes-pic-300x201.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/11/copes-pic-1024x688.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>Saturday, I got to see the production of Merrily We Roll Along, which if you are close to Muhlenberg, you should come out and see it. It is running for another week. It&#8217;s a wonderful performance filled with so much creativity and talent and everything from costumes to the set are gorgeous. I am still humming the tunes today. I also dressed up with my suitemates for Halloween. We were the Wizard of Oz so I got to be Dorothy and my suitemates dressed up as the lion, scarecrow and tinman. No matter what age, Halloween is such a fun time to dress up and get really creative, which I love. They also showed Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows during the day and the fact that they can get movies to come here before they get released on DVD is really cool.</p>
<p>All in all, as cold and snowy as it was this weekend, Halloweekend was full of really exciting activities and lots of great friends. I hope everyone got the chance to eat some candy corn and watch their favorite scary movie.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/something-delicious-this-way-comes/" rel="bookmark">Something delicious this way comes&#8230;</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/something-delicious-this-way-comes/" title="2:19 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 24, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>This weekend has been filled with food salty, sweet and everything in between. On Thursday, we had something on campus called Mules at the Market. This was when students could go down to the farmer&#8217;s market in Allentown ( you can walk there so easily) and get discounts for being a Muhlenberg student and try samples of food from a variety of stores at the market. There is really every kind of food you can imagine. You can find something new every time you go. I have an egg allergy so this is some place that I go and get incredible deserts that are egg free.  It was a fun trip and it made me realize just how much there is to try in Allentown and in the surrounding areas. It was a lot of fun having so much to try and eat this weekend. Here is a video of my trip to the farmer&#8217;s market and to Vegan Treats! Enjoy!</p>

			<div id="post-620" class="post-620 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/as-the-leaves-change/" rel="bookmark">As the leaves change&#8230;</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/as-the-leaves-change/" title="3:25 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">October 17, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>The leaves are changing colors and Seegers Union smells like Pumpkin Spice Lattes. Fall has officially arrived. I got to pull out my favorite sweaters and wear boots that have collected a little dust. I love fall at Muhlenberg. Walking down academic row or really anywhere on campus and starting to see trees and the array of colors is really beautiful.</p>
<p>Here are a couple pictures and a video from the common room in my dorm of the view. Check it out! <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dozjj-bqwPI">Lounge View</a></p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/DSC00006.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-625" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/DSC00006-168x300.jpg" alt="" width="168" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/DSC00006-168x300.jpg 168w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/DSC00006-575x1024.jpg 575w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/DSC00006.jpg 1324w" sizes="(max-width: 168px) 100vw, 168px" /></a></p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/DSC00003.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-624" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/DSC00003-300x168.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="168" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/DSC00003-300x168.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/DSC00003-1024x575.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>As you know from my last blog, I just finished my New Voices production, which was a great experience. My fellow blogger Eliana wrote one of the plays for the New Voices festival, and she has been writing about her experience so click on Eliana’s blog above and check that out. And while the door of one experience closed, another one opened. That door led to midterms.</p>
<p>Being on the other side of midterms, I can say that I made it. It was a little like running a marathon, but I crossed the finish line with a little time to spare. What is nice about this whole process though is that everyone is going through midterms as well. So I made some midterm study groups. It consisted of some of my friends, lots of coffee, snacks and hours of time. It made the fact that we were studying for hours on end more enjoyable. I had three huge exams to study for: Finance, Science and Operations and Information Systems. Lots of memorizing was done. Flashcards were my best friends for the last couple of days. But it is over now, and I have some time to relax. I got to see Lion King 3D recently, and that was a great break from textbooks, papers and calculations.</p>
<p>So with midterms behind me and fall days ahead of me, I think this should be a nice weekend. The door to midterms has closed and I can’t wait for another door with another experience to open. I would suggest taking a walk around outside if you get the chance. The air is nice and the scenery, at least here, is quite a sight to see.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/as-the-curtain-begins-to-rise/" rel="bookmark">As the Curtain Begins to Rise&#8230;</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/as-the-curtain-begins-to-rise/" title="11:13 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 29, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>The only thing glowing other than the stage in front of me is my laptop as I write this blog post. I am currently in the Studio Theatre working through the technical aspects of a production I am directing that goes up this Saturday. Every two years, students at Muhlenberg submit plays that they have written to be a part of a festival called New Voices. This year five shows were chosen and I have the pleasure of directing one of these shows. The show I am directing is called Transcranium written by alumni Shannon Wittenberger. The show is about a girl who is living with agoraphobia and will not leave her apartment. She lives with a boyfriend and she sees a therapist but both guys believe the other to be imaginary. Are you intrigued? It is a very cool show and you should come see it and all the other wonderful works in New Voices. Because of all this, I spent the past weekend and most of my weeknights this week in my home away from home: the theatre. No, its true. Tech is when you pull out the chocolate you eat when you are stressed and the sweatshirts you wear because it is so cold in the theatre. It is full of late nights and long hours but it is also full of watching theatre come to life with wonderful designs, lights, sets, costumes and sounds. Below are some photos from my tech rehearsal. Check them out!</p>
<p>All the shows looks fantastic and there are so much student collaboration, which makes the whole production an exciting process. There are student writers, directors, stage managers and lighting designer. Being a part of New Voices is really showing me all the incredible opportunities I can get here. I am getting hands on directing experience and learning so much from it. Not only are the students so talented but also the faculty mentors are invaluable in helping me grow through this learning experience. I could probably talk about this forever but its back into the darkness from my seat in the fourth row. I see the cast take their places and I see the stage manager call the first cue. The lights go out. The show is about to begin.</p>

			<div id="post-545" class="post-545 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/hello-and-happy-300th/" rel="bookmark">Hello and Happy 300th!</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/hello-and-happy-300th/" title="9:25 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 22, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hey everyone! Thanks for taking some time out to read my blog. It should be a very fun year between junior year fall adventures and spring semester abroad in PERUGIA, ITALY (I am incredibly excited for this experience), so I hope you enjoy hearing all about it 🙂</p>
<p>Let me take a minute to introduce myself. My name is Dani Barlow. I am currently a junior and a Theatre and Business Double Major. I am currently directing a one-act student written show in a festival called the New Voices Play Festival, which goes up October 1<sup>st</sup> (so soon!).  You will probably be hearing a lot about it in the next week because it has been an amazing experience so far. I am the secretary of the Muhlenberg Theatre Association for the fall, which is a lot of work but a lot of fun. I am also in a tap group on campus called Copasetic Rhythms. I am a campus delegate, which allows prospective students the chance to shadow a student on campus for the day. Between all of this and my classes, I have gained a special connection with coffee that will never be broken. It is a lot of work but I love every minute of it.</p>
<p>This past weekend, there was a huge celebration on campus. It was Homecoming and many alumni came back. It was wonderful to see friends and hear how well they are all doing post-Muhlenberg. I went to the Homecoming football game (where we won!) and I got to perform at half time with my tap group. After that it was off to rehearsal. I am the understudy for a tap piece in a dance show called Moving Stories, which gives student choreographers an opportunity to showcase their work. The piece is wicked hard but so much fun!</p>
<p>Then at 9pm that night there was another celebration. Homecoming weekend was in conjunction with the celebration of Henry Melchior Muhlenberg’s 300th Birthday! And to honor his birthday, there was a flashmob (a group of people who go to a public place and all happen to know the same dance to a song). A good amount of people showed up and it was a lot of fun. The alumni seemed to really enjoy it and a couple tried to join in. Check out the video here to see it.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fgaz7U_Vt1Y">Birthday Flashmob</a></p>
<p>So that was my homecoming adventures. Happy 300<sup>th</sup> Henry!</p>
