		<p>ELL: A majority of class this week was focus of SIOP. Me and a few other people worked on revising a lesson that was given to us. What really stood out to me the most is that I am finally on the other side. Yes, I am a student at school. However, I am no longer just the student. I am also the teacher and soon I will be a teacher. SIOP is the first step to this realization that someday soon I will be planning and executing lessons of my own. After all, I already did that last week in my fieldwork classroom, with the assessment.</p>
<p>GEOGRAPHY: Something that I find kind of funny about the &#8220;quinoa fad&#8221; is that seven years ago, before everybody really knew about it, my mom had be eating quinoa like no tomorrow. She said it was super healthy and I never really thought too much about it. When it came to processed foods like cereals or snacks my mom would always mention how they came from plants where stuff is processed and chemicals are added. However, she never really mentioned (probably because she does not know) that healthy food, like quinoa can come from &#8220;bad&#8221; places too. Yes, it may not be processed however it is still not from a pure place that is not hurting people.</p>
<p>FIELDWORK: Last week I gave my assessment in fieldwork and the previous week I wrote my paper regarding comprehensible input. This week, in ELL, we spoke of of SIOP, and this is something that made all of the moving pieces that we have been doing come together in my mind. SIOP is the overarching idea/concept that surrounds, not only the teaching of English Language Learners, but all learners in general. Personally, I feel that Comprehensible Input and SIOP are paired together, as one and that the assessment that I gave and bits and pieces of each, without me even realizing.</p>
