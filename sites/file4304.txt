			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/whipping-pediatric-cancer/" title="8:51 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 22, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/swedberg/" title="View all posts by Drew">Drew</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>September is Pediatric Cancer Awareness Month, and today one of the Resident Advisor&#8217;s here at Muhlenberg College set up an event to raise money for Alex&#8217;s Lemonade Stand Foundation (check out some of the information and how you can support at <a title="Alex's Lemonade Stand" href="http://www.alexslemonade.org" target="_blank">http://www.alexslemonade.org</a>)! Inspired by the ALS Ice Bucket Challenge, my friend took it upon herself to set up the Whipping Pediatric Cancer Challenge to raise money and awareness for a cause that really hits home for her, as her younger brother is one of the many, many, many children in the world fighting the battle against Pediatric Cancer.</p>
<p>Motivated by her kind heart, I got myself up at a reasonable time and took to the field to show my support. We lined up in a semicircle and took turns pie-ing the person to our left as the event was recorded. I don&#8217;t know if taking a pie to the face was my original idea of wrapping up the week nicely, but it turned out to be a sweet time!</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/happy-birthday-robin/" title="8:50 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 22, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>September 21st was Robin Riley-Casey&#8217;s birthday! </strong></p>
<p>Robin is the director of Multicultural Life here at Muhlenberg, but she&#8217;s so much more than that to those who have gotten to know her personally. She&#8217;s giving, caring and such an inspiration to those around her. I first got to know Robin through the Emerging Leaders Program (ELP; a pre-orientation program geared towards those who have an interest in expanding diversity and multicultural life at Muhlenberg). Because of her desire to create a welcoming space that students consider &#8220;home,&#8221; I instantly felt comfortable.</p>
<p>St. Thomas is a diverse community; the majority of people living there are African-Americans, and then you have Indians, Arabs, Hispanics, the French and whites and those from other Caribbean islands&#8230; any culture you are looking for, St. Thomas will probably have. At Muhlenberg however, the majority of people are white, and there is an incredibly small community of those from different backgrounds. Though the high school I went to on island was predominantly white, I was still shocked at how different the overall culture of the college was compared to home. Robin and the Emerging Leaders Program gave me a sense of relief knowing that I had a place to go to when I felt lost amongst the rest of peers&#8230; when the overwhelming &#8220;whiteness&#8221; of the student body made me feel like an outsider.</p>
<p>Being apart of the ELP enabled me to meet and befriend other students of color. The program prepared me for the rest of my college career and if I was not a part of it, I do not think I would be who I am today. From the moment I stepped onto this campus, I felt as though I was a part of something, and in addition to becoming my on campus family, the program and those involved assisted in the transition from high school to college. On the first day of the program we had homework to turn in and lessons on how to adapt to the college life-style.</p>
<p>So in honor of Robin and all that she has done, Jasmine Anderson (one of the interns at the Multicultual Center) put together a surprise birthday party! In attendance we had Emerging Leaders from the Class of 2015, 2016, 2017 and 2018. We also had faculty, the Dean of Students, and those who were not even a part of the Emerging Leaders but just love Robin! Jasmine put together such a wonderful event. There was food, music, dancing and you can tell that everyone had a wonderful time.</p>
<p>So thank you Jasmine for putting together this surprise party for Robin, thank you Robin for everything you have done for us, and I hope you had an amazing time. It&#8217;s important for us to recognize those who have made a difference in our lives, and Robin you have touched the hearts of us all.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/student-government/" rel="bookmark">Student Government</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/student-government/" title="8:34 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 22, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>This semester, 4 elected members of the Student Government team are abroad or unable to serve on SGA, so during last night&#8217;s meeting we had prospective representatives come and make a speech to be voted in.</p>
<p>There were 14 prospective candidates for only 4 spots. Everyone did a fantastic job and were so enthusiastic, but the decision was a difficult one. (If you did not get in last night, we have elections at the end of this semester, so there&#8217;s still a chance for you to serve as a representative if you are truly interested.)</p>
<p>However, we were able to shorten the list and then successfully vote in the top 4 choices. Congrats ladies and gentleman, and welcome the Student Government!</p>
<div id="attachment_3054" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00036.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3054" class="wp-image-3054 size-large" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00036-1024x768.jpg" alt="Welcome to the family! #stugovlove" width="640" height="480" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00036-1024x768.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00036-300x225.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3054" class="wp-caption-text">Welcome to the family! #stugovlove</p></div>
<p>Being a part of the Student Government Association (SGA) has allowed me to make and maintain relations with faculty and staff around campus. I get an inside look at how the campus is run, and I am a part of an organization that prides itself on being the voice of the students. If there are issues around campus a student wishes to bring attention to, we are who they come to. We also provide support for other clubs and organizations (i.e., cosponsoring events, allocating money, etc.).  All representatives love Muhlenberg, and we each bring our strengths together to work as a team in resolving these issues.</p>
<p>This is my 2nd term as a SGA representative, and it&#8217;s an organization that I see myself being a part of until I graduate from Muhlenberg. During my first term, I served on the Student Life Committee. On that committee, I was involved in resolving issues regarding Dining Services, Seegers Union and<span style="color: #000000;"> Athletics and Residential Services. I was also a part of the Curriculum Committee where I worked with faculty and staff in </span>finalizing<span style="color: #000000;"> the course offerings for the Class of 2018. This term, I&#8217;m serving on the Operations </span>Committee<span style="color: #000000;"> where we focus on the internal structure of the organization and completely redid our Bylaws and Standing Rules of Order. I just joined the Alternative Break trip Committee, where we will be redoing the requirements for proposals, creating a whole new process, and determining which trips the college will recognize, support and allocate a portion of our funds to. We have a lot of work this semester, and I have a feeling I&#8217;m going to enjoy every bit of it.</span></p>
<p>The representatives of SGA have become a family to me, and I consider many of them my good friends. As aforementioned in a previous post, I love getting involved and helping in any was I possibly can, and SGA allows me to do so. It tests my leadership skills, my diplomacy and my ability to resolve ethically strenuous matters. Every meeting I learn something new, and it&#8217;s amazing. I really love this organization, what it stands for, and those who are able to be a part of it.</p>
<div id="attachment_3052" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00026.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3052" class="wp-image-3052 size-large" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00026-1024x768.jpg" alt="My #Stugov brother, Max!" width="640" height="480" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00026-1024x768.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00026-300x225.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3052" class="wp-caption-text">My #Stugov brother, Max!</p></div>
<div id="attachment_3053" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00032.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3053" class="wp-image-3053 size-large" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00032-1024x768.jpg" alt="our meeting last night" width="640" height="480" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00032-1024x768.jpg 1024w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/09/DSC00032-300x225.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-3053" class="wp-caption-text">Our meeting last night</p></div>

<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/my-first-internship/" rel="bookmark">My First Internship</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/my-first-internship/" title="8:30 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 22, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Getting my internship this semester was actually fairly simple. (<em>Surprisingly!</em>) I am a part of the EnAct mailing list, and my onsite coordinator asked the club to send out a mass email to all members about internships this semester. I read through the descriptions and felt as though the Communication and Social Media Intern position looked the most relevant and useful for my future. I am currently trying to decide whether or whether not to minor in Sustainability Studies, and I have always been interested in environmental issues, so I decided that this internship would be perfect for me. It relates to my major and I am interested in the topics surrounding it. Immediately after reading the description, I emailed the coordinator to set up a meeting with her, only with the intention of getting to know more information about the requirements and necessities for the internship.</p>
<p>When I went to meet with the coordinator, I walked in with a few questions&#8230; I actually feel as though I interviewed her more than she interviewed me. Then again, I didn’t know I was walking into an interview in the first place&#8230; if I had known, I probably wouldn’t have gone in shorts and a t-shirt. During the meeting, I was told that since I was the first person to respond to the mass email, I had the position if I wanted it. Of course I jumped on that opportunity! It definitely paid to be an avid email checker and to be so responsive, something I learned from my on campus job working in the President&#8217;s Office. She wanted someone who was able to start right away so she gave me until the following Tuesday to give her a definitive answer.</p>
<p>She had my answer by the end of the day. That position was <strong>mine.</strong></p>
<p>I know that getting other internships won&#8217;t be as easy as it was to get this one, but I&#8217;m so excited to be working for the Office of Campus Sustainability this semester. It&#8217;s going to be so much work, and time consuming, but it will worth the experience.</p>
<p>Until next time,</p>
<p>Felisa</p>

			<div id="post-3072" class="post-3072 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog tag-challenges tag-college tag-english tag-freshman tag-fys tag-muhlenberg tag-writing">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/defeating-the-beast-aka-a-college-essay/" rel="bookmark">Defeating the beast, AKA, a college essay</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/09/defeating-the-beast-aka-a-college-essay/" title="6:56 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">September 20, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/jakegordon42/" title="View all posts by Jake Gordon">Jake Gordon</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>This post, I&#8217;m not going to talk about some really cool thing I did this weekend followed by an artsy picture. (Even though I went to a Toga Party which was really fun) I am going to describe a struggle that I, along with other freshman in the first month, am dealing with&#8212; attacking a difficult writing prompt.</p>
<p>It has been preached to me innumerable times that being successful in college writing requires a heightened ability to analyze content and efficiently articulate a point. This is a skill, however, that I am starting to realize is much easier said than done. &#8220;Connecting the dots&#8221; between the author&#8217;s underlying primary theme, multiple published articles, and a primary source, is, I&#8217;m not going to lie, a little bit scary.</p>
<p>I recognize, though, that it is scary in a good way &#8211; if that&#8217;s possible &#8211; because the challenge forces me to think and struggle with the material, ultimately developing that part of my brain. No longer is it sufficient to write a &#8220;five paragraph essay&#8221; with quotes and analysis layered &#8220;like a hamburger.&#8221; College writing begs for so much more in content, style, and presentation.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s not like I&#8217;m already an expert by any means in this fancy &#8220;style and presentation&#8221; but, there are people around me who have mastered such techniques. And times like this in which I am having difficulty, reminds me why I chose Muhlenberg in the first place. I have the resources around me to use at my disposal to help and cater to my academic needs.</p>
<p>Right now, I need help overcoming this writers block. So, I contacted my class writing assistant and scheduled a meeting, I texted an upperclassman for advice, and I asked my professor for time to debrief in the next few days &#8211; all steps that I don&#8217;t know would be available to me if I were somewhere other than Muhlenberg.</p>
<p>The ball is now in my theoretical court. Since I wrote this blog as a diversion to actually doing the assignment, I&#8217;m going to get started.. Wish me luck!</p>
