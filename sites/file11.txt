<ul>
	<li>To find a particular database, check the <a href="http://libraryguides.muhlenberg.edu/az.php" target="_blank">Databases A to Z</a> list. If you&#39;re not sure where to begin, an interdisciplinary database like <a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=https://search.ebscohost.com/login.aspx?profile=ehost&amp;defaultdb=a9h&amp;authtype=ip" target="_blank">Academic Search Complete </a>, <a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=https://www.jstor.org/" target="_blank">JSTOR</a>, or <a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=https://search.ebscohost.com/login.aspx?profile=ehost&amp;defaultdb=ofm&amp;authtype=ip" target="_blank">Omnifile</a> might be a good starting place.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
	<li>To find recommendations for subject-specific databases, try our <a href="http://libraryguides.muhlenberg.edu/c.php?g=698426" target="_blank">Subject Guides</a>.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Get the Full Text </strong></p>

<ul>
	<li>Some databases provide the full text of articles online. Look for <em>PDF</em> or <em>HTML Full Text</em> links or <em>View Now</em> links.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
	<li>If a full text link is not available in the database, use the <em>Check for Full Text </em>button to connect to the article full text from another database or to locate the print/microform version of the journal in the database.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
	<li>If&nbsp;<em>Check for Full Text </em>buttons aren&#39;t available, follow the steps below in the &quot;I Already Have the Article Citation...&quot; section.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
	<li>If Trexler Library does not have online, print, or microform access to the article, request a copy of the article from another library with&nbsp;<a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/library/lendingservices/borrowfromanotherlibrary/" target="_blank">Interlibrary Loan</a>.</li>
</ul>

		   </div>
								</div>
								
							</div>
						</div>
					</div>
							</div>
							<div id="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358321" class="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358321">
					<div id="s-lg-box-15572339-container" class="s-lib-box-container">
						<div id="s-lg-box-15572339" class="s-lib-box s-lib-box-std">
							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">I Already Have the Article Citation and Need the Full Text
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-15572339" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-33972235" class="  clearfix">
				<ul>
	<li>If you have citation information for the article (i.e., journal title, article title, publication year, etc.), type the information about your article in the <a href="https://muhlenberg.on.worldcat.org/atoztitles/search?#article" target="_blank" title="Citation Help Page">Article Locator</a> on the library website. If the library subscribes to the full text online or in print, you will be directed to the article.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
	<li>If Trexler Library does not have access to the journal for the year/volume you need, check to see if the article is held elsewhere by typing the article title into <a href="https://scholar.google.com/" target="_blank">Google Scholar</a>.&nbsp; The link on the right side of the search results page will direct you to full text.&nbsp; If there is no link, then request a copy of the article from another library with&nbsp;<a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/library/lendingservices/borrowfromanotherlibrary/" target="_blank">Interlibrary Loan</a>.</li>
</ul>

		   </div>
								</div>
								
							</div>
						</div>
					</div>
							</div>
							<div id="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358323" class="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358323">
					<div id="s-lg-box-15572417-container" class="s-lib-box-container">
						<div id="s-lg-box-15572417" class="s-lib-box s-lib-box-std">
							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Defining Key Terms
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-15572417" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-33972285" class="  clearfix">
				<p>You may be wondering what we mean when we refer to <em>scholarly</em> versus <em>popular</em>.</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong>Scholarly: </strong>In-depth, research-based articles, books, and book chapters, written by scholars and experts and intended for other researchers, experts, and students.</li>
	<li><strong>Popular: </strong>Articles, books, and book chapters written by journalists or professional writers for a general audience in common language (e.g., newspapers and magazines).</li>
</ul>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="padding-left: 30px;"><em>Learn more: <a href="http://libraryguides.muhlenberg.edu/content.php?pid=471100&amp;sid=4047073" target="_blank">What is Scholarly?</a></em></p>

<p>While we&#39;re at it, how about a few more key terms...</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong>Empirical: </strong>Based on experiment or observation<strong>.</strong></li>
	<li><strong>Literature:&nbsp;</strong>Scholarly articles, books, and book chapters published on a particular subject.</li>
	<li><strong>Peer-Reviewed: </strong>A process through which some scholarly articles and book chapters are critically evaluated by experts with in-depth knowledge of the research area before publication in order to ensure the information is valid, credible, well-written, and of high quality.<span>&nbsp;</span></li>
	<li><strong>Primary / Secondary:</strong> See the <a href="http://libraryguides.muhlenberg.edu/content.php?pid=471100&amp;sid=3874383" target="_blank">Find Primary Sources</a> guide. &nbsp;</li>
</ul>

								</div>
								
							</div>
						</div>
					</div>
							</div>
							<div id="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358325" class="s-lg-box-wrapper-18358325">
					<div id="s-lg-box-15571920-container" class="s-lib-box-container">
						<div id="s-lg-box-15571920" class="s-lib-box s-lib-box-std">
							<h2 class="s-lib-box-title">Verifying Peer-Review
                                </h2>
							<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-15571920" >
								<div class="s-lib-box-content">
									
			<div id="s-lg-content-33972074" class="  clearfix">
				<p>Are you looking especially for <strong>peer-reviewed</strong> articles?&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Many databases will tell you if an article has been peer-reviewed.&nbsp; You may even be able to use limits or filters in the database to narrow your search results.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>If you&#39;re still not sure if an article has been peer-reviewed, search the name of the journal in which the article was published in <a href="http://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://ulrichsweb.serialssolutions.com/" target="_blank">Ulrichsweb Periodicals Directory</a>.&nbsp; The symbol of a referee&#39;s jersey next to the journal name indicates the journal uses peer review.&nbsp; (Refereed is a synonym for peer reviewed.)&nbsp; Note, though, that not all content in peer-reviewed journals is actually peer-reviewed; book or film reviews, letters to the editors, opinion columns, and articles without references are typically not peer-reviewed.</li>
</ul>
