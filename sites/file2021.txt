<li><span style="font-weight: 400">Improve your interviewing and writing skills</span></li>

<li>Boost your portfolio with published and online clippings</li>

		<h4>By Bianca Johnson &#8217;11</h4>
<p><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/02/cbs3logo.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-296" src="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/02/cbs3logo-300x88.jpg" alt="" width="210" height="62" srcset="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/02/cbs3logo-300x88.jpg 300w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/02/cbs3logo-150x44.jpg 150w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/02/cbs3logo.jpg 516w" sizes="(max-width: 210px) 100vw, 210px" /></a>This fall I interned at CBS 3 Eyewitness News in Philadelphia. At first I was worried about how I would balance an internship with my other courses all while still managing to enjoy my senior year, but I soon found out it wouldn&#8217;t be hard at all. I commuted to Philly on Sundays and spent every Sunday and Monday at the station. I was interning in the heart of the newsroom, at the assignment desk. This is where all of the information regarding news comes into the station. My duties included answering viewer phone calls and tip lines, generating story ideas, checking fact, researching people and events, and setting up interviews. I also got to go out in the news van, shadow reports, and even was able to make a DVD of myself practicing reading the news from the teleprompter at the anchor&#8217;s desk. There was nothing more rewarding than going home and seeing segments of the news that I helped with. Overall, this internship was a great learning experience for me, and I was definitely able to apply many of the theories and concepts from my communication courses at Muhlenberg to my work a CBS.</p>

		<h4>By Hannah Mesouani &#8217;11</h4>
<p><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/09/20070912_CircleM.gif"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-229" src="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/09/20070912_CircleM.gif" alt="" width="150" height="150" srcset="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/09/20070912_CircleM.gif 150w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/09/20070912_CircleM-64x64.gif 64w" sizes="(max-width: 150px) 100vw, 150px" /></a>The New Media Specialist Assistant Internship at the Muhlenberg College Public Relations department is as much yours as you want to make it Bill Keller, the site supervisor, very tactfully balanced his role as both my boss and my mentor, encouraging me to help him out with his projects as well as allowing me to pursue my individual desires and interests. Bill allowed me to develop my own idea to document a series of interviews about features of the campus that range from everything from the Mailroom team and Prosser hall to what it means to be a Philosophy major and Multicultural student. The schedule is highly flexible and whilst hours are not to be taken lightly, the workload is always fair and even enjoyable. I utilized my Final Cut Pro editing skills and developed new skills with Photoshop. The internship not only taught me about the ethics of PR and the interview process as well as technical skills in photography, but it taught me about interpersonal communication, respect and professionalism at a level that I had yet to experience. The internship also taught me independence and coached me to explore my creative side to understand and expand upon what the PR department here at Muhlenberg College strives for every day.</p>
