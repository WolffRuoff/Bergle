	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=508" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-11-01T09:17:34-04:00">November 1, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-12-20T16:48:31-04:00">December 20, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=61">Phedra Henninger</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=508" rel="bookmark">What to expect while you are expecting the new phone system</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172334" rel="category">Phone System</a></li>
							<li class="meta-tag"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=mitel" rel="tag">mitel</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?tag=phone" rel="tag">phone</a></li>
						<li class="meta-comment">
			<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=508#respond">Leave a comment</a></span>
		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>OIT is in the midst of the installation of the new campus wide phone system, based on Mitel. We want to provide further clarity about the project and additional details about what you can expect during this transition.</p>
<p><strong>Project Goals</strong></p>
<p>The goal of the first phase, to be completed in 2016, is to replace our aging legacy architecture, which is past its maintenance life and viability in a crisis. Detailed information about the early planning stages of this project are posted on our <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/09/new-phone-system/">IT blog</a>.</p>
<p>The new system provides the same capabilities you have today, as well as some popular, easy-to-use new features. After the initial installation is complete campus wide, we will be expanding the range of supported features and options. Your new Mitel phone is just the beginning.</p>
<p><strong>What to Expect</strong></p>
<p>There is an enormous amount of behind the scenes work accomplished by three different teams. This takes place over a three-week period prior to switching over each campus location to the new system; each week, we start the cycle over again for the next building.</p>
<p>The last time we replaced the phone system was 17 years ago. We will need access to the wall jacks and cables, often behind your desks.  This may require moving books, paperwork, and so on.  We are on tight schedules, and apologize if we ever seem hurried. We will strive to do the work with minimum disruption. Your understanding is greatly appreciated.</p>
<p><strong>Timeline<br />
</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>1-3 weeks prior to cut-over &#8211; Wiring work</strong>
<ul>
<li>Our wiring partner, Westside Hammer, will upgrade existing wiring or repair/improve wall jacks prior to the phones being placed.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>1-2 weeks prior to cut-over &#8211; Network upgrades</strong>
<ul>
<li>OIT staff will install and configure new network equipment in wiring closets. This may result in an early morning interruption of service, depending on your desktop configuration. We usually get these done prior to 7am. If we expect an extended outage, we will communicate that to you.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>1 week prior to cut-over &#8211; Placing the new phones</strong>
<ul>
<li>Staff from Frontier Communications, our phone partner, will set up your new phone. Your new Mitel phone is NOT ready for use; it still needs to be programmed and tested.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Cut-over day</strong><strong>– Go live</strong>
<ul>
<li>The specific day your new phone goes live, or is <strong>cut-over</strong><em>, </em>will be communicated in an e-mail when the date has been scheduled. We usually know about a week ahead of time and will provide that date as early as we can. Dates have been posted on the <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/31/phone-system-installation-schedule/">IT blog</a>, but are subject to change as we move across campus. We will be on-site making sure the new phones work. Documentation basic phone functionality, including how to setup your voicemail, will be provided in a hard copy at the time of service and is <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/documentation/Mitel-3300-training-guide.pdf">available online</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>&#8220;Who are these people?&#8221;<br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>OIT Staff (Sergio Maciujec, Stephen Small, and Joe Shigo)</strong></p>
<p>They are doing site surveys to assess what work needs to be done. They are also installing new network equipment in each building to prepare the infrastructure for the new phone system. In many cases they will be speaking with you personally to address any concerns you have and/or better understand your phone needs.</p>
<p><strong>Westside Hammer (Todd Hanzl and his team)</strong></p>
<p>They are preparing the building wiring to accept the new phones, which use a technology called &#8220;power over Ethernet&#8221; or POE. Some buildings required more work than others.</p>
<p><strong>Frontier Communications (Jim and Steve and their team)</strong></p>
<p>They are the Mitel experts and are deploying, configuring and generally doing the work of installing the new phone system.</p>
<p><strong>Frequently Asked Questions</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>What is &#8220;Cut-over&#8221;?</strong><br />
This is the process of actually “moving” your line from the old system to the new. It is very simple and should only take a few minutes per desk. The new phones can already accomplish most functions &#8211; we just need to route incoming calls to them. The only things we need to do are make the actual change in our database, then a few test calls to make sure everything is working.</li>
<li><strong>How can I access my voicemail?</strong><br />
Voicemail will work as soon as the phone is live. Because they are separate systems, though, you will need to remove all voicemails (listen and delete) from the old system before the cut-over. You may do so by dialing <a href="tel:4846643000">484-664-3000</a> to access the messages on the old system.New voicemail will start working immediately, but you won&#8217;t be able to retrieve messages until you go through the activation process, which includes setting a new PIN. The temporary PIN is “1111”.</li>
<li><strong>What type of support will be provided?</strong><br />
Members of OIT and Frontier will be visiting offices to answer any questions and do on-the-spot training throughout the day. We will also provide documentation on the basic functionality of the phone and it is <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/media/contentassets/pdf/about/oit/documentation/Mitel-3300-training-guide.pdf">available online</a>. Additional training materials are being developed and will be released at a later date.</li>
<li><strong>What are the new features?</strong>There are many new features with the phones, but some notable ones are:
<ul>
<li>Voicemail to email &#8211; receive messages straight to your inbox for listening on the go, on your phone, etc. This is on by default.</li>
<li>Twinning &#8211; concurrent ringing of your desk phone to your cell phone. We have to configure this, and you have the ability to turn it on and off at will. If you are interested in this feature, please contact <a href="mailto:support@muhlenberg.edu">support@muhlenberg.edu</a>.</li>
<li>The new system gives us a great deal of additional functionality and control that will allow for easier management, easier support for people changing offices or new faculty and staff coming on board.</li>
<li>There are many other features, as well. Our plan is to slowly implement these over time to those interested. For example, there are powerful desktop and mobile software clients that some may be interested.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Below please find the installation schedule for our new phone system. You&#8217;ll see it is very aggressive, but it is important that we complete the transition before the end of the calendar year. We have built in &#8220;slack&#8221; weeks in October and November in case we fall behind.</p>
<p>You should receive an email reminding you of the installation the week before and the week of your installation.</p>
<p>Actual cut-over of your phone takes mere minutes per desk. An entire building might take the better part of a day. Training will be offered 1:1 at desks and/or at set times in a conference room or another meeting space. We will be asking for RSVP for the training sessions.</p>
<p>We will deploy phones to desks prior to actual cut-over.They&#8217;ll be installed and powered up and able to make calls, but not yet able to receive until after they are fully cut-over in our networking closets and data center.</p>
<p>The specific steps for us and you are:</p>
<ol>
<li>OIT or our partner Frontier Communications will install a new Mitel phone at your desk.</li>
<li>On the day of cut-over, you must retrieve all voicemail messages from the existing phone system and delete them all.</li>
<li>We will cut-over the phone and verify that calls from both on and off campus are routing correctly.</li>
<li>We will leave documentation with the new phone.</li>
<li>You will receive an email with your temporary voicemail PIN.</li>
<li>You will need to call into your voicemail box and configure your preferred PIN and outgoing messages.</li>
<li>We will come around to show you the features of your phone and/or offer a training session.</li>
</ol>
<p>If there is a reason why the day indicated is not acceptable, please <a href="mailto:support@muhlenberg.edu">let OIT know</a> as soon as possible. Note that we will make every effort to accommodate such requests, but cannot guarantee the change considering the size of any one building and impact on schedule.</p>
<p>We appreciate your understanding with a project of this logistical size.</p>

<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=22152" rel="category">Announcements</a>, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172334" rel="category">Phone System</a></li>

		</li>
					</ul>
</div>
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>For the past year, OIT has been working on a new phone system to replace our aging legacy architecture. Our current system is incredibly robust and reliable, but it&#8217;s past its maintenance life and we&#8217;re essentially relying on spare parts as our contingency plan for dramatic failures. It&#8217;s been time for us to move forward for a while now.</p>
<p>After an exhaustive (and exhausting) selection process, we chose <a href="http://www.mitel.com">Mitel </a>as our new phone solution in early spring. Their Voice over IP (VoIP) phone system offers a powerful, flexible, and expandable communication platform that will open up some interesting doors in the years to come. It&#8217;s much more than merely getting us to current &#8211; it&#8217;s getting us ready for the future, too.</p>
<p>Some of the higher end features will be quite compelling as we adopt them. For instance, there is a desktop app from which one can look up anyone in the phone book and, with a single click, launch a call on the phone. Not a computer call, but a phone call. There is a mobile app that does the same thing. Imagine you&#8217;re driving and need to pull over to call someone, but don&#8217;t have his or her number. Just look it up in the mobile app and fire up the call. We don&#8217;t see these features being in use on day one, but we&#8217;re hopeful that the community will adopt them over time.</p>
<p>Even the phones themselves are upgrades &#8211; they sport multiple &#8220;pages&#8221; of programmable keys, providing up to 36 speed dials/extensions if one wishes. There is visual voicemail &#8211; no need to dial in, just access your messages right there on the phone &#8211; and, finally, full two-way speakerphone capabilities. We are also deploying proper conference phones into as many dedicated meeting spaces as we can.</p>
<p>However, this isn&#8217;t <em>just</em> a phone system upgrade. New networking equipment is needed in literally all of our buildings, and new wiring in merely almost all of our buildings. The good news is that this is a valuable chance for us to make large, critically-important upgrades to our core infrastructure. The bad news is that this all takes a lot of time.</p>
<p>Right now, we&#8217;re looking at the bulk of the installation later in the fall term. Since phone deployment takes merely minutes, we likely won&#8217;t aim for a particular break, but it will be all done in a single day. We are doing a soft-launch currently in Moyer and Ettinger halls, however, to see what questions arise. The system itself is completely stable and tested &#8211; it&#8217;s a matter of what concerns arise, what user questions come up that we might not have anticipated, and how we can be most successful during the full launch.</p>
<p>The phones will operate exactly as they do now &#8211; dial &#8220;8&#8221; for an outside line, for instance. Voicemail is working. The only thing that won&#8217;t work for now is the ability to forward voicemails from one system to the other.</p>
