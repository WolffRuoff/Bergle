			<p><em>It’s Friday, Friday, and everybody’s looking forward to the weekend, weekend – and looking forward to another year of Muhlenberg athletics! Seven Mule teams open up this weekend, and here’s a preview:</em></p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-46" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>There’s no better way to start a season than with a game between two NCAA Tournament teams, and there are two of them on tap this weekend.</p>
<p><strong>Football at Delaware Valley (Saturday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p>Both teams earned their conference’s automatic bid to the NCAA Tournament last year, and both lost to national semifinalists (Muhlenberg to Wesley, Delaware Valley to Mt. Union). Both teams return veteran, stingy defenses, but while the Mules bring back a good chunk of their offense, the Aggies graduated nine offensive starters, including seven all-conference players. 		<header class="entry-header">
						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/01/brett-rosen-international-man-of-baseball/" rel="bookmark">Brett Rosen, International Man of Baseball</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/01/brett-rosen-international-man-of-baseball/" title="8:51 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-01T08:51:07-04:00">September 1, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>On June 30, we wrote about baseball player Brett Rosen, who had just completed his freshman year at Muhlenberg, <a title="Mule Sports Daily - June 30, 2011" href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/athletics/spring11/0630.html" target="_blank">qualifying to play for the British national team</a>. Here&#8217;s a follow-up on how he did:</em></p>
<p>After playing hundreds of games during his baseball career, Brett Rosen has grown accustomed to the normal routine of standing along the base line for the playing of the “Star-Spangled Banner” before first pitch. </p>
<p>So when that routine was thrown for a loop before one of his games this past summer, it took his mind a couple moments to remember just what jersey he was wearing.</p>
<p>Rosen spent part of the summer playing for Team Great Britain in the European Championship qualifying tournament in Tel Aviv, Israel. And according to Muhlenberg sophomore, it wasn’t until he heard something other than that familiar tune that he truly<a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4608.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-38" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4608-300x199.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="199" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4608-300x199.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4608-450x300.jpg 450w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4608.jpg 1000w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a> felt the magnitude of the moment.</p>
<p>“The overall experience of playing in an international competition was mind-blowing,” the catcher said. “It was surreal suiting up for, and representing, Great Britain. Honestly, it didn&#8217;t really hit me until they played ‘God Save the Queen’ before our first game. That was an incredible feeling.”</p>
<p>While he was born in the United States, Rosen’s mother was born in England, giving him dual citizenship. His brother, AJ, has competed for team Great Britain at the past two Winter Olympics.</p>
<p>Brett Rosen saw action in two games, both wins, to help Great Britain win the qualifier with a 4-1 record and advance to next summer’s European Championships in the Netherlands.</p>
<p>Rosen’s best game was against Georgia when he went 2-for-4 at the plate with a double and a stolen base in an 8-7 win. He also caught all nine innings in an 8-6 win over Israel <a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4614.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-39" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4614-300x199.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="199" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4614-300x199.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4614-450x300.jpg 450w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/IMG_4614.jpg 1000w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>and finished the tournament with a .286 batting average.</p>
<p>The trip was also Rosen’s first time traveling the world alone, so when he wasn’t playing he used the opportunity to take in a lot of Israel’s ancient history.</p>
<p>“Tel Aviv was beautiful,” he said. “But Jerusalem was truly amazing. It was the best part of the trip. Our entire team was led all over the city on a tour. We got to see all the holy sites and the history behind them. That was very fascinating.”</p>
<p>One of the youngest members on the squad, Rosen said it did not take long to grow accustomed to his new teammates and international competition.</p>
<p>The team was comprised mostly of Canadians, Australians and Americans with British heritage. But while the players’ backgrounds may have differed drastically, Rosen said they all shared one common passion that brought them all together: baseball.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/DSC_2800.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/DSC_2800.jpg" alt="" width="1000" height="407" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/DSC_2800.jpg 1000w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/DSC_2800-300x122.jpg 300w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/DSC_2800-500x203.jpg 500w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></a></p>
<p>“It was great bonding with everyone, especially since so many of my teammates are from all over the world,” Rosen said. “One was from Calgary and another was from Australia; it was great getting to talk about our cultures.</p>
<p>“It also helped on the field because many of my teammates have been on the team for awhile and play professionally in leagues all over the world. I was able to learn a lot from their veteran knowledge.”</p>
<p>Rosen said the style of play did not differ much from the one he plays at Muhlenberg. But one big difference he did note was the passion of the fans.</p>
<p>While American baseball fans tend to be more laid back from pitch-to-pitch, Rosen said the crowds in Tel Aviv were loud and rowdy the entire game, especially during Great Britain’s three games with the host Israeli team, including the final.</p>
<p>But that just made winning the qualifier even more special, he said, adding he hopes his team can achieve similar success at next summer’s championship tournament.</p>
<p>“The atmosphere was definitely much more hostile,” he said. “I think this definitely made me a better player and should help during this college season.</p>
<p>“I can’t wait to go to Holland next summer to try to win the European Championship.”</p>

						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/08/31/cross-country-preview/" title="7:07 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-08-31T19:07:40-04:00">August 31, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

			<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/08/yalangohs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-26" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/08/yalangohs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a></p>
<p><em>How did the cross country teams prepare for the 2011 season? Senior Kimberlee Yalango gives some insight.</em></p>
<p>This past preseason, the fall sports teams could be heard all around Muhlenberg. You could hear the whistles at football practice, the squeak of sneakers on the volleyball court, and the clanks of sticks from the field hockey team. However, the cross country team was pretty silent on campus. Instead, we were getting in the miles all around Allentown for the long season ahead of us. </p>
<p>Cross country is a unique sport since it’s pretty difficult to observe our practices (unless you’re running with us). As a first-year cross country athlete at Muhlenberg myself, I had my own misconceptions about the sport and what preseason would be like. </p>
<p>But cross country isn’t all just running, and preseason entails much more than hitting the pavement for a few miles every day. The team started the week with a run at the scenic Lehigh Valley Parkway, followed by an icing session in the cool creek that runs along the trail (the more daring ones even went for a full dip in the frigid waters). The traditional 3k time trial was the highlight of Tuesday, which showcased several impressive performances under the lights on Muhlenberg’s track. </p>
<p>The team also ventured to Jacobsburg State Park to meet up and run with East Stroudsburg’s cross country team. In between the major trips, the team did workouts in Trexler Park, went on runs around the neighborhoods of Allentown, and dipped in the pool for an aqua jogging workout. We also did doubles [running in the morning and evening to break up the miles] and focused on our strength training with new core workouts and lifting programs. </p>
<p>While putting in the work was important during the preseason, an equally essential component was the bonding of the team. Getting to know each other was especially important this season as we are welcoming a large amount of freshmen to the team.  Runs are the perfect opportunities to get to know fellow team members as there’s plenty of time for talking on five- or six-mile runs. </p>
<p>We also bonded over an ice cream social at the captains’ house and at the Rose Bowl for the annual preseason bowling competition. Teams were divided among the men and women’s roster, and it became clear after a few frames that there’s a reason many of us aren’t involved in hand-eye coordination sports! </p>
<p>Over the course of the week, both the men and women got to spend a lot of quality time together over meals and hangouts. While both teams are young, there is an incredible amount of potential for this season. </p>
<p>For the women, we have an excited and eager group of incoming freshmen at our base and at the top have two returning seniors with a plethora of experience and knowledge.  We’re led by our two junior captains, Sam Mayden and Erica Bash, who both are talented and well-respected athletes who have prematurely stood out as leaders on the team. Our main focus this season is for all our athletes to stay healthy and to keep a positive attitude throughout the long and strenuous season. While it’s still very early, I think it’s fair to say that this cross country team has a lot to look forward to and has several surprises in store.</p>

			<p>And now for something completely different &#8230;</p>
<p>Well, not <strong>completely</strong> different. This daily blog will be a companion site to the Muhlenberg College athletics website (<a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/athletics">www.muhlenberg.edu/main/athletics</a>). Both sites will cover Mule sports &#8211; the teams and their athletes and coaches. It&#8217;s just that the blog will come from a different angle.</p>
<p>Think of the Muhlenberg College athletics website as the news section of a newspaper, and The MuhlBox as the life section. Here, we&#8217;ll go beyond the results, statistics and releases to bring our teams to life.</p>
<p>What does that mean, exactly? We&#8217;re not 100% sure yet &#8211; as this is a new venture for the 2011-12 year, it will no doubt evolve over time. But there will be more feature-type stories and first-person accounts: Scheduled for the coming week, we&#8217;ll read outlooks for field hockey and women&#8217;s cross country from team members, as well as an account of the volleyball team&#8217;s trip to Ohio for a tournament. We&#8217;ll also have trivia questions at least once a week and looks ahead to important games.</p>
<p>The MuhlBox invites input from anyone with an interest in Muhlenberg College athletics. If you have something to contribute, please post a comment or contact us at <a href="mailto:falk@muhlenberg.edu">falk@muhlenberg.edu</a>.</p>
<p>The first delivery to The MuhlBox is scheduled for tomorrow. Thanks for reading!</p>
