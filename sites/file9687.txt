						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/09/11/remembering-9-11/" title="3:14 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-09-11T15:14:27-04:00">September 11, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

			<p><em>We asked several Mule athletes who hail from New York City to give their remembrances of 9-11.</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/vaughnhs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-111" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/vaughnhs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Isaiah Vaughn, Football (Bronx)</strong></p>
<p>Back in &#8217;01 I remember being in school on 9/11. I was in middle school, not sure what grade but the day started off as a typical day in school. Then all of a sudden I remember that there were no more classes, and all the classes were in their homeroom gathered around their teachers as the teachers tried to explain what was going on to us. I had heard that there had been a terrorist attack but I think I was too young to fully understand what it meant.</p>
<p>Looking out the window in my homeroom class, we were able to see the smoke from the Twin Towers being knocked down as everyone waited to be picked up early from school. My mother worked at my middle school, so it wasn&#8217;t long before we were on our way home. What I remember most was the overall quietness of the school. On a typical day the hallways are quite loud so this was confusing to me. There was a saddened mood going on around the school and again I think I was too young to understand why.</p>
<p>When I got home I remember seeing the video and images of the Towers being knocked down again and again since it was on every news channel. You could hear the people screaming in panic from the tragic incident. I feel that the impact of this event created an increased awareness of terrorism, not only in NYC but in America. Our safety as Americans had been challenged. All across the country, security and other efforts were stepped up to stop this from possibly happening again. But deaths and events that went down on 9/11 will never be forgotten. </p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/hargrovehs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-112" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/hargrovehs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Kevin Hargrove, Basketball (Queens)</strong></p>
<p>I was 10 when 9/11 occurred. My school was closed due to an infestation of fleas, so I was home sleeping when the first tower was hit. My brother woke me up and told me but I didn&#8217;t believe him, so I went back to sleep, and my brother again woke me again to tell me about the second tower. I finally got up to meet my mom and younger sister eating breakfast.</p>
<p>We didn&#8217;t have any family member that worked anywhere near there or was a fireman or policeman, so we weren&#8217;t directly affected. The only person I knew who was affected was my friend&#8217;s uncle who died. It was a terrible day for all and we&#8217;ll never forget it.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/davishs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-114" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/davishs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Darnell Davis, Football (East Harlem)</strong></p>
<div>What I remember is that I was in the fourth grade when it happened. All the parents of my classmates came to pick them up during the middle of school, and I had no idea what happened until I got home that day. It was all over the news for about a week. After 9/11 happened, though, you started seeing a lot of American flags on trains and on trucks and all other public service vehicles. You felt a sense of pride and togtherness while you walked the streets.</div>
<div> </div>
<div><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/newcombhs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-115" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/newcombhs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Kieran Newcomb, Cross Country (Brooklyn)</strong></div>
<div> </div>
<div>I actually did not live in New York City at the time. I was living in Chicago, but I do have friends who were in school right near the towers when they fell, and they had to be evacuated or had to stay in their school buildings and wait for the smoke to clear. I also have friends who had parents that died in the attacks. All of my friends remember the day vividly. They all say that it was a day where the city came together. That being said though, they all say that it was a day of horror and confusion.</div>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/malandruccolohs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-113" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/malandruccolohs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Frank Malandruccolo, Football (Brooklyn)</strong></p>
<p>I was in fifth grade when it happened. I don&#8217;t remember much but I do remember a giant state of confusion. I think it gave New Yorkers and Americans a greater sense of American pride. I remember seeing almost every house having an American flag.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/08/31/cross-country-preview/" title="7:07 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-08-31T19:07:40-04:00">August 31, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/08/yalangohs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-26" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/08/yalangohs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a></p>
<p><em>How did the cross country teams prepare for the 2011 season? Senior Kimberlee Yalango gives some insight.</em></p>
<p>This past preseason, the fall sports teams could be heard all around Muhlenberg. You could hear the whistles at football practice, the squeak of sneakers on the volleyball court, and the clanks of sticks from the field hockey team. However, the cross country team was pretty silent on campus. Instead, we were getting in the miles all around Allentown for the long season ahead of us. </p>
<p>Cross country is a unique sport since it’s pretty difficult to observe our practices (unless you’re running with us). As a first-year cross country athlete at Muhlenberg myself, I had my own misconceptions about the sport and what preseason would be like. </p>
<p>But cross country isn’t all just running, and preseason entails much more than hitting the pavement for a few miles every day. The team started the week with a run at the scenic Lehigh Valley Parkway, followed by an icing session in the cool creek that runs along the trail (the more daring ones even went for a full dip in the frigid waters). The traditional 3k time trial was the highlight of Tuesday, which showcased several impressive performances under the lights on Muhlenberg’s track. </p>
<p>The team also ventured to Jacobsburg State Park to meet up and run with East Stroudsburg’s cross country team. In between the major trips, the team did workouts in Trexler Park, went on runs around the neighborhoods of Allentown, and dipped in the pool for an aqua jogging workout. We also did doubles [running in the morning and evening to break up the miles] and focused on our strength training with new core workouts and lifting programs. </p>
<p>While putting in the work was important during the preseason, an equally essential component was the bonding of the team. Getting to know each other was especially important this season as we are welcoming a large amount of freshmen to the team.  Runs are the perfect opportunities to get to know fellow team members as there’s plenty of time for talking on five- or six-mile runs. </p>
<p>We also bonded over an ice cream social at the captains’ house and at the Rose Bowl for the annual preseason bowling competition. Teams were divided among the men and women’s roster, and it became clear after a few frames that there’s a reason many of us aren’t involved in hand-eye coordination sports! </p>
<p>Over the course of the week, both the men and women got to spend a lot of quality time together over meals and hangouts. While both teams are young, there is an incredible amount of potential for this season. </p>
<p>For the women, we have an excited and eager group of incoming freshmen at our base and at the top have two returning seniors with a plethora of experience and knowledge.  We’re led by our two junior captains, Sam Mayden and Erica Bash, who both are talented and well-respected athletes who have prematurely stood out as leaders on the team. Our main focus this season is for all our athletes to stay healthy and to keep a positive attitude throughout the long and strenuous season. While it’s still very early, I think it’s fair to say that this cross country team has a lot to look forward to and has several surprises in store.</p>
