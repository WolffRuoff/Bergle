		<p><em>With the spring sports season just about wrapped up, we will be taking a closer look at Muhlenberg‚Äôs senior student-athletes.</em></p>
<p><em>In the coming days, the MuhlBox will feature some of the highlights from the traditional senior question and answer profiles that run with the programs for Senior Day.</em></p>
<p><em>We start with women&#8217;s tennis, which beat Ursinus, 8-1, in its Senior Day match. Seniors¬†Emily Robbins and Lauren Wink were honored before the match.</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/robbinshs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1112" title=" Emily Robbins" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/robbinshs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Emily Robbins<br />

<p><strong>What‚Äôs the most memorable moment from your time with the tennis team? </strong>One of my favorite memories is a bus ride last year with the four seniors. They knew how to keep us all laughing, but get us ready for the match at the same time. I also enjoy our team dinners (especially at Wegmans) before important matches. üôÇ<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>Who‚Äôs your favorite pro player? </strong>I love watching Roger Federer play because of how hard he works and his strategy in matches. I have seen him live a few times, and he is incredible.<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>How have you been able to balance your time to make the Dean‚Äôs List the past two semesters? </strong>I really focus on my schoolwork and make that a priority. I enjoy spending time with my friends and family, which is also motivation for me to get my work done.<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>What made you choose psychology as your major?</strong> I took Intro to Psychology with Dr. Rudski and was very intrigued by the information we were learning. I ended up finishing my major in three semesters because I enjoyed my psychology classes that much.<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>What are your future plans?</strong> I intend to use the next year to do research and apply to graduate school in 2013 in neuropsychology to do concussion research.<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p><strong>What kind of person is Lauren Wink?</strong> She works hard, but also knows how to have fun. She is a great friend and truly enjoys spending time with the team. Even though she‚Äôs student teaching this semester, she joins us at practice whenever she can, which shows her dedication.<strong></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/winkhs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1113" title="Lauren Wink" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2012/05/winkhs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Lauren Wink</strong></p>
<p><strong>What made you decide to join the team as a junior? </strong>My parents always told me to live my dreams. With their motivation and encouragement, I was able to work hard and join the team as a junior. I always played tennis with my family, but never competitively. I was an avid soccer player growing up, and unfortunately soccer and tennis were the same season in high school. The summer after I had back surgery, I shied away from competing in physical contact sports and increased my time spent playing tennis. It was at that time that I decided to take lessons and try out for the team at Muhlenberg. I thought it would be a good opportunity to become part of a team, which was a huge part of sports that I missed from playing varsity soccer and lacrosse in high school.<strong> </strong></p>
<p><strong>What‚Äôs your most memorable off-the-court moment or accomplishment from your time at Muhlenberg? </strong>I participated in the career center‚Äôs Senior Year Experience. As part of the three-day event, we were given a short cooking demonstration (because we all have to be prepared to cook in the real world!). Then at night we had a ‚ÄúTop Chef‚Äù competition. I was captain of my team; we were assigned the theme ‚Äúmother-in-law is coming for dinner.‚Äù Two-and-a-half hours, a salad, a plate of spaghetti and meatballs, garlic bread and an elegant brownie dessert (with homemade whipped cream from Jordyn) later, we were selected as the ‚ÄúTop Chef‚Äù winners (shout out to Team Five!!!). It was a very fun event; they actually had chefs from local restaurants come in to judge all of the food! I am excited to see my name on the ‚ÄúTop Chef Winners‚Äù plaque hanging in the Wood Dining Commons!</p>
<p><strong>What made you decide to major in business administration while pursuing your elementary education certification? </strong>People always say, ‚ÄúSo you want to teach business to kindergarteners?‚Äù I always knew I wanted to receive my certification in education, but after enjoying my economics class in high school, I decided to take one at Muhlenberg. I decided that a major in business would give me many options. I have enjoyed taking the diverse classes from my marketing concentration to my certification in elementary education. I‚Äôm still trying to brainstorm ways I can combine the two ‚Äì maybe by being an administrator in a school in the future.<strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>What are your future plans? </strong>I plan on attending New York University in the fall to receive my master‚Äôs degree in literacy. After interning in New York last summer, I am very excited to have the opportunity to be there next year.<strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>What kind of person is Emily Robbins?</strong> Emily actually moved into my hallway in the middle of sophomore year, but it was not until the summer before junior year that we actually spoke! When I was deciding to try out for the tennis team, I sent Emily an e-mail asking her what it was like being on the team. She gave me the rundown, and told me she loved the team and loved being able to play tennis every day. It is because of her that I decided to try out! She has been a very supportive teammate!<strong></strong></p>
