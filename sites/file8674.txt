						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/18/going-dancing/" title="5:11 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-10-18T17:11:11-04:00">October 18, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

			<p>In the college sports vernacular, the &#8220;Big Dance&#8221; has come to mean the NCAA Tournament. And while the Muhlenberg women&#8217;s cross country team has as good a chance as it ever has to earn an NCAA bid &#8211; the Mules are ranked fifth in the Mideast Region &#8211; one of the team members has another &#8220;Big Dance&#8221; on her mind.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/greenberghs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-334" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/greenberghs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Senior Ashley Greenberg is one of the co-chairs of Dance for a Cure, a charity event scheduled for Friday, November 11, from 10 p.m. to midnight in the Seegers Union Event Space.</p>
<p>This is the third straight year Greenberg has been one of the committee chairs, and she is hoping to make her last year the best. This year the committee partnered with Colleges Against Cancer, an on-campus organization that helps run Relay for Life, to assist with promoting the event.</p>
<p>&#8220;We&#8217;re trying to make it a bigger deal,&#8221; said Greenberg, who estimates that the event raised a few thousand dollars the last two years. &#8220;In the past it was mostly a fraternity-sorority thing. This year we want to get more athletes and teams involved. The softball team has been helping with the planning.&#8221;</p>
<p>Money will be raised through a raffle (the big prize is a Kindle), admission tickets and donations. The proceeds will go to the American Cancer Society.</p>
<p>&#8220;I haven&#8217;t been directly affected by cancer, but some of my friends have so I&#8217;ve seen the <a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/GREENBERG.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-335" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/GREENBERG.jpg" alt="" width="309" height="325" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/GREENBERG.jpg 309w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/GREENBERG-285x300.jpg 285w" sizes="(max-width: 309px) 100vw, 309px" /></a>impact on others,&#8221; said Greenberg. &#8220;I just really like to help people. It&#8217;s fun to see people show up for a good cause.&#8221;</p>
<p>To do so with a dance is a natural for Greenberg, a dance minor who has been dancing she was in second grade. She went to a performing arts middle school, where dance was a part of her day, and danced competitively in the Maccabi Games for two summers.</p>
<p>Dancing has taken its toll on Greenberg and made her unable to run for most of this season. But she still has enjoyed the incredible success of the Mules, who finished 25th at the regional race just two years ago.</p>
<p>&#8220;I&#8217;ve really been proud of our team and my teammates,&#8221; she said. &#8220;I can&#8217;t believe how driven each teammate has been, and how much hard work and effort have gone into the team.&#8221;</p>
<p>Just like the effort she is putting into Dance for a Cure. &#8220;We want to make an impact on Muhlenberg&#8217;s campus with this event,&#8221; she said. &#8220;We really want to make people want to do something good for others.&#8221;</p>

						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/16/hometown-heroes/" title="5:19 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-10-16T17:19:18-04:00">October 16, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>If things had been just a little bit different, and the same people scored the goals, last night&#8217;s 2-1 men&#8217;s soccer win by Muhlenberg against Franklin &amp; Marshall could have gone the other way.</p>
<p><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/wayblog.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-320" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/wayblog-258x300.jpg" alt="" width="258" height="300" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/wayblog-258x300.jpg 258w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/wayblog.jpg 394w" sizes="(max-width: 258px) 100vw, 258px" /></a>Both of the Mule goal-scorers grew up not far from F&amp;M. Senior Josh Luginbuhl, who scored the first goal, hails from Denver, Pa., about 20 minutes from the F&amp;M campus. Junior Danny Way, who netted what proved to be the winning goal, is from right in Lancaster.</p>
<p>Way <em>(right)</em> never considered attending F&amp;M because he felt it was too close, but for Luginbuhl <em>(below)</em>, his final choice was between Muhlenberg and F&amp;M.</p>
<p>The two schools have plenty in common when it comes to men&#8217;s soccer. Both are top-notch programs from one of the most competitive Division III leagues. Both regularly appear in the regional rankings and are no strangers to the national top 25. Both are trying to break the Johns Hopkins-Swarthmore hold on the title that has existed for the last five years.</p>
<p>F&amp;M lost to Hopkins in the CC final in 2006 and was the top seed for the CC playoffs in 2007. Muhlenberg was the top seed in 2009 and lost to Swarthmore in the championship game last year.</p>
<p>Johns Hopkins and Swarthmore entered yesterday&#8217;s action in the top two spots in the CC, but both suffered une<a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/luginbuhlblog.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-321" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/luginbuhlblog-232x300.jpg" alt="" width="232" height="300" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/luginbuhlblog-232x300.jpg 232w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/luginbuhlblog.jpg 350w" sizes="(max-width: 232px) 100vw, 232px" /></a>xpected losses, tightening up the race. With three games remaining, one point separates the top four teams. Johns Hopkins is in first with a 4-1-1 record (13 points), while Swarthmore, Muhlenberg and Dickinson are all 4-2 (12 points).</p>
<p>This is the fifth time in the last seven years that the top seed for the CC playoffs will have at least two blemishes on its record. Anything can happen, and the difference between triumph and heartbreak can be very slim &#8211; it could even come down to what college someone chose to attend.</p>
<p>&#8220;For the last four years, and especially tonight, I&#8217;m very happy I chose Muhlenberg,&#8221; said Luginbuhl after the game.</p>

						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/12/a-bunch-of-zeroes/" rel="bookmark">A Bunch of Zeroes</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/12/a-bunch-of-zeroes/" title="12:57 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-10-12T12:57:16-04:00">October 12, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>The Muhlenberg men&#8217;s soccer team has more clean sheets* than the local laundromat.</p>
<p>Heading into tonight&#8217;s game with Drew, the Mules (7-2-1) have seven shutouts in their first 10 games &#8211; the first time they&#8217;ve done that since the 1990 team blanked eight of its first 10 opponents.</p>
<p>In the latest NCAA Division III statistical rankings, Muhlenberg was sixth in shutout percentage, ninth in save percentage (.906) and 14th in team goals-against average (0.48).</p>
<p>The Mules, who are ranked 20th by <a title="D3soccer.com" href="http://www.d3soccer.com/top25/men/2011/current" target="_blank">D3soccer.com </a>and 21st by the <a title="NSCAA" href="http://www.nscaa.com/seniorRes.php?it=1679" target="_blank">NSCAA</a>, have shut down some offenses that are used to scoring, too. DeSales ranks 25th in the nation in <a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/schauer_blog.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-308" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/schauer_blog.jpg" alt="" width="200" height="406" /></a>scoring offense and scored a goal against top-ranked Messiah, but was blanked by Muhlenberg. The latest two shutout victims, New Jersey and Washington, had both been shut out only once this season prior to playing the Mules.</p>
<p>&#8220;We don&#8217;t get any ink in the paper, so that is really what we have to go by,&#8221; said junior center back Kyle Plifka of seeing a zero on the scoreboard at the end of the game. &#8220;We take a lot of pride in that.&#8221;</p>
<p>Good defense has long been a hallmark of Muhlenberg men&#8217;s soccer; the 1988 team set a national record (since broken) with 13 consecutive shutouts. Last year&#8217;s &#8220;Sweet 16&#8221; squad allowed only 14 goals in 22 games, with 12 shutouts.</p>
<p>But that team lost an All-America goalie and two all-region backs. The Mules&#8217; impressive run of shutouts has come with a cast that is more than half new. Plifka and sophomore left back Ryan Barlotta are the lone returning starters from 2010. Center back Jonathan Schauer <em>(pictured)</em> started eight games last year as a freshman, some at back and some at midfield. Right back Bryan Attanasio is a freshman, and the two goalies, junior Ari Nutovits and sophomore Jas Chojnowski, played a total of five minutes in goal last year.</p>
<p>Senior Conor Choi and sophomore Bucky Aronoff have also filled in on the back line.</p>
<p>&#8220;They&#8217;re all so talented, it makes it easy,&#8221; said Plifka. &#8220;Most of those guys were battling for playing time last year, so we weren&#8217;t really worried about who we lost.&#8221;</p>
<p>So what&#8217;s the secret to the success of the Muhlenberg defense?</p>
<p>&#8220;Hard work on the back line, that&#8217;s about it,&#8221; said Plifka.</p>
<p><em>* Ever wonder about the origin of the phrase &#8220;clean sheet&#8221;? Here&#8217;s what Wikipedia has to say: The term first appeared in the 1930s. Sports reporters of the era used separate pieces of paper to record the different statistical details of a game. If one team did not allow a goal, then that team&#8217;s &#8220;details of goals conceded&#8221; page would appear blank, leaving a clean sheet.</em></p>

						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/10/athletes-of-the-week-6/" rel="bookmark">Athletes of the Week</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/10/athletes-of-the-week-6/" title="3:03 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-10-10T15:03:03-04:00">October 10, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><em>for contests between October 3 and October 9</em></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/hynsonhs.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-297" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/hynsonhs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Female Athlete of the Week &#8211; Holly Hynson, Volleyball</strong></p>
<div>Hynson hit .466 and averaged 3.63 kills per set to lead the Mules to road wins against Bryn Mawr and Washington. She had 18 kills in only 29 attempts against the Shorewomen, recording kills on seven consecutive hits and scoring four straight points in the pivotal second set. Her 18 kills were the most by the Muhlenberg freshman since 2002, and the two wins gave the Mules four in the CC (twice as many as they had all last year).</div>
<div> </div>
<div><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/chojnowskihs.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-298" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/10/chojnowskihs.jpg" alt="" width="100" height="150" /></a>Male Athlete of the Week &#8211; Jas Chojnowski, Soccer</strong></div>
<div> </div>
<div>Chojnowski made a splashy debut in goal, playing 155 scoreless minutes for the Mules, making 13 saves, in his first career varsity action. He came in at halftime after Muhlenberg’s starting goalie was injured and made seven saves in a scoreless tie against New Jersey. He then became the first Mule goalie since 2001 to record a shutout in his first career start, stopping six shots in a 1-0 win at Washington.</div>

		<header class="entry-header">
						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/07/weekend-preview-3/" rel="bookmark">Weekend Preview</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2011/10/07/weekend-preview-3/" title="9:28 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-10-07T09:28:03-04:00">October 7, 2011</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/sportsinfo/" title="View all posts by sportsinfo" rel="author">sportsinfo</a></span></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>Due to Yom Kippur and football having a bye, the schedule is a little different this weekend, with only men&#8217;s cross country competing on Saturday.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-46" src="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg" alt="" width="350" height="237" srcset="http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee.jpg 350w, http://sportsinfo.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/09/mulemarquee-300x203.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 350px) 100vw, 350px" /></a>Field Hockey at Washington (Friday, 3:00)</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg puts its three-game winning streak up against another team on a streak. Washington, a Centennial Conference semifinalist last year, has won five in a row and is currently tied for third place in the CC with a 3-1 record. Muhlenberg is 2-3 as it heads into a challenging second half of its league schedule.</p>
<p>Homefield advantage hasn&#8217;t been an advantage in this series of late &#8211; the road team has won the last five meetings, and the Mules are 4-1 in their last five trips to Chestertown.</p>
<p>The teams have a common result against a common non-conference opponent, both having beaten Drew in overtime.</p>
<p>The rejuvenated Muhlenberg offense has scored at least three goals in three straight games for the first time since 2006; the last time the Mules did that in four straight was 2000.</p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://justin.tv/wcathletics">http://justin.tv/wcathletics</a></p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.washingtoncollegesports.com/sports/fh/live_stats">http://www.washingtoncollegesports.com/sports/fh/live_stats</a></p>
<p><strong>Volleyball at Washington (Friday, 5:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules (5-12, 3-2) look to extend their CC winning streak to three and match their overall win total from all of last season. Washington (9-8, 0-4) has lost six of eight after a 7-2 start. Muhlenberg leads the all-time series 16-2, but one of the Shorewomen&#8217;s wins came last year.</p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://justin.tv/wcathletics2">http://justin.tv/wcathletics2</a></p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.washingtoncollegesports.com/sports/wvball/live_stats">http://www.washingtoncollegesports.com/sports/wvball/live_stats</a></p>
<p><strong>Women&#8217;s Soccer at Washington (Friday, 5:00)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules (4-5-2, 1-2-1) are perfect in 13 all-time meetings against Washington (2-7-1, 0-3-1). Muhlenberg hasn&#8217;t allowed a goal to the Shorewomen since 2003.</p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://justin.tv/wcathletics">http://justin.tv/wcathletics</a></p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.washingtoncollegesports.com/sports/wsoc/live_stats">http://www.washingtoncollegesports.com/sports/wsoc/live_stats</a></p>
<p><strong>Men&#8217;s Soccer at Washington (Sunday, 1:00)</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-family: mceinline">Like the women, the Muhlenberg men have enjoyed great success against Washington, with a 21-1-2 series lead. The Shoremen&#8217;s lone win came in Allentown in 2008. The Mules come in at 6-2-1 overall, 2-2 in the CC, while Washington is 3-5-2, 1-2-1.</span></p>
<p>Live video: <a href="http://justin.tv/wcathletics">http://justin.tv/wcathletics</a></p>
<p>Live stats: <a href="http://www.washingtoncollegesports.com/sports/wsoc/live_stats">http://www.washingtoncollegesports.com/sports/msoc/live_stats</a></p>
<p><strong>Men&#8217;s Cross Country at DeSales Invitational (Saturday, 10:30)</strong></p>
<p>The Mules will run the same team that won the Falcon Classic three weeks ago.</p>
