			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/01/polar-bear-plunge/" rel="bookmark">Polar Bear Plunge</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/01/polar-bear-plunge/" title="2:29 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 17, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>This Saturday the Muhlenberg Baseball team will be going to Easton to participate in the Polar Bear Plunge. To raise money for the special olympics many members of the team will be diving into the water, with a lot of others attending the event as well. This is a great way for us to do our part and put in some good community service hours during a very fun day.</p>
<p>Last year we did this and it was then that I realized the impact that we had on some of these families. Some of the special olympic athletes came up to us and were so excited to meet college athletes. This year we will have a lot more people in attendance and I hope we can have the same type of impact that we did last year.</p>
<p>The reason I bring this up is to stress the importance of community service, especially for student athletes. We have to all realize how privileged we are and the impact that we can have on other people. It doesn&#8217;t matter what you do, but get involved with something. It really does make a bigger impact then you realize.</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/01/muhlenbreak-2/" rel="bookmark">Muhlenbreak</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/01/muhlenbreak-2/" title="12:08 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">January 14, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hey readers!</p>
<p>So, winter break just ended. I gotta say, as my first break as a college student was excellent! Having a month off for vacation rather than the week or two we were allotted in high school made a world of difference.</p>
<p>Most of my break was spent at home, reconnecting with my loved ones. I spent a lot of time with my best friends from high school, comparing college experiences and telling funny stories. I also spent lots of time with my wonderful family and boyfriend. It was really nice to be home with the people I love.</p>
<p>On New Year&#8217;s Eve, my family and I travelled to Hawai&#8217;i! We spent ten days on the island of O&#8217;ahu. We missed the &#8220;polar vortex&#8221;, but somehow we still managed to have fun! I snorkeled, hiked, swam with dolphins, and even attended a luau! It was an absolutely wonderful time. Then, I returned home last Friday. I quickly packed and said my goodbyes, and yesterday I came back to Muhlenberg.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/01/DSC_0500.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-2671 aligncenter" alt="DSC_0500" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/01/DSC_0500-300x199.jpg" width="300" height="199" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/01/DSC_0500-300x199.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2014/01/DSC_0500-1024x680.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>(Me, in Hawai&#8217;i, on top of Diamond Head mountain!)</p>
<p>I could&#8217;ve stayed on break for another month, but I&#8217;m excited to see what this semester will bring at &#8216;Berg!</p>
<p>Emily</p>

			<div id="post-2659" class="post-2659 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/12/california-here-we-come-right-back-where-we-started-from/" rel="bookmark">California Here We Come, Right Back Where We Started From</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/12/california-here-we-come-right-back-where-we-started-from/" title="11:24 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">December 23, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/ginellewolfe/" title="View all posts by Ginelle">Ginelle</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>After another great semester at Muhlenberg, I am home again! As much as I miss school, it has definitely been nice to be in 80 degree weather.</p>
<div id="attachment_2663" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-12-16-at-5.12.27-PM.png"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2663" class="size-medium wp-image-2663" alt="I took the top picture from right outside my dorm room and the bottom picture is one that I took today at the beach!" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-12-16-at-5.12.27-PM-300x300.png" width="300" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-12-16-at-5.12.27-PM-300x300.png 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-12-16-at-5.12.27-PM-150x150.png 150w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-12-16-at-5.12.27-PM.png 652w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2663" class="wp-caption-text">I took the top picture from right outside my dorm room and the bottom picture is one that I took today at the beach!</p></div>
<p>Before I came home to my not so White Christmas, my suite decided to get in the holiday spirit.</p>
<div id="attachment_2661" style="width: 253px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-12-16-at-5.13.03-PM.png"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2661" class="size-medium wp-image-2661" alt="I decided to make brownie bites for all of us! We went a little overboard with the decorations but they tasted delicious!</p></div>

(Notice our little fake Christmas tree)</p></div>
<p>We did some de-stressing on our own but Muhlenberg also brought in puppies for us to play with, had free massages, and had lots of other activities to relax us during finals week.</p>
<p>One of my favorite ways to take break from studying is to see performances, Especially toward the end of the semester, all of the acapella groups and dance groups have their final showings. One performance that I loved watching was one called &#8220;Dancemax&#8221;. A group of students worked together to write a kids play that incorporated dancing. The show was about two girls who get sucked into a board game while they play and have to complete tasks in order to win the game and be transported back to real life.</p>
<div id="attachment_2662" style="width: 290px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-12-16-at-5.14.42-PM.png"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2662" class="size-medium wp-image-2662" alt="My friends Zoe and Noah were in the show so it was even more fun to watch! The cast aimed to educate kids about grammer and also about different dance styles!</p>

			<div id="post-2656" class="post-2656 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/12/finals/" rel="bookmark">Finals</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/12/finals/" title="11:23 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">December 23, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>After going through an entire semester of work, you finally get to Thanksgiving and realize the hardest work is still ahead of you. Months of hard work all lead into tests and presentations. This is the point in the semester where you have the most work and the least amount of energy to do it. There just feels like an endless amount of work with no light at the end of the tunnel.</p>
<p>Finding a place to study can sometimes be the hardest part. The library and all of the academic buildings are always filled with people. Unless you go earlier in the day, it is going to be tough to find a good place to study, especially if you are with a group of people. It is as if the whole school shuts down as people go their separate ways for the time leading up to it.</p>
<p>After you finally make it through all your studying, the tests are the easiest part. This is when you finally get the chance to make all of your studying pay off. Once your reach this point it leads to the best part, which is handing in your test and finishing off a class. Handing in your last exam is the greatest feeling there is. To know that you have finally finished the entire semester is well worth the wait. This is when you realize that you get an entire month off with nothing to worry about. When in doubt keep thinking about that moment and when you are all done with finals week, you will have a month to relax and not worry about anything.</p>

			<div id="post-2646" class="post-2646 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-admissions-blog">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/12/muhlend-of-the-semster/" rel="bookmark">Muhlend of the Semster</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/12/muhlend-of-the-semster/" title="11:52 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">December 13, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>(Wow, that was one of my better titles)</p>
<p>I can hardly believe the semester is drawing to a close! This week has been pretty…different.</p>
<p>After coming back from break, we had our last week of classes here at Muhlenberg. We filled out teacher evaluations, turned in projects, and generally wrapped up the semester. On Thursday, classes officially ended. Then, it was time for performance day! Performance day is the day after the last day of classes, completely dedicated to…well…performances! There are theatre performances in the morning, and then a dance showcase in the afternoon where all of the dance classes perform for each other. I especially enjoyed the dance portion, it was so great to see what everyone had been working on all semester, and it fostered a great feeling of camaraderie throughout the performing arts community here at Muhlenberg.</p>
<p>Later Friday night, it was time for the Muhlenberg Theatre Association and Muhlenberg Dance Association semi formal! It was fun to dress up with my friends and dance together to celebrate the end of performance day and classes. And there&#8217;s nothing like a party to get you in the holiday spirit!</p>
<p>Speaking of the holiday spirit, Muhlenberg is all decked out for the holidays. There are christmas lights, a giant tree, wreaths, and even menorahs sprinkled around campus. The a cappella choirs and performance ensembles have been having their final performances, and it&#8217;s not rare to hear students singing carols around campus. It&#8217;s a truly magical time at Muhlenberg.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/IMG_6190.jpeg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2644" alt="IMG_6190" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/IMG_6190-225x300.jpeg" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/IMG_6190-225x300.jpeg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/IMG_6190-768x1024.jpeg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>Of course, there is the stressful element of finals looming ever nearer. But to combat it, the Muhlenberg Activities Council has been sponsoring a number of activities. They brought in therapy dogs, gave out free massages, hosted a showing of Elf…finals may be stressful, but the Muhlenberg community is taking extra steps to make sure every student stays in good mental condition, which is something I really appreciate about the school.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/IMG_8000.jpeg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2645" alt="IMG_8000" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/IMG_8000-225x300.jpeg" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/IMG_8000-225x300.jpeg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/12/IMG_8000.jpeg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>Well, I&#8217;d better get back to studying! Here&#8217;s to hoping my first round of college finals goes well!</p>
