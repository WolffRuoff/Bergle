<p>The spring semester has ended and summer session has begun. Many changes have occurred over the last few months. The one constant is the support and collaborative efforts throughout the Muhlenberg College community, whether on or off campus. </p>



<p>While we are teaching, learning and working remotely, we want to make sure we stay connected and you stay informed. </p>



<p> IT Connects! blog posts will be published monthly keeping you up to date on the latest OIT news, important notices, maintenance schedules, training and professional development opportunities and more. </p>



<p><strong>Events</strong></p>



<ul><li>Weekly LIVE Google Meet on Wednesdays from 3pm-4pm<br>Start May 27. An open forum for faculty, staff and students to ask their technology questions.  <a href="https://calendar.google.com/event?action=TEMPLATE&amp;tmeid=NzZqODdlZGRwZWMyZDJqbWc0b243NzcyYWJfMjAyMDA1MjdUMTkwMDAwWiBtdWhsZW5iZXJnLmVkdV9ubjgzODRpbDYwc2gyOG1ndXRjMDhodHNmb0Bn&amp;tmsrc=muhlenberg.edu_nn8384il60sh28mgutc08htsfo%40group.calendar.google.com&amp;scp=ALL" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">The Meet link is available in the Google Calendar event</a>. You must be logged into your Muhlenberg account to access it.  </li><li>Access the OIT calendar for training opportunities, scheduled maintenance and other OIT events (Note:  You must be logged into your Muhlenberg account to access it). <a href="https://calendar.google.com/calendar/embed?src=muhlenberg.edu_nn8384il60sh28mgutc08htsfo%40group.calendar.google.com&amp;ctz=America%2FNew_York">Google Calendar link</a></li></ul>



<p><strong>Updates</strong></p>



<ul><li><strong>Extension of&nbsp;Adobe&nbsp;Creative&nbsp;Cloud&nbsp;At-Home Access</strong><br>Adobe provided temporary at-home access to Creative Cloud for impacted students due to campus closures, so that they may continue their work remotely.    Adobe has recently announced that temporary at-home access of Creative Cloud has been extended through July 6th, 2020.  </li><li><strong>G Suite</strong><ul><li><strong>Google Meet &#8211; video meetings made easy </strong><br>Advanced features available campuswide through September 30, 2020. Features include: video conference features, such as larger meetings (up to 250 participants) and recording.</li><li><strong>Google Drive shortcuts</strong><br>Create pointers to any file or folder in Drive.  Shortcuts make it easier for you or your team to find and organize files and folders in multiple Google Drives. Learn more at  <a href="https://support.google.com/drive/answer/9700156">https://support.google.com/drive/answer/9700156</a> </li></ul></li><li><strong>Workday Student</strong><br>The College is currently in the 12th month of a 32-month Workday Student Implementation project. Workday Student will provide Muhlenberg staff, faculty, and students many benefits, including a reduction in some current paper processes, quicker access to more data, and automated workflows. <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/initiatives/oit/workday/">Visit muhlenberg.edu/workday to learn more</a>.&nbsp; </li></ul>

Topics will include: communication, collaboration, security and working remotely. </li></ul>

		<p>It&#8217;s been a couple of months since our last &#8220;issue&#8221; of the newsletter. The Office of Information Technology (OIT) has been busy on a number of projects! A lot of the work has been behind the scenes, though, making for an outwardly quiet time. Things kick back into gear as we head into the spring months. Still, this is a bit of a short post this time around &#8211; look for more in the future.</p>
<p><strong>Google Suite for Education</strong></p>
<p>We move into &#8220;phase 2&#8221; with our G Suite implementation. <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-488 alignleft" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-300x158.jpg" alt="g-suite-logo" width="300" height="158" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-300x158.jpg 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-768x404.jpg 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-1024x539.jpg 1024w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-1200x632.jpg 1200w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo.jpg 1900w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>The focus now is to move &#8220;beyond the basics&#8221; of email and calendaring onto Google Drive, Docs, Sheets and Slides. Please note that we do have a policy on <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/oit/about/policies_procedures/cloudstorage/">what should or should not be stored on Google Drive</a>, and we&#8217;re working on more guidelines for usage of some of these new features.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.samanage.com">Samanage</a> &#8211; Our New Support Ticketing System</strong></p>
<p>We have begun internal usage of our new support ticketing system &#8211; Samanage. This new tool gives us more insight into our operations and a better look at how well w<a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Copy-of-logo-positive.png"><img class="size-medium wp-image-609 alignright" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Copy-of-logo-positive-300x45.png" alt="Copy of logo-positive" width="300" height="45" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Copy-of-logo-positive-300x45.png 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Copy-of-logo-positive-768x116.png 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Copy-of-logo-positive.png 855w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>e are meeting your needs. We are excited to make the most of it over time, and will soon be launching the self-service portal. This will allow for more efficient gathering of information as a ticket is created, meaning we can be more efficient in providing results.</p>
<p><strong>Workday &#8211; Our New Administrative System</strong></p>
<p>Last but certainly not least, we are excited to announce that we&#8217;ve selected <a href="http://www.workday.com">Workday</a> as our next administrative system. Work has already started on the project, and we&#8217;re on schedule to go-live with HR and Financials on January 1, with work starting on the Student portion shortly thereafter.</p>

<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">

								<li class="meta-comment">

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Just under the wire! It&#8217;s been a really full month with projects running right up to and through the break. Here are the highlights from the past 23 days:</p>
<p><strong>G Suite and G Day</strong></p>
<p>We&#8217;re moving ahead full speed with the transition to Google. <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/12/23/coming-soon-g-suite-google-day/">Google Day, or G Day, is on January 2</a>. Come to the Event Space that whole day for information sessions, drop-in support, and a chance to win some prizes.</p>
<p>Actual go-live will be across the 27th-29th of December (yes, some of OIT will be coming in over break to make this happen!). By then we&#8217;ll have moved over 20 million emails and thousands of calendar events.</p>
<p>A quick reminder &#8211; all faculty and staff emails and calendar items are migrating over, but students will get a &#8220;pristine&#8221; Inbox when they return. Groupwise will still work as a reference tool, and students can forward email to themselves (special instructions forthcoming).</p>
<p>For all the news on G Suite at Muhlenberg, check out the <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/category/projects/google-apps/">entire category of posts</a>. And don&#8217;t forget to log into the <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu">Application Portal</a> today to get it all set up, and on December 27, 28 or 29 to start using G Suite.</p>
<p><strong>Phones</strong></p>
<p>The phone system is installed! We have a few random phones here and there and some configuration work that still needs to be polished to a shine, but the last building has been cut-over. This has been a highly successful project considering the impact of telecommunications across the campus, and a big thanks to the Networking and Infrastructure and Client Support Services teams, in particular, for all their hard work.</p>
<p><strong>Printing</strong></p>
<p>The <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/12/19/update-on-print-quota-usage/">stats are in for Fall term</a>, and they are pretty impressive. Not only did we reduce paper usage by an equivalent of nearly 400,000 sheets of B&amp;W single-sided prints, but only 1.7% of students ran out of quota. As we say in the article, we know that some tough decisions about what to print or not still happened, but we&#8217;re excited to see these numbers. The quota ($75) and supplement ($50 for those with print-heavy courses) stay the same for Spring term.</p>
<p>That&#8217;s it for this newsletter. Happy holidays to all from OIT!</p>

<article id="post-472" class="post post-472 type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-connect-it-newsletter">

	<header class="entry-header">
				<div class="entry-date">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=472" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2016-11-01T13:14:52-04:00">November 1, 2016</time><time class="updated" datetime="2016-11-01T13:16:31-04:00">November 1, 2016</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=1499">Allan Chen</a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
		
		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=472" rel="bookmark">IT Connects! Newsletter, November 2016</a></h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
<div class="entry-meta">
	
	<ul class="meta-list">
					<li class="meta-cat"><a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?cat=172349" rel="category">IT Connects! Newsletter</a></li>

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Welcome to the first issue of our monthly newsletter! Our hope is to provide updates on our projects, address any concerns that have arisen since the last article, and speak towards the challenges facing information technology at Muhlenberg and in higher education.</p>
<p>Not all posts will be the same length with the same types of content, and it&#8217;s possible that we&#8217;ll miss the deadlines now and then. But these are our goals, along with advancing transparency into our operations and communication between OIT and the college community.</p>
<p><a href="#news">IT News</a><br />
<a href="#spotlight1">Project Spotlight: Google Suite for Education</a><br />
<a href="#spotlight2">Project Spotlight: Phone System Roll-Out</a></p>
<h3 id="news">First, some IT News</h3>
<p>We&#8217;ve had a number of major announcements since the start of the fall. We&#8217;ve launched a new <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/10/a-single-application-portal-for-muhlenberg-web-services/">Application Portal</a> and released <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/09/13/qualtrics/">Qualtrics</a> <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/q_logo_banner-300x135-1.png"><img class="size-medium wp-image-490 alignleft" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/q_logo_banner-300x135-1-300x135.png" alt="q_logo_banner-300x135" width="300" height="135" /></a>as a new campus-wide survey software solution, for instance. <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/10/lynda-com-our-new-online-training-resource/">Lynda.com</a> comes to campus, as well, as a new powerful training tool for the whole community. We also went live across campus with the new Canvas Learning Management System, and have started on projects involving Zoom video conferencing <a href="http://login.muhlenberg.edu"><img class="alignright wp-image-492 size-medium" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-300x197.jpg" alt="lynda-com-logo" width="300" height="197" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-300x197.jpg 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-768x504.jpg 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-1024x671.jpg 1024w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo-1200x787.jpg 1200w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/Lynda.com-logo.jpg 1600w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>among many others. Back in June, we launched a new version of the iMuhlenberg mobile app that will bring some new capabilities to our mobile efforts.</p>
<p>A big piece of news is the <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/category/projects/printing-and-wepa/">institution of a print quota</a>. With sustainability in mind, we&#8217;ve started a new system whereby the first $75 of printing is provided &#8220;free&#8221; to all full-time students, with printing after that on a fee-per-page basis. We&#8217;ve already seen a marked dip in print usage this September, and hope that it&#8217;s a result of greater awareness of what and when to print. We are still working on classes or other training materials on digital note-taking and similar tools.</p>
<p>Finally, October was Cybersecurity Awareness Month. We have a <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/10/19/it-protects-working-together-to-create-a-secure-environment/">new blog post</a> about all the efforts we&#8217;re making for data security, including bits on encryption, dealing with Personal Identifying Information, and how to spot phishing scams.</p>
<h3 id="spotlight1">Project Spotlight: Google Suite for Education</h3>
<p>Google Suite for Education, formerly Google Apps for Education, is still on pace for a January 1 launch for all of campus. We&#8217;ve been relatively quiet on this project so far, as we&#8217;ve been in a &#8220;steady state&#8221; of testing, <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-488 alignright" src="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-300x158.jpg" alt="g-suite-logo" width="300" height="158" srcset="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-300x158.jpg 300w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-768x404.jpg 768w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-1024x539.jpg 1024w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo-1200x632.jpg 1200w, http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/g-suite-logo.jpg 1900w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>but things will start to get &#8220;noisier&#8221; quite<br />
soon. We&#8217;ll have announcements on training, documents comparing features and tools between GroupWise and Google, and various other items intended to ease the transition.</p>
<p>Note that while faculty &amp; staff accounts will be fully migrated, with all existing GroupWise emails shifting over to the Google environment, students will receive an empty, pristine inbox. GroupWise will still be available through Spring term, and students can forward any needed emails to themselves from the old system. This decision is due to limitations on Google&#8217;s end on how much data we can migrate during our window of time.</p>
<p>OIT will be fully on Google by the end of October, so that we are actively working in the environment. Training for the college will start mid-November and run through the end of the semester. Moving email data takes multiple weeks, and we&#8217;ll start doing that for faculty and staff as soon as we can (there are a number of measures to make sure we have everything accurate right to the last minute before cut-over, so that nothing will be missed). Then we&#8217;ll modify some records and settings on the network and be ready to go in Google Suite when everyone returns for the new year.</p>
<p>During the first phase of implementation, training will focus on providing a smooth transition from GroupWise to Google Suite.  We want to ensure that the necessary information is available so that users can successfully perform their daily tasks.  Topics will include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>GMail – composing emails, moving from folders to labels, connecting with a mobile device</li>
<li>Calendar – scheduling events, creating reminders, creating new calendars, shared calendars &amp; settings</li>
<li>Contacts – Groups, Directory</li>
</ul>
<p>Training will be ongoing after the initial phase of implementation. Our team will continue to offer learning opportunities focused on the collaborative capabilities offered with the Google Suite. Topics will include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Drive &#8211; uploading files, sharing files and folders, version control</li>
<li>Docs/Sheets/Slides &#8211; creating files, concurrent editing</li>
<li>Groups &#8211; Host discussions, create forums, organize meetings and events</li>
<li>Integration of Google with Canvas and other systems</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="spotlight2">Project Spotlight: Phone Roll-Out</h3>
<p>The new phone system has been deployed to a number of buildings around campus. We&#8217;re excited to be expanding to more and more locations over the next few months. Check out <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/08/31/phone-system-installation-schedule/">the schedule online</a>, and see our latest post on <a href="http://it.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/11/01/phone-system-update/">What to Expect When You&#8217;re Expecting a New Phone System.</a></p>
