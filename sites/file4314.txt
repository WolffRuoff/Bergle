			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/muhlen-bored/" rel="bookmark">Muhlen&#8230;bored?</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/muhlen-bored/" title="2:13 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 10, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Can it be so? NOPE!</p>
<p>This week was fun. On Monday, we had a snow day! One of the great things about Muhlenberg is the diversity in students. My friend Ally is from California, so this was her first snow day ever. To celebrate, we took her sledding and made snow angels, before warming up with some hot chocolate! We also encouraged her to be extra careful when walking on the ice&#8230;luckily, she hasn&#8217;t fallen yet!</p>
<p>Tuesday was back to normal, but on Wednesday we had a delayed opening. The snow has kind of been freaking me out here, since it just doesn&#8217;t melt&#8230;it just seems to keep piling up on the side of the road! There was even rumor of a power outage, but luckily Muhlenberg is well-equiped with generators.</p>
<p>This weekend, there wasn&#8217;t any shortage of activities due to the weather. There was an improv show from the sketch-comedy (think SNL) group Fun with Science, and a stuff-a-plush event held by the Muhlenberg Activities Council on Saturday night. It&#8217;s like having out own little Build a Bear on campus, except they all get adorable tshirts that say &#8220;someone at Muhlenberg loves me!&#8221;</p>
<p>Well, I certainly hope we&#8217;re done with snow for the season. Although Muhlenberg has been generally good about clearing the sidewalks, it&#8217;s still kind of slippery out there!</p>
<p>Emily</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/the-super-bowl/" rel="bookmark">The Super Bowl</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/02/the-super-bowl/" title="10:00 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">February 7, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>There are not many events that can bring an entire country together. The Olympics always draw a huge audience, and the last episode of MASH was the most watched television program ever, but neither of these can come close to competing with the Super Bowl. This is the one thing that has it all. I have never seen something that can bring so many people together from all walks of life. I have broken it down into 4 aspects that make this game so special.</p>
<p>1) The game. All over the country there are football fans supporting many different teams. Football always has and always will be the most watched sport in America. For all of the fans that are out there, this is a chance to watch the biggest game of them all. It is the two best teams (at least it&#8217;s supposed to be) in the NFL squaring off on the biggest stage. That alone is a pretty good reason to watch.</p>
<p>2) The commercials. People pay millions of dollars to have a commercial that runs for 30 seconds. Companies spend so much money just to have the chance to break out their perfect commercial. This is a chance to sell your product to the biggest audience imaginable. Even if you don&#8217;t like football, this is the chance to see what every business has been saving up for. Besides, you don&#8217;t want to be the only one on Monday morning that didn&#8217;t see some of the funniest and most ridiculous commercials that will ever be made.</p>
<p>3) The party. The Super Bowl is the one excuse to throw a huge party on a Sunday. There is always that one person that has the big Super Bowl party that everyone wants to go to. All of the food and drinks are just too much to pass up on. Putting money down on your boxes and getting your numbers will give you a reason to cheer for one team, even if you couldn&#8217;t care less about the team that wins.</p>
<p>4) The half time show. This is usually the part that will bring in some of the younger people. A huge performance at half time almost feels as if you are at a concert. You get the greatest entertainment from the best performers out there. Whether it is Bruno Mars this year or Beyoncé last year, it is always a good watch.</p>
<p>So, sit back, relax and enjoy your Super Bowl Sunday. And remember, the day after the Super Bowl is the most missed day of school and work. I&#8217;m not giving you any suggestions, just a fact you may or may not want to know.</p>
