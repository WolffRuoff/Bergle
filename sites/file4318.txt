			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/11/muhlenmadness/" rel="bookmark">Muhlenmadness</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/11/muhlenmadness/" title="2:40 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 18, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/emilysakowitz/" title="View all posts by Emily Sakowitz">Emily Sakowitz</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Hey, all! Nothing Muhlenmad has actually happened, the title name just came to me&#8230;</p>
<p>This week has been pretty eventful. On Wednesday night, I attended a talk called &#8220;Shattered, Cracked, or Firmly Intact? Women and the Executive Glass Ceiling Worldwide&#8221; by Farida Jalalzai, the Associate Professor of Political Science at the University of Missouri-St. Louis. She spoke about the struggle of women to hold top positions in the executive branch of government (such as President), aiming to answer where women come to power and what the obstacles are in places they do not. I don&#8217;t want to spoil her book (of which the talk was based off of), so I won&#8217;t give any more away. I&#8217;ll just say it was extremely informative and insightful, and I&#8217;m glad Muhlenberg sponsored an event that is relevant to today&#8217;s issues.</p>
<p>Additionally, on Thursday, my first year seminar was visited by Lewis Gordon, a philosopher who was recently awarded the Nelson Mandela Visiting Professorship at Rhodes University. Dr. Gordon&#8217;s work focuses on race and racism. He spoke to my first year seminar about colonialism and the colonizers make the indigenous people of the colonized lands feel like they aren&#8217;t people anymore. He spoke on what it means to be human; how easy it is to get caught up thinking that we are solid beings likely to collapse if any shift in value occurs, when in reality we are porous. His words in class were echoed in the speech he gave later that night, entitled &#8220;Justice Otherwise&#8221;. Both his conversation in class and later speech were eye-opening, and I&#8217;m grateful to have the opportunity to learn from being uncomfortable in safe environment such as Muhlenberg.</p>
<p>Over the weekend, I lived in and around performances. First, on Friday night, I saw the dance main stage Moving Stories, a collection of student-choreographed works. I was absolutely blown away by the talent on the part of both the choreographers and their dancers. I&#8217;m not much of a dancer myself, but I could tell the grace and skill that the dancers put forth to enact their choreographer&#8217;s story was something special.</p>
<p>Later on Friday night, I saw an improv show called Girl Just Wanna Have Fun with Science! It was a mashup of two sketch comedy groups on campus, Damsels in Excess and Fun with Science. I always enjoy the improv shows here at Muhlenberg, and seeing two wonderful groups perform with each other was a special treat.</p>
<p>Then, on Saturday, I attended both the InAcchord holiday cabaret and Noteworthy family game nights! Both groups were wonderful. InAcchord performed a set of Christmas songs in solos, duets, and small groups, which definitely got me in the holiday spirit! Noteworthy family game night was mostly small group numbers, but the twist was the singers had to play games, such as jenga and pictionary, while they sang. I nearly missed how great they sounded because I was laughing so hard! It was a great night.</p>
<p>Then today, Class Council held our November event. This event was planned by myself and my friend Lauren: Caramel (and chocolate) apple dipping! We bought supplies at Wegmans, put in an order for apples with dining services, and took to the freshman quad this morning to set up the station. The event went really well, although we ran out of caramel rather quickly. I&#8217;m glad that it was unusually warm this late in November, because people were actually willing to spend time outside to make some delicious apples. I love event planning with Class Council, it&#8217;s always fun!</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/11/stay-motivated/" rel="bookmark">Stay Motivated</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/11/stay-motivated/" title="4:42 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 12, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/mattlawsky12/" title="View all posts by Matt Lawsky">Matt Lawsky</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I don&#8217;t know if you are like me, but this is about the time in the semester where I am out of energy when it comes to doing work. It has been a long stretch since the beginning of the semester. A lot of papers have been handed in and a lot of tests have been taken. This is usually the time where you need the most rest, but have the least amount of time to stop working.</p>
<p>There are a few things that I think help to get me through the end of the semester. The first thing is don&#8217;t look too far ahead. At this point, it is very easy to start looking to Thanksgiving Break and the end of the semester. I know both are so close, but STOP, if you start thinking this way you will have no desire to do any work. This is the time where you need to look at each week and stay focused on what you need to get done. Besides, that will only help break come that much faster.</p>
<p>The next thing to do is get some kind of exercise. Whether you want to lift, run, or go for a walk, just do something. You need to do anything to help keep the blood flowing in your body. By sitting around all day there is no way that you will be able to keep yourself sane. There is only so long you can sit at a desk before you want to flip it over.</p>
<p>Lastly, and most importantly, get some sleep! Staying up until 2 watching TV is just going to make you feel sluggish the next day. Go to sleep an hour or two earlier, and you will feel much more refreshed in the morning. This will help a lot when you need to stay focused.</p>
<p>If you are trying to keep track, there are only 32 more days until winter break. You will have plenty of time to stay out late and sleep in then.</p>

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Wow, it&#8217;s getting pretty hard to do clever titles&#8230;</p>
<p>This past week was Halloween and parent&#8217;s weekend! On Wednesday, an event called trick-or-treat the dorms took place. This is when kids from the Allentown community (and their parents) trick-or-treat throughout the dorms on campus! It was a fun time, I loved seeing little kids dressed up! They&#8217;re always so excited for candy.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5729.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2587" alt="IMG_5729" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5729-225x300.jpg" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5729-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5729-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>On Halloween itself, the a capella choir the Acafellas gave a performance. It was a little under two hours long, and since it was also a fundraiser, you could pay to do things like pie the singers while they were singing! There was a costume contest, the singing was beautiful, and overall it was a really great Halloween at Muhlenberg.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5770.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2588" alt="IMG_5770" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5770-225x300.jpg" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5770-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5770-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>Then on Friday, my parents came down for parents weekend. It was wonderful seeing them (and pretty great that they took me out to eat!). On Friday night, we attended a student recital, where my friends Ally and Lauren gave great performances, along with everyone else there. We also went to the student talent show.</p>
<p>Saturday morning, we watched the College Choir and Chamber Choir give a concert in the Chapel. The theme of the concert was Great American Jewish Songbook, and featured psalms and some of my favorite tunes by Jewish composers, such as &#8220;Blue Skies&#8221; by Irving Berlin. After lunch, we saw The Mystery of Edwin Drood, put on my Muhlenberg&#8217;s theatre department! The show was phenomenal; the dancing and singing were both excellent, and my family couldn&#8217;t stop laughing. I was really impressed with the production. After dinner, we went to the magic show of Mike Super, who won the NBC show Phenomenon. I still can&#8217;t believe some of the tricks he pulled off, like turning a paper rose into a real one! It was really cool to see someone who is a big name in the magic world at my own school.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5811.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2589" alt="IMG_5811" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5811-225x300.jpg" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5811-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5811-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Sunday, we went out to breakfast before saying our goodbyes. I had a pretty wonderful weekend. It was really nice to see my parents and to show them how great Muhlenberg is, and by the end of the weekend I didn&#8217;t want to say goodbye! I miss my family already, but I&#8217;ll see them in three weeks at Thanksgiving! And until then, I know I&#8217;m going to keep having a great time at Muhlenberg.</p>
<p><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5814.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2590" alt="IMG_5814" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5814-225x300.jpg" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5814-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/11/IMG_5814-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a></p>
