			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/04/dance-in-dublin-chocolate-waffles/" rel="bookmark">Dance in Dublin, Chocolate &amp; Waffles</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/04/dance-in-dublin-chocolate-waffles/" title="12:59 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 10, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/ml24513/" title="View all posts by Megan">Megan</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>This past week we began our internships in Dublin. For the remainder of my time here, in addition to my 2 DCU classes, I will be the Marketing and Programming intern for Dublin Dance Festival. Dublin Dance Festival (DDF) is a company that brings dance companies from all over for performances that span over a 2-week period in May. The company is super small, only 6 people work in the office, but there efforts are impactful as they organize and host dancers from places like Australia and Germany. I am so excited to be a part of such a cool team!</p>
<p>After a hard working week of studying and interning, I spent the long Easter weekend in Belgium with my sister. Our grandparents spent a couple years living in Brussels, Belgium, so it was exciting to hear about their experience and then to experience the city for ourselves. We didn’t have any specific sites we wanted to see while there, so we spent most of our time just exploring the various cities that we visited. Over the course of 5 days we saw Brussels, Gent, Brugge, and even made a day visit to Luxembourg, a country that neighbors Belgium.</p>
<div id="attachment_2144" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/04/IMG_0720.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2144" class="size-medium wp-image-2144" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/04/IMG_0720-300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/04/IMG_0720-300x225.jpg 300w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/04/IMG_0720-1024x768.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2144" class="wp-caption-text">My sister, Katelyn, and I in Brussels, Belgium!</p></div>
<div id="attachment_2155" style="width: 235px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/04/IMG_0695.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2155" class="size-medium wp-image-2155" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/04/IMG_0695-225x300.jpg" alt="" width="225" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/04/IMG_0695-225x300.jpg 225w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/04/IMG_0695-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2155" class="wp-caption-text">In the remains of an underground palace in Brussels.</p></div>
<p>As Belgium is known for its waffles, fries and chocolate, naturally that’s all we ate while there. I love chocolate, but after this weekend I think I’d be okay with not seeing chocolate for a little bit. We literally tried every kind of chocolate possible from almost every chocolate shop. Two very important stops while visiting were the chocolate museum and the fry museum. We never learned the secret as to why Belgian chocolate is so incredibly amazing compared to others though. As part of the Muhlenberg Media Communication Dublin program, there is a built-in week where the whole group visits London. While there we not only had the opportunity to see the sites of the city, but we also met and toured with various communication companies.</p>

My sister&#8217;s and Mary&#8217;s birthday are within 2 weeks of each other in March, so as part of their birthday presents we all went to The Script concert in London. It was such a great concert!!</p>

It was fun to be able to meet some British students! Being able to meet people from different places and cultures has been my favorite part of abroad thus far.</p>
<p>I was in London 3 years ago, but I wasn&#8217;t there for long so it was nice to explore the city a little more. We spent an entire day going to all the &#8220;must-see&#8217;s&#8221; of London. We saw Buckingham Palace, Big Ben, Westminster Abbey, Shakespeare&#8217;s Globe Theatre, London Eye, The Tower of London (including the opening ceremonies) and Trafalgar Sqaure. Something a couple of us felt was necessary to do was to have afternoon tea at Harrod&#8217;s. It was so delicious! After ordering our waitress said, &#8220;Sorry, but I think I may need to move you to a bigger table, you ordered too much food&#8221;. So funny! We just couldn&#8217;t decide between the different scones and treats, we had to have them all.</p>

I&#8217;m not the biggest fan of small spaces or heights. This time however, I was determined to go on it. With a little help from my friends we went on it! The views were amazing! The complete feeling of terror while waiting in line was totally worth it! It was incredible.</p>
<div id="attachment_2119" style="width: 291px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/London-Eye.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-2119" class="size-medium wp-image-2119" src="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/London-Eye-281x300.jpg" alt="" width="281" height="300" srcset="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/London-Eye-281x300.jpg 281w, http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2013/03/London-Eye.jpg 900w" sizes="(max-width: 281px) 100vw, 281px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-2119" class="wp-caption-text">(From left to right) Annie, Sarah, myself, and Jordana on the London Eye with the Parliament building in the background!</p></div>

It was last spring on April 19, and I was sitting on the floor of my friend’s bathroom in Italy, because that was the only place I could get Internet after our school closed. When I got the email that the play I had proposed, Iphigenia and Other Daughters, had been accepted, I was incredibly honored, very nervous but excited to take on the challenge.</p>

I was able to use the skills from my four years at college and put them into a production. I got to experience every step of the process, which included casting, callbacks, production meetings, design meetings, rehearsals, tech and every performance of the show. Each step allowed me to learn more and more about all that it takes to make a production happen.</p>

They were behind me 100% for my greatest ideas as well as my not so successful ideas.  The cast was always willing to try new things and their support of my vision made my collaboration with them to create a shared vision fantastic and a final product that was truly beautiful.</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2013/04/so-much-sushi/" title="8:55 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">April 2, 2013</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/db240243/" title="View all posts by Dani">Dani</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>As an Resident Liaison, my staff and I get to plan programs for our residents. We decided to do something that we had never done before: a sushi and foreign film program. We got a couple of people who are sushi-making experts to make sushi for the program, and there was a lot of sushi! We had a foreign film on in the background, and it was a great success. It is always fun to do a program that has never been done before. The chefs even let some of my friends make some sushi. It is really difficult, but they did a great job. Check out some pictures below!</p>
