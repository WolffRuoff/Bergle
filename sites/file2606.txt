		<p><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2018/11/wmuh_logo.png"><img class="alignleft wp-image-1803" src="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2018/11/wmuh_logo-300x172.png" alt="" width="475" height="273" srcset="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2018/11/wmuh_logo-300x172.png 300w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2018/11/wmuh_logo-768x441.png 768w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2018/11/wmuh_logo-1024x588.png 1024w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2018/11/wmuh_logo-788x452.png 788w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2018/11/wmuh_logo.png 1051w" sizes="(max-width: 475px) 100vw, 475px" /></a></p>
<p><a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/wmuh/">WMUH-FM</a>, the radio station of Muhlenberg College, has two internship opportunities for the Spring 2019 semester.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Audio Production</h3>
<p>WMUH is seeking an audio production associate to produce, record and edit audio content for broadcast and online distribution. Current projects include working with Muhlenberg Theatre and Dance, Student Engagement and the Living Writers Series to create, engineer and air monthly programming. Knowledge of audio editing software and recoding techniques is preferred.</p>
<p>Responsibilities include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Working with the General Manager to establish and execute a calendar for interviews and programs</li>
<li>Recording and editing original programs for broadcast and distribution</li>
<li>Uploading content to Soundcloud</li>
<li>Preparing and editing syndicated content for broadcast</li>
<li>Creating legal station IDs, liners, and promotional spots</li>
</ul>
<h3>Web Designer/Developer</h3>
<p>WMUH is planning to revamp our web presence and is looking for a web designer/developer to help with wmuh.org. Ideally this site will be ready to go live to coincide with our annual membership drive which begins April 8, 2019. Working knowledge of WordPress preferred. Graphic design skills a plus.</p>
<p>Responsibilities include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Working with the General Manager and student executive board to establish site style guide and content requirements</li>
<li>Coordinating with Muhlenberg Instructional Designer to create site prototype</li>
<li>Creating and developing content for site</li>
<li>Updating site as needed</li>
</ul>
<p>For more information, or to apply, contact <a href="mailto:wmuh@muhlenberg.edu">Paul Krempasky</a>.</p>

<p>Comcast brings together the best in media and technology. We drive innovation to create the world&#8217;s best entertainment and online experiences. As a Fortune 50 leader, we set the pace in a variety of innovative and fascinating businesses and create career opportunities across a wide range of locations and disciplines. <li>Working with streaming music providers to create deep-links to their content that can be merchandised across X1, especially on artist entity and genre pages</li>

		<h4>by Allison Hollows (&#8217;12)</h4>
<p><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/09/plannedtvarts.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-215" title="plannedtvarts" src="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/09/plannedtvarts.jpg" alt="" width="320" height="38" srcset="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/09/plannedtvarts.jpg 320w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/09/plannedtvarts-300x35.jpg 300w, http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2010/09/plannedtvarts-150x17.jpg 150w" sizes="(max-width: 320px) 100vw, 320px" /></a>My internship at <a href="http://www.plannedtvarts.com/" target="_blank"><span style="color: #ff0000"><strong>Planned Television Arts</strong></span></a> (a division of Ruder Finn, Inc.) was a great learning experience and very memorable. PTA is primarily a book publicity firm, so I spent the summer doing media outreach for authors. I got to work on some great accounts, such as Bill Strickland’s Tour de Lance and a biography of Randy Romero, a Hall of Fame jockey. Generally, I spent the morning calling radio producers across the country to pitch interviews with various authors. The afternoon was spent writing new one sheets (which are condensed, single topic, press releases), following up on emails and mailing out books. During my summer I got to call magazines such as Cosmopolitan, Vogue, Harper’s Bazaar, and Glamour. I emailed writers stationed abroad and spoke with radio hosts and producers from almost every state. PTA is a small company, so interns get to do the work of an assistant publicist, so there is no fetching coffee for the boss. Overall, my internship taught me a great deal about the media outreach aspect of public relations and provided invaluable hands-on experience.</p>
