<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><strong> – </strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">students in the American Foreign Policy course and the Comparative National&nbsp; </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Security Policy course prepare full policy analyses including policy options and cost-</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">benefit analyses of these options together with a final policy recommendation and 2-</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">page Executive Summary</span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><strong>Position Statements and Negotiating Text</strong></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><strong>-</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> International Environmental Policy - students prepare position statements and develop negotiating text in support of their assigned roles as they participate in a simulation of international negotiations regarding climate change</span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">- International Political Economy students prepare position statements and develop negotiating text in support of their assigned roles as they participate in a simulation of international negotiations regarding a Bangladeshi application to the World Bank to fund a development project</span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">-International Relations Theory- students prepare position statements and develop position statements and policy recommendations for their assigned roles within federal government during an international crisis</span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><strong>International Legal Research</strong></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">- International Law and Organization course - students undertake legal research to support their argument regarding the solution to multiple hypothetical cases involving issues such as human rights, environmental accidents, and intellectual property rights</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><strong>Research Proposal</strong></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">-International Relations Theory course-students develop a research proposal for testing the validity of hypotheses regarding some area of international relations such as the outbreak of internal conflict, success in economic development, etc.</span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><strong>Independent Research-examples of student research projects</strong></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>-</strong>analysis of national claims to the South China Sea</span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">-</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">development of a methodology to identify potential terrorists</span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">-</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">development of a methodology to identify the probability that art treasures will be </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">subject to looting or destruction</span></p>
