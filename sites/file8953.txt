	<header class="entry-header">
			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://fys142f16.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2016/10/10/googling-myself-2/" title="Permalink to Googling Myself" rel="bookmark">Googling Myself</a></h2>
	</header><!-- end entry-header -->
        
	<div class="entry-content">
					<p>Although I have Googled myself before, I never paid attention to any of the details – the articles about somebody else named Michael Ellick, the links that come up under the sub categories such as videos, news and shopping, or the pictures of other people who share the same last name as I do. All of the previous times I Googled myself, I was looking to see if I would show up on Google images after typing my name into the search bar, which I did. This time I paid attention to all of the details, and was surprised, to say the least.</p>
<p>The first few things that come up after Googling my name have nothing to do with me, but somebody else named Michael Ellick. It turns out that Michael Ellick is a former Minister, and a current Reverend at the Judson Memorial Church in New York City. The next few links took me to profiles of people named Michael Ellick on Facebook, and the Twitter account of the Minister. After clicking on ‘images’, I only found one picture of myself, which was my Twitter profile picture. I also came across a picture of my uncle, pictures that have been tweeted from my twitter account, and pictures of Michael Phelps, Michael Jordan, and the Minister Michael Ellick. I was surprised to have only seen one picture of myself. Next I clicked on ‘news,’ and was surprised. On September 23 I had been stopped on the way to one of my classes at Muhlenberg by the Lehigh Valley News, who were interviewing students about how they feel about political posts on social media. I had forgotten that I had been interviewed, so watching the interview was both exciting and nerve-racking, as I said that “I would probably unfriend them or unfollow them” (referring to people who post about politics) on camera. If I had known that they were going to post the interview online, I probably would have changed my answer, and said something a little nicer. Regardless, I am very satisfied and comfortable with what comes up when my name is entered into Google. There is nothing there that I am uncomfortable with or embarrassed about, and moving forward I will make it a habit to Google myself to assure that there continues to be nothing that can appear as negative under my name.</p>

	</header><!-- end entry-header -->
        
	<div class="entry-content">
					<p>Google, a popular search engine, is a tool for finding resources on the World Wide Web. Through the search engine, we can search for useful datas and get to know the current events. You can get to know everything you want to know, and you can find a lot of things you haven&#8217;t seen before. This is the power of the Internet.  The network is big, but the world is bigger than the Internet, There are always things you may not find.</p>
<p>I am a conservative person. In the past,  I rarely had any amazing achievements in my school life; therefore, when I searched my own name, just as I thought, I simply could not find any information about me, minus the information on Facebook. At first I used the English name ( Zhijiang Liu), and then I tried to use the Chinese name; using Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, and BaiDu which is the world&#8217;s largest Chinese search engine,  but the result is a famous professor with my name, not me. I feel this is ridiculous, then I asked myself what I have done in the past few years? In the Rosie&#8217;s place as a volunteer, I played with leukemia children, I was a student council member. In my opinion, that&#8217;s not so bad, at least I remember the things that I did; although these things are not recorded on the Internet,I left footprints in the physical world.</p>
<p>I can only say that on the protection of personal privacy, I have plenty of it. This is not troubling, but comforting, because my achievements were captured in my memory and in those who witnessed them. I learned that if you want to protect your privacy, the best way is  stay away from the Internet. If you want to become a famous and popular person, you need to show a variety of information about you on multiple sites and market yourself. If that&#8217;s your thing.</p>

It reads: her “low technology proficiency haunts her academic career.” Most teachers assume that students automatically know how to use technology which may exclude some students. Although these students are not well versed in technology, they actively embrace it. Going back to Lara, the article reads: “this low level of technology knowledge did not translate into negative attitudes toward technology, however. She loves computers…” which means that these students may have an ability to accept new technology while the older generations are not as inclined to do so. This could link to the ability of younger people to pick up new things quickly while the old dogs aren’t able to learn those new tricks the puppies can. Fortunately, schools are getting better with teaching students to use technology more efficiently, albeit at a slower pace than technology is progressing but it’s progress none the less.</p>
