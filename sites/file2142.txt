		<div><b>Summer Internships to Protect our Environment</b></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<p>If you care about the environment and want to make a difference, <a href="http://jobs.environmentamerica.org/internship.html?_ga=1.206775568.2061714493.1479237073" target="_blank">apply to be a PennEnvironment intern today</a>.</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<p>What do interns with PennEnvironment do?</p></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<p>You’ll help us educate and engage citizens on the most critical environmental issues of our time. You’ll help us get our issues into the media, build coalitions, organize events and lobby decision-makers. Ultimately, you’ll help us build the kind of public support it takes to win.</p></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<p><b>Our Campaigns</b></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<p>Do you want 100% renewable energy? We’re committed to a 100% renewable energy future, and we’re advocating for policies that will bring more solar and wind power to more people and communities right now.</p></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<p>Do you want to save bees and other pollinators from extinction? A recent U.N. report found that bees and other pollinators are facing extinction – and given that bees pollinate everything from almonds to strawberries, we know that “no bees” means “no food.” So we’re gathering support for a ban on toxic ‘neonicotinoid’ pesticides.</p></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<p>Do you want to keep our public lands permanently protected and in public hands? We believe we need more wilderness, more national parks, and more public lands that are permanently protected. So we’re getting the word out through the media to not only stop reckless moves to sell off our public lands or open them to mining, drilling and development. We want to expand their protection and establish more national monuments.</p></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<p>Thinking boldly about a green future is a start, but we know it’s not enough.</p></div>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p><strong>Food-Health-Climate – Education Program Development (summer)</strong></p>
<p>This internship involves a combination of research and writing for a creative project that requires thoughtful reflection and tangible results. It is possible to do some of the work off-site once someone is familiar with the project and its goals.</p>
<p>Too many elementary and secondary students do not understand the extent to which food from the industrialized food system harms our health, poisons the environment, and accelerates global warming. We will develop tools and resources to encourage and help teachers tackle these concerns.</p>
<p>In addition, we will continue to improve and spread the word about the previous phase of this project, which dealt with interdisciplinary teaching on global warming and greenhouse gas [GHG] emissions. (Except for the free School GHG Calculator, this information is now available online at <a href="http://teach-climate.net">teach-climate.net</a>.)</p>
<p>Throughout the project our focus is on student-centered, inquiry-based learning. For the food module, we are considering an assessment of school food for the hands-on element, but that has not yet been finalized. What would you do?</p>
<p>We use our online Sustainability Commons for collaboration and sharing content, coupled with frequent email communication on work in progress and regular meetings to meet with teachers, review progress, and brainstorm ideas. Activities will include some or all of the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Identify reliable resources and help create new resources that teachers can use to integrate topics related to food, health, climate and sustainability in their classes; help prepare a guide for teachers similar to that done for climate &amp; GHG emissions [see <a href="http://teach-climate.net">teach-climate.net</a>];</li>
<li>Assist with development of tools for a hands-on project related to food at school;</li>
<li>Identify or develop ways for teachers at all ages to encourage observation, analysis, and solving problems, including ways designed for younger children;</li>
<li>Prepare an updated version of materials from phase 1 (climate &amp; GHG) by adding information to help teachers link to curriculum standards that they are required to follow; post online and create PDF</li>
</ul>
<p>This is phase 2 of our project series on Interdisciplinary Teaching for Climate &amp; Sustainability. Phase 1 dealt with global warming &amp; climate change, with a hands-on project to assess the school&#8217;s greenhouse gas emissions [GHG] — see <a href="http://teach-climate.net">teach-climate.net</a>.</p>
<p>For more information, see:</p>
<p><a href="http://internships.sustainlv.org">internships.sustainlv.org</a></p>
<p><a href="http://www.sustainlv.org/schools">www.sustainlv.org/schools</a></p>

peter@sustainlv.org</p>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p>Help Create a Just &amp; Sustainable world!</p>
<p>Rethink the System, Work for Change</p>
<p>Do you want to use your talents, insight, and academic knowledge to help create a better world? Is an Alliance internship right for you?</p>
<p>“The most important thing I did during my internship was to meet people and find out how many factors go into changing the way something is done.…  I learned how to find out about things that tend to be hidden, talk to people about things they would rather pretend do not exist, and solve problems that seem overwhelming at first glance.” (JT, Lafayette College)</p>
<p>“Working with the Alliance allowed me to collaborate with individuals who shared my passion for sustainability, as well as voice my own ideas…. a truly rewarding and enjoyable experience…” (LB, Muhlenberg College)</p>
<p>2016–2017</p>
<p>Alliance internships blend research, analysis, communication, and activism to raise both public &amp; professional awareness. If you are interested in one of the possibilities listed below, contact Peter Crownfield as soon as possible. (Before considering specific internships we suggest you read this <a href="http://www.sustainlv.org/act-locally/internships-with-the-alliance/">brief description</a>.)</p>
<p>Following is a list of internships we are featuring this year. We will not be able to do them all at the same time, of course, but all are available at this time.</p>
<p>▪ Climate &amp; Sustainability for Teachers – Help develop ideas and tools to help teachers integrate climate &amp; sustainability concepts in all subject areas and grade levels — and use them to generate critical thinking &amp; dialogue that can result in a major increase in understanding in the K-12 school population. <a href="http://www.sustainlv.org/act-locally/internships-with-the-alliance/current-internship-opportunities/climate-sustainability-for-schools/">[More info]</a></p>
<p>▪ NEW Communication–Climate &amp; Sustainability for Teachers Project – This internship involves a blend of research, keeping up with developments in the project, and communicating with teachers via email and a new blog. Some of this work can be done off-site, once the intern is familiar with the project and its goals. [see preceding internship for more information about the project] <a href="http://www.sustainlv.org/act-locally/internships-with-the-alliance/current-internship-opportunities/communication-%E2%80%93-climate-sustainability-for-teachers-project/">[More info]</a></p>
<p>▪ NEW Communication–Combined Heat &amp; Power [CHP] Project – This internship involves a blend of research, keeping up with developments in the project, and helping to prepare an effective proposal and presentation to management. <a href="http://www.sustainlv.org/act-locally/internships-with-the-alliance/current-internship-opportunities/combined-heat-power-chp/">[More info]</a></p>
<p>▪ NEW Sustainable Events! – Moving towards zero-waste, climate-neutral events. Will include working with specific events to make them more sustainable, probably including one on-campus event and one community-based event.</p>
<p>▪ Communication – Communicating sustainability concepts to the community at large is a challenge, given the flood of misinformation in the mainstream media. This can be quite challenging, because these projects focus on public policy but need to make scientific concepts, technical data, and moral &amp; ethical &amp; moral issues clear and engaging to the public. <a href="http://www.sustainlv.org/act-locally/internships-with-the-alliance/current-internship-opportunities/communication/">[More info]</a></p>
<p>Many of our internships were developed many in response to student requests, so please contact us if you are interested in an internship that’s not listed.</p>
<p>To apply: contact Peter Crownfield peter@sustainlv.org</p>
<p>Sustainability &amp; Health Initiative</p>
<p>Alliance for Sustainable Communities-Lehigh Valley</p>
<p>Bethlehem, PA 18015</p>
<p><a href="http://www.sustainlv.org/act-locally/internships-with-the-alliance/">http://www.sustainlv.org/act-locally/internships-with-the-alliance/</a></p>

		<p>Work in Washington DC for the summer as a paid intern for the Center for Responsive Politics. Deadline: March 15.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Overview:</p>
<p><span style="color: #212b4a">If you&#8217;d like to learn how to serve the perfect cup of coffee, the Center for Responsive Politics won&#8217;t be for you. But if you&#8217;re looking for a challenging public-interest internship that will turn you into a detective in our nation&#8217;s capital, send us your résumé. Here at the Center we track the money and other behind-the-scenes forces shaping the government policies that affect each of us every day. Which members of congressional finance committees have benefited the most from contributions from the companies looking for relief from new regulations? How much money are lobbyists spending to bend legislation in their favor, and which industries do they represent? How much personal money do our lawmakers have invested in companies that do tens of millions of dollars worth of business with the government itself? As a </span><strong style="color: #212b4a">paid <a style="color: #3e72b5" href="http://www.opensecrets.org/about/intern.php#comm_intern">reporting</a></strong><span style="color: #212b4a">, </span><strong style="color: #212b4a"><a style="color: #3e72b5" href="http://www.opensecrets.org/about/intern.php#res_intern">research</a></strong><span style="color: #212b4a"> or </span><strong style="color: #212b4a"><a style="color: #3e72b5" href="http://www.opensecrets.org/about/intern.php#it_intern">IT</a></strong><span style="color: #212b4a"> intern at the Center, you&#8217;ll help us answer questions like these to inform voters and keep our politicians honest.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>See this website for details on reporting and research positions: <a href="http://www.opensecrets.org/about/intern.php">http://www.opensecrets.org/about/intern.php</a></p>

			<span class="post-date"><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=1278" title="Video Production with Communities in Schools Spring 2015">October 28, 2014</a></span>
			
			<span class="date-sep"> / </span>

	<div class="post-content">
		    		            			            	                                                                                            
		<p>Communities in Schools of the Lehigh Valley works with schools, communities, and families to surround our students with a caring network of support to help them stay in school and achieve in life. Our organization is seeking two film or media student interns who can shoot and edit a series of promotional videos during the Spring 2015 semester. The concept for the series would be &#8220;My Turn-Around Story.&#8221; In each video, a student would explain how CIS helped him or her go from being frustrated with school or disengaged to being a determined and involved student.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Duties &amp; Responsibilities:</p>
<ul>
<li>Travel to CIS schools in Allentown, Bethlehem, Easton, and/or Whitehall to meet with site coordinators and students</li>
<li>Assist students with articulating their experiences</li>
<li>Film ten different students (nominated by site coordinators) telling their “turn-around stories”</li>
<li>Edit footage to create ten separate videos, each one to two minutes long</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Qualifications &amp; Requirements:</p>
<ul>
<li>Experience filming, editing, and producing high-quality videos</li>
<li>Possession of or access to video camera(s) and editing equipment</li>
<li>Experience working with children preferred</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Position structure:</p>
<ul>
<li>Internships will span the length of the Spring 2015 semester</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Assigned supervisor – Vice President of Operations</li>
<li>Schedule may be subject to intern’s availability (Our office is open from 8:00 am – 4:00 pm)</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Applicants should email a resume and cover letter to Michael McCorristin at <a href="mailto:mccorristinm@cislv.org">mccorristinm@cislv.org</a>. Please include any previous work in video production, if possible. Applications will be accepted through December 1.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

		<p><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/01/renewlv_logo.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-238" src="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/files/2011/01/renewlv_logo.jpg" alt="" width="125" height="57" /></a><span style="color: #ff0000"><strong>Renew Lehigh Valley </strong></span>&#8212; a nonprofit “smart growth” organization that promotes the revitalization of our region’s urban cores and the preservation of open space &#8212; has an opening for a Social Media &amp; Writing Intern for this spring.</p>
<p><strong>RenewLV Intern Job Description</strong></p>
<p><strong> </strong></p>
<ul>
<li>To start as soon as possible</li>
<li>Reports to Executive Director</li>
<li>Office located at 1337 East Fifth Street, on the South Side of Bethlehem, PA</li>
<li>Interested individuals should send resume to: <a href="mailto:jmarin@renewlv.org">jmarin@renewlv.org</a></li>
<li>10-28 hours per week</li>
<li>$9 per hour</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Responsibilities</strong></p>
<p><strong> </strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Utilize online tools (website, blog, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, e-mail blasts) to engage and inform partners and allies</li>
<li>Update the Envision and Renew websites as needed</li>
<li>Design and write content for bi-monthly newsletters for RenewLV and EnvisionLV</li>
<li>Write minutes from RenewLV board meetings</li>
<li>Write 4-10 blog posts per month for Crossroads, <a href="http://www.renewlv.wordpress.com/">www.renewlv.wordpress.com</a></li>
<li>Provide staff support at public meetings for Envision Lehigh Valley</li>
<li>Conduct policy-related and issue-related research that helps inform RenewLV’s campaign and community-outreach strategies</li>
<li>Attend RenewLV board meetings, Envision consortium meetings and other partner meetings</li>
<li>Submit invoices, prepare travel reports, provide administrative support</li>
<li>Other duties as determined</li>
