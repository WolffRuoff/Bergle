			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/susab/" rel="bookmark">SUSAB</a></h2>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/2014/11/susab/" title="4:25 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">November 25, 2014</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://admissions.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/author/felisa/" title="View all posts by Felisa W">Felisa W</a></span>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

					<div class="entry-content">
				<p>I do a lot on campus, from Student Government, to my work study to volunteering off campus, to working for my internship and then I do SUSAB*.</p>
<p>*Seeger&#8217;s Union Student Advisory Board</p>
<p>Every two weeks we meet for about an hour and a half and talk about&#8230;( you guessed correctly!) Seeger&#8217;s Union. We discuss what can be improved, what we would like to see, what could happen in the future, what is definitely not going to happen in the future (we will never get a Chipotle in the Red Doors, I repeat, we will never get a Chipotle in the Red Doors&#8230; I know&#8230; awful right?). However, one of our faults is that we don&#8217;t get a chance to see any other schools student unions and dining services.</p>
<p>So, SUSAB planned a field trip to visit Lafayette and Lehigh and let me tell you:</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>We. Are. So. Lucky. To. Go. To. Muhlenberg.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">After visiting Lehigh and Lafayette, I was so unimpressed. The layout of Lafayette&#8217;s dining facility seemed strange. Â You walk in and it&#8217;s like a rectangle, and there aren&#8217;t a variety of searing options like the Woods DiningÂ Commons has. For instance,Â in the Woods Dining Commons,Â we can choose if we want to sit at an 8-top, a 4-top, a 2-top or by the fireplace or even on a long table if we have a larger group of friends? At Lafayette, every table was an 8-top with only one long table in the middle where the football team sits? Their food also sits out and you have to walk around throughout the room to go to different stations. At &#8216;Berg all the food is located in one place, yes you have to walk around from station to station, but there&#8217;s a room to get your food, and then there&#8217;s the main dining commons where you sit to eat your food. One good thing about Lafayette was that the pricing was more affordable than GQ&#8217;s (you can get a huge container of grapes for $2.99 there and for that same price, we only get about a quarter the amount).</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Lehigh&#8217;s dining services (which is supplied by Sodexo, just like Muhlenberg) was a little nicer than Lafayette&#8217;s, but once again seemed a little bit crammed. They did have an Asian station though, which was nice (then again I&#8217;m biased because I LOVE Asian food). As soon as you enter the student union where the dining options are located, you have a pizza place to your left, a place like GQ to your right and where students get their coffee in the morning right in front of you. In the morning during rush hours, if I went to Lehigh, I would probably stay away from the student union in the morning since everything is so close together it would be impossible to get in and out at a reasonable rate. If you suffer from claustrophobia, I would recommend staying away from the student union in the morning.Â In Seeger&#8217;s Union at &#8216;Berg everything is spaced out from each other and there&#8217;s thankfully room to breath! One cool thing about Lehigh was that they offer this awards program. At the pizza place, if you spend a certain amount of money, you get a free pizza! How cool is that? We have a similar thing at Java Joe, but unfortunately, it&#8217;s just for coffee&#8230;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Lehigh&#8217;s student programming was quite impressive, I will admit. On our visit we also had some students who are involved with Muhlenberg&#8217;s Activities Council, so hopefully they jotted down some ideas for events that could work at &#8216;Berg.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">After about 6 hours of visiting campuses and taking tours, I was so grateful to be back on campus and I definitely appreciate what Muhlenberg has to offer even more since that visit. 				<p>This isÂ a reflection on what ended up being a tough overall weekend for Muhlenberg Athletics.</p>
<p>In the span of 24 hours, Muhlenberg soccer, football, and basketball all lost in what would be considered &#8220;elimination&#8221; games. Soccer fell to Tufts in the sweet sixteen of the NCAA tournament after an exciting season that saw the team win a conference championship and the second round of the NCAA tournament in dramatic fashion with penalty kicks. Football lost in the first round of the NCAA&#8217;s to Widener after a great one-loss season by a field goal. Then after winning the first game of the Scotty Wood tournament handily on Friday night, Saturday night did not bode well for my own team, who lost by three in the championship game.</p>

<p>My family (two brothers, sister, and parents) also made the trip out for the tournament to catch the two games, and while it was great to finally see them out here, it was also sad&#8230; as my younger brother who is a freshman in high school is now taller than me. I was home two months ago and he was still an inch shorter!</p>
<p>Despite that, I always feel so blessed to have the opportunity to get a greatÂ education here at Muhlenberg and still have parents that are able to make the eight hour driveÂ out to see my games! I know they will be out for more games, as they were last year, and I cannot wait for them to be out here again!</p>
<p>WeÂ ended up going out to breakfast at Billy&#8217;s Downtown Diner twice this weekend (my siblings somehow convinced my parents to go a second time)! Part of the pleasure of going to school here is the access you have to the city of Allentown, that holds many secret diners and restaurants and shops that are just waiting to be discovered!</p>
<p>While two seasons ended this weekend, basketball still has a long season ahead, and while we know the season goes by quick, we will be doing our best to make every game count, as a win or a lesson, as we work towards our goal of a conference championship.</p>
