				<p><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"></span></span></p>

<div style="color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12.8px;"><i>Qualitative methods in linguistics and sociolinguistics are often use when you are trying to understand participants perceptions, beliefs, and ideologies about language. &nbsp;Discourse analysis and critical discourse analysis are also frequently qualitative and rely on the researcher to interpret the data rather than a statistical procedure. &nbsp;The most common research strategies associated with qualitative methods in applied linguistics are interviews, observations (ethnographic fieldwork), and case studies.&nbsp;</i></div>

<div style="color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12.8px;">&nbsp;</div>

<div style="color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12.8px;"><i></i></div>

<div style="color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12.8px;"><i>It&rsquo;s important to understand that, in general, qualitative research is not aimed at discovering something that is&nbsp;&ldquo;true&rdquo; or generalizable, but is concerned with the&nbsp;<b>meaning</b>&nbsp;associated with the data within the particular context in which the data was collected. &nbsp;For instance, if I wanted to understand how Latinx students felt about the use of Spanish on campus, I might interview a group of purposefully selected Latinx students and ask questions related to their perceptions and interpretations of what they experience. &nbsp;I might also observe in spaces where Spanish is used and record the interactions that I witness. &nbsp;These would result in text which I would analyze for meaning (content). &nbsp;</i></div>

			<div id="s-lg-content-38398172" class="  clearfix">
				<h2 class="s-lib-box-title" style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana; font-weight: 700; line-height: normal; color: rgb(250, 245, 245); margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; font-size: 9pt; background-image: none; padding: 6px 8px; border-bottom: 0px solid rgb(28, 25, 25); border-radius: 0px; background-color: rgb(0, 56, 107);"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;">Do&#39;s and Dont&#39;s</span></span></h2>

<div id="s-lg-box-collapse-8845865" style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana; font-size: 12px;">
<div class="s-lib-box-content pad-left-none pad-right-none" style="box-sizing: border-box; padding: 10px 0px;">
<div class="clearfix" id="s-lg-content-17901774" style="box-sizing: border-box;">
<h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: inherit; line-height: normal; color: inherit; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 10px; font-size: 24px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; font-weight: 700;">Do:&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;</span></span></span></h3>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;">Make your interviewee comfortable. &nbsp;Small talk helps!</span></span></p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;">Bring recording equipment.</span></span></p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;">Pay attention!</span></span></p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;">Come to the interview prepared.</span></span></p>

<h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: inherit; line-height: normal; color: inherit; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 10px; font-size: 24px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; font-weight: 700;">Don&#39;t:</span></span></span></span></h3>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;">Push your interviewee to answer questions they do not want to answer.</span></span></p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;">Be rigid. &nbsp;Continue on conversations of interest and ask questions.</span></span></p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;">Allow your interviewee to pursue conversations that are off topic.</span></span></p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px;"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"></span></span></p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px;"><a href="https://owl.english.purdue.edu/owl/resource/559/04/" target="_blank"><span style="font-size:12px;"><span style="font-family:arial,helvetica,sans-serif;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; line-height: 21px;">Driscoll, D. L. &amp; Brizee, A. &nbsp;&quot;Interviewing.&quot; &nbsp;Purdue Online Writing Lab. &nbsp;Purdue University. &nbsp;Web. &nbsp;6 December 2015.&nbsp;</span></span></span></a></p>

								<div class="s-lib-box-content">

                        <ul id="s-lg-link-list-41221325" class="s-lg-link-list s-lg-link-list-2"><li class="">
                        
			<div id="s-lg-content-38398679" class=""><span><a href="http://libguides.usc.edu/writingguide/fieldnotes" target="_blank"  onclick="return springSpace.springTrack.trackLink({link: this,_st_type_id: '2',_st_content_id: '38398679'});">Tips for Writing Field Notes</a> 
				</span><div id="s-lg-link-desc-38398679" class="s-lg-link-desc">from the University of Southern California</div>

			<div id="s-lg-content-38398747" class=""><span><a href="https://msu.edu/user/mkennedy/digitaladvisor/Research/guidetoobservations.htm" target="_blank"  onclick="return springSpace.springTrack.trackLink({link: this,_st_type_id: '2',_st_content_id: '38398747'});">Eight Important Features of Observational Research</a>

				<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana; font-size: 12px;">Think about how you might alter the scene being observed.</p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana; font-size: 12px;">&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;Will you participate in the event?</p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana; font-size: 12px;">&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;Will you interact with the people around you?</p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana; font-size: 12px;">How will you record what you observe?</p>

<p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana; font-size: 12px;">Should you give the people involved time to adjust to your presence?</p>
