				<div class="post-header">
					
				    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=1127" rel="bookmark" title="J&amp;J Corporate Science Writing, PR, and Communications Internship">J&amp;J Corporate Science Writing, PR, and Communications Internship</a></h2>
				    
				    <div class="post-meta">
					
						<span class="post-date"><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?p=1127" title="J&amp;J Corporate Science Writing, PR, and Communications Internship">April 1, 2014</a></span>
						
						<span class="date-sep"> / </span>
							
						<span class="post-author"><a href="http://mediacom.internship.blogs.muhlenberg.edu/?author=87" title="Posts by skahlenb" rel="author">skahlenb</a></span>
						
						<span class="date-sep"> / </span>
						
						<span>Comments Off<span class="screen-reader-text"> on J&amp;J Corporate Science Writing, PR, and Communications Internship</span></span>						
																		
					</div> <!-- /post-meta -->
				    
				</div> <!-- /post-header -->
													                                    	    
				<div class="post-content">
					    		            			            	                                                                                            
					<p><b>The J&amp;J Science Writing, Public Relations and Communications Internship</b> <b>Program</b> is designed to provide a real-world opportunity for up to five high-performing undergraduate or graduate students interested in a career that joins together science and corporate communications, writing and public relations.   Selected interns will work side-by-side with J&amp;J corporate communications professionals in developing and executing strategic public and media relations that support J&amp;J research activities.  Through such work, selected interns will gain an understanding of the responsibilities as well as the skills necessary in pharmaceutical, medical device and product communications involving public and media relations that engage stakeholders including journalists, health care professionals, researchers, employees and patients.</p>
<p><b>Program Description</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Duration of 10 to 12 weeks between May and August</li>
<li>Paid internship (travel and relocation expenses are not provided)</li>
<li>Located in Raritan and New Brunswick, N.J., depending on internship.</li>
<li>Managed by the J&amp;J corporate communications professionals staff</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Student Qualifications:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Junior, senior or 2014 graduate of an accredited four-year undergraduate institution or current student of an accredited graduate institution</li>
<li>3.0 GPA minimum</li>
<li>Major in science journalism; or major in the sciences or public health with completed courses in either journalism or public relations; or major in journalism, public relations or communications with at least four semesters of completed courses in the sciences or public health.</li>
<li>Excellent written and oral communications skills</li>
<li>Demonstrated leadership and teamwork skills</li>
<li>Desire for career in science journalism or science communications</li>
<li>18 years old prior to May 16, 2014</li>
<li>Legally authorized to work in the United States, without immigration sponsorship</li>
<li>Pass a drug screening test</li>
<li>Travel and housing are the responsibility of the intern.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Application process</b>:</p>
<p>Internships begin the week of May 19 (exceptions may be made at the discretion of the J&amp;J communications team) and will run 10 to 12 weeks, based on mutual agreement of the J&amp;J communications team and the selected intern.</p>
<p>Applicants should provide PDFs of the following to Devynne Scilingo at Janssen Research and Development, LLC via email to dsciling@its.jnj.com, <b>by Friday, April 11, 2014</b>:</p>
<ol>
<li>Resume</li>
<li>Letter of reference from the academic advisor for their major</li>
<li>Letter of reference from a previous employer, supervisor or faculty member</li>
<li>Two science-writing samples (please not academic papers)</li>
</ol>
<p><b>Applications will be reviewed based on qualifications and experience.  Candidates selected for interviews will be notified via email, and interviews will be held via phone the week of April 28 or earlier.  Selected interns will be notified on or before Monday, May 5, 2014.  </b>Due to the number of applicants, there is no guarantee of placement into this program.</p>
